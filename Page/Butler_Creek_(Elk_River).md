BUTLER CREEK is a stream in northwestern Benton County, Arkansas and southwestern McDonald County, Missouri.[1] It is a tributary of the Elk River.

The stream headwaters are in Arkansas east-southeast of Sulphur Springs at an elevation of about (). The stream flows west northwest past Sulphur Springs and turns to the north as it enters Missouri. Its confluence with the Elk is on the west side of Noel at an elevation of ().[2][3]

Butler Creek has the name of Charles Butler, a pioneer citizen.[4]
