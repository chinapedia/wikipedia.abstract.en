_EUCALYPTUS HELIDONICA_ is a eucalypt that is native to Queensland.[1]


Description

The tree typically grows to a height of around and forms a lignotuber. It has grey or grey-brown bark and finely fibrous bark over the trunk and the larger branches which become smooth on the smaller branches.[2] The dull green concolorous adult leaves taper evenly to the petiole had have a pointed apex. The leaf blade has a lanceolate to falcate shape with a length of and a width of . It is not known when this species flowers but it produces axillary unbranched inflorescences with white flowers.[3]


Taxonomy

The species was first formally described by the botanist Kenneth Hill in 1999 in the work _A taxonomic revision of the White Mahogonies, Eucalyptus series Acmenoideae (Myrtaceae)_ as published in the journal _Telopea_.[4]

_E. helidonica_ is part of the white mahogany group as recognised by Ken Hill, others in the group include; _Eucalyptus acmenoides_, _Eucalyptus mediocris_, _Eucalyptus carnea_, _Eucalyptus apothalassica_, _Eucalyptus latisinensis_, _Eucalyptus psammitica_ and _Eucalyptus umbra_.[5]


Distribution

It is endemic to a small area surrounding the town of Helidon to the west of Brisbane amongst the Great Dividing Range. It is found as a part of dry sclerophyll woodland communities where it is found on sandstone ridges and slopes growing in sandy soils.
