BODANONES (KADAMGIRI)(STATE) is a village and former non-salute princely state on Saurashtra peninsula in Gujarat, western India.


History

Bodanones (kadamgiri)state was a single princely state under a Kamliya (Kamaliya) chieftain.

It had a population of 136 in 1901, yielding a state revenue of 1,200 Rupees (mostly from land; 1903-4) and paying 112 Rupees tribute, to the Gaekwar Baroda State and to Junagadh State.

During the British Raj, the petty state in Gohelwar _prant_ was under the colonial Eastern Kathiawar Agency.
