FRANCESCA SEGAL (born 1980) is a British author and journalist. She was brought up in a Jewish community in north-west London where she still lives today.[1] She is best known for her novel, _The Innocents_, which won several book awards.[2] She is the daughter of American author, Erich Segal.[3]


Career

Segal studied at St Hugh's College, Oxford before becoming an author and journalist. Her writing has been published in many places including The Guardian, American Vogue, British Vogue, The Observer, and the Financial Times.[4]

Her first novel, _The Innocents_ was published in 2012 and is based in a Jewish community, similar to the one in which she grew up in.[5] It won several awards and was also longlisted for the 2013 Baileys Women's Prize for Fiction.[6]

Segal's second novel, _The Awkward Age_ was published in May 2017.[7] Her third novel, _Mother Ship_ is a memoir and due to be released in June 2019. It follows Segal in the aftermath of the premature birth of her twin daughters and their time spent in hospital.[8]


Awards

-   2012 - Costa Award for a first novel for _The Innocents_[9]
-   2013 - Betty Trask Award for _The Innocents_[10]
-   2013 - Sami Rohr Prize for Jewish Literature for _The Innocents_[11]
-   2013 - Harold U. Ribalow Prize for _The Innocents_[12]
