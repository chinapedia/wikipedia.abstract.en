In mathematics, the THREE SPHERES INEQUALITY bounds the L² norm of a harmonic function on a given sphere in terms of the L² norm of this function on two spheres, one with bigger radius and one with smaller radius.


Statement of the three spheres inequality

Let u be an harmonic function on ℝ^(n). Then for all 0 < r₁ < r < r₂ one has

∥_u_∥_(_L_²(_S__(_r_))) ≤ ∥_u_∥_(_L_²(_S__(_r_₁)))^(_α_)∥_u_∥_(_L_²(_S__(_r_₂)))^(1 − _α_)
where S_(ρ) := {x ∈ ℝ^(n): |x| = ρ} for ρ > 0 is the sphere of radius ρ centred at the origin and where

$$\alpha:=\frac{\log(r_2/r)}{\log(r_2/r_1)}.$$
Here we use the following normalisation for the L² norm:

∥_u_∥_(_L_²(_S__(_ρ_)))² := _ρ_^(1 − _n_)∫_(𝕊^(_n_ − 1))|_u_(_ρ__x̂_)|² _d__σ_(_x̂_).
