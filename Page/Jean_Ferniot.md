JEAN FERNIOT (1918–2012) was a French journalist and novelist. He won the Prix Interallié in 1961.


Early life

Jean Ferniot was born on 10 October 1918 in Paris, France.[1] He grew up in the 14th arrondissement of Paris.[2] He became an orphan at the age of eight, when his mother died.[3] He was raised as a Roman Catholic, and he considered becoming a priest as a young man.[4]

Ferniot was educated at the Lycée Louis-le-Grand in Paris.[5] He briefly served in World War II,[6] and he was subsequently awarded the Croix de Guerre.[7]


Career

Ferniot was a journalist. He began his career at the Agence France-Presse.[8] He subsequently worked for _Franc-Tireur_ and _France Soir_.[9] He then joined _L'Express_ as a political and culinary columnist.[10] He was also a contributor to _Radio Luxembourg_ until 1983.[11]

Ferniot was the author of several books. He won the Prix Interallié for _L’Ombre portée_ in 1961.[12] He was a Commander of the Ordre des Arts et des Lettres.[13]


Personal life and death

Ferniot was married three times.[14] With his second wife, journalist Christiane Collange, he had two sons, Vincent Ferniot and Simon Ferniot. He has five children.[15]

Ferniot died on 21 July 2012 in Paris.[16] He was 93 years old.[17]
