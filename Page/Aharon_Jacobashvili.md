AHARON JACOBASHVILI (also AARON and JAKOBASHVILI; אהרון יעקבשווילי; born September 18, 1964) is an Israeli former Olympic boxer.[1][2][3]

He was born in Georgia, and is Jewish.[4]


Boxing career

He competed for Israel at the 1988 Summer Olympics in Seoul, South Korea, at the age of 23.[5][6] He boxed in the Men's Middleweight competition, losing on points to West German Sven Ottke (who later was the IBF super-middleweight world champion from 1998 to 2004, and the WBA super-middleweight world champion from 2003 to 2004), and came in tied for 32nd.[7][8] When he boxed in the Olympics he was 5-11 (181 cm) tall, and weighed 165 lbs (75 kg).[9]
