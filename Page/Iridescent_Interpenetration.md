_IRIDESCENT INTERPENETRATION'' (_'') is the title of several artworks and studies in a series by Italian Futurist painter Giacomo Balla, created between 1912 and 1914, which feature intersecting triangles and other geometric patterns in kaleidoscopic color.

In _Iridescent Interpenetration_, Balla attempts to separate the experience of light from the perception of objects as such, in an approach he had experimented with in _Welcome to Düsseldorf_. The works suggest an extension of the pictured surface beyond the borders of the frame.[1]

The earliest known study in the series was on a postcard which Balla mailed to his friend and student Gino Galli on November 21, 1912. He referred to the images as __ ('spectrum' or 'rainbow').[2]
