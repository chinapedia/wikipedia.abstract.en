JOSEPH OLUWASEYI TEMITOPE AYODELE-ARIBO (born 21 July 1996) is an English professional footballer who plays as a midfielder for Rangers.


Club career

Early career

Aribo played for Staines Town under Marcus Gayle in the Isthmian League Premier Division, winning the club's academy player of the year award in April 2014.[1]

Charlton Athletic

Aribo joined Charlton Athletic in September 2015 following a successful trial, and signed a one-year contract in May 2016.[2] He made his first team debut as a 62nd-minute substitute for Andrew Crofts in a 2–0 defeat to Crawley Town in an EFL Trophy group stage match at The Valley on 16 October 2016.[3]

Rangers

Aribo was offered a new contract by Charlton at the end of the 2018–19 season,[4] but he instead opted to sign a four-year deal with Scottish Premiership club Rangers.[5] He made his competitive debut for the club on 9 July 2019 in a 4–0 win over St Joseph's of Gibraltar in the Europa League.[6]


Style of play

Speaking in October 2016, Aribo said that his strengths were "driving with the ball and getting up the pitch".[7]
