The following is a timeline of the history of the city of Guantánamo, Cuba.


19th century

-   1822 - Town established.
-   1856 - Ferrocarril de Guantánamo (railway) begins operating (approximate date).[1]
-   1899
    -   _La Voz del Pueblo_ newspaper begins publication.[2]
    -   Population: 7,137 city; 28,063 district; 327,715 province.[3]


20th century

-   1903 - U.S. military Guantanamo Bay Naval Base established near city.
-   1907 - Population: 14,559 city; 43,300 municipality; 455,086 province.[4]
-   1919
    -   Teatro Fausto built.[5]
    -   Population: 68,883.[6]
-   1955 - Local MR-26-7 political group active.[7]
-   1957 - Teatro Luisa opens.[8]
-   1964 - Population: 122,400.[9]
-   1998 - Roman Catholic Diocese of Guantánamo-Baracoa established.[10]
-   1999 - Population: 208,030 city; 512,300 province.[11]


21st century

-   2002 - U.S. military Guantanamo Bay detention camp established near city.[12]
-   2014 - Population: 217,978.[13]
