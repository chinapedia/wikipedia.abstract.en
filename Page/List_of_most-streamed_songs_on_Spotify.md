Ed_Sheeran_2013.jpg" by Ed Sheeran (pictured) is the most streamed song on Spotify with over 2 billion streams.]] This list contains the top 100 songs with the most streams on the audio streaming platform Spotify. As of July 2019, Ed Sheeran's "Shape of You" is ranked in the top position,[1] as the only song with more than 2 billion streams.[2] As many as 36 songs have exceeded 1 billion streams.

Prior to February 2014, Spotify's most-streamed song was "Radioactive" by Imagine Dragons;[3] it was overtaken by Avicii's "Wake Me Up", which became the first song to hit 200 million streams on 27 February 2014.[4] "Wake Me Up" held the streaming record for over a year before being surpassed on 22 May 2015 by Sheeran's "Thinking Out Loud",[5] which became the first song to hit 500 million streams on 12 October 2015.[6] A month later, however, it was surpassed by Major Lazer and DJ Snake's "Lean On", featuring MØ,[7] which held the streaming record for 11 months until Drake's "One Dance", featuring Wizkid and Kyla, overtook it on 18 October 2016.[8] "One Dance" became the first song to 1 billion streams 12 December 2016,[9] and held the streaming record for almost a year before being surpassed by "Shape of You" on 21 September 2017.[10]

The artists with the most singles in the top 100 are Justin Bieber with a total of 8 (3 as a lead artist and 5 as a featured artist), Post Malone with 6 (all lead), and Drake with 5 (4 lead and 1 featured). The fastest song to reach 1 billion streams on Spotify is "Shape of You", doing so in 153 days,[11] and it is also the fastest and only song to reach 2 billion streams, doing so in 702 days.[12] Queen's "Bohemian Rhapsody" from 1975 is the only song in the Spotify Top 100 that was released in the 20th century.

The albums with the most songs in the top 100 are Bieber's _Purpose_ with 4 entries, and Post Malone's _Beerbongs & Bentleys_ and Dua Lipa's _Dua Lipa_ with 3 entries each.
