GIOVANNI TITTA ROSA (5 March 1891 â€“ 7 January 1972) was an Italian literary critic, poet, and novelist.

Born in Santa Maria del Ponte, a frazione of L'Aquila, Titta Rosa graduated in letters at the University of Florence and then started collaborating as a literary critic with a large number of publications, notably _Lacerba_, _La Stampa_, _Il Secolo XIX_, _Il Resto del Carlino_ and _Corriere della Sera_.[1][2][3]

Titta Rosa was also author of novels, essays and collections of poems.[4][5][6] In 1931 he was awarded the Bagutta Prize for his collection of short stories _Il varco nel muro_.[7]
