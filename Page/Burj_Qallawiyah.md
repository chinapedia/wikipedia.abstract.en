BURJ QALLAWIYAH () is a village in the Bint Jbeil District in southern Lebanon.


Name

According to E. H. Palmer, the name _Burj el Alawei_ means "the tower of the Alawei".[1]


History

In 1881, the Palestine Exploration Fund's _Survey of Western Palestine_ (SWP) described Burj Alawei as: “A village, built of stone, containing about 300 Metawileh, situated on upland, with a few olives, figs, and some arable land round. The water supply is from a well and spring.”[2]
