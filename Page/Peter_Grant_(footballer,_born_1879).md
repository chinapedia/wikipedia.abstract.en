PETER GUNN GRANT (3 June 1879 – 10 December 1937) was a Scottish amateur football full back who played in the Scottish League for Motherwell, Hamilton Academical and Queen's Park.[1]


Personal life

In 1917, three years into the First World War, Grant joined the French Army's Special Ambulance Service as a driver/mechanic.[2] He was twice awarded the Croix de Guerre for "outstanding acts of bravery".[3] Grant died in the Castlecary rail accident on 10 December 1937.[4]
