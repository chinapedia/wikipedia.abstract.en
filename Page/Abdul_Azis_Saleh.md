Dr. ABDUL AZIS SALEH, a specialist in anthropology and education, was a leader of Indonesian Scouting and served as a member of the World Scout Committee and President of the World Organization of the Scout Movement Asia-Pacific Region.[1]

He became involved in the creation of the Gerakan Pramuka Indonesia in 1961 and was a member of the "Group of 5" mandated by President Sukarno, who took the opportunity of the absence of the Minister of Education to build a non-communist youth association.[2] In 1970, he became Gerakan Pramuka Secretary General.

In 1978, he was awarded the 129th _Bronze Wolf_, the only distinction of the WOSM, awarded by the World Scout Committee for exceptional services to world Scouting.[3]
