BAI ANDREW ANTONIOU (born 4 June 1997) is an Australian professional footballer who plays as a forward for Sutherland Sharks.


Club career

Antoniou played junior football for Sutherland Sharks before joining Sydney FC.

He finished third highest league scorer in the 2016 NPL NSW 2 season with 20 goals.[1]

In 2016, he was promoted to the Sydney FC first team alongside Charles Lokolingoy. On 10 August 2016, Antoniou made his official debut for Sydney, coming off the bench for George Blackwood in 3â€“0 win over Wollongong Wolves in the FFA Cup.[2]

Antoniou signed with Cypriot First Division side Alki Oroklini in mid 2018.[3] He moved to Cypriot Second Division side Onisilos Sotira on loan in January 2019.

After unsuccessful stint in Cyprus, he returned to play semi professional football in the NPL NSW for Sutherland Sharks.


International career

Antoniou has represented Australia under-17, and scored a goal in a loss to Iran in the quarterfinals of the 2012 AFC U-16 Championship.[4]
