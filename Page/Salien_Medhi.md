SALIEN MEDHI (d. 2016) was an Indian lawyer and politician, belonging to the Revolutionary Communist Party of India.[1][2] He was a state legislator in Assam 1967-1972.


Early career

Medhi entered the Guwahati High Court in 1962.[3] His legal career developed rapidly thereafter. He became a prominent member of the Guwahati High Court Bar Association.[4] As a RCPI leader he was closely linked to Kalaguru Bishnu Prasad Rabha.[5] Medhi helped Rabha during the latter's underground days.[6]


Legislator

Medhi was elected to the Assam Legislative Assembly from the Jalukbari constituency in the 1967 election.[7] He got 14,384 votes (61.60%).[8] Medhi lost the Jalukbari seat in the 1972 Assam Legislative Assembly election. He finished in second place with 5,764 votes (25.09%).[9]


Later years

At different occasions he represented the Bar Association at meetings overseas.[10] During his trips abroad he met with personalities such as Fidel Castro and Nelson Mandela.[11]

During his later years, Medhi was an active participant in the meetings of the Octogenarians' Club of Guwahati.[12] Salien Medhi died on July 10, 2016 at the age of 87.[13]
