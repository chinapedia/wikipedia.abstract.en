LALIYAD (or LALIAD) is a village and former non-salute Rajput princely state on Saurashtra peninsula in Gujarat, western India.


History

The petty princely state, in Jhalawar _prant_, was ruled by Jhala Rajput Chieftains. In 1901 it comprised only the village, with a population of 755, yielding 6,000 Rupees state revenue (1903-4, only from land), paying 362 Rupees tribute, to the British.[1]
