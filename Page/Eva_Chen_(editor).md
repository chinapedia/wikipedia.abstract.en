EVA CHEN (born 1980) is the director of fashion partnerships at Instagram[1] and a children's book author[2]. Previously she was editor-in-chief of _Lucky_[3] and beauty and health director at _Teen Vogue_.[4]


Early life

Chen grew up in New York. Her parents, who are originally from Taipei and Shanghai, own a consulting textile import-export business, and Chen attributes her early love of fashion to her mother's influence.[5] Chen attended the Brearley School,[6] then went to college at Johns Hopkins University where she was pre-med.[7]


Career

In college, Chen interned for fashion magazine _Harper's Bazaar_. After graduating from Hopkins in 2001, she worked briefly for a law firm before joining the then-recently launched shopping magazine _Lucky_, where she worked in the credits department logging the prices and sellers for the magazine's array of products and eventually became editor-in-chief. She next moved to _Elle_ where she worked in the beauty department for three years before she became beauty editor of _Teen Vogue_ [8]

At _Lucky_, Chen was the youngest editor-in-chief in the magazine's history.[9]. She also became the first head of a commercial arm for the publication, Lucky Shops, serving as both editor-in-chief for the magazine and chief creative officer[10].

Eva_Chen_and_Derek_Desierto_at_BookCon_(16316).jpg in 2019]] In July 2015, Chen joined Instagram, owned by Facebook, to develop partnerships with fashion brands[11]. She is presently Head of Fashion Partnerships for the social media platform, and has launched a number of fashion and e-commerce features at Instagram, including shopping fashion looks directly from influencers' feeds [12] and the Instagram Shop account [13].

Chen is also a book author [14] and has written two children's books, both illustrated by Derek Desierto: _Juno Valentine and the Magical Shoes_ (2018) and _A Is for Awesome: 23 Iconic Women Who Changed the World_ (2019). Her third book, _Juno Valentine and the Fantastic Fashion Adventure_ is also illustrated by Desierto and will be released in November 2019.


Personal life

In 2000, Chen studied abroad for a year in England at Oxford, where she met her husband, Thomas Bannister.[15] Chen and Bannister have two children together, Ren and Tao, born in 2015 and 2017.[16]
