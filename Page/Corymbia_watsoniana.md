_CORYMBIA WATSONIANA_, commonly known as the LARGE-FRUITED YELLOWJACKET[1] or WATSON'S YELLOW BLOODWOOD',[2] is a bloodwood native to Queensland.[3]


Taxonomy

The species was first formally described by the botanist Ferdinand von Mueller in 1877 as _Eucalyptus watsoniana_ as part of the work _Fragmenta Phytographiae Australiae_ It was reclassified as _Corymbia watsoniana_ in 1995 by Kenneth Hill and Lawrence Alexander Sidney Johnson in 1995 as part of the work _Systematic studies in the eucalypts. A revision of the bloodwoods, genus Corymbia (Myrtaceae)_ as published in the journal _Telopea_.[4]
