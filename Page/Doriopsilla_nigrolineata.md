_DENDRODORIS KREBSII_ is a species of sea slug, a dorid nudibranch, a marine gastropod mollusc in the family Dendrodorididae.


Taxonomy

It was previously considered a subspecies of _Doriopsilla areolata_ Bergh, 1880 by Valdés & Ortea (1997),[1] but Valdés & Hamann (2008)[2] confirmed that it is a distinct species.[3]


Distribution

Distribution of _Doriopsilla nigrolineata_ includes Panama and Honduras.[4]


Description

The shape of the body is oval to elongate.[5] Dorsum is rigid and covered with rounded tubercles.[6] Background color is translucent white to orange, with a series of irregular black lines over the entire dorsum.[7] Bases of tubercles are densely spotted with white.[8] Rhinophores and gill are yellow.[9] The maximum recorded body length is 30 mm.[10][11]


Ecology

Minimum recorded depth is 3 m.[12] Maximum recorded depth is 12 m.[13] It was found in the depth of 3–6 m of water in Panama.[14]
