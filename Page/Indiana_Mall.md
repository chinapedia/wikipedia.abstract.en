INDIANA MALL is a one-level indoor regional mall located in Indiana, Pennsylvania. It has of retail space, and has over 50 stores. It is located on the western edge of town near the intersection of Oakland Ave (PA 286) and Indian Springs Road (US 422 Business).

Anchor tenants are J. C. Penney and Harbor Freight, with two vacant anchors last occupied by Sears and Kmart.[1] Old Navy joined the mall in 2006,[2] and closed six years later.[3] The bon-ton closed in 2018.

The mall's movie theater, formerly owned by Carmike Cinemas, was sold to Golden Star Theaters in 2015.[4]

On June 6, 2017, Sears Holdings announced that the Kmart store would be closing as part of a plan to close 72 stores nationwide. The store closed in October 2017. Kmart was expanded in 1992.

On November 2, 2017, Sears Holdings announced that the Sears store would also be closing as part of a plan to close 63 stores nationwide. The store closed in January 2018.
