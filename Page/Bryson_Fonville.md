BRYSON FONVILLE (born May 26, 1994[1]) is an American professional basketball player who last played for the Texas Legends of the NBA G League.[2][3] He played college basketball for the Catholic University Cardinals. He was drafted as the ninth pick of the fifth round of the 2016 D-League draft.[4]


Early life

Fonville, the son of Kim and Tracie Fonville, is from Greensboro, North Carolina.[5][6] He has an older sister.[7]

He attended Walter Hines Page Senior High School where he was a three-year starter on the basketball team.[8] During his senior year, he averaged 13 points, 7 assists and 2 steals a game, and he reached the Regional Final of the state tournament.[9] He was a first Team All-Conference selection and helped his team with the conference champions as a junior and senior.[10] Fonville was also a member of National Honor Society.[11]

As a player in the Amateur Athletic Union league, Fonville played for the Greensboro Warriors and Greensboro Galaxy. With the Warriors Fonville placed eighth at nationals.[12] During high school he was 5'11 and weighed 160 pounds,[13] and had offers to attend Division II colleges.[14]


College career

Fonville is one of the most decorated men's basketball players in the history of The Catholic University of America.[15] He started in every game except for one in the 2014-2105 season.[16] As a four-year starter he scored 1,649 career points, placing him in the top-10 in University history.[17] His total steals also places him in the top-four.[18] He competed in the NCAA Tournament three times.[19]

As a student, he majored in business management.[20]

Senior year

During his senior year he led the team to the Landmark Conference Championship[21] after starting all 29 games of the season where he averaged 34.8 minutes per game.[22] Fonville led the team with 178 shots made from the field, shooting 43.3% on the season.[23] He had a total of 486 points on that season, averaging 16.8 points per game.[24] Shooting from the free throw line, he sank 78% of his shots and hit and 32.1% from the 3-pt line.[25]

Fonville had 34 offensive and 92 defensive rebounds that season, leading the team with 155 assists and 52 steals.[26] During the game against Alvernia University in the Division III NCAA tournament,[27] he led team with career high 38 points.

Honors and awards

Fonville was named an All-American by several media outlets in 2015 and 2016 and was the 2015 Landmark Conference Player of the Year.[28] He was twice names a Landmark Champion and was a three-time regular season champion.[29] In 2015, the CUA Athletic Department named him Outstanding Senior Student-Athlete.[30]

He was named NABC third-team All-American, a D3Hoops.com fourth-team All-American, a DIII News fourth-team All-American, and an ECAC Division III South All-Star first team.[31] In addition, Fonville earned a spot on the Middle Atlantic All-District first team, the D3Hoops.com All-Middle Atlantic Region first team, the first-team All-Landmark Conference, and was the Most Valuable Player of the CUA-Holiday Inn College Park Classic.[32]


Professional career

On October 30, 2016, Fonville was drafted as the ninth pick of the fifth round of the NBA Development League 2016 draft by the Texas Legends.[33]
