He was the first Catholic and first member of the country's among Christian community to become a government minister in Bangladesh. Later, his son, Jewel Areng, elected MP after his death in his constituency.[2]


Early life and career

Mankin was born on 18 July 1938 at Ramnagar village in Bakalijora Union, Durgapur Upazila, Netrokona to Megha Tozu and Hridoy Cecelia Mankin. He joined Awami League in 1991. He was the President of Bangladesh Christian Association.[3] He was elected Member of Parliament from Mymensingh-1 four times.[4] He was a member of the Garo community. He fought in the Bangladesh liberation war.[5]


Personal life and death

On 29 January 1964, Mankin married Momota Areng. They had five daughters and one son.[6] He died on 11 May 2016 at Holy Family Hospital in Mumbai, India.[7]
