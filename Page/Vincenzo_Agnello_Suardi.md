VINCENZO AGNELLO SUARDI (1582 – September 1644) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Mantua (1620–1644) and Bishop of Alba (1616–1620).[1]


Biography

Vincenzo Agnello Suardi was born in Mantua, Italy in 1582.[2] On 5 December 1616, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul V as Bishop of Alba.[3] On 21 December 1616, he was consecrated bishop by Giovanni Garzia Mellini, Cardinal-Priest of Santi Quattro Coronati, with Ulpiano Volpi, Archbishop Emeritus of Chieti, and Francesco Sacrati (cardinal), Titular Archbishop of _Damascus_. serving as co-consecrators.[4] On 13 May 1619, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul V as Bishop of Mantua; and succeeded on 2 March 1620.[5] He served as Bishop of Mantua until his death in September 1644.[6] While bishop, he was the principal co-consecrator of Ludovico Gonzaga (bishop), Bishop of Alba (1619); and Scipione Agnelli, Bishop of Casale Monferrato (1624).[7]
