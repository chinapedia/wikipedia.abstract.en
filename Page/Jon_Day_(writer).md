JON DAY is a British writer, critic and academic. He teaches English at King's College London.[1] His essays and reviews have appeared in the _London Review of Books_,[2] _n+1_,[3] the _New York Review of Books,_ _The Times Literary Supplement,_[4] and _The Guardian_.[5] He is also a regular fiction critic for _The Daily Telegraph_[6] and the _Financial Times_,[7] and writes about art for _Apollo_ magazine.[8]

Day's first book, _Cyclogeography_, a philosophical memoir about the years he spent as a London bicycle courier, was published in 2015 to critical acclaim.[9][10][11][12][13][14][15][16] His second, _Homing_, was published in 2019.[17] He was a judge for the 2016 Man Booker Prize,[18] and for the 2019 Wellcome Book Prize.[19]
