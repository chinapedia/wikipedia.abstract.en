__NOTOC__ POINT LABATT AQUATIC RESERVE is a marine protected area in the Australian state of South Australia located in the waters of the Great Australian Bight adjoining the west coast of Eyre Peninsula at the headland of Point Labatt.[1][2]

It was proclaimed under the _Fisheries Act 1982_ in 1988 and was re-proclaimed in 2007 following the enactment of the _Fisheries Management Act 2007_. The creation of the aquatic reserve was for the purpose of protection of a breeding colony of Australian sea lion, a species protected under the _Fisheries Management Act 2007_ and scheduled as ‘rare’ under the _National Parks and Wildlife Act 1972_. This purpose is achieved via the prohibition of public access as well as any activity involving fishing or the collection and removal of marine organisms. Since 2012, it has been located within the boundaries of a ‘restricted access zone’ within the West Coast Bays Marine Park.[6]

The aquatic reserve is classified as an IUCN Category Ia protected area.[7]
