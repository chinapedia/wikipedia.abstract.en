YAIR PANTILAT (also, Pantillat; יאיר פנטילט; born January 10, 1939) is an Israeli former Olympic runner.[1] He was the Israeli Men's Champion in the 800 metres in 1960, 1964–65, and 1967–69, and in the 1500 metres in 1961-62 and 1964-66.

He was born in Tel Aviv, Israel, and is Jewish.[2][3]


Running career

His personal bests are 1:51.3 in the 800 metres (in 1967) and 3:52.4 in the 1500 metre run (in 1967).[4] He was the Israeli Men's Champion in the 800 metres in 1960, 1964–65, and 1967–69, and in the 1500 metres in 1961-62 and 1964-66.[5]

He competed for Israel at the 1960 Summer Olympics in Rome, Italy, in Athletics at the age of 21.[6] In the Men's 800 metres he came in 5th in Heat 1 with a time of 1:54.7 (an Israeli record), and in the Men's 1,500 metres he came in 12th in Heat 3 with a time of 3:59.8 (an Israeli record).[7][8] When he competed in the Olympics, he was 5-9.5 (177 cm) tall, and weighed 150 lbs (68 kg).[9] He was named Israel's Sportsman of the Year in 1960.[10]
