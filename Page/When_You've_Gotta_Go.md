"WHEN YOU'VE GOTTA GO" was written by Lynsey de Paul (credited to her birth name, Lynsey Rubin) and Ron Roker.[1] It was recorded by Solomon King at 10cc's Strawberry Studios and released as a single in 1972.[2] The single was a chart hit in Australia,[3] and also spent four weeks on the Dutch "Tipparade", peaking at #17.[4]

Another version of the song was also released as a single in Australia by Jay Justin (a regular on national TV shows _Bandstand_ and _the Johnny O'Keefe Show_),[5] in November 1973 on the RCA label.[6][7]

The song was covered by German artist and producer Frank Farian in 1973 as "Was kann schöner sein", with German lyrics by Fred Jay and released as a single[8][9] as well as a track on his 1973 album, _So Muß Liebe Sein_.[10] He performed the song on the German TV programme _ZDF Hitparade_ on 17 February, 1973 and it reached No. 48 on the German singles chart on the 16 March that year.[11] His version of the song was featured on the 2009 CD compilation album, _Frank Farian – Das beste aus 40 Jahren ZDF Hitparade_[12] and is listed as one of Farian's song highlights by "AllMusic".[13] It was also recorded as a track by Jürgen Marcus on his album, _Ein Festival Der Liebe_.[14] The duo Wyn Hoop & Andrea Horn also recorded "Was kann schöner sein" and released it as a single in Austria and Germany on the Decca Record label.[15][16][17]

A Finnish version of the song entitled "Kun Mentävä On" by the artist Danny was released as the B-side to his single "Yksinäinen Ilta",[18] as well as the title track on his 1973 album.[19] A Spanish version of the song with the title "Adios Amigo" (albeit with lyrics partially in English) was also recorded and released as a single by the pop group Los Albas in 1972,[20] as well as a track on their self-titled album in 1974.[21] A French language version with lyrics by J.-C. Deseure entitled "Le Temps De Vivre" was recorded by French group Virus and released as a single in 1973.

In 1974, it was released by the British vocalist, Ricki Disoni (aka Gordon Waddison), a regularly appearing artist at venues such as The Night Out Theatre Restaurant), as a track on his UK album, _This Is Ricki Disoni_.[22]
