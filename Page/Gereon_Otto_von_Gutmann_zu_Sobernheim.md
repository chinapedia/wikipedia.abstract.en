GEREON OTTO VON GUTMANN ZU SOBERNHEIM (1571 – 25 September 1638) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Auxiliary Bishop of Cologne (1616–1638).[1][2]


Biography

Gereon Otto von Gutmann zu Sobernheim was born in Koblenz, Germany in 1571 and ordained a priest in 1598.[3][4] On 30 August 1606, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul V as Auxiliary Bishop of Cologne and Titular Bishop of _Cyrene_.[5][6] On 9 October 1616, he was consecrated bishop by Antonio Albergati, Bishop of Bisceglie.[7] He served as Auxiliary Bishop of Cologne until his death on 25 September 1638.[8][9]

While bishop, he was the principal consecrator of Johannes Pelking, Auxiliary Bishop of Paderborn (1620); and the principal co-consecrator of Otto von Senheim, Auxiliary Bishop of Trier (1634).[10]
