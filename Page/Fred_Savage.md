FRED AARON SAVAGE (born July 9, 1976)[1] is an American actor, director, and producer.[2] He is best known for his role as Kevin Arnold in the American television series _The Wonder Years_, which ran from 1988 to 1993.[3] He has earned several awards and nominations, such as People's Choice Awards and Young Artist Awards. He is also known for playing the Grandson in _The Princess Bride_.


Early life

Savage was born in Chicago, Illinois, the son of Joanne and Lewis Savage, who was an industrial real estate broker and consultant.[4] Savage grew up in Glencoe, Illinois, before moving to California. His younger brother is actor Ben Savage, and his younger sister is actress/musician Kala Savage. His grandparents were Jewish immigrants from Poland, Ukraine, Germany, and Latvia.[5] He was raised as a Reform Jew.


Education

Savage was educated at Brentwood School, a private co-educational day school in Brentwood, in the Westside area of Los Angeles County in California. He graduated from Stanford University in 1999, with a bachelor's degree in English and as a member of Sigma Alpha Epsilon.


Career

Acting

thumb Savage's first screen performance was in the television show _Morningstar/Eveningstar_, at the age of 9. He then appeared onscreen in _The Boy Who Could Fly_, _Dinosaurs! - A Fun-Filled Trip Back in Time!_, and several television shows, including _The Twilight Zone_ and _Crime Story_ before gaining national attention as the grandson in the 1987 film _The Princess Bride_ opposite Peter Falk.

In 1988, Savage appeared as Kevin Arnold on _The Wonder Years_, the role for which he is best known, and for which he received two Golden Globe nominations and two Emmy[6] nominations for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Comedy Series. At the age of thirteen he was the youngest actor ever to receive these honors. He remained on the show until it ended in 1993. During this period, he appeared in several films, most notably _Vice Versa_ (1988), and also starred in _Little Monsters_. After _The Wonder Years_ ended, Savage returned to high school at age 17, and later attended Stanford. His first TV role after high school was the NBC sitcom _Working_, which Savage starred in for its two-season run.[7] Savage also had a series of guest and supporting roles in the late 1990s and 2000s such as the show _Boy Meets World_ (which starred his younger brother Ben Savage) and in the film _Austin Powers in Goldmember_ as The Mole.

Savage has lent his voice to several animated projects, including _Family Guy_, _Kim Possible_, _Justice League Unlimited_, _Oswald_, and _Holidaze: The Christmas That Almost Didn't Happen_. His two lead roles since _The Wonder Years_ were on the short-lived sitcoms _Working_ and _Crumbs_. He appeared as a serial rapist on a 2003 episode of _Law & Order: Special Victims Unit_ and as a womanizing professor on _Boy Meets World_. He ranked at #27 on VH1's _100 Greatest Kid Stars_.

In July 2008, Savage guest-starred in the web series _The Rascal_ on Crackle.[8]

In 2015, Savage returned to acting with the Fox series _The Grinder_.[9] Producer Nick Stoller approached Savage about playing the role of Stewart on _The Grinder_.[10] Savage was uninterested at first, but agreed to meet with the producers of the series because his children attended school with Stoller's children.[11] Savage eventually agreed to take on the role.[12] _The Grinder_ was canceled by Fox on May 16, 2016.[13]

In 2017, he joined the cast of the Netflix series _Friends From College_ as Max Adler, a gay literary agent.[14]

In 2018, he began to host _Child Support_ (originally called _Five to Survive_) with Ricky Gervais.

In 2019, he will host a show called _What Just Happened??! with Fred Savage_.[15]

Directing and producing

In 1999, Savage began his directing career in which he helmed episodes of over a dozen television series. Savage's first directing credit was on the short-lived NBC sitcom _Working_ which also starred Savage.[16] Following _Working_, Savage began observing production on the Disney Channel show _Even Stevens_ to further learn the craft of directing.[17] Savage also learned by shadowing Amy Sherman-Palladino, Todd Holland, and James Burrows.

His credits include _Boy Meets World_, _Drake & Josh_ and _Ned's Declassified_ for Nickelodeon, as well as _That's So Raven_, _Hannah Montana_, and _Wizards of Waverly Place_ for Disney Channel. Additionally, Savage has directed for prime-time network sitcoms including _Modern Family_ and _2 Broke Girls_.[18]

Besides directing several episodes, Savage co-produced the Disney Channel Original Series _Phil of the Future_. In 2007, he was nominated for a Directors Guild award for the _Phil_ episode "Not-So-Great-Great Grandpa".

Savage has served as a producer for several episodes of _It's Always Sunny in Philadelphia_, _Friends with Benefits_, _Party Down_, _Phil of the Future_, _The Crazy Ones_, and _Happy Endings_.

In 2007, he made his feature film directing debut with the film _Daddy Day Camp_.[19]
