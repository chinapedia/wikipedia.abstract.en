The following is a timeline of the history of the municipality of Delft, Netherlands.


Prior to 18th century

-   1075 - Town established by Godfrey III, Duke of Lower Lorraine.
-   1246 - Delft granted city rights by William II of Holland.[1]
-   1250 - St. Bartholomew's Church construction begins.[2]
-   1383 - Nieuwe Kerk (Delft) (church) construction begins.[3]
-   1389 - Canal to the Meuse river created.[4]
-   1400 - Eastern Gate (Delft) built.[5]
-   1403 - Agathaklooster (convent) established.[6]
-   1470 - Carthusian monastery established.
-   1477 - Printing press in operation; Delft Bible issued.[7][8]
-   1536 - 3 May: .
-   1584 - William I, Prince of Orange assassinated.[9]
-   1586 - Scientists Simon Stevin and Jan Cornets de Groot conduct a landmark experiment on the effects of gravity from the roof of the Nieuwe Kerk.[10]
-   1611 - Guild of Saint Luke founded (approximate date).
-   1618 - City Hall (Delft) rebuilt.
-   1621 - Bank of Delft established.[11]
-   1632 - Future scientist Antonie van Leeuwenhoek and future artist Johannes Vermeer born in Delft.[12]
-   1647 - Production of Delft pottery begins to expand.[13]
-   1652 - Fabritius paints _A View of Delft_ artwork.
-   1653 - Royal Delft porcelain factory begins operating.
-   1654 - 12 October: .
-   1661 - Vermeer paints _View of Delft_ artwork.
-   1692 - Armamentarium built.[14]

20th century
