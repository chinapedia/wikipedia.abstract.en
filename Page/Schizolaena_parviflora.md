_SCHIZOLAENA PARVIFLORA_ is a tree in the family Sarcolaenaceae. It is endemic to Madagascar. The specific epithet __ is from the Latin meaning "small flowers".


Description

_Schizolaena parviflora_ grows as a tree up to tall. Its inflorescences are small and dense. The involucre is glabrous.[1] It is thought to attract lemurs, bats and birds who in turn disperse the tree's seeds. The fruit is considered edible.[2]


Distribution and habitat

_Schizolaena parviflora_ is known only from the northern regions of Diana and Sofia.[3] Its habitat is subhumid forests from sea-level to altitude.[4] Some subpopulations of the species are in protected areas.[5]


Uses

The timber of _Schizolaena parviflora_ is used in construction and as firewood. It is also used to make charcoal.[6]


Threats

_Schizolaena parviflora_ is threatened by deforestation due to shifting patterns of agriculture. The species is also threatened by timber harvesting and wildfires.[7]
