YU LINA 俞丽拿 (born 1940 in Ningbo, China) is an esteemed violinist and most influential pedagogue in China.

In 1959 at Shanghai, she made her debut as violinist at the nationwide celebration of the 10th anniversary of the founding of China, premiered _Butterfly Lovers' Violin Concerto_ and hence gained great success. In the late 1970s, it gained overwhelming popularity for the second time. Her performance presented the Chinese music to the world as well as introducing the exotic western instrument, violin, to many Chinese people. The album of _Butterfly Lovers' Violin Concerto_ presented by Yu Lina is estimated to have sold over 1 millions copies worldwide. She is currently professor of violin at Shanghai Conservatory of Music.

Her educational career spans over 50 years in China and her students occupy influential positions throughout the music world and won international competition prize constantly. As a distinguished soloist and one of the most influential pedagogues, she motivated millions people to study western classical music and hence influenced generations of Chinese people.

Yu Lina has made invaluable contribution to the western classical music education in China.[1]
