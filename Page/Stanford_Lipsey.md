Early life

Stanford Lipsey was born on October 8, 1927 in Omaha, Nebraska.[1][2][3] His parents, Molly Brick and Jacob Lipsey, were Jewish Russian immigrants.[4]

Lipsey was educated at the Omaha Central High School.[5] He graduated from the University of Michigan in 1948.[6]


Career

Lipsey was the founder of the Sun Newspapers, a publisher of newspapers in Omaha, which he sold to Warren Buffett in the 1960s.[7] He was an early investor in Berkshire Hathaway.[8] He served as the publisher of the _Buffalo News_ from 1983 to 2012.[9]


Personal life and death

Lipsey was married twice: first to Jeanne Blacker, and secondly to Judith Hojnacki.[10] He had a son and a daughter from his first marriage.[11] He resided in Rancho Mirage, California, where he died on November 1, 2016.[12][13]
