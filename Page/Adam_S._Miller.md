ADAM S. MILLERis an American author of religious criticism and interpretation, with an focus on contemporary Latter-day Saint lay theology. Miller is also a professor of philosophy at Collin College in McKinney, Texas, where he directs the college's honors program.[1][2][3]

Miller's work offers fresh interpretation on topics such as Christ's atonement,[4] the appropriate relationship of faith and scholarship,[5][6] and the nature of Latter-day Saint testimony.[7] Miller co-founded Salt Press, a publisher of Mormon studies sold to BYU's Maxwell Institute,[8] and also founded and serves as co-director of the annual colloquia, the Mormon Theology Seminar.[9]
