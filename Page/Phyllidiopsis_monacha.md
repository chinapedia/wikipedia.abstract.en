_PHYLLIDIOPSIS MONACHA_ is a species of sea slug, a dorid nudibranch, a shell-less marine gastropod mollusk in the family Phyllidiidae.[1]


Distribution

This species was described from Creek, Jeddah, Red Sea . It is endemic to the Red Sea.[2]


Description

This nudibranch has a white dorsum with a pattern of black and orange. The black pigment forms an irregular ring around the middle of the back with radiating lines extending to the mantle edge. The centre of the back and the edges of the mantle between the black markings have patches of orange-yellow. The rhinophores are also orange-yellow. It is a small Phyllidiid, growing to 20Â mm in length.[3][4]


Diet

This species feeds on a sponge.
