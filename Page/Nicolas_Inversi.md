NICOLAS INVERSI, O.S.M. of NICOLÒ DEGL'INVERSI (died 1471) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Chioggia (1463–1471).[1]


Biography

Nicolas Inversi was ordained a priest in the Servite Order.[2] On 8 February 1463, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Pius II as Bishop of Chioggia.[3][4] On 15 May 1463, he was consecrated bishop by Andrea Bondimerio, Patriarch of Venice.[5] He served as Bishop of Chioggia until his death in 1471.[6][7]
