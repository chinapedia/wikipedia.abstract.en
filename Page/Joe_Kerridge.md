JOSEPH KERRIDGE (born September 17, 1992) is an American football fullback who is currently a free agent. He played college football at Michigan. Kerridge was signed by the Washington Redskins as an undrafted free agent in 2016.


College career

Kerridge attended the University of Michigan, where he played on the Michigan Wolverines football team from 2011 to 2015.[1] He was elected captain of the 2015 team by his teammates.

Professional career

Washington Redskins

After going undrafted in the 2016 NFL Draft, Kerridge signed with the Washington Redskins on May 6, 2016.[2] On September 3, 2016, he was waived by the Redskins during final team cuts.[3] Kerridge was signed to the Redskins' practice squad the following day.[4] On September 5, 2016, he was released by the Redskins.

Green Bay Packers

On October 3, 2016, Kerridge was signed to the Green Bay Packers' practice squad.[5] He was promoted from the practice squad to the active roster on November 7, 2016.[6]

On March 15, 2017, the Packers re-signed Kerridge.[7] The contract was a one-year, 550k deal.[8] He was released on September 8, 2017. He was signed to the Packers' practice squad on October 30, 2017.[9] He was promoted to the active roster on November 18, 2017.[10] He was waived on December 19, 2017 and re-signed to the practice squad.[11] He was promoted back to the active roster on December 30, 2017.[12]

He was re-signed on March 14, 2018.[13] He was waived on September 1, 2018 and was signed to the practice squad the next day.[14][15] He was released on October 30, 2018.[16]
