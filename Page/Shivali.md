SHIVALI is a village and gram panchayat in India, situated in the Mawal taluka of Pune district in the state of Maharashtra. It encompasses an area of .[1]


Administration

The village is administrated by a sarpanch, an elected representative who leads a gram panchayat. At the time of the 2011 Census of India, the village was the headquarters for the eponymous gram panchayat, which also governed the village of Bhadawali.[2]


Demography

At the 2011 census, the village comprised 239 households. The population of 1484 was split between 780 males and 704 females.[3]
