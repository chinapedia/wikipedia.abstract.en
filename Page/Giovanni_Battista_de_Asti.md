GIOVANNI BATTISTA DE ASTI, O.S.A. (1566–1620) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Titular Bishop of _Thagaste_ (1620).[1][2][3]


Biography

Giovanni Battista de Asti was born in Albenga, Italy in 1566 and ordained a priest in the Order of Saint Augustine.[4] On 18 May 1620, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul V as Titular Bishop of _Thagaste_.[5] On 31 May 1620, he was consecrated bishop by Giovanni Garzia Mellini, Cardinal-Priest of Santi Quattro Coronati, with Vincenzo Landinelli, Bishop of Albenga, and Nicolò Spínola, Bishop of Ventimiglia, serving as co-consecrators.[6] He served as Titular Bishop of _Thagaste_ until his death on 20 September 1620.[7]
