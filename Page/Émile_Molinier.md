ÉMILE MOLINIER (26 April 18575 May 1906) was a 19th-century French curator and art historian.


Career

Following his elder brother Auguste, Émile Molinier studied at the École Nationale des Chartes. He wrote a thesis on medieval history entitled _Étude sur la vie d'Ernoul, sire d'Audrehem, maréchal de France_ which earned him the archivist paleographer degree in 1879.[1]

He first worked at the before joining the Louvre, where he served as curator of the newly created art objects department. He published books on stained glass, ceramics, enamels and furniture and organized major exhibitions, including the _Exposition Rétrospective_ held at the Petit Palais in 1900.[2] A specialist of French decorative art, he wrote the first catalog of the Wallace Collection at the time of its opening.[3]
