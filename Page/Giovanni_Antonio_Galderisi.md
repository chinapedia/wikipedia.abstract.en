GIOVANNI ANTONIO GALDERISI (1577–1658) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Bovino (1616–1658).[1][2][3]


Biography

Giovanni Antonio Galderisi was born in Monopoli, Italy in 1577.[4] On 11 January 1616, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul V as Bishop of Bovino.[5] On 24 January 1616, he was consecrated bishop by Giovanni Garzia Mellini, Cardinal-Priest of Santi Quattro Coronati, with Lucio de Morra, Archbishop of Otranto, and Alessandro Guidiccioni (iuniore), Bishop of Lucca, serving as co-consecrators.[6] He served as Bishop of Bovino until his death in 1658.[7]
