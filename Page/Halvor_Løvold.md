HALVOR LØVOLD (14 May 1875–20 Oct 1950[1]) was a Norwegian naval officer and politician.

He was born in Øyestad to merchant Thomas Løvold and Birgithe Jensen.[2] From 1907 to 1945 he was appointed managing director in Arendals Dampskibsselskab.[3] He was elected representative to the Storting for the periods 1916–1918 and 1931–1933, for the Conservative Party.[4] He was decorated Knight, First Class of the Order of St. Olav in 1932.[5][6]
