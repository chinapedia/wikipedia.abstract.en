LORENZO LANDI (1567 – 12 December 1627) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Fossombrone (1612–1627).[1]


Biography

Lorenzo Landi was born in Velletri, Italy in 1567.[2] On 4 July 1612, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul V as Bishop of Fossombrone.[3] On 29 July 1612, he was consecrated bishop by Giovanni Garzia Mellini, Cardinal-Priest of Santi Quattro Coronati, with Ottavio Accoramboni, Bishop Emeritus of Fossombrone, and Paolo De Curtis, Bishop Emeritus of Isernia, serving as co-consecrators.[4] He served as Bishop of Fossombrone until his death on 12 December 1627.[5]


Episcopal succession

While bishop, he was the principal co-consecrator of:[6]

-   Ottavio Ridolfi, Bishop of Ariano (1612);
-   Francesco Cennini de' Salamandri, Bishop of Amelia (1612);
-   Francesco Diotallevi, Bishop of Sant'Angelo dei Lombardi e Bisaccia (1614); and
-   Girolamo Curlo, Bishop of Ventimiglia (1614).
