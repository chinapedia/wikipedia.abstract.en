Naval career

Lambert joined the Royal Navy in circa 1790 and, having been promoted to captain he was given command of the third-rate HMS _Duncan_ in 1812.[1] He became commander-in-chief of the Cape of Good Hope Station in 1820.[2] His responsibilities included command of the British garrison on Saint Helena where Napoleon died in May 1821.[3]
