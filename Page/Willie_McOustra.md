WILLIAM MCOUSTRA (1881–1953) was a Scottish professional football left half and inside right who played in the Football League for Manchester City.[1] He also played in the Scottish League for Celtic.[2][3] He and John Blackwood were signed by the club after playing in a friendly against The Kaffirs, a touring team from the Orange Free State.[4] He later served on the groundstaff at Celtic Park.[5]


Personal life

In September 1914, one month since the outbreak of the First World War, McOustra enlisted as a private in the Scots Guards.[6] He arrived on the Western Front in March 1915 and was medically discharged from the army in September 1917.[7]
