_SCHIZOLAENA MICROPHYLLA_ is a tree in the family Sarcolaenaceae. It is endemic to Madagascar. The specific epithet __ is from the Latin meaning "small leaves".


Description

_Schizolaena microphylla_ grows as a tree up to tall, exceptionally up to . Its leaves are elliptic to ovate or roundish in shape and are hairy on the underside.[1]


Distribution and habitat

_Schizolaena microphylla_ is known only from the central regions of Vakinankaratra and Amoron'i Mania. Its habitat is subhumid forests from altitude. Some subpopulations of the trees are within protected areas.[2]
