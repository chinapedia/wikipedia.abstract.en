OLIVER MICHAEL RATHBONE (born 10 October 1996) is an English professional footballer who plays as a midfielder for Rochdale.[1][2]


Career

Rathbone started in the youth ranks of Manchester United. On 21 February 2013, Rathbone scored a 40-yard winner in an U18s game against Derby County.[3] The goal won Manchester United's Goal of the Month award.[4] After failing to make the breakthrough at Manchester United, Rathbone was released in June 2016 and subsequently, on 24 June, agreed to join EFL League One side Rochdale, signing a one-year contract.[5][6] He made his competitive debut on 4 October in the EFL Trophy against Notts County. Four days later, Rathbone made his league bow in a win against Southend United.[7] Rathbone scored his first goal over Walsall on 22 November.[8][9]

After two goals in thirty-three appearances in his debut season, Rathbone signed a new contract with the club until June 2019 on 18 August 2017.[10] His 100th match for Rochdale arrived on 9 March 2019 during a victory versus Shrewsbury Town.[11] He was offered a new contract by Rochdale at the end of the 2018–19 season.[12] On 24 June, Rathbone signed a new three-year deal to keep him under contract until the summer of 2022.[13][14]


Personal life

He is the son of former footballer Mick Rathbone.[15]
