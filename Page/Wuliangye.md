WULIANGYE () is a Chinese _baijiu_ that made from proso millet, maize, glutinous rice, long-grain rice, and wheat. The formula was at least created in the Ming dynasty. However the name _wuliangye_ was created in 1905. Since 1959 the formula was nationalized and standardized. Currently Wuliangye Yibin is the propriety maker of the drink.[1] The highest grade of Wuliangye retails for (US$3,375).[2]
