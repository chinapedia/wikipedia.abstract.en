BOONE CREEK (sometimes called BOONES CREEK) is a stream in the U.S. state of Missouri.[1] It is a tributary of the Bourbeuse River. It runs through Crawford and Franklin Counties.

The stream headwaters are in Crawford County just north of the community of Coffeyton at . The stream flows north passing under Interstate 44 and then northeast past Bourbon then turns north and enters Franklin County. The stream flows north-northwest passing between Japan and Elmont to enter a meander on the Bourbeuse between Strain and Champion City at .[2][3][4]

Boone Creek bears the name of frontiersman Daniel Boone.[5]
