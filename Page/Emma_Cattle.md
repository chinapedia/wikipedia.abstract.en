EMMA CATTLE (born 1988 in Luton)[1] is a British Paralympian competing in S10 events. She competed at the 2008 Summer Paralympics. Cattle trains at Luton swimming club.[2] She attended Barnfield College.[3]


Career

Cattle has cerebral palsy, and competes in S10 and SM10 events.[4] She began swimming aged 8 at her local swimming club in Luton.[5] She was selected to compete at the 2008 Paralympics,[6] coming sixth in the final of the 100 metre backstroke S10 competition.[7] She also competed in the 50 metre freestyle S10, where she finished last in her heat,[8] and the Women's 200 m Individual Medley SM10, where she was disqualified in her heat.[9] Cattle qualified to compete at the 2009 IPC Swimming European Championships, her third appearance at the event.[10] She won a bronze medal in the 400 metre freestyle S10.[11] In 2009, Cattle broke the British 100m backstroke S10 record with a time of 1:12.55; her record was later broken in 2013.[12] Cattle failed to qualify for the 2012 Summer Paralympics after finishing eighth in the 400m mixed category freestyle final at the British Championships.[13] In 2015, Cattle won the mixed category 100m freestyle C final of a 2015 British Para-Swimming International Meet event in Glasgow.[14] She also competed in the 50m and 100m freestyle and 100m backstroke events in the East Region Winter Championships.[15]
