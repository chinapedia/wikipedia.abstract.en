STEVE SAMMARTINO is an Australian futurist, author and entrepreneur.


Sharing economy

According to Forbes, Sammartino launched Rentoid in 2007, one of the early sharing-economy,[1] peer-to-peer online hire and rental marketplaces. [2][3] Rentoid was featured in Rachel Botsman[4] and Roo Rogers’s book _What’s Mine is Yours: How Collaborative Consumption is Changing the Way We Live_. Rentoid was later sold to an ASX listed company in 2014.[5]


Experimental Projects

According to the World Records Academy,[6] Sammartino collaborated with Raul Oiada in 2011 to launch the world’s first Lego Space Shuttle into space.Propelled by a helium-filled weather balloon, the Lego Space Shuttle[7]model 3367 was launched from German airspace into the stratosphere to an altitude of about 35,000 meters (114,800 feet).[8][9] According to _Wired_ and ABC,in 2013 Sammartino and Oaida developed the world's first life-sized,[10] drive-able Lego car with a compressed air-powered Lego engine.[11][12][13][14].
