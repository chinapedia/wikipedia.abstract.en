ALBERT GEORGE POWER (16 November 1881 – 1945) was an Irish sculptor in the academic realist style.[1][2] He is particularly known for his iconic statue of the Irish writer Pádraic Ó Conaire.[3]


Life

Power was born at No.8 Barrack Street (now Benburb Street) in Dublin on 16 November 1881.[4] As a child he played in local clay brickyards and sculpted busts of his friends. He became an apprentice to the family of renowned Irish sculptor Edward Smyth.[5][6] Power's son, James, was also a renowned artist.


Career

Power was considered the leading Irish sculptor of the 1920s and 1930s. He was an Irish nationalist and promoted the use of Irish materials.[7] He was noted for his academic realist style.[8]


Death

Power died in 1945. His body was buried in Glasnevin Cemetery.
