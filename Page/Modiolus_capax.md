_MODIOLUS CAPAX_, common name FAT HORSEMUSSEL, is a species of "horse mussel", a marine bivalve mollusc in the family Mytilidae, the mussels.[1]


Description

Shells of _Modiolus capax_ can reach a length of about , a width of about and a diameter of about .[2][3] This species has a quite variable form, but it always shows serrate hairs on the periostracum. The left valve is more inflated than the right one. The external surface of the shell is bright orange brown, while the inside is reddish-violet on the posterior half.[4]


Distribution

This species is present in Santa Cruz, California, to Playa, Peru and in Galapagos Islands.[5]
