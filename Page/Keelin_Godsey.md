KEELIN GODSEY (born Kelly Godsey, January 2, 1984, in Massachusetts) was the first openly transgender athlete to compete for a spot on the United States Olympic team.[1][2][3] Godsey was born biologically female, has openly identified as male since 2005, and competed in women's hammer throw competitions.


Early life and education

Godsey graduated in 2006 with a bachelor's degree in English from Bates College.[4]


Women's hammer throw

Collegiate competition

Godsey won two national collegiate championships in the women's hammer throw.[5] He set the NCAA Division III record in the hammer throw twice (in 2005 and 2006),[6] a record which he still held as of May 2015.[7] He earned All-America status in four different throwing disciplines: shot put, weight throw, discus throw, and hammer throw.[8] He was named USTFCCCA New England Region track and field athlete of the year twice,[9] and was named to the NCAA Silver Anniversary Division III track and field championship team.[10]

U.S. Olympic trials and international competition

At the 2008 U.S. Olympic trials for the 2008 summer Olympics, Godsey finished in eighth place in the hammer throw.[11]

In 2011, Godsey finished third at the USATF national championships, earning a spot on the U.S. team at the 2011 Pan Am Games, where he finished fifth in the hammer throw.[12]
