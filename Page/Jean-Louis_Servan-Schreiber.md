JEAN-LOUIS SERVAN-SCHREIBER (born 1937) is a French journalist. He is the co-founder of _L'Expansion_ and the founder of _Psychologies_ and Radio Classique. He is the author of several books.


Early life

Jean-Louis Servan-Schreiber was born on 31 October 1937 in Boulogne-Billancourt, France. His father, Émile Servan-Schreiber, was a Jewish author.[1] His mother was Denise Bresard. The Servan-Schreibers (up to 200 members) have a family reunion every five years.[2]

Servan-Schreiber graduated from Sciences Po.[3]


Career

Servan-Schreiber is a journalist.[4][5] He first wrote for _Echos_, followed by _L'Express_.[6] He co-founded _L'Expansion_ with Jean Boissonnat in 1967.[7] He founded Radio Classique in 1982.[8] He was the owner of _La Vie Éco_, a Moroccan newspaper, from 1994 to 1997.[9] He founded _Psychologies_ in 1997, and sold it to the Lagardère Group in 2008.[10] He founded another magazine, _Clés_, in 2010.[11]

Servan-Schreiber is the author of several books. He has served as the chairman of the French chapter of Human Rights Watch since 2007.[12]


Personal life

Servan-Schreiber was married twice. He married his first wife, Claude Sadoc, in 1957. They had four children; she is now married to a woman, Françoise Gaspard.[13] In 1987, he married his second wife, Perla.[14]
