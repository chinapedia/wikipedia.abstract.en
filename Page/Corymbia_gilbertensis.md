_CORYMBIA GILBERTENSIS_, commonly known as the GILBERT RIVER GHOST GUM,[1] is a bloodwood native to Queensland.[2]

The tree typically grows to in height and has smooth bark throughout that is persistent on the lower trunk. The bark is tessellated, grey to grey-black, dull, white or grey or cream to salmon in colour, it sheds in short ribbons or in small polygonal flakes.[3]
