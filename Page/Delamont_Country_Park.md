DELAMONT COUNTRY PARK is located on the shores of Strangford Lough in County Down, Northern Ireland. It covers an area of approximately 200 acres 2 km south of Killyleagh. It is currently maintained by Newry, Mourne and Down District Council having previously been under the control of Down District Council.[1] Delamont_Country_Park,_June_2012_(01).JPG


Points of interest

In addition to a visitor centre with office, shop and information point the park contains a campsite managed by the Camping and Caravanning Club.[2] There is also a seasonal miniature railway with a 1km track and at the highest point stands the Strangford Stone, the tallest megalith in Ireland which was erected in 1999 using the power of 1000 volunteers. Events

The park is licensed for the performance of civil partnership and Marriage ceremonies.

In July 2016 the World community, coastal rowing championships "Skiffie Worlds 2016" was held over a 2km course on Strangford Lough at Delamont.[6]
