Personal life

Galloway worked as an architect.[3][4] A territorial, he was called up for service when the First World War broke out in August 1914 and joined the Royal Field Artillery.[5] After a long period on the Western Front, he was commissioned as a second lieutenant on 16 September 1917.[6] Galloway was posted to India late in the war and died of pneumonia in Kasauli on 17 November 1918, just six days after the end of the war.[7] He was buried in Kasauli Cemetery.[8]
