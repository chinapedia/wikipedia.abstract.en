Rear Admiral ROBERT SWANTON (died 11 July 1765) was a Royal Navy officer who became commander-in-chief of the Leeward Islands Station.


Naval career

Swanton joined the Royal Navy in January 1734.[1] Promoted to captain in 1743, he took command of the fifth-rate HMS _Mary Galley_ in 1745, the third-rate HMS _Hampton Court_ in 1757 and the third-rate HMS _Vanguard_ later that year.[2] In HMS _Vanguard_ he saw action at the Siege of Louisbourg in 1758 and at the Battle of Neuville in 1760 during the French and Indian War.[3] He became commander-in-chief of the Leeward Islands Station in 1763.[4]
