NADIR BOSCH (born 16 May 1973) is a retired French runner who specialized in the 1500 metres and the 3000 metres steeplechase.

He was born in Algiers.[1] He finished twelfth in the 1500 metres at the 1997 World Championships[2] and reached the final in the 3000 metres at the 2000 European Indoor Championships (did not start).[3]

He also competed at the 1994 European Indoor Championships, the 1996 Olympic Games, the 1997 World Indoor Championships, the 1998 European Championships and the 1999 World Championships without reaching the final.[4][5][6][7] He became French steeplechase champion in 1995 and 1996; and French indoor champion three times in three different events.[8][9]
