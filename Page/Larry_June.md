LARRY JUNE (born April 8, 1991) is an American rapper from San Francisco, California.[1] He is known for his EPs _Larry_ and _Orange Season_ and has toured with musicians such as Cousin Stizz and Post Malone.[2][3]


Early life

Larry June is originally from San Francisco's Hunters Point neighborhood.[4] At age 4 he moved to Atlanta, Georgia, where he stayed until the age of 13. After middle school Larry returned to the Bay Area and attended high school at Jesse Bethel High School in Vallejo, California.[5]


Career

June released numerous tracks in 2016, including the full-length mixtape _Sock It To Me_.[6] He also released the EPs _Larry_ and _Orange Season_, both of which were produced by Cookin' Soul.[7] The release of Orange Season was accompanied by an iOS game of the same name, inspired by Nintendo's early era 8-bit graphics.[8] He also toured with Cousin Stizz and Post Malone in 2016 and was featured on Stizz's song _Down Like That_ as well as Malone's single _Never Understand_.[9][10] Additional collaborations in 2016 include rapping with Asher Roth and Michael Christmas on the track Laundry.[11]

Outside his career as a musician, June plans on releasing his own brand of Oranges based on a suggestion from a friend whose family owns a farm.[12]
