PHILIP OF OXFORD (died after 1191) was an Augustinian canon and head of the Priory of St Frideswide, Oxford.[1]

Philip is the author of a collection of miracles attributed to Frithuswith, _The Miracles of St Frideswide_ (_Miracula sancte Frideswide_).[2] In the work, he names himself as prior on the day of the translation of her relics, 12 February 1180.[3][4]
