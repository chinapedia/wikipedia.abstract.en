RACHEL BAKAM is a Nigerian actress, writer, TV presenter and producer, and an anti-human trafficking ambassador. She is the CEO and Managing Director of Rayzeds Media Ltd,[1] Founder & President of the Nigerian Water Ski And Wakeboard Federation (NWWF) and media consultant for the Performing Musician Employers Association (PMAN) & African Fashion Reception Paris.[2] Bakam is a peace and tourist ambassador. She is 2015 alumna of United States International Visitors Leadership Programme.[3][4] She hosts _Trends & Rachel_,[5] a popular television show that airs on Nigerian Television Authority(NTA), African Independent Television(AIT), DSTV, and Startimes. She also produces and presents Rachel the PIPER and hausa version RACHEL MAI KAKAAKI; an entertaining yet highly humanitarian television programme[6]


Early life

Bakam was born and brought up in Kaduna State by Mr. and Mrs. Adams and Rose Bakam where she had her primary, secondary, and tertiary education.[7] She studied English and theatre arts at Ahmadu Bello University[8] and digital filmmaking at New York Film Academy.[9] She lost her father at the age of 12. At the age of 16 she started working, first as a salesgirl in supermarket, later in a recording studio and played a lead role in Hausa drama series known as _Rayuwa_.[10] She was president of Literary and Debating Club then Head Girl in Kaduna Capital School, best graduating female student and best graduating student of faculty of arts in Ahmadu Bello University Zaria[11]


Career

She did her National Youth Service Corps at Nigerian Television Authority International, where she produced and presented different TV programmes like _Trends_, _Saturday Morning_, and _Style Code_. She proceeded to study digital film making at New York Film Academy and thereafter established Rayzed Media Ltd.[12] Bakam brought water skiing and wakeboarding to Nigeria.[13] In 2016, she got an endorsement deal with Numatville Megacity. She has joined non-government organizations,[14] such as Devatop Centre for Africa Development in campaigning against sex trafficking and other forms of modern slavery in Nigeria.[15]

She is a Media Consultant for the African Fashion Reception Paris.[16]


Awards and nominations

Bakam has received several awards and nominations for her work, some of which include:[17]

-   2015 Pan African Humanitarian Award as the Most Promising TV Personality of the Year
-   Sir Tafela Balewa Leadership Award as the Media Entrepreneur of the year
-   2016 United Nations World Habitat Humanitarian Award
-   Nigerian Dream Personality Honors
-   2016 Ambassador of Peace Award[18]
-   2015 African Achievers' Award[19]
-   Nigerian Unity Museum & Trade Tourism Village Ambassador, 2016
-   Africa Diaspora Merit Awards Amsterdam
-   Nigerian Entertainment and Lifestyle Awards London

Southern Kaduna Killing

After the Southern Kaduna crisis, where more than 800 people, mostly Christians were killed,[20] Rachel Bakam and John Fashanu joined Big Church Foundation to support the victims with relief materials.[21]
