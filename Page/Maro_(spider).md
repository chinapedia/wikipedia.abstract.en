_MARO_ is a genus of dwarf spiders that was first described by Octavius Pickard-Cambridge in 1907.[1]


Species

it contains sixteen species:[2]

-   _Maro amplus_ Dondale & Buckle, 2001 – Canada, USA
-   _Maro borealis_ Eskov, 1991 – Russia (Siberia, Far East, Sakhalin)
-   _Maro bulbosus_ Zhao & Li, 2014 – China
-   _Maro bureensis_ Tanasevitch, 2006 – Russia (Far East)
-   _Maro flavescens_ (O. Pickard-Cambridge, 1873) – Russia (South Siberia, Far East), Mongolia
-   _Maro khabarum_ Tanasevitch, 2006 – Russia (Far East)
-   _Maro lehtineni_ Saaristo, 1971 – Europe (Central and Northern)
-   _Maro lepidus_ Casemir, 1961 – Europe
-   _Maro minutus_ O. Pickard-Cambridge, 1907 (type) – Europe
-   _Maro nearcticus_ Dondale & Buckle, 2001 – Canada, USA, Mexico
-   _Maro pansibiricus_ Tanasevitch, 2006 – Russia (Europe to Far East)
-   _Maro perpusillus_ Saito, 1984 – Japan
-   _Maro saaristoi_ Eskov, 1980 – Russia (Siberia, Far East, Sakhalin)
-   _Maro sibiricus_ Eskov, 1980 – Russia (Europe to Far East/East Siberia)
-   _Maro sublestus_ Falconer, 1915 – Europe, Russia (Europe to West Siberia)
-   _Maro ussuricus_ Tanasevitch, 2006 – Russia (Far East)
