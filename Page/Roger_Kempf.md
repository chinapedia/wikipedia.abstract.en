ROGER KEMPF (6 July 1927[1] – 9 September 2014) was a French writer, philosopher, Germanist and ethnologist of literature, and _emeritus_ professor at the École polytechnique fédérale de Zurich.

He was awarded several literary prizes, including the Prix Alfred Née of the Académie française in 1969 for his book _Sur le corps romanesque_, the Prix Ève Delacroix of the Académie française in 2005 for _L'indiscrétion des frères Goncourt_ and the Prix Femina essai in 2004 for the same work.
