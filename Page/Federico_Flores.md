FEDERICO JESÚS FLORES (born 18 May 1992) is an Argentine professional footballer who plays as a midfielder for Gualaceo.[1][2]


Career

Flores started in the youth teams of Rosario Central, he subsequently made his first-team debut on 16 June 2013 in a Primera B Nacional win over Deportivo Merlo as he was substituted on for the final five minutes.[3] Rosario Central ended that season by winning promotion to the 2013–14 Argentine Primera División.[4] Two years later, in June 2015, after making only one appearance for Rosario Central, Flores was loaned out to Bolivian Primera División side Real Potosí.[5][6] His first match came on 9 August in a league defeat against Jorge Wilstermann.[7] On 10 December, Flores scored a hat-trick in a 6–1 victory versus Oriente Petrolero.[8]

Those were his only three goals in sixteen appearances in all competitions for the team.[9] Upon returning to Rosario Central, he departed the club in 2016 to join fellow Argentine Primera División team Arsenal de Sarandí.[10][11][12] His debut for Arsenal came in a Copa Argentina tie against Defensores de Belgrano on 12 August.[13] After zero league appearances, he was released in July 2017.[14][15] Following a spell with Sportivo Figherense, Flores signed for Ecuadorian Serie B side Gualaceo in February 2018.[16][17]
