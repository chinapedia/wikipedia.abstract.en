EILEEN DUPUCH CARRON, CMG (born 13 March 1930) is a lawyer and newspaper publisher in the Bahamas.


Life

The daughter of Sir Étienne Dupuch and Lady Marie Dupuch, she was born EILEEN DUPUCH in Nassau[1] and was educated at Queen's College and St. Francis Xavier's Academy there and at St Francis' College in Hertfordshire. She went on to receive a BA in philosophy from St. Michael's College, Toronto,[2] a master's degree in journalism from Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism in New York City and to study law in London.[3]

While she was in her twenties, Carron became the first woman pilot in The Bahamas.[4]

In 1962, she became assistant editor at _The Tribune_ in Nassau. In the same year, she became the second woman called to the Bahamas Bar.[5][6] She was recognized by the International Press Institute for her "never-ending commitment to free press and the highest journalistic standard".[7]

In 1972, she became publisher for _The Tribune_, becoming only the second woman publisher in The Bahamas. As of August 2016, she was the longest currently serving editor and publisher in The Bahamas at 53 years.[8]

In 1993, Carron became the first CEO of a private radio station, The People’s Radio Station, 100 JAMZ. She is the only Bahamian to have an editorial read into the record of the United States Senate[9]

In 2000, she was named a Companion of the Order of St Michael and St George.[10]

She was married to Roger Peter Carron, now deceased.[11]
