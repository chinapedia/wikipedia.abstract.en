MARJORIE QUARTON (born 1930-) is an Irish novelist.


Life and work

Born Marjorie Smethwick to Standish Smethwick and Dolly Webb in 1930 in Nenagh, County Tipperary.[1][2][3][4] She was educated in Dublin. Quarton did not start her writing career until she was in her 50s.[5] She has written novels, memoirs, children's books and newspaper articles.[6][7][8]

Quarton was a horse, sheep and cattle breeder and also worked for the National Council for the Blind of Ireland.[9][10][11] She has written on dogs, particularly sheepdogs. She is a member of PEN and resides near Nenagh.[12][13][14][15]
