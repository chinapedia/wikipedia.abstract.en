_PHYLLIDIELLA MEANDRINA_ is a species of sea slug, a dorid nudibranch, a shell-less marine gastropod mollusk in the family Phyllidiidae.[1]

B140884.jpg


Distribution

This species was first collected in Mauritius. It was confused with _Phyllidiella annulata_ by Brunckhorst.[2] It is a frequent species in Mauritius and RÃ©union.[3]


Description

This nudibranch has a black dorsum with longitudinal pink ridges at the sides of the mantle and a series of oval rings in the centre of the back. The rhinophores are black.[4]


Diet

This species feeds on sponges.
