BISHOP PAOLO ARESE, C.R. (1574 – 14 June 1644) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Tortona (1620–1644).[1][2][3][4]


Biography

Paolo Arese was born in 1574 and ordained a priest in the Congregation of Clerics Regular of the Divine Providence.[5] On 20 July 1620, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul V as Bishop of Tortona.[6][7] On 20 September 1620, he was consecrated bishop by Giovanni Garzia Mellini, Cardinal-Priest of Santi Quattro Coronati, with Attilio Amalteo, Titular Archbishop of _Athenae_, and Paolo De Curtis, Bishop Emeritus of Isernia serving as co-consecrators.[8] He served as Bishop of Tortona until his resignation in 1644.[9] He died soon after on 14 June 1644.[10]
