LORENZO ZANNI or LORENZO ZANE (died 1485) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Brescia (1478–1480),[1] Titular Patriarch of Antioch (1473–1478),[2] Bishop of Treviso (1473–1478),[3] Titular Patriarch of Jerusalem (1458–1473),[4] and Archbishop of Split (1452–1458).[5]


Biography

On 5 June 1452, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Nicholas V as Archbishop of Split.[6][7] On 13 March 1458, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Callixtus III as Titular Patriarch of Jerusalem.[8][9][10][11] On 28 April 1473, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Sixtus IV as Bishop of Treviso and Titular Patriarch of Antioch.[12][13][14][15] On 27 February 1478, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Sixtus IV as Bishop of Brescia.[16][17][18] He served as Bishop of Brescia until his resignation in 1480.[19][20][21] He died on 15 October 1485.[22][23]
