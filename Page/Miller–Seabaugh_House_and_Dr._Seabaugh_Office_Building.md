The house was built about 1883, and is a 1 1/2-story, irregular plan brick dwelling. It features arched windows and corbelling along the eaves. The doctor's office was moved to the property about 1911, and is a one-room frame building on a sandstone foundation.[1]

It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1996.[2]
