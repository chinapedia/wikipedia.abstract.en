ALEXANDER SFORZA or ALESSANDRO SFORZA (1658–1701) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Titular Archbishop of _Neocaesarea in Ponto_ (1695–1701) and Apostolic Nuncio to Savoy (1695–1701).[1][2][3][4][5]


Biography

Alexander Sforza was born in Viterbo, Italy on 6 August 1658.[6][7] On 13 June 1695, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Innocent XII as Titular Archbishop of _Neocaesarea in Ponto_.[8][9][10] On 19 June 1695, he was consecrated bishop by Galeazzo Marescotti, Cardinal-Priest of Santi Quirico e Giulitta, with Prospero Bottini, Titular Archbishop of _Myra_, and Sperello Sperelli, Bishop of Terni, serving as co-consecrators.[11][12] On 24 Jun 1695, he was named Apostolic Nuncio to Savoy.[13] He served as Apostolic Nuncio to Savoy until his death on 8 Apr 1701.[14][15][16]

While bishop, he was the principal consecrator of Michel-Gabriel Rossillon de Bernex, Bishop of Genève (1697).[17]
