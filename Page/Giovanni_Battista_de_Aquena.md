GIOVANNI BATTISTA DE AQUENA (1576–1615) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Bosa (1613–1615).[1][2][3][4]


Biography

Giovanni Battista de Aquena was born in Sassari, Italy in 1576.[5] On 18 March 1613, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul V as Bishop of Bosa.[6][7] On 14 April 1613, he was consecrated bishop by Giovanni Garzia Mellini, Cardinal-Priest of Santi Quattro Coronati with Marco Cornaro, Bishop of Padua, and Alessandro Guidiccioni (iuniore), Bishop of Lucca, serving as co-consecrators.[8] He served as Bishop of Bosa until his death in 1615.[9]
