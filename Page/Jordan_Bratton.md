JORDAN BRATTON (born January 23, 1995) is an American singer, songwriter, multi-instrumentalist, and producer. Born and raised in New York, he was encouraged by his family to join the choir in kindergarten and he got his first moment in the spotlight signing a solo at the Lincoln Center during the holidays. Bratton knows how to play the drums, piano, the organ, guitar, bass, and the clarinet. Around the age of eleven Bratton hit Broadway. He joined the touring production of _The Lion King_ as young Simba and he also starred in _The Color Purple_.[1] On November 5, 2014, Bratton signed to RCA records. He has released two albums called _The Grey Area_ and _YOUTH_. _The Grey Area_ album was released on December 10, 2013. The album consisted of 14 songs. The _YOUTH_ album was released May 12, 2015 and consisted of 6 songs. Bratton's main genre's are soul and R&B.[2]
