JEAN-MARC PARISIS (born 1962) is a French writer and journalist. He is the author of seven novels, five stories and a biography, as well as various prefaces and anthologies.


Biography

After studying letters (hypokhâgne and khâgne at lycée Lakanal at Sceaux, which inspired him _Le Lycée des artistes_), Jean-Marc Parisis made his debuts at _Libération_ at age 20. Since 1983, he has written articles in numerous newspapers and magazines: _Libération_, _Le Figaro Littéraire_, _Le Quotidien de Paris_, _Metro_, _Le Monde_, _Le Point_, _Le Figaro Magazine_...

For the cinema, he wrote with Philippe Eineck the scenario of __ (2000) by Alain Robak which stars Claude Brasseur, Olivier Martinez, Gilbert Melki, Saïd Taghmaoui and .

He directed the collection "La Désinvolture" at éditions du Quai Voltaire from 1987 to 1989 and the collection "Colère" at Éditions du Rocher from 2000 to 2005.
