LYCÉE SAINT-EXUPÉRY, nicknamed SAINT-EX, is a sixth-form college/senior high school in the 15th arrondissement of Marseille, France.[1]

the attendance zone of Saint-Exupéry covers those of nine different junior high schools (collèges).[2]


History

It began construction in 1957, when there was a "baby boom" in the Marseille area. It was originally called LYCÉE NORD. [3]

In 2013 Sylvia Zappi of _Le Monde_ described the school as a "lycée ghetto", saying that staff members used that name.[4]

That year former teacher Franck Baillot stated that only poor families attend the school; Zappi stated that wealthier families in the area tried their best to make sure their children did not attend the school. 40% of residents in the high school attendance zone who apply to go to sixth-form/senior high through the procedure were assigned to Saint-Exupéry, but the school only received 19% of the students who could have applied, based on previous year attendance in the corresponding high school zone.[5]

80 female students, making up about 5% of the student body, remove veils before going to classes.[6]

Teachers went on strike in 2016.[7]


Campus

It includes a boarding (_internat_) facility.[8]

In 2008 a new wing was added to the building.[9]
