A _KÜNSTLERROMAN_ (; plural -ane), meaning "artist's novel" in English, is a narrative about an artist's growth to maturity.[1][2] It could be classified as a sub-category of _Bildungsroman_.[3]

Notes

-   A semiautobiographical narrative takes up two of the four books of Gray's _Lanark_.
-   In John Dos Passos' U.S.A. trilogy, the _Camera Eye_ sections add up to a modernist autobiographical _Künstlerroman_.
-   John Barth's _Lost in the Funhouse_ is a collection of short stories that are often read as a postmodernist _Künstlerroman_.
