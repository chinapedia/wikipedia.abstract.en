_SINGER_ 2017 was the fifth season of Chinese television series of the rebranded version of _I Am a Singer_. The title name was renamed to _Singer_ due to the banning of Korean-related branding and artists in China. As seen on Season 1, no competing singers also double as MCs, with hosting duties shared by the singers' music partners, who had prior hosting experience. If not, they were partnered by someone who does. The singers' introductions were made by their music partners. The finals were hosted by He Jiong after a year's absence.

The series premiered on January 21, 2017, on Hunan Television, and ended on April 22, 2017. Hong Kong singer Sandy Lam was named the winner of the season, Kazakhstan's Dimash Kudaibergen and Taiwanese band Lion were named runner-up and third place, respectively.


Competition rules

Due to the renaming of the show, however, the format was otherwise similar from previous seasons of _I Am a Singer_. As seen in the last season, the competition began with eight singers and the first episode featured elimination from the lowest-ranked singer. Those results were decided from the 500-member audience panel, with each member cast their vote for the top three singers of their preference. The Challenge round twist which first seen in the third season, also returned with a modification where two singers entering the competition (one Challenger and one Returning Singer from previous seasons); like the previous series, challengers had to beat a majority of singers (by ranking in top four or better) in order to be safe, and the results from the Challenge rounds are counted individually; the returning singer, however, are not required to do so.

This is currently the only season to have returning singers, as well as an increased number of finalists (ten). This was the last season to feature Singer voting which was introduced in Season 3.

Breakout

-   TAPING DATE: March 23, 2017
-   AIRDATE: April 1, 2017

Three of the six singers who were first-round singers (Lam, Lion and Kudaibergen) were exempt for this round, while the other three singers (with the exception of Cheung, Tan and Lei; Lei was unable to take part due to scheduling conflicts on his concert[4]) participated with eliminated singers for a chance to enter the semi-finals. The order was determined through ballot, with the exception of surviving non-first-round singers (Li, Lin and Jason) who could choose their performance order for the night without ballot.

For the first time, the number of finalists was increased from seven to ten, thus seven Breakout spots were offered, and the top seven singers receiving the highest number of votes advanced to the semi-finals.
