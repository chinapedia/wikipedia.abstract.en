ʿILM AL-ḤURŪF () or THE SCIENCE OF LETTERS is an Arabic esoteric science or magic practice concerned with occult properties assigned to the letters of the Arabic alphabet, and the words and names formed from those letters.

Using these properties of letters, ʿilm al-ḥurūf then sought to combine letters based on these magical properties to form words to achieve a desired magical or divinatory effect. It also sought to use these properties to analyze significant names and words, such as the 99 names of God, to reveal and understand divine secrets.


Etymology

ʿIlm al-Ḥurūf is composed of the two words _ʿilm_ () meaning "science", and _ḥurūf_ (), the plural of the word _ḥarf_ (), meaning "letters".
