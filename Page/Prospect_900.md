The PROSPECT 900 is a sailboat that was designed by Dutch naval architect Ericus Gerhardus van de Stadt and first built in 1975.[1][2][3]


Production

Prospect_900_Sailboat_Keewaydin_3950.jpg Prospect_900,_moored_on_the_Beaulieu_River,_Hampshire.jpg The boat was built by Rydgeway Marine in the United Kingdom, but is now out of production.[4]


Design

The Prospect 900 is a small recreational keelboat, built predominantly of fiberglass. It has a masthead sloop rig, an internally-mounted spade-type rudder and a lifting keel. It displaces and carries of iron ballast. The boat has a draft of with the lifting keel extended.[5][6]
