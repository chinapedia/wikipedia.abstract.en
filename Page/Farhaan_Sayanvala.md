FARHAAN SAYANVALA (born 8 July 1997) is a South African cricketer.[1] He made his Twenty20 debut for Gauteng against Eastern Province in the 2016 Africa T20 Cup on 23 September 2016.[2] Prior to his T20 debut he was named in South Africa's squad for the 2016 Under-19 Cricket World Cup.[3]

He made his List A debut for Gauteng in the 2016–17 CSA Provincial One-Day Challenge on 15 January 2017.[4] He made his first-class debut for Gauteng in the 2017–18 Sunfoil 3-Day Cup on 12 October 2017.[5]

In September 2018, he was named in Northern Cape's squad for the 2018 Africa T20 Cup.[6] He was the leading run-scorer for Northern Cape in the 2018–19 CSA Provincial One-Day Challenge, with 215 runs in eight matches.[7]
