The was a Japanese coin worth one fifth of a Japanese yen, as 100 _sen_ equalled 1 yen.[1] These coins were minted from 1870 to 1911 in 80% silver.[2][3]

MEIJI

The following are circulation figures for the _twenty sen coin_, all of which were minted between the 3rd, and 44th year of Meiji's reign. The dates all begin with the Japanese symbol 明治 (Meiji), followed by the year of his reign the coin was minted. Each coin is read clockwise from right to left, so in the example used below "一十二" would read as "year 21" or 1888.

-   "Year" ← "Number representing year of reign" ← "Emperor's name" (Ex: 年 ← 一十二 ← 治明)
