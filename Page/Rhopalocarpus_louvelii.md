_RHOPALOCARPUS LOUVELII_ is a tree in the family Sphaerosepalaceae. It is endemic to Madagascar.


Distribution and habitat

_Rhopalocarpus louvelii_ is a widespread species in Madagascar. Its habitat is both dry and wet forests from sea-level to altitude. Some populations are within protected areas.[1][2]


Threats

_Rhopalocarpus louvelii_ is threatened by shifting patterns of agriculture, resulting in deforestation. Because the species is used as timber, subsistence harvesting is also a threat.[3]
