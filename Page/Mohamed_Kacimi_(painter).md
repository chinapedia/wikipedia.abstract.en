_For the Algerian novelist, see Mohamed Kacimi._ MOHAMED KACIMI (1942–2003) was a Moroccan painter. He was the recipient of the _Grand Prix du Mérite_ from the King of Morocco in 2000.


Early life

Mohamed Kacimi was born on December 28, 1942 in Meknes, Morocco.[1][2]


Career

Kacimi was a painter.[3] He was a member of the _Association Marocaine des Arts Plastiques_ (AMAP).[4] He was invited to paint murals in Asilah in 1978.[5] His work was published in _Le Monde Diplomatique_ on a regular basis.[6] Meanwhile, _Revue Noire_ published a special issue about his work in 1996.[7] Kacimi was awarded the _Grand Prix du Mérite_ from the King of Morocco in 2000.[8]

Kacimi was also a human rights activist.[9] He exhibited his work for the Organisation Marocaine des Droits Humains (OMDH) in 2002.[10] In September 2003, he wrote an open letter to Sidiki Kaba, the president of the International Federation for Human Rights, calling for the release of journalist Tayseer Allouni.[11]


Death and legacy

Kacimi died in a Hepatitis C-induced coma on October 27, 2003 in Rabat.[12][13] Shortly after his death, the Moroccan Ministry of Culture called him an "ambassador of Moroccan painting throughout his life."[14] In 2005, the ministry dedicated the ''Galerie Mohamed Kacini in Fez.[15] By 2007, some of his work was auctioned by Christie's, with one painting being sold for US$67,000.[16]
