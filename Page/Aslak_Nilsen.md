ASLAK NILSEN (born 8 December 1898) was a Norwegian politician.

He was born in Kragerø to Knut Andreas Nilsen and Karen Thorbjørnsen. He was elected representative to the Storting for the periods 1934–1936 and 1937–1945, for the Labour Party.[1]
