GABRIEL ALEJANDRO SANABRIA (born 2 July 1992) is an Argentine professional footballer who plays as a midfielder for Primera B Nacional side Sarmiento.[1][2]


Career

Sanabria started with Primera B Metropolitana side Atlanta during 2010–11, a season that Atlanta finished with promotion into the 2011–12 Primera B Nacional but were relegated back to Primera B Metropolitana at the first time of asking.[3] During this time, Sanabria made thirty appearances for the club and scored three goals.[4] Sanabria then departed Atlanta to join Torneo Argentino B club Gutiérrez before going onto join Villa Dálmine of Primera B Metropolitana.[5] Across two seasons with Villa Dálmine he scored three goals in thirty-seven games. In January 2016, Sanabria joined Argentine Primera División club Arsenal de Sarandí.[6]

He made his top-flight Argentine debut on 12 February against Banfield.[7][8] In July 2017, Sanabria was released by Arsenal.[9][10] On 5 August, Sanabria joined second tier team Los Andes on a free transfer.[11] His Los Andes debut came on 15 September in a goalless draw against Deportivo Morón.[12] On 26 November, Sanabria made his 100th career appearance versus Estudiantes.[13] In January 2018, Sanabria signed for fellow Primera B Nacional side Sarmiento.[14][15] He scored on his first appearance for Sarmiento, netting the club's fourth goal in a 3–5 away win against Flandria.[16]
