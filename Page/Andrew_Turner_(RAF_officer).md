Air Marshal ANDREW MARK TURNER, (born 1967) is a senior Royal Air Force officer and helicopter pilot.


Early life

Born in 1967, Turner was educated at Kingswood School.[1]


RAF career

Turner joined the Royal Air Force in 1985 and was commissioned as an acting pilot officer in 1986,[2] initially training at RAF Gütersloh.[3] He was regraded as pilot officer in 1987,[4] and promoted to flying officer in 1987,[5] flight lieutenant in 1991,[6] squadron leader in 1995,[7] and wing commander in 2000.[8] He was appointed an Officer of the Order of the British Empire in 2003 in recognition of service in the Iraq War.[9]

Turner has commanded No. 28 Squadron RAF[10] and RAF Odiham.[11] In July 2014 he went on to be Air Officer Commanding No. 22 Group RAF[12] and Chief of Staff Training at RAF Air Command,[13][14] in which capacity he also oversaw safety for the Red Arrows.[15] He became Assistant Chief of the Defence Staff (Operations) in October 2017.[16] Turner was promoted to air marshal and appointed as Deputy Commander (Capability) at RAF Air Command on 23 May 2019.[17]

Turner was advanced to Commander of the Order of the British Empire in the 2010 New Year Honours[18] and appointed a Companion of the Order of the Bath in the 2019 New Year Honours.[19]
