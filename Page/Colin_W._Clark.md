COLIN WHITCOMB CLARK (born 1931) is a Professor Emeritus of Mathematics at The University of British Columbia. Clark specializes in behavioral ecology and the economics of natural resources, specifically, in the management of commercial fisheries.[1] Clark was named a Fellow of the International Institute of Fisheries Economics & Trade (IIFET) in 2016 for his contributions to bioeconomics.[2] Clark's impact upon fisheries economics through his scholarly work is encapsulated in _Mathematical Bioeconomics: The Mathematics of Conservation,_ which is considered to be a classic contribution in environmental economic theory.[3]
