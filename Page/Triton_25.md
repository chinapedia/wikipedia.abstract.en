The TRITON 25, also called the PEARSON 25, is an American sailboat, that was designed by Gary Mull and first built in 1984. The design is out of production.[1][2][3]


Production

The boat was built by Pearson Yachts in the United States.[4]

The Triton 25 is a development of the US Yachts US 25 and the Buccaneer 250, with the Triton 25 actually built from tooling and molds purchased from US Yachts.[5]

The Pearson Yachts series of Triton boats were named for the Alberg Triton, which had been introduced in 1958.[6]


Design

The Triton 25 is a small recreational keelboat, built predominantly of fiberglass, with wood trim. It has a masthead sloop rig, an internally-mounted spade-type rudder and a fixed fin keel. It displaces and carries of ballast.[7][8]
