The LOS ANGELES CENTER FOR DIGITAL ART (LACDA), established in April 2004, acts a gallery for the display digital artworks in Los Angeles, California, United States.[1]

The founder and director of the gallery is Rex Bruce.[2] The first LACDA venue was on Melrose Avenue, Hollywood, using space at RBC Studios.[3] In February 2011, LACDA moved to 102 West 5th Street in Los Angeles,[4] at the former location of Bert Green Fine Art, which moved to Chicago. Artists such as Andy Lomas[5] and Kerry Mitchell have exhibited at LACDA.
