GLENNONVILLE is an unincorporated community in Dunklin County, in the U.S. state of Missouri.[1]


History

Glennonville was originally built up chiefly by German Catholics.[2] The community was named after John J. Glennon, the Archbishop of St. Louis.[3] A post office called Glennonville was established in 1907, and remained in operation until 1941.[4]
