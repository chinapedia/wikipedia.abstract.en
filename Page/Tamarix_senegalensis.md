_TAMARIX SENGALENSIS_ is a species of flowering plants of the Tamaricaceae family.[1] It is a tree or twiggy shrub, that grows in saline soil, sandy desert and sea-shore.[2]


Description

_Tamarix senegalensis_ is a large shrub, reaching 2-5 metres height. It has many small leaves. In Cape Verde it is called _tarrafe_, and the related name _tarrafal_ has been given to a number of localities.[3]


Distribution and ecology

Foglie_tamarix_senegalensis.jpg _Tamarix senegalensis_ occurs on and near the coasts of northwestern Africa (Morocco, Mauritania, Senegal) and the Cape Verde islands.[4][5]
