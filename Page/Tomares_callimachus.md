_TOMARES CALLIMACHUS_, the CAUCASIAN VERNAL COPPER, is a butterfly of the family Lycaenidae. It is found in Anatolia, Iraq, Iran, the Caucasus, and Transcaucasia.

The wingspan is 18–23 mm. The species inhabits semi-deserts and arid mountain steppes. It occupies an elevation range from 1000 to 2000 m above sea level.[1] The butterfly flies from late March to early June depending on latitude and elevation.

The larvae feed on the _Astragalus_ species _A. physodes_ and _A. vulpinus_.
