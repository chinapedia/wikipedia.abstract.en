LIST OF REPRESENTATIVES AND SENATORS OF THE ARIZONA LEGISLATURE BY LEGISLATIVE DISTRICTS AFTER THE 2010 REDISTRICTING.


Background

Redistricting in Arizona occurs every 10 years and is conducted by the Arizona Independent Redistricting Commission. In 2010 the commission held 58 business meetings and 43 public hearings in locations all over the state, for a total of over 359 hours in an 11-month period. There were a total of over 5,000 attendees at those meetings, and 2,350 speaking requests were granted. The commission streamed almost every meeting to the internet, allowing another 1,850 distinct viewers to join.[1] After these meetings the AIRC submitted the final Legislative Maps to the Department of Justice on February 28, 2012 for preclearance under the Voting Rights Act. The Justice Department approved the maps on April 26, 2012.[2] The Supreme Court upheld the 2010 redistricting in _Harris v. Arizona Independent Redistricting Commission_.[3] The first election using the newly drawn districts occurred on November 6, 2012.
