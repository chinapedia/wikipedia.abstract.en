DAVID FRANCIS KESSLER, OBE, (6 June 1906 - 24 November 1999) was a British publisher and author. He was the managing director of _The Jewish Chronicle_.


Early life

David Kessler was born on 6 June 1906 in Pretoria, South Africa.[1][2][3] His father, Leopold Kessler, was a friend of Theodore Herzl, an early proponent of Zionism, and a shareholder of _The Jewish Chronicle_.

Kessler graduated from the University of Cambridge, where he earned a bachelor's degree in law and economics.[4][5][6]


Career

Kessler started his career by working for Antonin Besse,[7] an oil and shipping businessman with ties to the Royal Dutch Shell in Aden, Yemen.[8] He subsequently worked for the Palestine Potash Company, later known as the Dead Sea Works, in Jerusalem.[9][10][11]

Kessler became the managing director of _The Jewish Chronicle_ in London in 1935.[12] In 1946, he dismissed the editor, Ivan Greenberg, who was deemed too divisive.[13] Instead, he hired John Maurice Shaftesley until 1958, when he hired William Frankel.[14]

Kessler was the author of two books.[15] He was a founding member of the Minority Rights Group.[16] He served as the chairman of the Falasha Welfare Association and the Wiener Library in London.[17][18] He became OBE in 1996.[19][20]


Personal life and death

Kessler had a wife, Matilda, a son, and three daughters.[21] They resided in Stoke Hammond, Buckinghamshire, England, where he died on 24 November 1999.[22][23]
