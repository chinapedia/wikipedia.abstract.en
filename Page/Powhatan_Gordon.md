Major POWHATAN GORDON (1802-1879) was an American farmer and politician. He served in the Tennessee Senate in 1842 and 1845. During his tenure, he proposed a bill for the creation of Lewis County, Tennessee. By 1854, his bid for Congress as a member of the Know Nothing party was unsuccessful.


Early life

Powhatan Gordon was born on November 15, 1802 in Nashville, Tennessee.[1][2] His father, John Gordon, built John Gordon House in Williamsport, Tennessee.[3]


Career

Gordon was a corn farmer in Williamsport, Tennessee.[4] He sold his corn in Louisiana.[5]

During the Second Seminole War of 1835-1842, he served as a Major in the First Tennessee Mounted Militia.[6] He subsequently served in the Mexican–American War of 1846-1848.[7] When Veracruz was occupied by the United States, he sold goods in the city.[8]

Gordon joined the Democratic Party.[9] He served in the Tennessee Senate in 1842 and 1845.[10] It was Gordon who proposed a bill for the creation of Lewis County, Tennessee out of parts of Hickman County, Lawrence County, Wayne County, and Maury County, naming it after explorer Meriwether Lewis.[11]

Gordon joined the Know Nothing party and ran for the United States House of Representatives in 1854, but he lost the race.[12]


Personal life and death

Gordon married Caroline Mary Coleman on June 26, 1828.[13] They had eleven children, including Judge William Osceola Gordon and state representative R. H. Gordon.[14][15] He died on January 29, 1879 in Bryan, Texas.[16]
