The WILDERNESS 38 is an American sailboat, that was designed by Gary Mull and first built in 1979. The design is out of production.[1][2]

The boat was built by Wilderness Yachts of Santa Cruz, California, United States, who completed at least one example.[3]

The Wilderness 38 design was developed into the Wilderness 40 in 1980, using the same tooling.[4]


Design

The Wilderness 38 is a small recreational keelboat, built predominantly of fiberglass, with wood trim. It has a fractional sloop masthead sloop rig, an internally-mounted spade-type/transom-hung rudder and a fixed fin keel. It displaces and carries of lead ballast. The boat has a draft of with the standard fin keel.[5]

The boat is fitted with a Japanese Yanmar 3GM30 diesel engine.[6]
