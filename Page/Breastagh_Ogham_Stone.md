BREASTAGH OGHAM STONE (CIIC 010) is a ogham stone and National Monument located in County Mayo, Ireland.[1]


Location

Breastagh Ogham Stone stands in a field north-northwest of Killala town.[2][3]


History

The stone is believed to have been erected during the Bronze Age, and the carving added perhaps around AD 550–900.[4][5]

The ogham stone was found lying in a field in April 1874 by an English tourist, W.K. Dover, and brought to the attention of Sir Samuel Ferguson, who had it re-erected.[6][7]


Description

Breastagh Ogham Stone is a pillar of stone measuring 366 × 76 × 60 cm and has Ogham carvings incised on two edges. (L[ ... ]G̣G̣[ ... ]SD[ ... ]LENGẸṢCẠ[D] / MAQ CORRBṚI MAQ AMMLLỌṆG̣[I]ṬT, "Legescad, son of Corrbrias, son of Ammllogitt") is carved on it. This is believed to refer to a grandson of Amalgaid mac Fiachrae (d. AD 440), King of Connacht of the Uí Fiachrach, who gives his name to the barony of Tirawley and earlier to the túath of (_Tír Amhlaidh_).[8][9]
