GARRETT M. BROWN (born November 7, 1948) is an American character actor.[1][2][3]


Life

Brown was born in Michigan but grew up in Connecticut.[4] In 1972 he moved to New York City, where he worked as an orderly in a nursing home and as a waiter, before he started taking acting classes.[5]

He is known for portraying Bob Russell in _Uncle Buck_ (1989) and James Lizewski in _Kick-Ass_ (2010) and _Kick-Ass 2_ (2013). He also played the titular lead role in _Hello, My Name Is Frank_ (2014), for which he won the Best Actor Award at the Manhattan Film Festival.[6]

On television, he is best known for playing Taylor Brown in the sitcom _What a Country!_ (1986–1987) and for his recurring roles as John Whitsig in _Sisters_ (1991–1996) and Philip Evans in _Roswell_ (1999–2002).

In _The People v. O. J. Simpson: American Crime Story_ (2016) he played Nicole Brown Simpson's father Lou.
