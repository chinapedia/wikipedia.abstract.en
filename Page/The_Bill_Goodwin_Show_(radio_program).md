_THE BILL GOODWIN SHOW_ is an old-time radio situation comedy in the United States. It was broadcast on CBS April 26, 1947 - December 13, 1947.[1] In October 1947, the program's name was changed to _LEAVE IT TO BILL_.[2]


Format

_The Bill Goodwin Show_ centered around "a hotshot insurance salesman"[3] who was "an eager-beaver civic-improvement volunteer, with genius for landing behind eight-balls."[4]

Larry Burns was the director and producer.[7]


1957 program

Goodwin had another radio program a decade later. The 55-minute[8] _Bill Goodwin Show_ was broadcast on NBC radio beginning March 25, 1957. It was carried by 95 of the network's affiliates and sponsored by Schick razors.[9]
