The 1936 PACIFIC TIGERS FOOTBALL TEAM represented the College of the Pacific (COP)[1] during the 1936 college football season.

COP competed in the Far Western Conference (FWC).[2] The team was led by head coach Amos Alonzo Stagg, and played home games at Baxter Stadium in Stockton, California. This was Stagg's 47th year as a head coach and his fourth at College of the Pacific. The Tigers finished as champion of the FWC, with five wins, four losses and one tie (5–4–1, 4–0 FWC). Overall, the Tigers outscored their opponents 107–63 for the season.[3]
