_BAPHIASTRUM_ is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, found in west Africa.[1] It belongs to the subfamily Faboideae. _Baphiastrum_ was traditionally assigned to the tribe Sophoreae;[2] however, recent molecular phylogenetic analyses reassigned _Baphiastrum_ to the Baphieae tribe.[3][4]


Species

_Baphiastrum_ comprises the following species:[5][6][7]

-   _Baphiastrum boonei_ (De Wild.) Vermoesen
-   _Baphiastrum brachycarpum_ Harms
-   _Baphiastrum calophylla_ (Harms) De Wild.
-   _Baphiastrum elegans_ (Lester-Garland) De Wild.
-   _Baphiastrum klainei_ (De Wild.) De Wild.

-   _Baphiastrum pilosum_ (Baill.) De Wild.
-   _Baphiastrum spathaceum_ (Hook. f.) Staner
-   _Baphiastrum vermeuleni_ (De Wild.) De Wild.


Species names with uncertain taxonomic status

The status of the following species is unresolved:[8]

-   _Baphiastrum bequaerti_ De Wild.
-   _Baphiastrum claessensi_ De Wild.
-   _Baphiastrum spathacea_ Staner
