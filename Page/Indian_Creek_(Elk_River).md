INDIAN CREEK is a stream in Newton and McDonald counties of southwest Missouri.[1] It is a tributary of the Elk River.

The headwaters are in Newton County at and the confluence with Elk River is at .[2] The stream starts at the confluence of North and South Indian creeks just east of Boulder City and flows southwest into McDonald County. It flows past the communities of McNatt and Erie and under U. S. Route 71 and Missouri Route 76 just southeast of Anderson. The stream turns south and flows parallel to Missouri Route 59 past Lanagan to its confluence with the Elk River about midway between Pineville and Noel.[3]

Indian Creek was named for the fact American Indians had settled near its course.[4]
