Biography

Francesco Barozzi was born in Italy.[2] On 17 April 1466, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul II as Bishop of Treviso.[3][4] On 6 July 1466, he was consecrated bishop by Gautier de Forcalquier, Bishop of Gap, with Placido Pavanello, Bishop of Torcello, and Nicolas de Crucibus, Bishop of Hvar, serving as co-consecrators.[5] He served as Bishop of Treviso until his death in 1471.[6][7]
