ABU ASAD MOHAMMED OBAIDUL GHANI (1903–1973), also referred to as A.M.O. GHANI, was an Indian politician, belonging to the Communist Party of India.[1][2]


Youth

Obaidul Ghani's grandfather, Maulvi Elahad, had moved from Darbhanga, Bihar to Calcutta in the mid-19th century.[3] After obtaining his M.B.B.S. degree he founded the Park Circus charitable dispensary in 1950, serving the poor and lower middle-class populations of the city.[4][5] Obaidul Ghani contested the 1952 West Bengal Legislative Assembly election as an independent, standing in the Baniapukur Ballygung constituency.[6] He obtained 524 votes (0.88%).[7]


Legislator

Obaidul Ghani represented the Entally constituency in the West Bengal Legislative Assembly 1957–1971, standing as a CPI candidate in the 1957, 1962, 1967 and 1969 elections.[8] As a politician, he obtained significant popularity for his provision of medical services to the poor people of Calcutta.[9] He stayed with CPI in the 1964 split.[10] In August 1969 the Modern Furnishers Mazdoor Union was registered, with Obaidul Ghani as its president.[11] The union was affiliated to the All India Trade Union Congress.[12]

He lost the Entally seat to Mohammed Nizamuddin of the Communist Party of India (Marxist) in the 1971 West Bengal Legislative Assembly election.[13] He managed to defeat Mohammed Nizamuddin in the 1972 West Bengal Legislative Assembly election and regained the Entally seat.[14]


Death and legacy

Obaidul Ghani died following an attack of coronary thrombosis on 24 September 1973.[15][16][17] After Obaidul Ghani's death, a by-election was held for the Entally seat on 3 June 1974.[18] The by-poll was won by Sachindra Kumar Dhar.[19]

There is a road named "Dr. A.M.O. Ghani Road" in Ward 64 of Kolkata.[20] Obaidul Ghani used to live on this road, which was previously known as 'Pearl Road'.[21] The new name was issued on 1 April 1974.[22] There is also a "Dr. A.M.O. Gani Memorial Primary School" and a "Dr. A.M.O. Gani Memorial Girls School" in Ballygunge.[23]
