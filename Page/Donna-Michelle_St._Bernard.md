DONNA-MICHELLE ST. BERNARD is a Canadian playwright and theatre director.[1] She is a two-time nominee for the Governor General's Award for English-language drama, at the 2011 Governor General's Awards for _Gas Girls_[2] and at the 2016 Governor General's Awards for _A Man A Fish_.[3]

_Gas Girls_ also won a Dora Mavor Moore Award in 2010 for Best New Play in the independent theatre division,[4] as well as an Enbridge PlayRites Award from Alberta Theatre Projects and the Herman Voaden Playwriting Competition.[5] Her other plays have included _Cake_, _The House You Build_[6] and _Salome’s Clothes_.[7]

Originally from the Grenadines,[8] St. Bernard resides in Toronto, Ontario.[9] She is currently artistic director of New Harlem Productions.
