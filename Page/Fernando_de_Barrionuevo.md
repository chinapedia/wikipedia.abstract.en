FERNANDO DE BARRIONUEVO, O.F.M. (died 1571) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Santiago de Chile (1566â€“1571).[1][2][3]


Biography

Fernando de Barrionuevo was ordained a priest in the Order of Friars Minor.[4] On 11 Nov 1566, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Pius V as Bishop of Santiago de Chile.[5] In Dec 1569, he was consecrated bishop.[6] He served as Bishop of Santiago de Chile until his death on 26 Jul 1571.[7]
