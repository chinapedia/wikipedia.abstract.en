SANGISE is a village and gram panchayat in India, situated in the Mawal taluka of Pune district in the state of Maharashtra. It encompasses an area of .[1]


Administration

The village is administrated by a sarpanch, an elected representative who leads a gram panchayat. At the time of the 2011 Census of India, the village was the headquarters for the eponymous gram panchayat, which also governed the villages of Budhavadi and Velhavali, as well as the uninhabited village of Budhele.[2]


Demographics

At the 2011 census, the village comprised 149 households. The population of 694 was split between 383 males and 311 females.[3]
