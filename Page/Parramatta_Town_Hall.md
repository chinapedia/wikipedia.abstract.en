The PARRAMATTA TOWN HALL is a heritage-listed town hall located in the Sydney suburb of , New South Wales, Australia. Designed by Messrs Blackmann and Parkes in the Victorian Free Classical architectural style, the town hall was completed in 1883 at a cost of AÂ£2,300 to serve as the town hall and municipal chamber for the Borough of Parramatta.


History

Established by passage of the and given assent on 4 February 1879,[1] the Town Hall is listed on the (now defunct) Register of the National Estate[2] and as a local government listing on the New South Wales Heritage Database.[3] The Town Hall was officially opened on 30 August 1873.[4]


Design

The walls are of stuccoed brickwork; with stucco moulding includes bracketed pediment-labelled moulds to upper windows; upper parapet to match balustrade on first floor balcony; broken segmental pediments rising from upper parapet over the entrance bay.[5] In addition to the main hall that holds up to 300 people, the town hall has four additional rooms: the Jubilee Hall (accommodates up to 100 people), Charles Byrnes Room (accommodates up to 40 people), two meeting rooms (accommodating 30 and ten people respectively).[6]
