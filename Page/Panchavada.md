PANCHAV(A)DA is a village and former non-salute Rajput princely state on Saurashtra peninsula in Gujarat, western India.


History

The petty princely state, in Gohelwar _prant_, was ruled by Survaiya Rajput Chieftains. In 1901 it comprised a single village, with a population of 287, yielding 1,700 Rupees state revenue (1903-4, mostly from land), paying 241 Rupees tribute, to the Gaikwar Baroda State and Junagadh State.[1]
