FARIDA YASMIN is a Bangladeshi journalist,[1] the senior journalist of _The Daily Ittefaq_ and present general secretary of Bangladesh National Press Club.[2][3] In 2017 she was elected the first female general secretary of Bangladesh Press Club.[4][5] She was the former joint secretary of National Press Club.


Early life and education

Yasmin was born in Raipura Upazila, Narsingdi. Her father Shakhawar Hossain Bhuiyan and mother is Jahanara Hossain. She was the student of Shibpur Girls High School, Narsingdi. She passed SSC exam from Shibpur Girls High School and HSC exam from Eden Mohila College. Yasmin completed masters from University of Dhaka.[6]


Career

Yasmin was elected Joint secretary of the National Press Club of Bangladesh in 2011,[7] and 2012.[8][9] In 2013 she announced convicted war criminals Abdul Quader Molla and Muhammad Kamaruzzamann were expelled from the press club, saying "The unanimous decision came at the JPC managing committee meeting,".[10] She is the joint secretary of South Asian Women's Media Forum and was a founding member of the forum.[11][12] She spoke for keeping the age of marriage in Bangladesh at 18 for women.[13]

In 2016, Yasmin was made the vice-president of Dhaka University Mass Communication and Journalism Alumni Association.[14] She was elected General Secretary of Jatiya Press Club on 1 January 2017, she was first female General Secretary of the Jatiya Press Club.[15] She was re-elected to the post of General Secretary of the Jatiya Press Club on 18 December 2018.[16]


Published books

-   Ujjal Narir Mukhomukhi, 2005
-   Itehasher Aynay Bangabandhu, 2017


Personal life

Yasmin is married to journalist Naem Nizam. They have one son and one daughter.[17]
