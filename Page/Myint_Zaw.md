MYINT ZAW is a Burmese journalist and activist.

He was awarded the 2015 Goldman Environment Prize for Asia,[1][2] for bringing forward environmental and social impacts from the planned Myitsone Dam, a large Irrawaddy River dam project that is estimated to have impacts on millions of people, and displacing about 18,000 inhabitants.[3] In 2010, he and others issued the picture album _The Sketch of a River: The Ayeyarwady_, and organized several exhibitions of photographs from the Irrawaddy.[4]
