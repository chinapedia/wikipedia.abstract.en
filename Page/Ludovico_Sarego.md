LUDOVICO SAREGO (1558 – 5 August 1625) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Adria (1612–1622) and Apostolic Nuncio to Switzerland (1613–1621).[1][2][3]


Biography

Ludovico Sarego was born in Verona, Italy in 1558.[4] On 17 September 1612, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul V as Bishop of Adria.[5] On 14 October 1612, he was consecrated bishop by Giovanni Garzia Mellini, Cardinal-Priest of Santi Quattro Coronati with Coriolani Garzadori, Bishop of Ossero, and Marco Cornaro (bishop), Bishop of Padua, serving as co-consecrators.[6] On 15 September 1613, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul V as Apostolic Nuncio to Switzerland.[7] on 15 April 1621, he resigned as Apostolic Nuncio to Switzerland.[8] on 24 September 1622, he resigned as Bishop of Adria.[9] He died on 5 August 1625.[10]


Episcopal succession

While bishop, he was the principal co-consecrator of:[11]

-   Bartolomeo Cartolario, Bishop of Chioggia (1613);
-   Andreas Corbelli, Bishop of Canea (1613);
-   Vincenzo Bucchi (Buschio), Bishop of Kotor (1622);
-   Lazzaro Carafino, Bishop of Melfi e Rapolla (1622);
-   Fulgenzio Gallucci, Titular Bishop of _Thagaste_ (1623);
-   Eitel Friedrich von Hohenzollern-Sigmaringen, Bishop of Osnabruck (1623); and
-   Hyacinthus Arnolfini, Bishop of Milos (1625).
