MAURICE OLENDER (1946, Antwerp) is a French historian, professor at the École des hautes études en sciences sociales (EHESS) in Paris. His teaching focuses in particular on the genesis of the idea of "race" in the nineteenth century. As editor, he heads the journal __[1] and _La librairie du XXIe siècle_[2] at Éditions du Seuil


Scientific responsibilities

-   Cofounder of the _Scuola internazionale di alti studi scienze della cultura_ of Modena (Italy) in 1995. Founding member of its Scientific Advisory Board (1995–2009)
-   Associate professor and member of the board of the Martin Buber Institute of the Free University of Brussels.
-   Member of the _Steering Committee des Mishkenot Encounters for Religion and Culture_, in Jerusalem.
-   Member of the first editorial board, created in 1988, of the _Revue de l'histoire des religions_ founded in 1880.[3]
-   Directs the interdisciplinary journal _Le genre humain_, since its creation in 1981
