HENRY RAIWALUI (born 24 February 1989) is a Fijian international rugby league player who plays for the Mount Pritchard Mounties in the New South Wales Cup. He plays as a Halfback.[1][2]


Early life

Born in Sydney, Australia, Raiwalui is of Fijian descent. He grew up in Suva, Fiji from the age of two until year 4 of his schooling.[3] He played his junior rugby league for the Earlwood Saints.[4]


Playing career

In 2008 and 2009, Raiwalui played for the St. George Illawarra Dragons in the NYC,[5] In 2012, he represented a Fijian XIII against an Italian XIII, scoring a try.[6] before moving onto the Illawarra Cutters.[7]

In 2016, Raiwalui made his Test debut for Fiji against Samoa.[8][9]

In 2016 he also played for the Blacktown Workers in the Ron Massey Cup.[10] He played for Wentworthville when they competed in the Ron Massey Cup.[11]

In 2017, he joined the Mount Pritchard Mounties in the Intrust Super Premiership. Raiwalui was selected to play for Fiji in the 2017 Rugby League World Cup.
