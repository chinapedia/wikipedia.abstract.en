CARL WILHELM HARTMANN (3 July 1880 – 1957) was a Norwegian public prosecutor, judge and politician.

He was born in Kongsberg to physician Carl Christian Andreas Hartmann and Marie Sophie Sylow Thorne,[1] and was a brother of Paul Ernst Wilhelm Hartmann.[2] From 1928 to 1930 he served as mayor of Skien. He was elected representative to the Storting for the period 1931–1933, for the Liberal Party.[3][4]
