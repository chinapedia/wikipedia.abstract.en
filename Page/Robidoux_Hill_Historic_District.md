ROBIDOUX HILL HISTORIC DISTRICT is a national historic district located at St. Joseph, Missouri. The district encompasses 61 contributing buildings in a predominantly residential section of St. Joseph. It developed between about 1865 and 1909, and includes representative examples of Italianate, Second Empire, and Queen Anne style architecture. Bassett (c. 1860, 1880s), and U.S. Weather Bureau Building (1909).[1]

It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1989.[2]
