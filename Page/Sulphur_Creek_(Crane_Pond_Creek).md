SULPHUR CREEK is a stream in eastern Iron County in the U.S. state of Missouri.[1] It is a tributary of Crane Pond Creek.

The stream headwaters arise at and the confluence with Crane Pond Creek is at .[2] The source area is in Clark National Forest and the stream flows south and southeast out of the forest area. It passes under Missouri Route C before its confluence with Crane Pond Creek south of the community of Minimum.[3]

Sulphur Creek was so named due to the occurrence of iron ore along the stream which settlers mistook for sulphur.[4]
