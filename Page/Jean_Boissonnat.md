JEAN BOISSONNAT (16 January 1929 – 25 September 2016) was a French economic journalist. He was the co-founder and editor-in-chief of _L'Expansion_. He was the author of several books.


Early life

Jean Boissonnat was born on 16 January 1929 in Paris, France.[1] He graduated from Sciences Po.[2][3] While he was a student, he joined Jeunesse Étudiante Chrétienne.[4][5]


Career

Boissonnat was a faculty member at his alma mater, Sciences Po, from 1960 to 1971.[6]

Boissonnat was an economic journalist for _La Croix_ from 1954 to 1967.[7][8] In 1967, he co-founded _L'Expansion_ with Jean-Louis Servan-Schreiber.[9] He served as its editor-in-chief from 1967 to 1986, and as its senior editor from 1986 to 1994.[10] He also served as the editor-in-chief of _La Tribune_ from 1987 to 1992. He later founded _L'Entreprise_.[11]

Boissonnat was also a commentator on _Europe 1_.[12] Additionally, he wrote articles for _Le Parisien_, _Le Midi libre_, _Le Progrès_, _L’Est républicain_ and _Ouest-France_.[13] He served on the board of directors of Bayard Presse.[14]

Boissonnat was the author of several books.[15]


Death

Boissonnat died of a stroke on 25 September 2016 in Paris, France.[16][17][18] He was 87 years old.[19][20]
