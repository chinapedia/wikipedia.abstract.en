GEORGE STRACHAN RAMSAY (18 October 1892 – 8 August 1918) was a Scottish professional football inside right who played in the Scottish League for Queen's Park, Rangers, Ayr United and Partick Thistle.[1]


Personal life

Ramsay attended Allan Glen's School and in 1911 was working as an apprentice marine engineer in Glasgow.[2] Ramsay served as a flight lieutenant in 110 and 49 Squadrons of the Royal Air Force during the First World War and was flying a Airco DH.9 when he and his observer were shot down and killed near the bridge at Béthencourt-sur-Somme, during the Battle of Amiens on 8 August 1918.[3][4] He is commemorated on the Arras Flying Services Memorial.[5]
