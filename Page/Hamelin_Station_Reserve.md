HAMELIN STATION (also known as HAMELIN POOL STATION) was a pastoral lease in Western Australia. Since 2015 it has been known as HAMELIN STATION RESERVE, and is owned by Bush Heritage Australia.[1][2]

It is located at the south eastern side of the Shark Bay World Heritage area. It is also the location of the Shark Bay stromatolites.[3] The property occupies an area of including of coastline.[4]


Sheep station

It has been a sheep grazing station.[5][6] In 1884 the section of telegraph line to Roebourne that passed through the property had been completed.[7]
