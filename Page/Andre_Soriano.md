ANDRE SORIANO (born September 17, 1970) is a Filipino-born immigrant, American fashion designer, reality television star, who is actively involved in the MAGA movement.


Early life

Soriano was born and raised to a Filipino family[1] in Manila, Philippines residing in San Diego, California. His mother is named Josephine. His interest in fashion design started early, when as a child his mother designed draperies for the rest of the family.


Career

Soriano attended FIDM, a fashion school in San Francisco. In 2004, he opened a store in San Francisco selling active wear and ready-to-wear clothing. His true passion has been to make couture gowns. He moved to San Diego in 2008 and established an atelier from home with the help of his boyfriend Thomas Brown. In 2013, he competed on the first season of _Styled to Rock_ and came in 7th place.[2] On the show, he created a dresses for Carly Rae Jepsen as well as for other celebrities. His first runway show was at San Diego Fashion Week.[3]

Politics

Soriano has openly spoken about LGBT issues.[14] Being a Trump supporter,[15] he designed a gown for Villa for the 2017 Grammy Awards which was emblazoned with the name "Trump" and his "Make America Great Again" catchphrase.
