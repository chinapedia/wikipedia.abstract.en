The RANGER 32 is an American sailboat, that was designed by Gary Mull as an International Offshore Rule 3/4-ton racer and first built in 1973. The design is out of production.[1][2][3]

The boat was built by Ranger Yachts in the United States, which completed 147 examples.[4]

The Ranger 32 is a development of _Swampfire_, the winner of the first 3/4 ton cup championship held at Miami in 1974.[5]


Design

The Ranger 32 is a small recreational keelboat, built predominantly of fiberglass, with wood trim. It has a fractional sloop masthead sloop rig, a rudder mounted on a skeg and a fixed fin keel. It displaces and carries of lead ballast. The boat has a draft of with the standard keel.[6][7]
