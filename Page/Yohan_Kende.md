YOHAN KENDE (; born March 3, 1949) is an Israeli former Olympic swimmer.[1] He was born in Timişoara, Romania.[2]


Swimming career

Kende competed for Israel at the 1968 Summer Olympics in Mexico City, Mexico, in swimming at the age of 19.[3] He swam in the Men's 100 metre Breaststroke and came in 6th in Heat 4 with a time of 1:12.3, and in the Men's 200 metre Breaststroke and came in 8th in Heat 4 with a time of 2:44.3.[4] When he competed in the Olympics he was 5-10 (178 cm) tall, and weighed 154 lbs (70 kg).[5]
