VENETTA SEALS is an American politician and businesswoman who has been the Mayor of Pecos, Texas since May 2010. Her current term expires May 2019.[1] Before serving as mayor, Seals was the President of the Pecos Area Chamber of Commerce.[2]


Education and career

Seals graduated from Tyler Junior College with an Associate of Arts, and was a member of Phi Theta Kappa.[3] She continued her education at the University of Texas at Tyler and the University of Texas at El Paso, eventually graduating with a bachelor's degree in Commercial Art Design with a minor in English Literature.[4] While working towards her bachelor's degree, she was a member of Phi Beta Kappa.[5]

Prior to becoming mayor, Seals served as the President of the Pecos Area Chamber of Commerce,[6] and is currently a board member.[7] She is the owner of Hang Your Hat Storage, and is the Director of Public Relations, Marketing, Community Development, Physician Recruitment, and Grants for the Reeves County Hospital District.[8]

Prior to being elected mayor, Seals was a member of the Pecos City Council for four years.[9]


Mayor of Pecos

On May 8, 2010, Venetta Seals won the mayoral election with 49% of the vote. She defeated David L. Flores by two percentage points.[10] During her first two-year term as mayor, she saw a spike in housing demand due to increased oil drilling activity in the Wolfbone formation under Reeves County.[11] Pecos' population increased from 8,680 in 2010, to around 9,500 in early 2012, and the Pecos City Council voted to open a 500-capacity RV park to deal with the increase.[12] She also had to deal with the sudden death of City Manager Joseph Gilbert Torres on March 31, 2011, following his indictment for indecency with a child.[13]

On May 12, 2012, Seals was re-elected for a second term, with 496 of 956 votes. She defeated Bernadette Portillo Lopez and Noel Mata Ybarra.[14] During her second term, she defended O'Hara Flying Service, the city's air ambulance, after it was found to have state EMS rule violations.[15] She also dealt with the prolonged closure of the Pecos Zoo due to new regulations.[16]

On May 10, 2014, Seals was re-elected for a third term, defeating David L. Flores with 51% of the vote to his 49%.[17] In September 2014, she oversaw the city's response to heavy flooding after the Pecos River overflowed, with help from state and federal agencies.[18][19] In June 2015, the Mayor supported the Council's ban on plastic bags.[20]

Seals was re-elected for a fourth term on May 7, 2016, with a 35-vote lead.[21]
