Early life and education

Whittow read Modern History at Trinity College, Oxford and earned a DPhil in Byzantine history and archaeology.[4]


Academic career

Whittow was a research fellow and lecturer at Oriel College and held faculty positions at the University of Reading and at King's College, London before returning to Oxford in 1998 as a fellow of St Peter's College and University Lecturer in History. He became a fellow of Corpus Christi and University Lecturer in Byzantine Studies in 2009.[5][6] He was Senior Proctor of the university for the 2016/2017 academic year.[7] In November 2017, he was announced as the next Provost of Oriel College, Oxford; he was to take up the post in September 2018.[8][9]


Personal life

Whittow was married to Helen, a QC and Deputy High Court Judge.[10]

He died in a car accident in Oxfordshire late in the evening of 23 December 2017.[11]
