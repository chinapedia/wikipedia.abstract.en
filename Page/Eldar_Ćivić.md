ELDAR ĆIVIĆ (; born 28 May 1996) is a Bosnian professional footballer who plays as a left back for Nemzeti Bajnokság I club Ferencváros and the Bosnia and Herzegovina national team.

Ćivić started his professional career at Slovácko, before joining Sparta Prague in 2017. In 2018, he was loaned to Spartak Trnava. The following year he signed with Ferencváros.

A former youth international for Bosnia and Herzegovina, Ćivić made his senior international debut in 2018.


Club career

Early career

Ćivić began his football career at Sloboda Tuzla, before moving to Slovácko in 2012.[1] He made his professional debut against Příbram on 14 March 2015 at the age of 18.[2] More than a year later, on 2 April 2016, Ćivić scored his first professional goal.[3]

Sparta Prague

In May 2017, Ćivić was transferred to Sparta Prague on a four-year contract.[4] He made his competitive debut for the club on 27 July, in UEFA Europa League qualifier against Red Star Belgrade.[5] Three days later, he made his league debut against Bohemians 1905, which ended in a 1–1 draw.[6]

In February 2018, Ćivić was loaned to Spartak Trnava for the remainder of season.[7]

Ferencváros

On 26 June 2019, Ćivić was transferred to Hungarian outfit Ferencváros for an undisclosed transfer fee.[8]


International career

Ćivić represented Bosnia and Herzegovina on all youth levels. He also served as vice-captain of the under-21 team under coach Vinko Marinović.[9]

In May 2018, he received his first senior call-up, for friendly games against Montenegro and South Korea.[10] Ćivić debuted in a comfortable 3–1 victory over latter on 1 June 2018.[11]
