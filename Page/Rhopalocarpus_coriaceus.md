_RHOPALOCARPUS CORIACEUS_ is a tree in the family Sphaerosepalaceae. It is endemic to Madagascar.


Distribution and habitat

_Rhopalocarpus coriaceus_ is known from populations along the east coast of Madagascar, specifically in the regions of Sava, Alaotra Mangoro, Atsimo-Atsinanana, Analanjirofo and Anosy.[1] Its habitat is coastal forests from sea-level to altitude. Some populations are within protected areas.[2]


Threats

_Rhopalocarpus coriaceus_ is threatened by shifting patterns of agriculture. Because the species is used as timber, subsistence harvesting is also a threat.[3]
