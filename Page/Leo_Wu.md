LEO WU LEI (, born December 26, 1999) is a Chinese actor. He is known as the "Nation's Little Brother" in China.[1]


Career

Pre-debut; 2006–2012: Beginnings

In 2002, Wu appeared in the commercial of Chinese dietary supplement brand Huang Jin Da Dang, and featured in more than fifty commercials in the next two years. He made his acting debut in Chinese television drama, _The Legend and the Hero_ (2006).[2]

Wu first gained media attention for his roles in the children television dramas _Home with Aliens_ (2009)[3] and _Naughty boy Xiaotiao Ma_ (2010),[4] for which he won the Outstanding Child Actor award at the Flying Apsaras Awards.[5] He further raised his profile with his role in wuxia drama _Little Heroes_ (2012).[6]

2014–present: Rising popularity

In 2014, Wu featured in wuxia drama _Romance of the Condor Heroes_ produced by Yu Zheng, and received acclaim for his portrayal of young Yang Guo.[7]

In 2015, Wu starred as Li Xiaoyao in the web drama _Pal Inn_ and endorsed the video of game of the same name. The series gained over 300 million views, and became a popular topic online.[8] This was followed by well-received turns in youth sports drama _The Whirlwind Girl_ and wuxia drama _Nirvana in Fire_, which led to increased recognition for Wu.[9][10]

Wu took on his first small screen leading role in the fantasy web drama, _Magic Star_ alongside other child stars, which premiered in 2017.[11] He also co-starred in the British-Chinese action thriller _S.M.A.R.T. Chase_ directed by Charles Martin;[12] as well as Chinese-New Zealand production _Into the Rainbow_.[13]

In 2018, Wu starred in the fantasy epic _Asura_.[14][15] Coined as China's most expensive production with a budget of $110 million, the film was a box office bomb and was pulled from cinemas only two days after its premiere.[16] Wu then returned to the small screen in the fantasy adventure series _The Tomb of Sand_, written by the author of _Daomu Biji_;[17][18][19] and starred in _Battle Through the Heavens_, a drama based on the popular xianxia novel of the same name.[20] The same year, he featured in historical film _Shadow_ by acclaimed director Zhang Yimou.[21]

In 2019, Wu starred in the esports drama _Cross Fire_.[22] Wu also starred in the film _Adoring_.[23]
