Nicole_Alek'aq_Colbert_9478.jpg]] Founded in 1983, MISS INDIAN WORLD is a five-day competition held in Albuquerque, New Mexico.[1] The event is part of the annual Gathering of Nations, the largest Native American powwow in the world.[2][3] Young women from across North America represent their tribes and communities as they compete to win the crown. Rather than emphasizing contestants' outward appearance, Miss Indian World aims to select a winner who demonstrates a deep understanding of her culture, traditions, people and history.[4][5]

Once awarded the title, Miss Indian World acts as a cultural representative, promoting individually-selected platforms, sharing Native American culture, and representing the annual Gathering of Nations event.[6] This role is important to the native community because it helps perpetuate traditions and overturn negative stereotypes.[7]

In addition to the title, Miss Indian World also wins prize money, the Miss Indian shawl and banner, a four-day ocean cruise, and a travel stipend to attend powwows, conferences, workshops, and festivals.[8] Previous Miss Indian World winners have continued to be sought-after speakers, even after they have passed on the crown.[9][10]


Competition requirements

To qualify for Miss Indian World, contestants must be native or indigenous Americans between the ages of 18 and 24 as age requirements were updated for the 2018 pageant[11] with a verifiable tribal affiliation. They must also be single, never married, and have no dependents.[12]

Each contestant participates in four categories: Public Speaking and Personal Interviews, Traditional Talent Presentation, Dance Competition, and Essay. Contestants are evaluated based on their skill, as well as cultural knowledge about tribes and traditions.[13] The woman who accumulates the most points in each category will hold the Miss Indian World title for one year.

The reigning titleholder is Taylor Susan who is a member of the White Mountain Apache/Walker River Paiute tribes of Whiteriver in eastern Arizona. She was one of 30 contestants seeking the title, which was bestowed at a ceremony April 28, 2018 that drew 10,000 people to Tingley Coliseum at Expo New Mexico in Albuquerque.
