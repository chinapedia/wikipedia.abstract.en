TELECOMMUNICATIONS IN PUERTO RICO includes radio, television, fixed and mobile telephones, and the Internet.

Broadcasting in Puerto Rico is regulated by the US Federal Communications Commission (FCC).[1]


Radio

-   Stations: 140 radio stations (as of January 2015).[2][3]


Television

-   Stations: more than 30 TV stations operating; three stations of the US Armed Forces Radio and Television Service; cable TV subscription services are available (2007).[4]
-   Television sets: 1.0 million sets (1997).


Telephones

-   Calling code-area codes: +1-787, +1-939[5]
-   International call prefix: 00[6]
-   Main lines: 780,200 lines in use, 87th in the world (2012).[7]
-   Mobile cellular: 3.1 million lines, 130th in the world (2012).[8]
-   System: modern digital system integrated with that of the United States (2011).[9]
-   Satellite earth stations: Intelsat with high-speed data capability (2011).[10]
-   Communications cables: provide connectivity to the US, Caribbean, Central and South America (2011).[11]


Internet

-   Top-level domain: .pr[12]
-   Internet users: 1.9 million users, 92nd in the world; 51.4% of the population, 83rd in the world (2012).[13][14]
-   Fixed broadband: available to 65% of Puerto Ricans (2012).[15]
-   Wireless broadband: available to 98.7% of Puerto Ricans (2012).[16]
-   Available broadband technologies as a percentage of population: 48.2% DSL, 0.1% Fiber, 59.6% cable, 84.7% wireless (2012).[17]
-   Internet hosts: 469 hosts, 184th in the world (2012).[18]
-   IPv4: 1 million addresses allocated, less than 0.05% of the world total, 250.2 addresses per 1000 people (2012).[19][20]
-   Internet Service Providers: 19 ISPs (1999).

Internet censorship and surveillance

Being a state dependency that controls the US territory, the country's Internet censorship is from the United States.
