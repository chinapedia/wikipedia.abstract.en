The Una River runs through the western section of the refuge.


History

On 15 May 2006 the Brazilian Institute of Environment and Renewable Natural Resources (IBAMA) began public consultations on the proposed creation of the Una and Rio dos Frades wildlife refuges, as well as the proposed expansion of the Pau-Brazil and Descobrimento national parks and of the Una Biological Reserve. The Una Wildlife Refuge was created by federal decree on 21 December 2007 with an area of about in two separate areas to protect natural environments that ensure conditions for existence or reproduction of flora and local or migratory fauna. It became part of the Central Atlantic Forest Ecological Corridor, created in 2002.
