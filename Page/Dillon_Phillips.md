DILLON PHILLIPS (born 11 June 1995) is an English professional footballer who plays as a goalkeeper for club Charlton Athletic. He had brief loan spells at Whitehawk and Bishop's Stortford, before helping Cheltenham Town to win the National League whilst on a season-long loan during the 2015–16 campaign.


Career

Phillips came through the Charlton Athletic academy to sign professional forms in summer 2013, and joined Conference South side Whitehawk on a one-month loan on 8 November 2013.[1] He returned to the Conference South on a one-month loan with Bishop's Stortford on 3 January 2015.[2] He joined National League club Cheltenham Town on a six-month loan in July 2015.[3] He started every game for the "Robins", keeping ten clean sheets in 26 games, and had his loan extended until the end of the 2015–16 season.[4] He picked up an injury in March 2016, and missed the end-of-season run-in as Cheltenham secured promotion as National League champions.[5] He signed a new two-year contract with Charlton in May 2016.[6]
