OLOF ALGOT LENNART SVAHN,[1] later OLOF LENNART SWAHN[2] (8 April 1927 – 27 August 2008) was a Swedish journalist and radio and television personality.


Career

Swahn was born in Stockholm, Sweden, the son of salesman Karl Algot Swahn (1900–1976) and his wife Ellen Fryklund (1904–1999).[3] He had a 15-year younger brother,[4] Eigil Svahn.[5] He graduated from _Stockholms stads handelsgymnasium_ in 1947 and was a reporter at the Associated Press from 1948 to 1950 as well as at the newspaper _Arbetaren_ and Sveriges Radio from 1950 to 1953.[6] Swahn was a music critic in the newspaper _Stockholms-Tidningen_ from 1953 to 1957 and studied at the University of Minnesota in the United States in 1955. He was a PR man for Svensk Filmindustri in 1957 and opera and ballet critic in the journal _Scen och Salong_ from 1957 and culture reporter at Sveriges Radio from 1960.[7]

Swahn was producer for _Riksronden_ and _På minuten_ and music and entertainment programs on radio and television.[8] When he in the early 1970s came to television and became the host of the cafe program _Halvsju_ he became nationally famous.[9] He was TV host for _Riksronden_, _På minuten_, _Babbel_, _Halvsju_, _Gäster med gester_ (_Give Us a Clue_), _Razzel_ and _Blåsningen_. He also presented New Year's concerts and the Nobel Banquet as well as Melodifestivalen 1986 together with Tommy Engstrand.

Swahn was chairman of _Stockholms sångarförbund_ from 1983.[10]


Personal life

Swahn was for many years living with David Bonnier[11][12] (born 1933), the son of publisher Kaj Bonnier and Ulla (née Wetterlind).[13] Swahn and Bonnier lived together in an apartment, each with a door and nameplate on Odengatan in Stockholm. Two weeks before his death Swahn changed his address to Bonnier's summer house in Tyresö, where he spent much of his final time.[14] Swahn moved to Maria Regina Hospice in Nacka in March 2008 where he spent four months. In late July 2008, Swahn was admitted to Södersjukhuset in Stockholm. On 19 August 2008 he was transferred to the Handen Hospital where he died a week later.[15] Swahn was buried at Tyresö Begravningsplats close to Tyresö Church.[16]
