Career

Ebeling was born in Zaandam on 8 February 1924.[3] He finished his in Slavic languages at the University of Amsterdam in 1947. He then moved to the United States and studied Slavic languages at Harvard University and obtained his PhD there under Roman Jakobson in 1950 with a thesis titled: _The parts of the sentence in modern Russian: a structural analysis_.[4][5]

Ebeling returned to the Netherlands and from 1955 to 1960 he was professor of Slavic languages. He subsequently became professor of both Slavic and Baltic languages. He retired in 1985.[6]

In 1954 Ebeling started formulating his ideas on syntax in an article. In 1978 he wrote his _magnum opus_ on the topic, _Syntax and Semantics. A Taxonomic Approach_. He followed this up in 2006 with the Dutch-language _Semiotaxis. Over theoretische en Nederlandse syntaxis_.[7] In the 1960s Ebeling came up with a law-like theory of verbal paradigmic accentuation in Slavic, and possibly Balto-Slavic languages.[8]

Ebeling was elected a member of the Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences in 1979.[9]

Ebeling died in Graft on 24 February 2017.[10]
