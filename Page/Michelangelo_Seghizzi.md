MICHELANGELO SEGHIZZI, O.P. (1565 – March 1625) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Lodi (1616–1625).[1][2]


Biography

Michelangelo Seghizzi was born in Lodi, Italy in 1565 and ordained a priest in the Order of Preachers.[3] On 13 June 1616, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul V as Bishop of Lodi.[4] On 26 June 1616, he was consecrated bishop by Giovanni Garzia Mellini, Cardinal-Priest of Santi Quattro Coronati, with Ulpiano Volpi, Archbishop Emeritus of Chieti, and Alessandro Guidiccioni (iuniore), Bishop of Lucca, serving as co-consecrators.[5] He served as Bishop of Lodi until his death in March 1625.[6]
