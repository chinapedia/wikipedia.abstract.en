SOCIAL TABLES is a global SaaS company headquartered in Washington, DC that was founded in 2011 by Dan Berger[1] and Matthew Tendler.[2] With a focus to “inspire face-to-face experiences by empowering those who plan and attend them,”[3] the company has created a collaborative web-based event planning platform.[4] The technology includes three-dimensional floor plans where host venues and event organizers can build rooms to position items needed for the event at hand.[5]

Social Tables has 120 employees[6] and 5,500 customers in 100 countries.[7] Over 4.5 million events have been planned inside the Social Tables event technology.[8] Major clients include Intercontinental Hotels Group[9], Hyatt Hotels, and Goldman Sachs.[10]


History

Social Tables has raised $8M in a Series A funding round led by Bessemer Venture Partners and Thayer Ventures, [11] and $13MM in a Series B funding round led by QuestMark Partners. Social Tables was acquired by Cvent for $100m on October 16, 2018.[12]


Recognition

In 2015, the company was named one of The Washington Post's, “Top Places to Work”.[13] Social Tables was recognized by Deloitte as one of the 2018 Technology Fast 500 Winners, meaning they were one of the 500 fastest growing technology companies in North America.[14] In January 2016, Social Tables moved to a new 30,000 square foot office located in Washington, DC's Metro Center.[15] Social Tables has dedicated 10,000 square feet of its headquarters to holding events for the DC community.[16]
