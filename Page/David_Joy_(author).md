DAVID JOY (born December 11, 1983) is an American novelist[1] and short-story writer.


Career

David Joy is the author of the Edgar Award-nominated[2] novel _Where All Light Tends To Go_ (G. P. Putnam's Sons, 2015),[3] as well as the novels _The Weight Of This World_ (G. P. Putnam's Sons, 2017)[4] and _The Line That Held Us_ (G. P. Putnam's Sons, 2018).[5] He is also the author of the memoir _Growing Gills: A Fly Fisherman's Journey_ (Bright Mountain Books, 2011),[6] which was a finalist for the Reed Environmental Writing Award and the Ragan Old North State Award.

Joy is the recipient of an artist fellowship from the North Carolina Arts Council.[7] His writing has appeared in numerous magazines and journals, such as _Garden & Gun_, _Time_,[8] and _The New York Times Magazine_.

He lives in the Little Canada community of Jackson County, North Carolina.[9]
