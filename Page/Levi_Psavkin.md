LEVI PSAVKIN (also "Levy"; לוי פסבקין; born April 12, 1939) is an Israeli former Olympic runner.[1] He was the Israeli Champion in the 100 metres (1964 and 1967), and in the 200 metres (1964).

He is Jewish, and his entire family was killed in the Holocaust.[2][3]


Running career

His personal best in the 100 metres was 10.6, in 1963.[4]

He won the Israeli Championship in the 100 metres in both 1964 and 1967, and in the 200 metres in 1964.[5]

He competed for Israel at the 1964 Summer Olympics in Tokyo, Japan, at the age of 25 in Athletics.[6] In the Men's 100 metres he came in 7th in Heat 5, with a time of 11.1.[7] He placed 64th out of 72 runners.[8] When he competed in the Olympics, he was 5-8 (173 cm) and weighed 168 lbs (76 kg).[9]


Running administration

He was Chief of the Israeli delegation at the 2002 European Track and Field Championships.[10] He has been a member of the European Athletic Association's Development Committee, and the Veterans Committee of the International Association of Athletics Federation.[11]

He is now the Secretary of the Israeli Athletic Association, in Tel Aviv, Israel.[12]
