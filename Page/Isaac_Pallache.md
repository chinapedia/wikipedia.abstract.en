ISAAC PALLACHE (1593–1650) was born in 1593, possibly in Fez, Morocco, son of Joseph Pallache and nephew of Samuel Pallache. He came from the Sephardic Pallache family.[1]


Career

Pallache studied at the University of Leiden, where he registered February 21, 1629.[2]

With his brother Abraham, he served as an agent from the Netherlands to Morocco on his father's behalf.[3]


Personal and death

Pallache converted to Christianity before 1629.[4]

Little is known about his death.
