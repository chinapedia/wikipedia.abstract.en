Major-General SIR WILLIAM ERNEST VICTOR ABRAHAM CBE FGS (21 August 1897 â€“ 6 February 1980)[1] was a British Army officer who served in India and Burma during the Second World War. He was nicknamed 'WEVA'.[2]


Early life and education

Sir William was born in Enniskillen and educated at Methodist College Belfast and the Royal College of Science, Dublin.[3]


Military service

He visited Burma and India as a geologist from 1920 to 1937 with the Burmah Oil Company.[4][5] Abraham also commanded the Upper Burma Battalion of the Burma Auxiliary Force from 1932-1937 as lieutenant-colonel.[6][7] In 1940 he rejoined the army as a second lieutenant in the Royal Engineers after the War Office asked him to analyse the events in France.[8] He was awarded an OBE and mentioned in dispatches twice for his service in the Middle East. His actions in Tunisia resulted in him being appointed a CBE in 1942.[9]

In 1945 Sir William was Comptroller General of Military Economy in India.[10]

His report, _Summary of the economic developments in the Far East during the six months ending 30th June, 1944_ is held in the Liddell Hart Centre for Military Archives, King's College London.[11]


Later career

He worked as managing director of Burmah Oil Company until 1955.[12] From 1962 until 1977 Sir William was national chairman of the Burma Star Association.[13]


Personal life

Sir William married Susan Jeanette Bidwell in 1928. They had one son and two daughters. After Susan's death in 1965, Sir William married Rosemary Eustace.[14]
