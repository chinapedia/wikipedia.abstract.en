Biography

On 1 October 1618, Giulio Monterenzi was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul V as Bishop of Faenza.[2] On 7 October 1618, he was consecrated bishop by Giovanni Garzia Mellini, Cardinal-Priest of Santi Quattro Coronati, with Ulpiano Volpi, Archbishop Emeritus of Chieti, and Francesco Sacrati (cardinal), Titular Archbishop of _Damascus_, serving as co-consecrators.[3] He served as Bishop of Faenza until his death on 23 May 1623.[4]
