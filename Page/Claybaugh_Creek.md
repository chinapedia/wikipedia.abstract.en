CLAYBAUGH CREEK is a stream in the Saint Francois Mountains of Iron County in the U.S. state of Missouri.[1]

The headwaters arise high on the south slope of Taum Sauk Mountain about 2000 feet south of the lookout at an elevation of about 1620 feet[2] at .[3] The stream flows south and east to its confluence with Minor Creek is at the head of Royal Gorge adjacent to Missouri Route 21[4] at and at an elevation of 1063 feet.[5]

Claybaugh Creek has the name of an early charcoal producer.[6]
