GIORGIO GIORGICCI or GEORGIUS GEORGICEO (1614 – February 1660) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Krk (1653–1660) and Bishop of Nona (1649–1653).[1][2][3][4][5][6]


Biography

Giorgio Giorgicci was born in Spalati in 1614.[7] On 21 June 1649, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Innocent X as Bishop of Nona.[8][9] On 8 September 1652, he was consecrated bishop by Marcantonio Franciotti, Cardinal-Priest of Santa Maria della Pace, with Giovanni Alfonso Puccinelli, Archbishop of Manfredonia, and Patrizio Donati, Bishop Emeritus of Minori serving as co-consecrators.[10] On 22 September 1653, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Innocent X as Bishop of Krk.[11][12] He served as Bishop of Krk until his death in February 1660.[13][14]

While bishop, he was the principal co-consecrator of Baldassarre Bonifazio, Bishop of Capodistria (1653); and Francesco de Andreis, Bishop of Nona (1653).[15]
