The 2003 LIGA PERDANA 2 season is the sixth and final season of Liga Perdana 2. A total of 12 teams participated in the season.[1]

Kuala Lumpur and Negeri Sembilan were relegated from Liga Perdana 1 to join the Liga Perdana 2.[2]

The season kicked off on 10 February 2003.[3] Public Bank won the title and the playoff for the newly formed top-tier league in Malaysia, the Malaysia Super League.
