VASCO GUEDES DE CARVALHO E MENESES (5 April 1822[1] – 1 January 1905) was a Portuguese colonial administrator and a military officer. He was a younger brother of José Guedes de Carvalho e Meneses who was governor of Mozambique and Cape Verde.[2]

He was governor general of Mozambique from 24 April 1854 until 26 September 1857.[3] He was governor general of Cape Verde from 22 December 1876[4] until 7 May 1878, when he became governor of Angola,[5] an office he fulfilled until July 1880.[6] He was governor of Portuguese India from 16 June 1889 until 10 March 1891.[7]

He was Commander of the Order of Nossa Senhora da Conceição de Vila Viçosa.[8]
