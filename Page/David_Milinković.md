MANUEL DAVID MILINKOVIĆ (, ; born 20 May 1994) is a French-Serbian professional football player who plays as a striker for EFL Championship club Hull City in England.


Club career

Born in Antibes,[1] France,[2] Milinković has Serbian origins. His grandfather Mirko came to France from the outskirts of Sarajevo. Already in France, David´s father Igor was born, who worked at Partus corporation dedicated to casinos, hotels, thermal centers, restaurants, etc.[3] Mirko´s grandson, Igor´s son, David, started playing football in the youth team of AS Cannes.[4]

During the winter-break of the 2011–12 season he moved to Serbia and joined the youth team of Red Star Belgrade.[5] He then joined FK Rad which loaned him to third level side FK BASK[6] in the 2012–13 winter-break. In summer 2013 he joined BASK permanently[7] and played with them until the winter-break of the 2014–15 season.

Then he moved to Italy, where, after a short time spent in A.S. Roma,[8] he joined Ternana Calcio,[9] playing with them in the second half of the 2014–15 Serie B season.[10] In summer 2015, he joined another Serie B side, Salernitana.[11]

In the winter-break of the 2015–16 season, he signed with Genoa C.F.C.[12] which immediately loaned him to Serie B side Virtus Lanciano[13] where he played the second half of the 2015–16 Serie B.[14] In summer 2016, Genoa loaned him to Messina[15] playing in the Lega Pro, before he signed a season long loan with Heart of Midlothian F.C. in 2017. On 9 July 2018 he signed a 3 year deal (with an option for an additional year) with Hull City.[16] He made his debut in the first match of the 2018–19 season on 6 August 2018 at home to Aston Villa, coming off the bench as a 67th-minute substitute for Todd Kane, in a 1–3 defeat.[17]
