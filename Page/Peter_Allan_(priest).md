PETER GEORGE ALLAN (born 1950) is a British Anglican priest and monk. Since 2011, he has been principal of the College of the Resurrection, an Anglo-Catholic theological college in Mirfield, West Yorkshire, England.


Early life and education

Allan was born in 1950.[1] He studied music at Wadham College, Oxford, graduating with a Bachelor of Arts (BA) degree in 1972:[2][3] as per tradition, his BA was promoted to a Master of Arts (MA Oxon) degree in 1976.[4] In 1972, he matriculated into the College of the Resurrection, Mirfield, to study theology and train for ordination.[5][6] He left the college after three years to be ordained in the Church of England.[7]


Ordained ministry

Allan was ordained in the Church of England as a deacon in 1975 and as a priest in 1976.[8] From 1975 to 1978, he served his curacy at St Andrew's Church, Stevenage, an Anglo-Catholic parish in the Diocese of St Albans.[9][10][11] Then, from 1978 to 1982, he was chaplain of Wadham College, Oxford, and a curate of the University Church of St Mary the Virgin, Oxford.[12][13]

In 1982, Allan joined the Community of the Resurrection as a novice.[14][15] In 1985, he made his vows, thereby becoming a monk and a full member of the Community of the Resurrection.[16] Since 1985, he has been the precentor of the community.[17] In 2011, he was also appointed principal of the College of the Resurrection, an Anglo-Catholic theological college that is associated with the Community of the Resurrection.[18][19] He is also a lecturer in moral theology and liturgical music at the college.[20] He will step down as principal at the end of the 2018/2019 academic year and is the be succeeded by Mark Sowerby, currently Bishop of Horsham.[21]

Views

In January 2016, Allan signed an open letter addressed to the Archbishops of Canterbury and York, asking for "Acknowledgement that we, the Church, have failed in our duty of care to LGBTI members of the Body of Christ around the world" and calling for "Repentance for accepting and promoting discrimination on the grounds of sexuality, and for the pain and rejection that this has caused."[22]
