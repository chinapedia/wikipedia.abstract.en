In the CHICAGO MAYORAL ELECTION OF 1977, interim mayor Michael A. Bilandic was elected to complete the remainder of an unexpired mayoral term, defeating Republican Dennis H. Bloc by a landslide 56% margin.[1]


Background

After to the death in office of Richard J. Daley on December 20, 1976, Bilandic had been selected by the Chicago City Council to serve as acting mayor for six months until an election would be held.


Primaries and nominations

Democratic primary

Bilandic won the Democratic Party's primary.

Bilandic won a plurality of the vote in 38 of the city's 50 wards.[2] Pucinski won a plurality of the vote in 7 wards.[3] Washington won a plurality of the vote in 5 wards.[4]

Republican primary

The Republican nomination was captured by 47th Ward alderman Dennis H. Bloc.[8]

Bloc was the first mayoral candidate since Martin H. Kennelly to be a resident of Edgewater.[9] Thus, he was the first candidate from Edgewater since 1955 and the first general election nominee since 1951 to hail from Edgewater. He was the fourth overall mayoral candidate from the neighborhood, and would have been the third mayor from the neighborhood if elected.[10]
