EDGAR BRIGHT WILSON (1874-1953) was an American lawyer and politician. He served as the Speaker of the Tennessee House of Representatives from 1901 to 1903.


Early life

Edgar Brighton Wilson was born in 1874.[1] His father, James A. Wilson, was a veteran of the Confederate States Army during the American Civil War.[2] His mother was Mary Graves.[3] His paternal uncle, Samuel Franklin Wilson, was a Confederate veteran and a judge.[4]

Wilson was educated at the Chapel Hill Academy.[5] He graduated from Cumberland University in 1893.[6] He studied the law under his uncle, and he was admitted to the bar in 1894.[7]


Career

Wilson practised the law in Gallatin, Tennessee from 1894 to 1901.[8] He was a lawyer in Nashville, Tennessee from 1901 onward.[9]

Wilson served as a member of the Tennessee House of Representatives from 1898 to 1903, representing Sumner County.[10] He also served as the Speaker of the House from 1901 to 1903.[11]


Personal life and death

Wilson married Anna Lackey in 1902.[12] He was a Freemason and a member of the Knights of Pythias.[13] He was a Presbyterian.[14] He died in 1953.
