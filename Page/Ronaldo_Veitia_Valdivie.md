RONALDO VEITIA VALDIVIE is the former Coach for the Cuban Olympic Judo team. Born October 21, 1947.[1] Valdivie led the women's Judo team to multiple Olympic medals, and world championships.[2] Valdivie also was responsible for the team winning consecutive gold medals at the Pan Am Games since 1975.[3] His players Dayaris Mestre , Magdiel Estrada, and Idalys Ortiz all won gold for Cuba under him.[4] In fact, he coached his team to coached at six Olympic Games from 1992 to 2012 and obtained 5 gold, 9 silver and 10 bronze medals.[5] This made him the coach with the largest amount of Olympic Medals in the history of Judo.[6] In Munich, he held a seminar on how to build a fighting machine.[7] In 30 years he earned 308 international medals for Judo.[8]
