LIAM ANTHONY KELLY (born 22 November 1995) is a professional footballer who plays for Eredivisie club Feyenoord as a midfielder.


Club Career

Reading

In November 2013, Kelly signed his first professional contract with Reading,[1] In May 2016, Kelly signed a two-year contract with Reading.[2] On 12 February 2016, Kelly joined Bath City on an initial one-month loan,[3] extending it for another month on 11 March,[4] before extending it till the end of the season 10 April 2016.[5] Kelly made his debut for Reading on 23 August 2016 at home to Milton Keynes Dons in the EFL Cup, coming on for George Evans,[6] with his league debut coming on 22 October 2016 away to Rotherham United.

On 30 December, Kelly signed a new contract, keeping him at the Reading until the summer of 2019.[7]

He scored his first goal for Reading in a 3-2 win against Bristol City on 2 January 2017.[8]

Kelly extended his Reading contract on 6 July 2017, signing a new three-year deal.[9]

Feyenoord

On 8 July 2019, Kelly signed for Feyenoord on a three-year contract for an undisclosed fee.[10][11]


International career

Republic of Ireland

Kelly made his Republic of Ireland under-19 debut against Slovenia under-19 in September 2013.[12] Whilst playing for the Republic of Ireland U19's against Sweden under-19, Kelly scored a wonder goal from the halfway line.[13] Kelly made his Republic of Ireland under-21 debut against United States under-20 in November 2014.[14]

Kelly received his first call up for the Republic of Ireland on 13 March 2017, being named in the 39-man provisional squad for the games against Wales and Iceland.[15]

Kelly rejected a call-up to the Republic of Ireland senior squad in March 2018, due to personal reasons.[16]
