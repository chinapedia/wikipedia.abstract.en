KONG VISUDHAROMN (, , 2 May 1907 â€“ 1 October 1985) was a Thai educator. His work contributed to the development of physical education, sport, and the Scout Movement in Thailand. He served as Director-General of the Department of Physical Education, a member of the Olympic Committee of Thailand, and the Deputy International Commissioner of the National Scout Organization of Thailand.

In 1980, Visudharomn was awarded the 142nd _Bronze Wolf_, the only distinction of the World Organization of the Scout Movement, awarded by the World Scout Committee for exceptional services to world Scouting.[1]
