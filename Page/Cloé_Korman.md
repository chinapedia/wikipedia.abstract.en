CLOÉ KORMAN (born 1983 in Paris) is a French writer.

Cloé Korman studied Anglo-Saxon literature before settling for two years in New York. During trips in 2005, she discovered the west coast of the United States and Mexico that inspired her first novel __ (2010) which earned her the Inter Book Prize[1] and the Prix Littéraire Valery Larbaud.


Work

-   2010: _Les Hommes-couleurs_, éditions du Seuil
-   2013: _Les Saisons de Louveplaine_, Seuil, series "Cadre rouge",
