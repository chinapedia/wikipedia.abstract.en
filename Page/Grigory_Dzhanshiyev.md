GRIGORY AVETOVICH DZHANSHIYEV (; , 29 May 1851, Tiflis, Russian Empire, now Georgia, — 30 July 1900, Moscow, Russian Empire) was Russian lawyer, publicist and historian of Armenian descent. A Moscow University alumnus, Dzhanshiyev authored 25 books, the best-known of which, _On the Times of the Great Reform_ (Из эпохи великих реформ, 1892), was re-issued several times in his lifetime and is considered one of the best treatises on Alexander II's reforms in law and jurisdiction. Dzhanshiyev was also one of the authors of the Brockhaus and Efron Encyclopedic Dictionary.[1][2]
