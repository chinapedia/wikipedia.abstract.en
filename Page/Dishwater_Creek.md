DISHWATER CREEK is a stream in Montgomery County in the U.S. state of Missouri.[1] It is a tributary of the Loutre River.[2]

The stream headwaters arise at [3] at an elevation of 800 feet.[4] The stream flows to the south-southwest through the Danville State Wildlife Area and passes under Missouri Route J to cross the Loutre floodplain[5] to its confluence with the Loutre River at and an elevation of 515 feet.[6]

According to tradition, the stream was named because a local woman used the stream to wash dishes.[7]
