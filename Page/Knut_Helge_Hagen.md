KNUT HELGE HAGEN (born 20 November 1971) is a retired Norwegian football midfielder.

He played youth football for Borg,[1] but was picked up by second-tier club IF Pors in 1989.[2] Pors was eventually relegated, but Hagen joined second-tier club Odds BK, where he became captain. Ahead of the 1996 season he joined the newly promoted Tippeligaen club Moss FK,[3] and after one season there he commenced a spell with Skeid.[4] During his time at Skeid, the team contested the 1998 Tippeligaen. In 2001, he moved home to Grenland to play for newly promoted second-tier club Tollnes BK. After three seasons there and two in Pors, he ended his career in Borg.
