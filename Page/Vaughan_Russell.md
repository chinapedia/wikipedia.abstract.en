JOSEPH VAUGHAN RUSSELL (13 January 1890 – 1979) was a Scottish amateur football centre forward who appeared in the Scottish League for Queen's Park.[1]


Personal life

Russell worked as a commercial traveller.[2] He served as a private in the Scottish Horse and as a sergeant in the Royal Engineers during the First World War,[3] including service in Egypt.[4] Russell later had a successful business career, becoming a director of Charles Tennant & Co and managing the Lochaline optical glass mines during the Second World War.[5]
