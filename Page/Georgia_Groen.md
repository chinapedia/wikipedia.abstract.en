GEORGIA GROEN is a New Zealand barefoot water-skier. In 2016, she won the overall open women's title at the Barefoot Waterski World Championships held in Wisconsin, United States.[1]


Biography

Groen grew up in the North Island town of Otaki, where she attended Kapiti College.[2] In 2013, she moved to Christchurch to study at the University of Canterbury.[3]

Competitions

Groen competed in her first event at the age of 8,[4] and her first world championship event at the age of 12, in 2009. In 2013, she attended the Barefoot Waterski World Championships in Mulwala, Australia, and won silver in the overall open womenâ€™s section.[5]

In the 2016 championship, Groen won the overall open women's title, and also her third consecutive open world trick title, silver in open slalom, silver in open jump and a bronze in the open team overall event.[6]

Awards

In 2011, Groen won the Kapiti Emerging Sportsperson of the Year Award,[7] and the Sportsperson of the Year in 2012.[8]
