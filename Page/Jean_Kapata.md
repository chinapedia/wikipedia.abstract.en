JEAN KAPATA (born 25 December 1960) is a Zambian politician who currently serves as Minister of Lands and Natural Resources since September 2016.


Early life and education

Kapata has a Diploma in Nursing.


Career

Kapata worked as a theatre nurse before being elected to represent the Mandevu constituency at the 2006 election for the Patriotic Front. She was re-elected in 2011 and 2016.[1]

Kapata served as Deputy Minister of Community Development, Mother & Child Health. On 2 October 2009, she was arrested with nine other Patriotic Front MPs after they protested the acquittal of former president Frederick Chiluba with car honks and whistles. They were released on 7 October.[2]

In 2014, Kapata was appointed by President Michael Sata as Minister for Tourism and Arts.[3] Under her tenure, the ban on big-game hunting cats in Zambia was lifted.[4][5] In response to the international media attention surrounding the killing of Cecil the lion in neighbouring Zimbabwe, Kapata said, "the West seemed more concerned with the welfare of a lion in Zimbabwe than of Africans themselves."[6][7]

Kapata was a member of the Zambia delegation to the United Nations 60th Session of the Commission on the Status of Women in March 2016.[8] In the lead up to the August 2016 election Kapata received death threats and her driver was attacked by armed men demanding to know where she was.[9] The MMD candidate for her constituency, Mary Phiri, accused Kapata of sending people to attack her campaign team.[10] On 27 September 2016, she became Minister for Lands, Natural Resources and Environmental Protection.[11] She is also the Patriotic Front's Chairperson for Elections.[12][13]


Personal life

Kapata is married and is a mother and grandmother.[14] In 2013, First Lady Christine Kaseba officiated the wedding of Kapata's daughter.[15]
