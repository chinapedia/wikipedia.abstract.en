Campden_Hill_Road,_London_W8_-_geograph.org.uk_-_624239.jpg CAMPDEN HILL ROAD is a street in Kensington, London W8. It runs north to south from Notting Hill Gate to Kensington High Street.


History

Campden Hill Road was originally called Plough Lane.[1] By 1879, William Abbott, a stockbroker, "held the lease of the site between Phillimore Walk, Campden Hill Road, and Hornton Street".[2]

Notable residents include the novelist Ford Madox Ford (1873–1939) who lived at no 80, the poet Cecil Day Lewis,with his children,including the actor Sir Daniel Day Lewis and wife Jill Balcon,and the publisher Andrew White Tuer (1838–1900).Anglo-American landscape painter George Henry Boughton (1834-1905) lived & died at no 118,West House,in a house built for him.

The Kensington Central Library is located at the bottom of the street, just up from Phillimore Walk.[3]
