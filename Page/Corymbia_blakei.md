_CORYMBIA BLAKEI_, commonly known as the GHOST GUM,[1] is a bloodwood native to eastern Australia.

The tree typically grows to a height of and forms a lignotuber.

_C. blakei_ is native to an area in arid South West and Central West Queensland extending from Cory Range south west of Winton to the upper Paroo and Bulloo River area between Charleville and Quilpie.[2]

Two recognised subspecies are known:

-   _C. blakei_ subsp _blakei_[3]
-   _C. blakei_ subsp _rasilis_
