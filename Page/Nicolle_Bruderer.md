NICOLLE MARIE BRUDERER COFIÃ‘O (born 15 August 1993) is a Guatemalan professional racing cyclist, who currently rides for UCI Women's Team .[1]


Career

A keen participant in multiple sports in her childhood, she took up cycle racing after trying track cycling with the encouragement of her father in her senior year of high school. She then became a student at the University of Colorado Boulder, combining undergraduate studies in Business Management with competing for the University in road cycling, as well as representing her country on the track during the last two years of her course. She competed for Guatemala at the 2015 Pan American Games.[2]

She rode in the women's road race at the 2016 UCI Road World Championships, finishing in 38th place.[3][4] In November 2016, she was named as a member of the team for the 2017 season.[5] She became Guatemalan national champion for the road race and time trial in 2017,[6] and successfully defended her time trial title the following year.[7]
