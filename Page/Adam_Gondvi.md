ADAM GONDVI (born RAM NATH SINGH; 22 October 1947 â€“ 18 December 2011)[1][2] was an Indian poet from Aata Paraspur, Gonda, Uttar Pradesh.[3][4] He wrote poetry in Hindi, highlighting the plight of marginalized castes, Dalits, impoverished people.[5] Born in a poor farmer family, Gondvi's poetry was known for social commentary, scathing view of corrupt politicians and revolutionary in nature.[6][7]

In 1998, Madhya Pradesh government awarded him with Dushyant Kumar Prize.[8]

Gondvi died on 18 December 2011 in SGPGI, Lucknow due to stomach ailments.[9][10]

His poetry collections _Dharti Ki Satah Par_ (Surface of the earth) and _Samay Se Muthbhed_ (Encounter with time) are quite popular.[11][12]
