TODD GRANT KIMSEY (June 6, 1962 â€“ September 16, 2016) was an American film, stage and television actor. He appeared in more than forty television roles throughout his career, as well as film roles including _The Perfect Storm_ in 2000, _Planet of the Apes_ in 2001, and _Hidalgo_ in 2004. However, Kimsey may be best known for guest-starring as Ned Isakoff, Elaine Benes's communist boyfriend, in _The Race_, a 1994 episode of _Seinfeld_.[1] Kimsey's character was later included in _Rolling Stone_ magazine's "100 Best _Seinfeld_ Characters" in 2014.[2]


Biography

Kimsey, one of four siblings, was born to Dr. William and Linda (Grant) Kimsey on June 6, 1962, in Athens, Georgia.[3] He graduated from Clarke Central High School in Athens in the 1980s.[4] Kimsey initially enrolled in Davidson College for two years, before transferring to Florida State University (FSU), where he earned a bachelor's degree in theater.[5] He then interned at Burt Reynolds Celebrity Dinner Theater, which was owned by Burt Reynolds, in Jupiter, Florida, following his graduation from FSU.[6] Kimsey moved to New York, where he acted off-Broadway and television commercials, followed by a year with the touring production of _Biloxi Blues_.[7]

Kimsey relocated to Los Angeles to pursue film and television. He appeared in more than forty television roles, including _Doogie Howser, M.D._ and _Growing Pains_.[8]

Kimsey was perhaps best known for his guest starring role in the December 1994 episode of _Seinfeld_, _The Race_, during the sixth season.[9] He plays Ned Isakoff, Elaine Benes' (Julia Louis-Dreyfus) new boyfriend, whom she discovers is a communist.[10] In the episode, Ned, who wears olive-color clothes and reads the _Daily Worker_, influences Kramer (Michael Richards) with his communist ideology, leading Kramer to be fired from his job as a department store Santa Claus.[11] _Rolling Stone_ magazine named Kimsey's Ned to its "100 Best _Seinfeld_ Characters" in 2014.[12]

Todd Kimsey died on September 16, 2016, in Westwood, California at the age of 54 following a three-and-a-half-year battle with lymphoma. [13] He was survived by his wife, Lisa (Ardizzoni); three sons, Jackson, Judah and Kellen; his parents; and siblings, Terri Langford, Tracey Evans and Troy Kimsey.[14]
