MELLOR VILLAGE AND MOUNDS ARCHEOLOGICAL DISTRICT, also known as SITE 23CP1, is a historic archaeological site and national historic district located near Lamine, Cooper County, Missouri. It is a Middle Woodland Period village site situated on a terrace in the Lamine River locality of the Missouri River Valley. The pottery and stone tools from the site belong to the technological/artistic tradition that is described as "Hopewell."[1]

It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1969 with a boundary increase in 1974.[2]
