NICOLAS ANTONIO, O.P. was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Salpi (1422–?) and Bishop of Lucera (1394–1422).[1][2]


Biography

Nicolas Antonio was ordained a priest in the Order of Preachers.[3] On 4 Jul 1394, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Boniface IX as Bishop of Lucera.[4][5] On 22 Apr 1422, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Martin V as Bishop of Salpi.[6][7] It is uncertain how long he served as Bishop of Salpi.[8] While bishop, he was the principal co-consecrator of Antonio Ventura, Bishop of Croatia (1425); and Filippo Ventorelli, Bishop of Amelia (1426).[9]
