_CORYMBIA NESOPHILA_, commonly known as the MELVILLE ISLAND BLOODWOOD,[1] is a member of the genus _Corymbia_ native to northern Australia.

The tree typically grows to a height of and forms a lignotuber.[2] It has fibrous, crumbly and tessellated[3] grey-brown bark with adult leaves are glossy green and are predominantly alternate with a blade that is lanceolate to falcate and long and wide with a base tapering to the petiole. The petioles are long.[4]

It blooms between May and August producing cream-white flowers.[5]

_Corymbia nesophila_ has a disjunct distribution throughout the tropical north, found in the east Kimberley[6] region of Western Australia, the top end and islands of the coast of the Northern Territory and on the Cape York Peninsula of Queensland.[7] It grows on basalt or laterite on the lower slopes and flat areas with sandy or sandy-loam soils.
