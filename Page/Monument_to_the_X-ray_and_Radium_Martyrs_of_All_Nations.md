The MONUMENT TO THE X-RAY AND RADIUM MARTYRS OF ALL NATIONS (also known as the X-RAY MARTYRS' MEMORIAL) is a memorial in Hamburg, Germany, commemorating those who died due to their work with the use of radiation, particularly X-rays, in medicine.[1][2][3][4] It was unveiled on the grounds of St Georg (St George's) Hospital (now the __), on 4 April 1936 by the __ (the Röntgen Society of Germany).[5][6]

When unveiled, the memorial included 169 names,[7][8] from fifteen nations, listed alphabetically;[9][10] by 1959 there were 359,[11] with the additions listed on four separate stone plaques, beside the original columnar stone memorial.[12]
