QIU JIANLIANG () is a Chinese Muay Thai kickboxer. He joined the Chinese National Muay Thai Team in September 2012. In October 2013 he joined the Da Dong Xiang fight club.[1] As of 1 November 2018, he is ranked the #1 featherweight in the world by Combat Press.[2]


Career

On 24 May 2014 in Xinyang Stadium at Yokkao 9 Qiu Jianliang beat Imwiset Pornnarai.[3] [4]

On 2 April 2016 in China at the inaugural Glory of Heroes event Qiu Jianliang beat Jomthong Chuwattana.[5] [6]

May 7, 2016 in Shenzhen, China at Baoan Stadium on Glory of Heroes 2, Qiu Jianliang beat Jaidee Noppakit.[7]

September 17, 2016 in Chaoyang, China on a new event series dubbed "Rise of Heroes" Qiu Jianliang defeated Melvin Wassing.[8]

On Saturday, October 1, 2016 Glory of Heroes 5 in Zhengzhou, China Qiu Jianliang beat Ilias Bulaid by decision.[9] [10]

January 13, 2017 Qiu Jianliang was in action at Glory of Heroes 6, where he earned a violent, spinning back kick finish of Kem Sitsongpeenong to climb into the second slot.[11]

March 4, 2017 Glory of Heroes debuted in Brazil for Glory of Heroes 7. In the night’s action, top-10 featherweight Qiu Jianliang kept his winning streak alive against Wilson Djavan.[12]

July 16, 2017 Krush and Glory of Heroes collaborated on a Krush.77‘Japan vs. China’in Korakuen Hall Tokyo, Japan. Qiu Jianliang victory over former R.I.S.E. champion Yukihiro Komiya.[13]

On Jan 27, 2018 Glory of Heroes traveled to the Huandong Gymnasium in Qingdao, China, for Glory of Heroes 9, Jianliang defeated Yuichiro Nagashima by TKO. Round 3.[14]

On Saturday, May 26, 2018 Glory of Heroes held its 31st event from Beijing, China. Qiu Jianliang defeated Kaew Weerasakreck by decision.[15]
