KHANEWAL–WAZIRABAD BRANCH LINE () is one of several branch lines in Pakistan, operated and maintained by Pakistan Railways. The line begins from Khanewal Junction station and ends at Wazirabad Junction station. The total length of this railway line is . There are 40 railway stations from Khanewal Junction to Wazirabad Junction.[1] The line is important in that it connects Faisalabad with other parts of the country by rail.


History

The Khanewal–Wazirabad Branch Line was originally named the _Wazirabad–Multan Railway_ under the North Western State Railway. A survey for a railway line began in 1892.[2] The Wazirabad–Lyallpur began construction in 1894 and opened in 1896. The Lyallpur–Multan section began construction the following year and opened in 1899.[3]
