BUCK ELK CREEK is a stream in southern Osage County in the U.S. state of Missouri.[1] It is a tributary of the Gasconade River.

The stream headwaters arise at [2] just one mile north of Belle in adjoining Maries County and just west of Missouri Route 89 at an elevation of about .[3] The stream flows generally northwest to its confluence with the Gasconade at at an elevation of .[4][5]

Buck Elk Creek was so named due to the presence of elk in the area.[6]
