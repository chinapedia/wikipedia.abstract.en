Sir ALFRED JOHN CALLAGHAN (29 October 1865 – 31 May 1940), was an Irish/British barrister and Liberal Party politician.


Background

Callaghan was born the son of James Walker Callaghan, of Northumberland Road, Dublin. He obtained a Doctor of Law, from the University of Dublin. In 1914 he married Irene Knighton-Warren of St John's Wood, London. He was knighted in 1915.[1]


Professional career

Callaghan was member of the Irish Bar and of the Inner Temple, London.[2]


Political career

Callaghan was Liberal candidate for the Unionist Eastbourne division of Sussex at the 1918 General Election. His Unionist opponent received endorsement from the Coalition Government and Callaghan finished third. He was Liberal candidate for the Chatham division of Kent at the 1922 General Election. Another Unionist seat, this time he finished second as there was no Labour candidate. In 1923 he again contested Chatham, this time a Labour candidate intervened and he again finished second. He did not stand for parliament again.[3]
