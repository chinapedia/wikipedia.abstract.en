DAVID ANDJELIC (Serbian: David Anđelić; Cyrillic: Давид Анђелић) is a Serbian rugby league footballer. Primarily playing as a , Andjelic is a Serbian international representative and was contracted with Penrith Panthers in his junior career.


Early career

Born in Serbia, Andjelic and his mother Milijana moved to Mount Druitt, New South Wales, Australia as a child.[1] He played his junior rugby league for St Patricks Blacktown, Minchinbury Jets, and Penrith Panthers.[2] In 2016, Andjelic participated in the Nine Network's rugby league reality TV show, _The NRL Rookie_, being cut from the competition in week 5.[3] His audition video, featuring his running through a wall of bricks, went viral around the world.[4]


Representative career

Andjelic made his international debut for Serbia in their match against the Philippines on 5 February 2016.[5][6] He played in the match against Spain on 24 September 2016.[7] On 5 October, he was named in Serbia 22-man squad for their 2017 World Cup qualifying campaign.[8] He played in both of their matches, against Wales and Italy.[9][10]
