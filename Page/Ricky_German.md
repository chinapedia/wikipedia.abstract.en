RICARDO DE NIRO GERMAN (born 13 January 1999) is an English professional footballer who plays as a striker for Crawley Town.


Club career

German started his career in the academy of Chesterfield, and made his senior team debut on 17 September 2016 against Northampton Town, being subbed on after 89 minutes for Kristian Dennis, whilst still an academy player.[1] He eventually signed professional terms on 10 October 2016, with manager Danny Wilson saying he could potentially be 'very good'.[2] On 25 August 2017, German joined National League North side Alfreton Town on a one-month loan deal.[3]

In the summer of 2018, German moved to Southern League Premier South side Hendon.[4][5][6]

On 1 January 2019, German secured a move to League Two side, Crawley Town on a three-year deal.[7]


Personal life

German is the younger brother of Antonio German, who is also a footballer whom represents the Grenada national football team.[8]
