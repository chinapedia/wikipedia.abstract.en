_Neighbours_ is an Australian television soap opera. It was first broadcast on 18 March 1985 and currently airs on digital channel Eleven. The following is a list of characters that appeared in the show in 2017, by order of first appearance. All characters are introduced by the shows executive producer Jason Herbison. The 33rd season of _Neighbours_ began airing from 9 January 2017.[1] Andrea and Willow Somers made their debuts during the same month. Teacher Finn Kelly was introduced during March, while Dipi, Kirsha and Yashvi Rebecchi, along with Dipi's sister Mishti Sharma, arrived in April. Jeremy Newell and Mannix Foster appeared in June. Evan Lewis and Fay Brennan were introduced during the following month. September saw the first appearance of Hamish Roche, while Cassius Grady (uncredited until May 2018) and Rory Zemiro made their debuts in November. Joanne Schwartz appeared in December.


Andrea Somers

ANDREA SOMERS, played by Madeleine West, made her first appearance on 20 January 2017.[2] West previously played Dee Bliss, who was presumed dead in 2003 when her husband Toadfish Rebecchi (Ryan Moloney) accidentally crashed their car into the sea. On 15 September 2016, Tiffany Dunk of News.com.au reported that West had reprised the role of Dee.[3] West returned to filming that same week.[4] However, during the episode broadcast on 2 February 2017, it was revealed that Dee was actually an impostor named Andrea Somers.[5] Andrea is impersonating Dee to gain money from the Bliss family estate.[6] She also includes her daughter Willow Somers (Mieke Billing-Smith) in the scam, by pretending that she is Toadie's daughter.[7] Five months after Andrea's exit, West made a surprise return to the show on 14 August 2017. Her appearance was not announced beforehand and the closing credits stated it was a "Special appearance by Madeleine West", leading viewers to speculate that it was the real Dee and not Andrea.[8] West later appeared during the season finale on 8 December 2017. Her character was seen watching Toadie's vow renewal from a taxi. Her character name was again withheld.[9] She was seen again during the return episode on 8 January 2018.[10] It was later confirmed on-screen that Andrea had been in Erinsborough to check up on Toadie, and Daniel Kilkelly of _Digital Spy_ believed Andrea was the woman in the taxi.[11] A set of flashbacks broadcast on 27 June 2018 showed Andrea had fallen pregnant after having sex with Toadie. She later fled town leaving her son Hugo Somers (John Turner) with Sindi Watts (Marisa Warrington).[12] With Andrea set to meet the real Dee Bliss during the 5 July 2019 episode, West revealed that they are twins, who were separated at birth.[13]

Andrea turns up at Number 30 Ramsay Street pretending to be Dee Bliss. She tells Toadie that after the car crashed, she kept being dragged down by the waves. She eventually washed up on the beach, but hit her head on some rocks, causing a brain injury. She was found by a woman named Emily, who did not call for help, as she believed she was protecting her from Toadie. Andrea shows Toadie a photo of her injuries and says she suffered from memory loss, blackouts and mood swings. When Toadie asks why she did not find him sooner, Andrea explains that she saw him with Sindi Watts (Warrington) and did not want to ruin his happiness. Andrea asks Toadie to help her gain closure. Andrea meets Dee's close friend Stephanie Scully (Carla Bonner), whose partner Mark Brennan (Scott McGregor) is suspicious of her story. He raises the issue of getting a DNA test to prove her identity, but Andrea says they cannot ask Dee's sister Cecile Bliss (Molly McCaffrey) for a sample, as she is dead. Mark later finds that Cecile took part in a medical experiment prior to her death and he obtains a sample of her DNA. Andrea gives Mark a hair and the DNA results confirms that it belongs to Dee. When Toadie unexpectedly turns up at the motel room, he meets Willow and Andrea tells him that she is his daughter. Toadie's wife, Sonya (Eve Morey) asks for a paternity test and Andrea produces a home kit that she had bought earlier. Andrea later explains to Willow that she replaced Willow's swab with one she took from Sonya and Toadie's daughter Nell Rebecchi (Scarlett Anderson).

It soon emerges that Andrea is after the money from the Bliss family estate and once she has it, she and Willow will leave Erinsborough. Willow expresses her reservations over deceiving the Rebecchi family and letting Toadie believe that she is his daughter. Andrea tells her daughter to go along with the plan, so she can give her the life she deserves. Sindi comes to the motel and it emerges that she told Andrea about her resemblance to Dee. It also emerges that Sindi posed as Emily on the phone to Mark, and is after a cut of the Bliss estate in exchange for helping Andrea with the information needed to scam Toadie. Sindi expresses her regret about being involved, and demands her initial cut of $20,000 be doubled to $40,000; however it is revealed that there is in fact no money left in the estate, it having disappeared under insolvency. Andrea tells Toadie that she has feelings for him. After Toadie gives Andrea a cheque for $100,000 for Willow's education, Andrea and Willow leave for London. Toadie follows them, so he can bring them home. Toadie assumes Sonya has had an affair with Mark, and he has sex with Andrea when she comes to his hotel room. While she is in the shower, Toadie finds Andrea's passport. He confronts her and she initially tries to pass it off as a fake that Willow made. But she eventually admits that she is not Dee. She tries to convince Toadie that her deception is unimportant and tells him that she loves him, but he rejects her and leaves. Willow attempts to convince Andrea to return the $100,000 to Toadie, but she refuses and remains in London.

Nearly a year later, Sindi confirms to Toadie that Andrea is back in Australia and has visited Erinsborough to check up on him. Toadie and Sonya later learn Andrea is in Perth. Sindi then turns up on their doorstep with Andrea's son Hugo Somers (John Turner), and tells Toadie that he is Hugo's father. Willow explains that Andrea turned up at Toadie and Sonya's vow renewal, and left Toadie a note asking him to visit her. Willow then learned that Andrea was pregnant and convinced her not to tell Toadie. Andrea developed postnatal depression and left Hugo in Sindi's care. Sonya learns that Andrea is in a hospital in Tasmania and visits her alone. A doctor tells her that he thinks Andrea is suffering from postnatal psychosis. Sonya and Toadie take Willow and Hugo to visit Andrea, who fails to recognise either of them. When she is alone with Toadie, Andrea claims to be Dee. She later refers to the night of Dee and Toadie's first kiss. Sonya and Toadie agree to pay for Andrea's ongoing treatment. Months later, Andrea's mother Heather Schilling (Kerry Armstrong) visits her and she reveals that she has conned the Rebecchis into hiring her as their nanny, Alice Wells, so she can destroy their marriage, leaving Toadie free to be with Andrea. Toadie visits a couple of days later and Andrea tells him that she knows she is not Dee. He shows her some recent photos of Hugo, and she admits that she has been thinking about own mother recently. Heather's schemes fail, and she is forced to flee Erinsborough after attempting to murder Sonya. Toadie later discovers Andrea was aware of her mother's con.

After Andrea sends a social worker to assess Sonya in the custody case for Hugo, Sonya visits her in the hospital and reveals that she has ovarian cancer. She asks Andrea to sign over full custody of Hugo to herself and Toadie, in return for them dropping the fraud charges, to which Andrea agrees. Just before Sonya leaves, Andrea tells her that while she was in the Salamanca Market, a man approached her and was convinced that she was a woman named Karen, who Andrea suspects could be the real Dee Bliss. Weeks later, Andrea receives a text message from Toadie's brother Shane Rebecchi (Nicholas Coghlan). When she asks if Toadie knows they are in contact, Shane replies that he does not, and she asks him to stop texting her. Following her release from the hospital, Andrea moves to Geelong, where Shane visits her. He informs her of Sonya's death, and then asks her to explain about the Salamanca Markets story. Andrea tells him that the man she met was sure that he had spent an afternoon with her. She asks to see Hugo in exchange for more information. Andrea follows Shane back to Erinsborough, where she gets a cold reception from Toadie.


Jasmine Udagawa

Jasmine Udagawa, played by Kaori Maeda-Judge[14], made her first appearance on 26 January 2017. Maeda-Judge joined the show's cast on a recurring basis, and on-screen Jasmine challenges business rival Paul Robinson (Stefan Dennis).[15][16] Jasmine soon becomes involved in the mystery story surrounding the paternity of David Tanaka (Takaya Honda) and Leo Tanaka (Tim Kano). Jasmine shifts her attention from Paul to Leo, who she believes is related to her. Not wanting him to inherit Lassiter's she conocts a plan to sabotage his backpackers business and force him to leave Erinsborough.[17] Terese Willis (Rebekah Elmaloglou) soon becomes suspicious of Jasmine's vendetta against Leo and snoops in a secret file and is shocked to learn he could potentially gain control of Lassiter's.[18] Discussing Jasmine's ruthless plan to run Leo out of Erinsborohough, Daniel Kilkelly from Digital Spy said that "talk about a u-turn! [...] she certainly doesn't mess about."[19] He added that the "very intriguing" story would progress with Amy Williams (Zoe Cramond) discovering the truth about Jasmine's file on Leo.[20] It soon became clear that Jasmine's theories were incorrect.[21]

Terese sees Jasmine working with her investor James Udagawa (Samuel David Humphrey) and mistakes her for his new assistant. Jasmine introduces herself as James's sister, before leaving to look around the Lassiter's complex. James explains that Jasmine has convinced their dying grandfather that she needs to step in and he warns Terese to watch her, as he is being relocated. Jasmine returns and has some questions for Terese after going through the books. A couple of weeks later, Jasmine brings her grandfather. Toshiro Udagawa (Lawrence Mah), to see an exhibition at Lassiter's Hotel and reacts to the mention of Leo name. Terese raises the moment with Jasmine, who decides to look into it. She asks Terese to continue to bring down Paul's business, but later changes her mind and tells Terese to drive Leo and his business out of Erinsborough.

Terese becomes suspicious of Jasmine's intentions and reads her secret file on him. Jasmine warns Terese that Leo could inherit the hotel. Amy overhears their exchange and decides to investigate. Jasmine tries to bribe Leo with money which leads him to suspect that they are related. When Jasmine confronts Toshiro, he contacts Kim Tanaka (Jenny Young) who is forced to reveal that her sons are not related to the Udagwas and that they are actually Paul's biological children.


Willow Somers

WILLOW SOMERS, played by Mieke Billing-Smith, made her first appearance on 31 January 2017. Billing-Smith's casting and her character details were announced on 23 January.[22] Willow was introduced as the teenage daughter of Andrea Somers (Madeleine West), who tells Toadfish Rebecchi (Ryan Moloney) that Willow is his daughter.[23] Daniel Kilkelly of Digital Spy praised Billing-Smith for her role in the Andrea/Toadie storyline, saying she had been "a great discovery for the show". He also hoped Willow would return, especially as the show had a history of character's taking in "random waifs and strays".[24] Executive producer Jason Herbison stated that he loved the character, and explained, "It's always a great dynamic when you have a child who's forced to parent an adult. She really was a leaf blown about in the breeze created by Andrea. I thought Mieke did a terrific job." He added that there was a chance Willow could return in the future.[25] It was later confirmed that the character would be returning on 11 July.[26] Willow departed again on 4 August, after deciding to live with her father Fergus Olsen (Andrew Percy),[27] before making a guest appearance from 8 December for Toadie and Sonya Rebecchi's (Eve Morey) vow renewal.[28] Billing-Smith reprised her role for another guest stint as Willow from 10 June 2019.[29]

Willow's mother Andrea brings her to Erinsborough when she decides to scam Toadfish Rebecchi by posing as his deceased wife Dee Bliss. When Toadie finds Willow in Andrea's motel room, Andrea tells him Willow is his daughter. Willow is forced to go along with her mother's plan and she spends time with Toadie. They bond over her desire to become a pilot and he helps her get into a private school. Willow becomes involved in a fake ID scheme with Piper Willis (Mavournee Hazel), and tries to blackmail Elly Conway (Jodi Anasta) with a photoshopped picture of Elly in bed with student Angus Beaumont-Hannay (Jai Waetford). After Willow and Andrea flee to London, Toadie is able to use Willow's social media to track them down. After Toadie discovers Andrea's real identity, Willow tries to talk to him, but he walks into the road and is struck by a taxi. Willow visits him in the hospital. She also tries, unsuccessfully, to convince her mother to give back the $100,000.

Months later, Willow returns to Erinsborough and tidies up Sonya Rebecchi's garden nursery following a storm. Sonya later catches Willow outside her home and Willow sprains her wrist when she falls. Sonya takes her to the hospital, where Toadie meets them. Willow explains that she ran away from Andrea, after they failed to find her real father and Andrea lied about repaying Toadie. As Willow cannot support herself, Toadie decides that she can stay with him instead of going into care. Sonya gives Willow a job at the nursery. Toadie later decides that he and Willow should move into one of the Eclipse apartments at Lassiter's, as Number 32 is too crowded. Willow learns that her father, Fergus Olsen (Andrew Percy), is living in Port Macquarie and she decides she wants to meet him. Fergus comes to Erinsborough to meet Willow. He tells her how a swimming accident that left him paralysed helped him change his life around. He is married and has two young daughters. After spending some time with him, Fergus invites Willow to stay with him and his family. Willow weighs up the pros and cons of going to Port Macquarie, and eventually decides to go. Toadie decides to go with her to see her settle in. She tells Ben during her farewell party that she always thought something romantic would happen between them, and he agrees, but he was taking his time. Willow visits Sonya and Nell to say goodbye. She tells Sonya that if she wants Toadie back, not to take too long as Amy Williams (Zoe Cramond) has feelings for him. Willow returns to Erinsborough on Christmas Day to act as a bridesmaid at Sonya's surprise vow renewal for Toadie.

Toadie contacts Willow to let her know that he has Andrea's phone number, and she advises him to throw it away. Willow then tells Sindi Watts (Marisa Warrington) that she does not want to see Andrea, although she has been in contact. She also explains that there is something that Toadie and Sonya do not know about Andrea. Months later, Toadie informs Willow that a private detective has found Andrea in Perth. Willow flies to Erinsborough and asks that he and Sonya do not contact her. They explain that they need to try, as they want to get their money back. Willow's secret is revealed when Sindi abruptly arrives with Andrea and Toadie's baby son, Hugo Somers (John Turner). She explains that a heavily pregnant Andrea returned to Erinsborough on the day of the vow renewal, and Willow persuaded her to stay away from the Rebecchis. Toadie and Sonya assume custody of Hugo in Andrea's absence, and eventually track her down to a psychiatric facility in Tasmania. Willow accompanies them to visit her mother, who believes she is Dee Bliss and does not remember either of her children. Months later, Willow returns to Erinsborough and finds that her grandmother, Heather Schilling (Kerry Armstrong), is posing as "Alice Wells", the Rebecchis' nanny. Willow tricks Heather into revealing she has poisoned Sonya and left her to die. She warns Toadie and they rescue Sonya in time. Willow is devastated by how her family has hurt the Rebecchis again, but Toadie insists she is part of their family and should not feel guilty for her mother and grandmother's actions. The following year, Sonya dies of ovarian cancer and Willow returns for her memorial. Months later, Willow returns to Erinsborough when she hears Andrea has made contact with Toadie, wanting to reconnect with Hugo. She decides to stay for a while to make sure Andrea is not trying to con Toadie again.


Finn Kelly

FINN KELLY, played by Rob Mills, made his first screen appearance on 15 March 2017.[30][31] Mills hinted at his casting by posting a series of images on social networking website Instagram confirming his move to Melbourne, where the show is filmed.[32] His casting and character details were officially announced on 15 November 2016.[33] Luke Dennehy of the _Herald Sun_ reported that Mills had begun filming his first scenes during the same week.[34] Mills revealed that his character's storyline would last for four to five months.[35] Of joining the show, Mills stated "I can't wait to get started on the show. As a youngster, I played baseball just behind where Ramsay Street is located and I thought at the time, how great it would be to be on _Neighbours_, and now it has happened!"[36] Rob_Mills_(6724855523).jpg Finn is "a progressive and respected school teacher", who joins the staff of Erinsborough High after being hired by Susan Kennedy (Jackie Woodburne).[37] The show's executive producer, Jason Herbison, called Finn "a really intriguing character", who has some unexpected secrets.[38] While Mills commented, "Yeah he's got a few secrets, some that will end early and some that will end a little bit later."[39] Network Ten producer Claire Tonkin added that Finn would be charming and have "an unexpected agenda".[40] Finn is Elly Conway's (Jodi Anasta) former partner.[41] Johnathon Hughes of _Radio Times_ branded Finn "one of the nastiest characters _Neighbours_ has ever seen." Hughes also called him a "toxic teacher" and added "We're not used to this level of evil in sunny Erinsborough".[42] Finn made his final appearance on 29 June 2017. Mills commented that he would not rule out a return for his character.[43] Mills reprised the role the following year, and Finn returned on 22 May 2018.[44] In March 2019, _Neighbours_ announced via its social media platforms that Mills had signed a permanent contract, and Finn would become a member of the regular cast.[45]

Finn takes a teaching job at Erinsborough High as a replacement for Brad Willis (Kip Gamblin). The principal, Susan Kennedy, introduces Finn to his class, which is being covered by Elly Conway. Elly asks Finn to go home and resign, as she does not want to see him again. It soon emerges that Finn and Elly dated, while he was married. Elly also blames Finn for an incident at their last school in which her presentation was sabotaged. She asks Finn to leave again, but he refuses. Finn moves in with Terese Willis (Rebekah Elmaloglou) on Ramsay Street, and starts tutoring Xanthe Canning (Lilly Van der Meer). Elly wrongly blames Finn for a critical posting about her on the internet. When he approaches her, Elly pushes him away and Finn falls down some stairs. He is taken to the hospital, where he declines to pursue legal action against Elly, saying he still loves her. During her work experience at the hospital, Xanthe discovers that Finn has a brain aneurysm. He asks her to keep it a secret. Finn encourages Susan's grandson Ben Kirk (Felix Mallard) to leave school and pursue a career as a mechanic. He keeps notes on Susan's failings, hoping that it will help him to become principal. Finn and Elly get back together. He applies for the acting assistant principal position, and reveals his relationship with Elly to get Susan removed from the interview board, as she has a conflict of interest. Elly tells Finn that she can not trust him and breaks up with him. Susan orders Finn to stop coaching Xanthe, but they agree to meet secretly.

Finn involves Xanthe in his dishonest scheme to become principal. Finn sneaks into Elly's bedroom and alters several grades on essays she has marked, causing an investigation. He later swaps Susan's vitamins for Piper Willis's (Mavournee Hazel) pain medication. Finn is made acting principal when Susan becomes ill. He also learns Elly spoke to his former wife and tells her that he wants her to resign or he will force her out. He then admits to sabotaging her presentation at their previous school. Finn continues to manipulate Xanthe, who agrees to stay quiet about the medication swap. Finn implements a vertical integration scheme at the school, but the teachers are not prepared and the students dislike it. Piper organises a student strike and notifies the _West Waratah Star_. Xanthe tells Piper about Finn's aneurysm, and Piper calls off the strike. Finn's medication swap is discovered after Nell Rebecchi (Scarlett Anderson) swallows some of the pain killers and is hospitalised. Finn decides to fly to Hong Kong and Xanthe decides to go with him, however, they are escorted off the plane by the federal police. Finn frames Xanthe by putting the medication in her bag. Xanthe later meets with Finn and secretly records a confession. When Finn tries to grab her phone, he collapses after suffering a haemorrhagic stroke. He undergoes surgery to stop the bleed, which results in paralysis. Finn denies everything heard in the confession. Elly visits him and he accuses her of trying to smother him with a pillow. Mark Brennan (Scott McGregor) and Karl Kennedy (Alan Fletcher) prove Finn is faking his paralysis and he retracts his accusation. He also gives Mark a full confession. Finn asks to see Elly, Ben, Xanthe and Susan. He tells Elly that he still loves her, he apologises to Xanthe for manipulating her, and he admits to using Ben to further his own agenda. When Finn tries to blame his poor health for his actions, Susan tells him that he should have been living his best life, not trying to destroy everything around him. Finn begs her to help him, but Susan realises that he is not remorseful and leaves. Finn is then transferred to a prison hospital.

Finn returns to Erinsborough when his girlfriend, and Elly's younger sister, Beatrix Nilsson (Bonnie Anderson) comes to reconnect with Elly. Bea is unaware of who Finn is, as he calls himself Patrick. Finn convinces Bea to spend time with Elly and befriend Xanthe. He also encourages her to accept a job at the local garage. Bea thanks Finn for his support and for saving her from being harassed by a group of men, who then assaulted Finn and left him in a wheelchair. After Elly almost finds Finn in the apartment, Bea asks why he could not come out and meet her sister, and Finn tells her it is her fault he is in a wheelchair. He later steals Bea's key to the garage and sabotages some invoices. It also emerges that he can walk. Finn berates Bea for talking to her family about him, but he later gives her a dress as an apology, which is similar to one owned by Elly. Finn meets with Rita Newland (Lisa Kay) to apply for the position of Karl's assistant on an MS trial at the hospital. Rita initially turns him down, until he tells her he has MS and would be willing to work as an unpaid intern. He later gains access to Susan's medical file. When Bea comes home early and finds Finn's empty wheelchair, Finn lies that he has been regaining movement in his legs, and fell when he went out to test them. She helps him home, where Finn proposes to her. Bea accepts on the condition that he meets her family, but he pulls out of the gathering at the last minute.

Finn gets Bea to give her family a tea chest, which contains a hidden camera so he can spy on their movements. Finn alters the data from the overseas MS trials ahead of Karl's talk at a medical conference. Finn then informs Clive Gibbons (Geoff Paine), who puts a stop to the hospital's trial while an investigation is carried out. Finn breaks into Harold's Store to get the clues for a local scavenger hunt, which he knows Xanthe and Susan are taking part in. The following day, he asks Bea to wear an outfit identical to the one being worn by Elly, before suggesting they enter the scavenger hunt together. Finn makes sure Bea is seen on various security cameras dressed as Elly. He then asks that they return to the apartment as he is in pain, but he later goes out under the pretence that he is writing his vows. Finn lures Elly to a national park using Bea's phone and he steals her car, while she is looking for her sister. He then comes across Xanthe and Chloe Brennan (April Rose Pengilly) and drives at them. Xanthe sees him and manages to push Chloe out of the way, as she takes the full impact of the car. After returning home, Finn tells Bea that he went to see Elly and she refuses to attend their wedding. But during their drive to Maryborough, Bea becomes suspicious and leaves the car. Finn then chases after her. Susan and Elly later locate Bea's car and they then meet Finn, who they realise is Bea's boyfriend Patrick. Susan finds Bea in a shipping container, and when Elly goes in to help, Finn locks the door behind her. The three women are eventually rescued by Mark and Karl, while Finn is believed to have fled the country.

Bea goes to a cabin in the bush and is confronted by Finn. She tells him that she still loves him and he asks her to prove it. She kisses him, but he reveals that he has her file from her therapist and knows she is lying. Bea runs off and Finn pursues her through the bush, where he finds her with Elly, Susan and Xanthe at the edge of a cliff. Finn is pushed over the edge and the women leave him there. He is later found and taken to the hospital, where he remains in a coma. Weeks later, several Erinsborough High students and teachers fall ill from hydrogen cyanide poisoning, and it emerges that Finn placed canisters of cyanide in the school air vents. Susan and Elly deduce that he was going to open the canisters during the school's graduation ceremony to kill everyone in the auditorium. Finn wakes from his coma four months later. He believes it is 2007, he is 19, and is about to take his teaching exams. The neurologist explains that it is 2019 and he fell from a cliff top, which caused a traumatic brain injury. Finn receives several visitors, including Susan and Bea, but he does not recognise them. He becomes close with Susan and his half-brother Shaun Watkins (Brad Moller) visits. However, Karl later confronts Finn, telling him to drop the act, before revealing all his crimes. Finn attempts to flee the hospital, but Karl finds him collapsed in the car park. Beverly Marshall (Shaunna O'Grady) assesses Finn's condition, and tells him that Susan pushed him from the cliff. Finn revisits the town and his victims, but fails to recover his memories. Karl persuades Finn to move to another hospital, but Imogen Willis (Ariel Kaplan) tells him to stay and she will represent him as his lawyer. Finn and Shaun's mother Claudia Watkins (Kate Raison) arrives in town and initially refuses to see Finn, but she later visits him to ask about his lawyer's skills, before telling him that she has kept a photo from an album he made her. All of the physical evidence in Finn's case is destroyed in a fire, so Shaun tries to convince Finn to change his plea, but Imogen says she will not represent him if he does. Finn saves Susan and Imogen from being hit by a speeding car and is injured.


Mishti Sharma

MISHTI SHARMA, played by Scarlet Vas, made her first appearance on 18 April 2017.[46] The character and casting details were announced on 27 March.[47] Mishti is Dipi Rebecchi's (Sharon Johal) sister. She arrives in Erinsborough with Dipi's husband Shane Rebecchi (Nicholas Coghlan).[48] Coghlan explained that Mishti helps her brother-in-law when he considers moving to the area. The actor also described Mishti as "a dear friend" to Shane and said the two characters "share some pretty serious confidences."[49] Mishti comes to Erinsborough wanting a fresh start. However, Daniel Kilkelly of Digital Spy thought she had a dark secret, noting "there's also an air of sadness about her."[50]


Dipi Rebecchi

DEEPIKA "DIPI" REBECCHI,[51] played by Sharon Johal, made her first appearance on 24 April 2017.[52] The character and casting details were announced on 27 March.[53] Johal originally auditioned for the role of Dipi's sister Mishti Sharma, but the character's age was lowered and Scarlet Vas was cast. The producers liked Johal, so they decided to make Dipi work for her.[54] Dipi is the wife of Shane Rebecchi (Nicholas Coghlan), the older brother of established regular Toadfish Rebecchi (Ryan Moloney), who returns to Erinsborough with his new family after 22 years.[55] Dipi and Shane move to Erinsborough with their two daughters Yashvi (Olivia Junkeer) and Kirsha Rebecchi (Vani Dhir). The character was billed as "a ray of sunshine to those around her. Enthusiastic, bubbly and having an infectious lust for life, she always sees the best in people."[56] Dipi takes over the lease of Harold Café.[57]


Kirsha Rebecchi

KIRSHA REBECCHI, played by Vani Dhir, made her first appearance on 24 April 2017.[58] The character and casting details were announced on 27 March.[59] Dhir received the audition for the role through her stage school Young Australian Broadway Chorus.[60] Of her casting, Dhir commented "I was happy and thrilled to get this role as I am now doing what I love and enjoying every bit of it. I wish I could be filming everyday but I have to go to school."[61] When she is on set, Dhir films her scenes in a couple of hours, so she can keep up with her schooling.[62] Kirsha is Shane (Nicholas Coghlan) and Dipi Rebecchi's (Sharon Johal) younger daughter. She is intelligent and enjoys reading. Kirsha is also shy and finds the move to Erinsborough terrifying, as she questions whether she will be accepted.[63] Dhir told Preeti Jabbal of _Indian Link_ that she is the opposite of the "anxious and nerdy" Kirsha.[64]

Kirsha arrives in Erinsborough with her mother Dipi and older sister Yashvi Rebecchi (Olivia Junkeer). Her father, Shane, reveals that he has rented Number 32 Ramsay Street for the family, and has bought Harold's Café for Dipi. Dipi bakes Anzac Biscuits and asks Kirsha and Yashvi to give them out to the community. Kirsha struggles with the move, but befriends Jimmy Williams (Darcy Tadich). Shane later buys back Kirsha's dog Clancy to cheer her up. Dipi, Kirsha and her aunt Mishti Sharma (Scarlet Vas) show Aaron Brennan (Matt Wilson) Shane's solar-powered lawnmower in the hope he can help them sell it to an investor. They take it out to show Aaron what it can do, but Kirsha accidentally drives it into the lake. Kirsha is paired with Tia Martinez (Erica Brown) for a school assignment, and Tia later invites her to a sleepover, which Kirsha does not want to attend. Dipi encourages her to go and Kirsha eventually agrees. However, she goes missing after Dipi drops her off at Tia's house. She is found the following morning sleeping in the garden bar. Kirsha tells her parents that she does not feel like she belongs in Erinsborough. Tia later forces Kirsha to steal Elly Conway's (Jodi Anasta) clothes, while she is showering at the school. Ben Kirk (Felix Mallard) begins teaching Kirsha how to play the guitar, and she joins him in a couple of public performances. Kirsha accidentally drops a lit sparkler into a box of Yashvi's fireworks, which go off and result in Kirsha suffering minor burns to her face and hearing loss, caused by nerve damage. She is given a course of steroids to help her recovery. Yashvi apologises to Kirsha, who tells her it was her fault and forgives her. Kirsha undergoes a hearing test and later finds a leaflet about learning sign language, leading her to realise that she will not regain her hearing. On returning to school, Kirsha is bullied by Tia Martinez, whereupon Xanthe stands by her. Kirsha's hearing starts to return and she hears Mishti telling Leo that she's pregnant.


Yashvi Rebecchi

YASHVI REBECCHI, played by Olivia Junkeer, made her first appearance on 24 April 2017.[65] The character and casting details were announced on 27 March 2017.[66] Yashvi is Shane (Nicholas Coghlan) and Dipi Rebecchi's (Sharon Johal) older daughter. She was described as being "a boisterous troublemaker who's fearless and confident."[67] Yashvi likes to try tricks and jokes out on new people, and enjoys being the centre of attention. She is more of an "opportunist" than a "bad girl".[68]

Hamish Roche

HAMISH ROCHE, played by Sean Taylor, made his first appearance on 4 September 2017.[88] The character's introduction was announced on 16 August 2017,[89] while Taylor's casting details were announced on 28 August 2017.[90] Hamish is Tyler Brennan's (Travis Burns) biological father, who arrives shortly after Tyler's mother Fay Brennan (Zoe Bertram) reveals that she had an affair, while married to Russell Brennan (Russell Kiefel).[91] Bridget McManus of _The Sydney Morning Herald_ described Taylor as being "marvellously suave" in his role of Hamish Roche.[92] She also called the character a "smooth operator", and thought he worked "a little too hard" in his attempt to bond with Tyler and his brothers.[93] McManus found the mystery of why he has a playing card in his wallet to be a "complicated familial conundrum on which soaps thrive".[94] Hamish was killed off on 7 November 2017,[95] but he made a brief reappearance on 22 August 2018[96], in which his murder is depicted in a flashback.[97]

While he is in The Waterhole, Hamish notices Tyler Brennan playing pool and joins him for a game. He later turns up at Number 24 and introduces himself, and Tyler realises that Hamish is his father. Hamish accepts Tyler's invitation to dine at The Waterhole with him and his brothers, Mark and Aaron Brennan (Matt Wilson). Hamish reveals that he never met their father Russell, but it emerges that he has the missing ace of spades playing card sent to him by Russell. Hamish gets to know Sheila Canning (Colette Mann). Hamish visits the boat Russell left to Tyler and Aaron, and encourages him to leave a message for Russell on his voicemail, like Aaron is doing. Hamish later pulls out Russell's phone and listens to Tyler's message. Hamish asks if he can stay on the boat, as he does not like the noise from the renovations at the hotel. Hamish continues to charm Sheila, before he meets up with his partner Louise McLeod (Maria Theodorakis) to tell her that he is working hard to secure the boat, so they can leave the country and avoid paying a large tax bill. He also plans to bring Tyler with them. When Tyler tells Hamish of his plans to sell the boat, Hamish convinces him to keep it and fix the engine himself.

Hamish learns about Tyler's history at the garage and that he is on his last chance with the boss. Hamish takes Tyler's keys to the garage and sabotages the mobile hoist, which later falls on Amy Williams, resulting in Tyler's dismissal from the garage. Louise turns up unexpectedly and thinks they are ready to go, but Hamish wants to wait until Tyler has his boat licence. He convinces Louise to return to Port Lincoln. The next morning Hamish is found dead in the hot tub of the Canning's family home by Stephanie Scully (Carla Bonner). The police conclude that he was murdered. Tyler later confesses to hitting Hamish with a garden gnome, and he is sent to prison. Months later, Hamish's other son Cassius Grady (Joe Davidson) reveals that after Tyler left, he drowned Hamish.
