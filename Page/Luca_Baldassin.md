LUCA BALDASSIN (born 28 May 1994) is an Italian footballer who plays for Serie C club Viterbese.


Club career

Born in Vittorio Veneto, Italy, Baldassin started his career at Northeastern Italian club Udinese. He was a player for their under-15 team in 2008–09 season,[1] under-17 team in 2009–10[2] and 2010–11 season. He was booked 6 times in 2010–11 season.[3]

After 2 more seasons for Udinese's reserves,[4] Baldassin moved to Lega Pro 2nd Division club Poggibonsi. The club relegated, both due to the league position and the reduction of total teams and divisions of Lega Pro.

In 2014, he was signed by Lega Pro club Lumezzane.[5] On 1 February 2016 Baldassin moved to Padova in a 5-month contract.[6][7]

On 10 June 2016 Baldassin was signed by Chievo as a free agent. He and Matteo Brunelli were farmed to Lega Pro Messina on loan.[8] He wore number 18 shirt,[9] He was the starting midfielder in the 4-3-3-formation that winning Siracusa in the round 1 of 2016–17 Lega Pro.[10]

On 1 September 2016 Baldassin and Brunelli were left for fellow third-tier club Lupa Roma from Chievo via Messina, with Felice Gaetano moved to Messina from Napoli via Lupa Roma.[11]

On 24 August 2018, Baldassin joined Serie C club Viterbese on permanent basis, after playing for them on loan in the 2017–18 season.[12]


International career

Baldassin was a player for Lega Pro under-21 representative team at 2013–15 International Challenge Trophy.[13][14] He also played twice in unofficial friendlies, against Italy national under-20 football team[15] and Livorno.[16]
