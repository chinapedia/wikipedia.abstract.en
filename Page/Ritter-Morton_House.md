The RITTER-MORTON HOUSE is a historic two-story house in Spring Hill, Tennessee, U.S..


History

The house was built in 1878 for Peter Ritter and his wife, Elizabeth.[1] It was designed in the Carpenter Gothic architectural style.[2] It was acquired by John C. Witt on March 1, 1886.[3] By January 1899, it was purchased by A. P. Odil.[4]

The house has been listed on the National Register of Historic Places since December 12, 1976.[5]
