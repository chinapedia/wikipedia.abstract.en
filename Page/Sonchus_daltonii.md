_SONCHUS DALTONII_ is a species of flowering plants of the Asteraceae family. The species is endemic to Cape Verde.[1] It is listed as endangered by the IUCN.[2] The genus was named by Philip Barker Webb in 1849. Its local name is _coroa-de-rei_ ("king's crown").[3]


Distribution and ecology

_Sonchus daltonii_ occurs on the Capeverdean islands of Santo Antão, São Vicente, São Nicolau,[4] Santiago and Fogo. It grows in humid and sub-humid zones, mainly between 800 and 1,800 metres elevation. It is used for grazing animals, and it is highly threatened by nomadic grazing.[5]
