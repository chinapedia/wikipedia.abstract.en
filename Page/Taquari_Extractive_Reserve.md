The TAQUARI EXTRACTIVE RESERVE () is an extractive reserve in the state of São Paulo, Brazil. It protects a coastal area of mangroves.


Location

The Taquari Extractive Reserve is in the municipality of Cananéia, São Paulo. It has an area of . The reserve covers an area of mangroves and salt water at the mouth of the Taquari River.


History

The Taquari Extractive Reserve was created by state law 12.810 of 21 February 2008. This law broke up the old Jacupiranga State Park and created the Jacupiranga Mosaic with 14 conservation units.
