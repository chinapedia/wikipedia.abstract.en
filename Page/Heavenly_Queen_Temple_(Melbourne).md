Mazu_temple_in_Melbourne_-_Australia_2010.jpg The HEAVENLY QUEEN TEMPLE[1][2] is a temple to the Chinese sea-goddess Mazu, the deified form of the 10th-century Fujianese girl Lin Moniang, located in Footscray, a neighborhood of Maribyrnong in Melbourne, Australia. It is Australia's largest Chinese temple and includes a statue of Mazu imported from Nanjing, China. Construction on the temple complex is slated to continue until 2019, but the main hall was opened to the public by 2015.[3]
