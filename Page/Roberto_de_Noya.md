ROBERTO DE NOYA, O.P. or ROBERTO DE NOJA (died 1515) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Archbishop of Naxos (1504–1515),[1] Bishop of Acerra (1497–1504),[2][3] and Bishop of Minervino Murge (1492–1497).[4]


Biography

Roberto de Noya wasborn in Apulia, Italy[5] and ordained a priest in the Order of Preachers.[6][7] On 23 January 1492, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Innocent VIII as Bishop of Minervino Murge.[8][9][10] On 15 March 1497, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Alexander VI as Bishop of Acerra.[11][12][13][14] On 15 April 1504, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Julius II as Archbishop of Naxos.[15][16][17] He served as Archbishop of Naxos until his death on 22 April 1515.[18][19][20]
