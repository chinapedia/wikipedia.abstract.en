MONTAGUE GLUCKSTEIN (18 July 1854 – 7 October 1922) was a director of Salmon & Gluckstein tobacco merchants, and one of the founders of J. Lyons and Co., a restaurant chain, food manufacturing, and hotel conglomerate created in 1884 that dominated British mass-catering in the first half of the twentieth century.[1]


Early life

Montague Gluckstein was the son of Samuel Gluckstein, the founder of Salmon & Gluckstein.[2]


Career

He succeeded his brother Isidore Gluckstein as chairman of J. Lyons and Co..[3]


Personal life

He married Matilda Franks (b. 1861) in 1884.

His son Samuel Montague Gluckstein (1884–1928) was a director of J. Lyons and Co.[4]

His son Isidore Montague Gluckstein (1890–1975) became managing director, then chairman, then president of J. Lyons and Co.[5]
