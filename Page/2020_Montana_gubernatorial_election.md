The 2020 MONTANA GUBERNATORIAL ELECTION will be held on November 3, 2020, to elect the Governor of Montana, concurrently with the 2020 U.S. presidential election, as well as elections to the United States Senate and elections to the United States House of Representatives and various state and local elections. Incumbent Democratic Governor Steve Bullock, who is term-limited and cannot seek re-election to a third term, is running for President of the United States.


Democratic primary

Candidates

Declared

-   Mike Cooney, Lieutenant Governor of Montana and former Secretary of State of Montana[1]
-   Reilly Neill, former state representative[2]
-   Casey Schreiner, minority leader of the Montana House of Representatives[3]

Potential

-   Ryan Busse, businessman[4]
-   John Heenan, attorney and candidate for Montana's at-large congressional district in 2018[5]
-   Brian Schweitzer, former Governor of Montana[6]
-   Whitney Williams, businesswoman and daughter of former U.S. Representative Pat Williams[7]

Declined

-   Wilmot Collins, mayor of Helena (running for U.S. Senate)[8]
-   Michael Punke, writer and former U.S. ambassador to the World Trade Organization[9][10]
-   Kathleen Williams, former state representative and nominee for Montana's at-large congressional district in 2018 (running for U.S. House)[11]


Republican primary

Candidates

Declared

-   Tim Fox, Attorney General of Montana[12][13][14][15]
-   Greg Gianforte, U.S. Representative for Montana's at-large congressional district and nominee for Governor in 2016[16][17]
-   Albert Olszewski, state senator[18][19][20][21]

Withdrawn

-   Corey Stapleton, Secretary of State of Montana, candidate for Governor in 2012, and candidate for the U.S. Congress in 2014 (running for U.S. House)[22]
-   Gary Perry, former State Senator[23][24]
-   Pete Ziehli[25][26]

Declined

-   Matt Rosendale, Montana State Auditor and nominee for U.S. Senate in 2018 (running for U.S. House)[27]
-   Ryan Zinke, former United States Secretary of Interior and former U.S. Representative for Montana's at-large congressional district[28][29]


Libertarian primary

Candidates

Declared

-   Ron Vandevender, nominee for lieutenant governor in 2016 and nominee for governor in 2012[30]
