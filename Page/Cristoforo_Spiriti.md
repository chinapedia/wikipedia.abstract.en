CRISTOFORO SPIRITI (died 5 November 1556) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Titular Patriarch of Jerusalem (1550–1556), and Bishop of Cesena (1510–1550).[1]


Biography

On 8 April 1510, Cristoforo Spiriti was appointed during the papacy of Pope Julius II as Bishop of Cesena.[2] On 28 February 1550, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Julius III as Titular Patriarch of Jerusalem.[3] He served as Patriarch of Jerusalem until his death on 5 November 1556.[4]


Episcopal succession

While bishop, he was the principal co-consecrator of:[5]

-   Giovanni Giacomo Barba, Bishop of Teramo (1546);
-   Bartolomeo Guidiccioni, Bishop of Teramo (1546); and
-   Giulio Canani, Bishop of Adria (1554).
