BEN CAUCHI (born 1974, Auckland, New Zealand)[1] is a New Zealand fine art photographer, specialising in the use of early photographic techniques, most notably the wet collodion or ambrotype process.[2][3]

Cauchi was taught at Massey University from where he graduated in 2000. He was the University of Otago Frances Hodgkins Fellow in 2007,[4] and in 2011 held the Rita Angus Cottage residency in Wellington. He has exhibited throughout New Zealand since 2001, as well as in Australia, the United Kingdom, France, Germany, and the United States.[6]
