_CORYMBIA PAPILLOSA_, commonly known as the MANINGRIDA BLOODWOOD,[1] is a member of the Corymbia genus native to northern Australia.

The tree typically grows to a height of and has thick, flaky and tessellated bark.[2]

It grows in flat areas in sandy soils with lateritic gravels and has a range limited to tropical areas in the Kimberley region of Western Australia,[3] and the top end of the Northern Territory.[4]
