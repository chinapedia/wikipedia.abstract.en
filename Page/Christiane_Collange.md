CHRISTIANE COLLANGE (born 1930) is a French journalist and author.


Early life

Christiane Collange was born as CHRISTIANE SERVAN-SCHREIBER on October 29, 1930.[1] Her father, Émile Servan-Schreiber, was a Jewish journalist and author.[2] Her mother, Denise Brésard, was Roman Catholic. Her paternal father, Joseph Schreiber, was a Jewish-Prussian immigrant.[3] She has two brothers, Jean-Jacques Servan-Schreiber and Jean-Louis Servan-Schreiber, and two sisters, Brigitte Gros and Bernadette Gradis.[4] The Servan-Schreiber family (up to 200 members) have a family reunion every five years.[5]

Collange graduated from Sciences Po.[6]


Career

Collange is a journalist. She was the editor of _Madame Express_, a supplement of _L'Express_.[7] She became the editor-in-chief of _Le Jardin des Modes_ in 1970.[8]

Collange is the author of many books. In her first book, _Madame et le management_, published in 1969, she argued that women should run their households like a business.[9]

Collange serves on the honorary committee of the Association pour le droit de mourir dans la dignité, a pro-euthanasia organization.[10]


Personal life

Collange married and divorced three times.[11] With one of her husbands, journalist and author Jean Ferniot, she has a son, Vincent Ferniot.[12]
