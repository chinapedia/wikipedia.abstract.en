MINIM (or Minim II, or Menim) is a village in the commune of , in the department of Vina, in the Adamawa Region, Cameroon. The village contains Cameroon's main source of bauxite.[1]


Population

In 1967, Minim contained 90 inhabitants, mostly from the Kaka ethnic group.[2] In the 2005 census, the settlement contained 432 people.[3]


Economy

Along with Ngaoundal, Minim is part of a large project to mine the bauxite of the Adamawa Plateau.[4][5] Covering an area of 1000 km², the deposit represents an important component of the Adamawa plateau and the gentle slopes which surround it make it easy to access.[6] Studies of feasibility and environmental impact were carried out[7] and a mining development plan was completed in May 2016.[8] Financing for the construction of a facility for refining the bauxite into aluminium and for transport infrastructure is still outstanding.[9]
