GIOVANNI BATTISTA FEDERICI (12 April 1615 – August 1657) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Sagone (1655–1657).[1][2]


Biography

Giovanni Battista Federici was born in La Spezia, France on 12 April 1615 and ordained a priest on 18 December 1649.[3] On 30 August 1655, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Alexander VII as Bishop of Sagone.[4][5] On 14 September 1655, he was consecrated bishop by Giovanni Battista Maria Pallotta, Cardinal-Priest of San Pietro in Vincoli, with Patrizio Donati, Bishop Emeritus of Minori, and Taddeo Altini, Bishop of Civita Castellana e Orte, serving as co-consecrators.[6] He served as Bishop of Sagone until his death on in August 1657.[7][8]
