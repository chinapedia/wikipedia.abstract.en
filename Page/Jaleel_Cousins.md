JALEEL COUSINS (born December 1, 1993) is an American basketball player for the Santa Cruz Warriors of the NBA G League. He played college basketball for Navarro College and South Florida.


High school career

Cousins first attended LeFlore Magnet High School in his native Mobile, Alabama, however, he transferred as a senior to Spanish Fort High School.[1] As a senior for the Toros, he was a member of the ASWA All-State Class 5A.[2]


College career

Cousins began his college career at Navarro College, where he averaged 3.7 points and 2.9 rebounds as a freshman and improved those numbers to 7.1 points and 4.8 rebounds per game as a sophomore.[3][4]

In his junior season, Cousins transferred to South Florida. He averaged 8.5 points, 7.9 rebounds, 1.6 blocks and 25.0 minutes per contest 33 games as a senior.[5][6]


Professional career

Texas Legends (2016—2017)

After going undrafted in the 2016 NBA draft, Cousins joined the Dallas Mavericks for the 2016 NBA Summer League.[7] On October 17, 2016, he signed with the Mavericks,[8] but was waived five days later.[9] On October 30, 2016, he was acquired by the Texas Legends of the NBA Development League as an affiliate player of the Mavericks.[10] On January 29, 2017, he was waived by the Legends.[11]

Reno Bighorns (2017)

On February 3, 2017, Cousins was acquired by the Reno Bighorns.[12]

Formosa Dreamers Taiwan (2017)

Cousins was acquired as an import player for the Formosa Dreamers and debuted in his first ASEAN Basketball League game in November 18, 2017 in a loss to the CLS Knights.

Santa Cruz Warriors (2018–present)

For the 2018–19 season, Cousins joined the Santa Cruz Warriors of the NBA G League.[13]


Personal life

He is the son of Monique Cousins and the younger brother of Los Angeles Lakers' center DeMarcus Cousins. He majored in communications.[14]
