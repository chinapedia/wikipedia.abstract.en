AHMEDUR RASHID CHOWDHURY আহমেদুর রশীদ চৌধুরী (also TUTUL টুটুল) is a Bangladeshi publisher and writer. He won 2016 Pinter International Writer of Courage Award, selected by writer Margaret Atwood,[1] The Jeri Laber international freedom to publish Award,[2] 2016 and Ossietzky Prize 2018[3] His writings were published in newspapers, magazine, and blogs.[4]


Career

In 1990, Chowdhury founded _Shuddhashar_ magazine. He established a publishing house, under the same name, in Dhaka in 2004.[5] _Shuddhashar_ won Shahid Munir Chowdhury Award by the Bangla Academy in 2013.[6] In February 2015, he received a death threat, for publishing materials of atheist writers.[7]

On October 31, 2015, he was attacked by assailants with machetes.[8][9] He was hospitalized in a critical condition.[10] Ansar Al Islam (AQIS Bangladesh) claimed the responsibility.[11] He went into exile and settled in Norway in January 2016.[12] Now he is editing his online magazine Shuddhashar.[13]

Chowdhury was the finalist of 2016 IPA Freedom to Publish Prize[14] and International Publishers Association(IPA), Prix Voltaire Short List, 2018.[15] In 2017 he gave an "inspiring" presentation at the Oslo Freedom Forum.[16]
