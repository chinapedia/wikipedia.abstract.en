Jacksonville_Amtrak_station_train_91_stop.jpg The JACKSONVILLE TERMINAL SUBDIVISION is a railroad line owned by CSX Transportation in the U.S. State of Florida. The S Line runs from Jacksonville, Florida to Baldwin, Florida for a total of 17.8 miles. At its north end it continues south from the Nahunta Subdivision and at its south end it continues south as the Florida Gulf & Atlantic Railroad. The A Line runs from Jacksonville, Florida to St. Johns, Florida for a total of 13.0 miles. At its north end it continues south from the Nahunta Subdivision and at its south end it continues south as the Sanford Subdivision.[1][2]


History

Jacksonville_railroads.png CSX's A Line north of Jacksonville (which leads to the Nahunta Subdivision) was originally part of the East Florida Railway. The A Line south of Jacksonville (which leads to the Sanford Subdivision) was part of the Jacksonville, Tampa and Key West Railway. Both lines became part of the Plant System and later the Atlantic Coast Line Railroad.

The S Line from Jacksonville east to Baldwin Junction was part of the Florida Central and Peninsular Railroad, which became part of the Seaboard Air Line Railroad.
