FRANCO BELLOCQ (born 15 October 1993) is an Argentine professional footballer who plays as a midfielder for Superleague Greece side Asteras Tripolis.[1][2]


Career

Bellocq started in the youth ranks of Buenos Aires club Estudiantes, before joining the Independiente youth team in 2007.[3][4] Six years later, Bellocq was promoted into Independiente's first-team and featured in the matchday squad for the first-time in May 2013 for a Copa Argentina match against Boca Unidos.[5][6] He made his debut in Primera B Nacional on 27 February 2014 in a home loss to Atlético Tucumán. He went onto make nine more league appearances in 2013–14 as Independiente won promotion into the 2014 Argentine Primera División.[7] In the subsequent two seasons, Bellocq made twenty-four league appearances.[8]

On 10 January 2016, Bellocq joined fellow Primera División club Arsenal de Sarandí on loan until 2017.[9][10] In total, he participated in thirty-one games in all competitions and scored four goals.[11] In July 2017, Bellocq joined Olimpo on loan.[12] His first appearance for Olimpo came on 30 October in a 1–1 draw with Defensa y Justicia.[13] He returned to his parent club on 16 May 2018.[14] Bellocq signed for Superleague Greece side Asteras Tripolis on 23 June.[15]
