Early life

Findlay was born in Ayr, Scotland.[2] He was educated at Strathallan School and the University of Glasgow.[3] Findlay graduated from the Glasgow Veterinary College in 1966 having read veterinary science.[4]


Career

Upon qualifying Findlay became an assistant vet with Henderson and Keywood in Surbiton, London.[5] He then bought his own practice in Putney.[6] However, he did not enjoy the administrative side of the business and turned to locum work.[7]

In 1984 he joined Ciba-Geigy Pharmaceuticals as public affairs advisor.[8] Thereafter, a freelance public relations advisor in 1993.[9] He became one of the main architects of the National Vocational Qualification scheme for those caring for animals, helping to devise the course standards and materials.[10] Latterly, he was chief executive of the PRO Dogs charity.[11]

In the early 1980s he was the regular vet on the BBC One children's tv programme "Saturday Superstore".[12] He was also a regular broadcaster appearing on Jimmy Youngs radio show, LBC and Capital Radio.[13]

Findlay was a member of the Governing Council of the Cat Fancy (GCCF) Veterinary Sub-committee (now Veterinary Advisory Committee) for 46 years.[14] He was a duty vet and had officiated at GCCF shows for as many years.[15] Findlay was also vice-president of the London Cat Club and a member of The Kennel Club.[16]
