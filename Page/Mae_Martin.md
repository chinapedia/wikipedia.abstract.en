MAE MARTIN (born 2 May 1987) is a Canadian comedian and actress.[1][2][3][4][5][6] Martin has won two Canadian Comedy Awards as part of the comedy troupe "The Young and the Useless".[7][8]


Comedy career

Martin started her career in Canada where she was involved in the comedy troupe "The Young and the Useless".[9] At the age of 16 she was is the youngest ever nominee for the Tim Sims Encouragement Fund Award.[10]

Her work in Canada also includes writing for the Canadian sketch comedy series _Baroness von Sketch Show._[11] She is a two-time Canadian Screen Award winner for Best Writing in a Variety or Sketch Comedy Series for her work with that show's writing team.[12]

In 2011 she moved to the UK, and has since done several shows which she toured, and took the Edinburgh Fringe Festival.[13] Her 2015 show was entitled _Mae Martin:Us_.[14]

Martin has contributed to various programmes on British television and radio.[15] On Radio she presents _Mae Martin's Guide to 21st Century Sexuality_ on BBC Radio 4,[16] and has contributed to The Now Show on the same station.[17] Since 2018 she has also co-hosted the Radio 4 podcast _GrownUpLand,_ which is targeted at millennials.[18] In 2018 she delivered her first Netflix special, which was part of the Comedians of the World collection.


Personal life

She is out as LGBT,[19] but has resisted explicitly identifying her sexuality as bisexual or lesbian.[20]

Martin was previously addicted to various illegal substances, including cocaine, and consequently underwent rehabilitation. [21] She has used stories of this as the basis for some of her stand up routines.[22]

Her father is James Chatto, a former child actor who appeared in Peter Brook’s film of _Lord of the Flies_ (her uncle, Daniel Chatto, is married to the Queen’s niece, Lady Sarah Armstrong-Jones).[23]
