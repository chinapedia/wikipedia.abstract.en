AMOS GRODZINOWSKY (also "GRODZINOWSKY" and "GROZHINOWSKI"; עמוס גרודז'ינובסקי; born June 3, 1940) is an Israeli former Olympic runner.[1] He was the Israeli Men's Champion in the 100 metres in 1960, and in the 200 metres in 1962 and 1963.

He was born in Tel Aviv, Israel.[2]


Running career

His personal bests were 21.97 in the 200 metres (in 1960); and 48.1 in the 400 metres (in 1966).[3] He was the Israeli Men's Champion in the 100 metres in 1960, and in the 200 metres in 1962 and 1963.[4]

He competed for Israel at the 1960 Summer Olympics in Rome, Italy, at the age of 20 in Athletics.[5] In the Men's 100 metres he came in 6th in Heat 1 with a time of 11.1, in the Men's 200 metres he came in 4th in Heat 9 with a time of 21.8, and in the Men's 400 metres he came in 6th in Heat 9 with a time of 48.9.[6] When he competed in the Olympics, he was 5-8.5 (174 cm) tall, and weighed 143 lbs (65 kg).[7]
