IDOOL (also Idol) is a village in the commune of Belel in the Adamawa Region of Cameroon, on the road from Ngaoundéré to Bebel and Mbang.[1]


Population

In 1967, Idool contains 272 inhabitants, mainly Fula people[2]

At the time of the 2005 census, there were 1952 people in the village.[3]


Economy

The economy of Idool is based primarily on agriculture and stockraising, with some tourism as a result of its cool climate.[4] However, the insecurity of the region has led a number of the inhabitants to move away to Ngaoundéré, a settlement away.[5][6]


Infrastructure

Idool possesses a mosque, a health centre built in 2003, a lycée which has been operating since 2007/2008,[7] and a high school.[8]
