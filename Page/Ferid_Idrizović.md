FERID IDRIZOVIC (born September 10, 1982) is a Bosnian-Herzegovinian former footballer who played in the Premier League of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Brunei Premier League, Albanian Superliga, Montenegrin First League, and the Canadian Soccer League.


Playing career

Idrizovic began his career in 1998 with FK Sarajevo in the Premier League of Bosnia and Herzegovina. Where he played in the 2002–03 UEFA Cup against Beşiktaş J.K.[1] In 2004, he went overseas to Brunei to play in the Brunei Premier League with Brunei DPMM FC. In 2005, he returned to Bosnia to play with NK Posušje, later he was transferred to KF Teuta Durrës of the Albanian Superliga.[2][3]After his tenure in Albania he returned to Bosnia to play with Sarajevo, NK Žepče, NK Kreševo-Stanić, and NK Travnik. In 2009, he went abroad to play in the Montenegrin First League with FK Dečić, and FK Rudar Pljevlja.[4][5] During his stint with Rudar Pljevlja he played in the 2010–11 UEFA Champions League against Litex Lovech.[6]In 2011, he went overseas to Canada to play with Brantford Galaxy in the Canadian Soccer League.[7] At the conclusion of the CSL season he returned to Montenegro to play and retire with FK Berane.
