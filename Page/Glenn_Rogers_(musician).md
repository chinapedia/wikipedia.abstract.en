GLENN ROGERS is an American guitarist who performs mainly thrash metal. He has most notably performed with Hirax, Deliverance, Vengeance Rising and Once Dead.[1]


History

Glenn Rogers started his career in 1986, with his first band Blind Decree, later known as Dissenter, with his friend Brett Eriksen.[2] He later joined the band Vengeance Rising and wrote music that would eventually end up on _Human Sacrifice_.[3][4] He left the band and joined a project with former Holy Soldier vocalist Robbie Brauns named H.E.R.O.[5] He joined Deliverance soon after joining H.E.R.O. Rogers stated his first show with the band went so well, he left H.E.R.O. and recorded the demo _Greetings of Death_ with the band.[6] The band recorded the debut self-titled album in 1989 before leaving. During his time in Deliverance, Rogers joined the band Steel Vengeance. His stint with Steel Vengeance lasted about four years, before taking a break in his musical career. Rogers joined Hirax in 2003 and stuck with them for several years, leaving and rejoining several times.[7][8] The first time he left, he gave an explanation on why he left He launched a one-off project with Daniel Cordova (formerly of Vengeance Rising) called Lambs Among Wolves in 2010. In 2011, Rogers reformed the band Metal Blade Records recording artists Viking and a project of his own with Cordova entitled Final Decree.[9] Neither band progressed with Rogers, until 2016, when Rogers resurrected Final Decree with Cordova and Martin DeBourge.[10] DeBourge later on left the band and was replaced by Sammy DeJohn (Ruthless).[11] In 2017, it was announced that George Ochoa had departed from Deliverance yet a second time, being replaced by Rogers who had originally been replaced by Ochoa.[12][13] Rogers appeared on _The Subversive Kind_ in 2018.
