_CAPPPARIS MICRACANTHA_[1] is a species of flowering plant in the caper family (Capparaceae). It grows as a shrub or small tree, sometimes with a more or less climbing habit.

It is native to Southeast Asia - the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, Indochina, Indonesia and the Philippines. Certain parts of the plant are locally used as a source of medicines and rarely for food.[2]


Subspecies

The Catalogue of Life lists:[3]

-   The nominate subspecies _C. micracantha micracantha_
-   _C. micracantha korthalsiana_ (Miq.) M.Jacobs (was _C. korthalsiana_ Miq.)
-   _C. micracantha_ var. _henryi_ (Matsum.) M.Jacobs (a synonym of _C. henryi_) is accepted in the Plant List.[4]
