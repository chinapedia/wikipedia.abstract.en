CARRINGTON is a locality in the Mid-Coast Council local government area of the Hunter and Mid North Coast regions of New South Wales, Australia, located on the northern shores of Port Stephens.[1]

Carrington was named in 1828[2] by Robert Dawson, the first commissioner of the Australian Agricultural Company.[3] The Newcastle suburb of the same name is named after his grandson, Lord Carrington, who became Governor of New South Wales.[4]


Former church

A former church, built in 1847, which had subsequently been used as a youth hostel, is a heritage site.[5][6]
