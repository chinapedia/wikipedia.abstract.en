TEDDY BERGQVIST (born 16 March 1999) is a Swedish footballer who plays for Kristianstad FC as a striker.


Club career

Malmö FF

On 14 October 2015 16-year-old Teddy Bergqvist signed a two-year apprenticeship contract with Malmö FF after spending a decade in the club's youth system.[1] A year later, on 1 October 2016, Bergqvist made his first team debut in a 4-2 win against BK Häcken when he came on as a substitute for Jo Inge Berget in stoppage time.[2]

On 12 July 2017, Bergqvist came on as a substitute for Paweł Cibicki in the 85th minute of Malmö FF's 1-1 first leg draw with FK Vardar in the second qualifying round of the Champions League.[3] On 18 July, he joined Åtvidabergs FF on loan in Superettan for the rest of the 2017 season.[4] In September, Bergqvist signed a one-year contract extension with Malmö FF to keep him at the club through the 2018 season.[5]

On 1 February 2018, Bergqvist joined Varbergs BoIS on loan in Superettan for the 2018 season.[6]

Kristianstad FC

On 18 January 2019, Bergqvist signed a two-year contract with Kristianstad FC.[7] He scored his first league goal during the second match of the season, the third goal in a 2–6 defeat at Oskarshamns AIK on 13 April.[8]
