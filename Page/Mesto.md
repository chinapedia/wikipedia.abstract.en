MELLE STOMP (born ),[1] better known by his stage name MESTO, is a Dutch electronic musician, record producer, remixer and DJ.[2] He gained recognition after collaborating with Martin Garrix (born in the same town of Amstelveen) on their single "WIEE".[3][4]


Biography

Early life

Stompje was born on September 30, 1999[5] in Amstelveen, Netherlands. When he was 6, he started playing the violin.[6] By the age of 11, he began playing the drums and shortly became interested in making music. He also developed an interest in deep house and future house when he heard "Gecko" by Oliver Heldens[7][8] and taught himself how to do it by playing it off YouTube.[9][10][11] At the age of 14, he became inspired when he 'touched' a DJ set for the first time, prompting him to download specialist software, FL Studio,[12] and enabling him to start composing.[13] He started making deep house music at that same age, but moved to slightly harder house, as well as future house.[14] He studies at Herman Brood Academy, a production school in Utrecht where Martin Garrix and Julian Jordan also went to.[15]

Music career

Mesto began his career in 2014, when he was only 15 years old and his official first release was the single "Wait another day", a collaboration with Alex Ranzino which was released as a free download on November 10, 2014.[16] Shortly afterwards, he released a remix with Benfield of "Rude", a single by Magic!. He then collaborated with Mike Williams to release a bootleg of "Raise Your Hands" by Ummet Ozcan. He started 2015 by releasing a single titled "New York". It was followed by his third single "Tokyo" in February.[17] In April, he released a bootleg with of "Lean On" by Major Lazer and DJ Snake featuring MØ in April.[18] The single "Rio" was released in May[19] before the bootleg of "Satisfied" by Showtek featuring Vassy in September. Mesto's single "Tetris (Truffle Butter Mashup)" was released as a free download on December 9, 2015.[20] He was featured in production alongside Justin Mylo in Martin Garrix's single "Bouncybob", which was released as a free download on December 31, 2015.[21][22][23][24]

On January 6, 2016, Mesto performed alongside Martin Garrix and Justin Mylo at Bij Igmar on SLAM!.[25][26] In April, he remixed "Me, Myself & I" by G-Eazy and Bebe Rexha, officially released on April 26.[27] The remix was followed by the bootleg of "Another You" by Armin van Buuren featuring Mr. Probz in that same month and the remix of "We're Guna Fight Em Off" by Ill Phil in June. In July, he and Justin Mylo remixed "Final Call", a single by Florian Picasso for the Remixes EP, officially released on July 8.[28] On October 10, 2016, it was announced that Mesto signed a contract with Spinnin' Records.[29][30][31] That same month, he released "Wiee", a collaboration with Martin Garrix on STMPD RCRDS.[32][33][34] The single was played by Garrix as an ID at Home Festival in Treviso, Italy in early September,[35] and was a part of his seven-track extended play _Seven_.[36] He also performed at the Amsterdam Dance Event for the first time.[37] On December 16, 2016, he released a remix of "Alone" by NERVO and Askery featuring Brielle Von Hugel, which was his first release on Spinnin' Records.[38] A day later, he was a guest and performed at Midden In Je Weekend Show on SLAM!.[39] He then performed at Mezz in Breda during the "3FM Clubhuis presents" and "DJs for 3FM Serious Request".[40][41]

On January 16, 2017, Mesto released "Chatterbox", a collaboration with Fox Stevenson through Spinnin' Records.[42][43] Shortly afterwards, he revealed "Step Up Your Game", his drum and bass debut single on Spinnin' Premium.[44] The single was available as a free download on Spinnin' Records website until March 17, after that date it was released on iTunes, Beatport and Spotify. He then released a remix for "Not Going Home", a single by DVBBS and CMC$ featuring Gia Koka.[45] On April 10, 2017, he collaborated with Curbi on "Bruh", which would be his first release through Tiësto's label Musical Freedom.[46][47] On July 10, 2017, Mesto released the single "Chances", featuring Brielle Von Hugel.[48] An official music video for the song was released.[49][50]
