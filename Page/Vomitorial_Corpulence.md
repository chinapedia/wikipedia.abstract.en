VOMITORIAL CORPULENCE was a grindcore band that originated in Melbourne, Australia. The band was started by Paul Green.[1] The band's name comes from Revelation 3:15-16.[2] Vomitorial Corpulence is widely known as one of the first Christian grindcore band.[3][4][5]


Background and impact

Vomitorial Corpulence started in 1992 as a vision of Guitarist/Vocalist Paul Green, but didn't get moving until 1993, when he hired on Bassist Mark Hamilton and Drummer Alexander O'Neil.[6] O'Neil quit in 1995 and was replaced by Christopher Valentine.[7] The band recorded their debut album, _Skin Stripper_, before the band went on hiatus for the first time. The Album includes two bluegrass banjo tracks composed by Mike Keady.[8][9] In December 2001, Green moved to Minnesota and married Maria, who became the band's Bassist, and hired on Ziggy Peters and Johnathan Kellerman on Vocals.[10][11] Due to lack of a Drummer, the band went on a second hiatus.[12] In August 2006, Green started the band back up with Hamilton and Valentine.[13] The band ended in 2008.

Alexander O'Neil, the band's former drummer, joined Cryptal Darkness and Desolate Eternity after departing from the band. Valentine ran the label known the label known as Christ Core Records, which signed the act Flactorophia.
