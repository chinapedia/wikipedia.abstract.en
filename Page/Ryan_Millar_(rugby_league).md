RYAN MILLAR (born 12 May 1994) is a rugby league footballer who plays on the for the Sheffield Eagles in the Betfred Championship.[1][2]


Background

Millar was born in Biederfeld, Germany but brought up in Birmingham, England. He is a trainee Architect in Sheffield.[3]


Playing career

Millar is a graduate of the Eagles Academy system[4] He made his professional debut for the Eagles on 26 June 2015 in an away triumph at London Broncos, the score was 14-22 and he scored two consecutive tries in 5 minutes to put his team 10-6 in front just after half-time. Millar made his home debut against Hull Kingston Rovers. in The Qualifiers at Bramall Lane. This time he scored a hat-trick in 28-38 loss. Millar had now forced his way into the team ahead of the 2016 season, and he expected to be the newest young talent to come out the Eagles' youth set-up. During that season he made eight starts and scored a total of 5 tries, he also received the Man of the Match against Dewsbury Rams away. The was now considered a permanent fixture in the Eagles line-up. Throughout the course of their 2017 campaign he showed his versatility in playing as a as well as his natural position on the wing. Millar played a total of 22 games (21 starts) during 2017, but his scoring run dried up as he only managed the one try against Oldham on the first day of the season.
