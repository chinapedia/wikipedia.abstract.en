LUKA ADŽIĆ (; born 17 September 1998) is a Serbian footballer, who plays as a midfielder for R.S.C. Anderlecht.


Club career

Red Star Belgrade

2015–16 season

Born in Belgrade,[1] Adžić joined Red Star Belgrade at the age of 6.[2] He joined the first team of Red Star Belgrade for the 2015–16 season, after he passed all youth categories of the same club. At the beginning of 2016, during the winter break off-season, Adžić earned an anterior cruciate ligament injury,[3] and later he was out of the field until May same year.[4] He signed his first three-year professional contract with club on 3 March 2016.[5]

2016–17 season

After fully recovery, Adžić returned in squad for the next season, but also stayed with youth team until the end of first half-season in youth league, when he rejoined the first team.[6] In the meantime, he made his official debut for Red Star Belgrade in a cup match against BSK Borča, played on 26 October 2016 at the Rajko Mitić Stadium.[7] Adžić made his Serbian SuperLiga debut in away match against Metalac Gornji Milanovac replacing Srđan Plavšić in 83 minute, and he also scored his first senior goal for 2–1 victory in last minutes of the game.[8] Adžić scored his second goal for the team in 23rd fixture match of the 2016–17 Serbian SuperLiga season, against OFK Bačka, when he also joined the game as a substitution.[9] Adžić started his first senior match on the field in last fixture match of the season, against Radnički Niš.[10]

2017–18 season

After he overgrown youth selection, Adžić became fully senior in 2017. During the summer pre-season, Adžić presented as an one of the club's top 10 youth prospects.[11] He converted his squad number before the 2017–18 UEFA Europa League qualifications, taking jersey 11. On 29 June 2017, Adžić made his first continental appearance, replacing Ricardo Cavalcante Mendes during the match against Floriana.[12] Adžić joined his first eternal derby, replacing Slavoljub Srnić in 90 minute of a match played on 27 August 2017.[13] Adžić scored his first season realising direct free kick goal in the Serbian Cup match against Dinamo Vranje 11 October 2017.[14] Later, during the same match, he completed his first hat-trick in professional career, by two Aleksandar Pešić's assists.[15] In opening match of the second half- season of the 2017–18 Serbian SuperLiga campaign, played on 17 February 2018, Adžić scored his second league goal, against Javor Ivanjica.[16] Several days later, on 21 February, Adžić also joined the in second leg against CSKA Moscow in Round of 32 match of the UEFA Europa League.[17] On 18 March 2018, Adžić realised a direct free kick in 28 SuperLiga fixture against Mačva Šabac.[18] Replacing Slavoljub Srnić in second half of the match against Čukarički, played on 25 April 2018, Adžić scored and made an assist to El Fardou Ben Nabouhane in 3–0 victory.[19] At the beginning of May 2018, Adžić suffered a toe injury and missed the rest of season.[20]

Anderlecht

At the beginning of May 2018, it was announced R.S.C. Anderlecht had expressed an interest for signing Luka Adžić, after which he refused to sign a new contract with Red Star.[21] Finally, his home club agreed on a deal after the end of the 2017–18 season, and Adžić moved to Anderlecht on 23 May 2018. Adžić signed a four-year contract with the new club and the transfer fee was reported as 800k euros.[22]


International career

Adžić was called into the Serbia national under-18 football team and played several matches in 2015 under coach Ivan Tomić.[23][24] He was also a member of the Serbia U19 squad between 2016 and 2017.[25][26] He scored his first goal for the team in a friendly match against Bulgaria, played on 21 February 2017.[27] Adžić got his first call in Serbian under-21 team by coach Goran Đorović in August 2017.[28] Adžić made his debut for the team replacing Aleksandar Mesarović in 80 minute of the match against Gibraltar on 1 September 2017.[29]
