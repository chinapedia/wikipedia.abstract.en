CATHERINE FOURNIER (born 7 April 1992) is a Canadian politician, who was elected to the National Assembly of Quebec in a by-election on December 5, 2016.[1] She represents the electoral district of Marie-Victorin. Fournier is the youngest woman to be elected to the Quebec National Assembly.[2]

Originally elected as a member of the Parti Québécois, Fournier quit the PQ on March 11, 2019 to sit as an independent MNA, stating that she believed the party had lost its way ideologically.[3][4]


Early life

Fournier was born in Sainte-Julie, Quebec on 7 April 1992. She holds an economics major and political science minor from the Université de Montréal. She was a political blogger and columnist for 103.3 FM.[5]
