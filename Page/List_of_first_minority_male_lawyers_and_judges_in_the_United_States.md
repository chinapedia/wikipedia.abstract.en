This is a list of the FIRST MINORITY MALE LAWYER(S) AND JUDGE(S) in each state. It includes the year in which the men were admitted to practice law (in parentheses). Also included are other distinctions such as the first minority men in their state to obtain a law degree or become a political figure.


Firsts nationwide

George_Lewis_Ruffin.jpg Macon_B_Allen.jpg Hong_Yen_Chang1.jpg Herbert_Choy.jpg

Law degree

-   George Lewis Ruffin (1869):[1][2][3] First African American male to earn a law degree in the U.S. (1869)
-   Hong Yen Chang (1888):[4] First Chinese male immigrant to earn a law degree in the U.S. (1886)
-   Josiah McIntyre:[5][6] First blind male to earn a law degree in the U.S. (1889)

Lawyers

-   Moses Levy (1778):[7][8][9][10] First Jewish American male lawyer in the U.S. He would later become a judge.
-   James McDonald (c. 1820s):[11] First Native American (Choctaw) male lawyer in the U.S.
-   Macon Bolling Allen (1844):[12][13] First African American male lawyer in the U.S.
-   John Rock (1861):[14][15] First African American male lawyer admitted to practice before the U.S. Supreme Court (1865)
-   Augustin Haidusek (c. 1870):[16] First male lawyer of Czech descent in the U.S.
-   Samuel R. Lowery (1874):[17] First African American male lawyer to argue a case before the U.S. Supreme Court (1880)
-   Joseph G. Parkinson (1880):[18] First deaf male lawyer in the United States
-   Hong Yen Chang (1888):[19] First Chinese male lawyer in the U.S.
-   Thomas L. Sloan (1892):[20][21][22] First Native American (Omaha people) male lawyer to argue a case before the U.S. Supreme Court (1904)
-   Masuji Miyakawa (1905):[23] First Japanese American male lawyer in the U.S.
-   Roger Demosthenes O'Kelly (1908):[24][25] First African American deaf male lawyer in the U.S.
-   Pablo Manlapit (1919):[26] First male lawyer of Filipino descent in the U.S.
-   You Chung Hong[27][28] (1923): First Chinese American male lawyer to practice before the U.S. Supreme Court (1933)
-   Charles Hamilton Houston (1924):[29][30] First African American male lawyer to argue and win a case before the U.S. Supreme Court
-   Manuel Ruiz (1930):[31][32][33] First Latino American male lawyer to argue a case before the U.S. Supreme Court (1952)
-   Herbert Choy (1941):[34] First Korean American male lawyer in the U.S.
-   Benjamim Gim (c. 1949):[35][36] First Asian American lawyer to argue a case before the U.S. Supreme Court (1957)
-   Leonard Staisey (c. 1950):[37][38] First blind male lawyer in the U.S.
-   Michael A. Chatoff (c. 1960s):[39][40][41] First deaf male lawyer to argue a case before the U.S. Supreme Court (1982)
-   Thelton Henderson (1962):[42] First African American male lawyer in the U.S. Department of Justice's Civil Rights Division
-   Rodney B. Lewis (1972):[43][44][45] First Native American (Gila River Indian Community) male lawyer to win a U.S. Supreme Court case (1980)
-   Harris L. Kimball:[46][47][48] First openly LGBT male lawyer in the U.S. (1973)
-   Christopher Thang Tao (c. 1986):[49] First Hmong American male lawyer in the U.S.
-   Sergio C. Garcia (2014):[50] First undocumented immigrant male admitted to practice law in the U.S. and the California State Bar

Law Clerks

-   William Thaddeus Coleman Jr. (1946):[51][52] First African American male to clerk for a U.S. Supreme Court Justice (1947)
-   Louis Henkin:[53] First Orthodox Jewish male (who graduated in 1940 and pursued an academic career) to clerk for a U.S. Supreme Court Justice (1946-1947)
-   Vikram Amar:[54] First South Asian male (who graduated in 1988 and pursued an academic career) to clerk for a U.S. Supreme Court Justice (1989-1990)
-   Ted Cruz (c. 1995):[55][56][57] First Hispanic American male (a lawyer and now politician) to clerk for a Chief Justice of the United States (1996)

Judicial officers

Justice of the peace

-   Wentworth Cheswell:[58] First African American male Justice of the Peace in the U.S. (1805)

Judges

-   Issac Miranda:[59][60][61][62] First Jewish American male to serve as a judge in the U.S. and Pennsylvania (1727)
-   Macon Bolling Allen (1844):[63][64] First African American male judicial officer in the U.S. and Massachusetts (1848)
-   James Dean (1884):[65][66] First African American male elected as a county judge in the South since Reconstruction and in Florida (1888)
-   Lawson E. Thomas (1923):[67] First African American male judge in the South since Reconstruction and in Florida (1950)
-   Dalip Singh Saund:[68][69] First Asian Indian-American male elected as a judge in the U.S. (1950)
-   John Aiso (1941):[70][71][72][73] First Japanese American judge in the contiguous U.S. and California (1952)
-   Delbert E. Wong (1948):[74][75][76] First Chinese American male judge in the contiguous U.S. and California (1959)
-   Stephen Lachs (1963):[77] First openly LGBT male judge in the U.S. and California (1979)
-   William McKinley Branch:[78][79][80] First African American male elected as a probate judge in the U.S. and Alabama (1970)
-   Mel Red Recana (1974):[81][82][83] First Filipino American male judge in the U.S. and California (1981)
-   Paul C. Lo (1994):[84] First Hmong American male judge in the U.S. and California (2013)
-   Firdaus Dordi:[85][86] First Zoroastrian male judge in the U.S. and California (2017)
-   Mike Jacobs:[87] First openly LGBT judge in the U.S. and Georgia (2018)[88]

State judgeships

Justices

-   Jonathan Jasper Wright (1865):[89] First African American male to serve on a state supreme court (1870)
-   Masaji Marumoto:[90][91] First Japanese American male to serve on a state supreme court (1956)
-   Wilfred Tsukiyama (c. 1924):[92][93][94] First Japanese American male to serve as the Chief Justice of a state supreme court (1959)
-   Ben Menor:[95][96][97] First Filipino American male to serve on a state supreme court (1974)
-   Robert N. C. Nix Jr. (c. 1954):[98] First African American male to serve as the Chief Justice of a state supreme court (1984)
-   Luis Rovira:[99][100] First Hispanic American male to serve as the Chief Justice of a state supreme court (1990)
-   Richard B. Teitelman (1973):[101][102] First blind male (who is Jewish) appointed as a state supreme court justice (2002)
-   Rives Kistler (1981):[103] First openly LGBT male appointed as a state supreme court justice (2003)

Federal judgeships

-   Jacob Trieber:[104] First Jewish American male appointed as a federal judge in the U.S. (1900)
-   William H. Hastie (1931):[105][106] First African American male appointed as a federal judge in the U.S. (1937)
-   Shiro Kashiwa (1936):[107] First Japanese American male appointed to a federal judgeship in the U.S. (1972)
-   William B. Bryant (1939):[108] First African American male to be appointed as a chief federal judge in the U.S. (1977)
-   Reynaldo Guerra Garza (1939):[109] First Mexican American male appointed to a federal court (1961) and a U.S. Court of Appeals circuit (1979)
-   Herbert Choy (1941):[110] First Korean American male to serve as a federal judge in the U.S.
-   Thomas Tang (1950):[111][112] First Chinese American male appointed to the federal judiciary in the U.S. (1977)
-   José A. Cabranes (1965):[113] First Puerto Rican male appointed to a federal judgeship in the continental U.S. (1979)
-   Dickran Tevrizian (1965):[114] First male of Armenian descent to become a U.S. federal judge (1985)
-   Ronald S. W. Lew (1971):[115][116] First Chinese American male appointed to the federal bench in the contiguous U.S. (1987)
-   Michael Burrage (1974):[117][118] First Native American (Choctaw Nation of Oklahoma) male appointed as a federal judge in the U.S. (1994)
-   Eduardo C. Robreno (1978):[119] First Cuban American male appointed as a federal judge in the U.S. (1992)
-   Samuel Der-Yeghiayan (1978):[120] First Armenian immigrant federal judge in the U.S. (2003)
-   Darrin P. Gayles (1993):[121] First openly LGBT African American male appointed as a U.S. federal judge (2014)

District court

-   James Benton Parsons (1949):[122] First African American to serve as a U.S. federal judge in a U.S. District Court (1961)
-   Dick Yin Wong (1950):[123] First Chinese American appointed as a federal district court judge (1975)
-   Richard C. Casey (1958):[124] First blind male to become a federal district court judge (1997)
-   Denny Chin (1978):[125] First Asian American male appointed as a U.S. district court judge outside of the Ninth Circuit (2010)
-   J. Paul Oetken (1991):[126] First openly LGBT male appointed as a U.S. district court judge (2011)
-   Amul Thapar (1994):[127] First South Asian American male appointed as a federal district judge (2007)

Circuit court

-   Chuck Mau:[128][129] First Chinese American male appointed as a state circuit court judge (1950)
-   Ben Menor:[130][131][132] First Filipino American male appointed as a state circuit court judge (1968)
-   Arthur Lawrence Alarcon:[133][134] First Latino American male appointed as a Judge of the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit (1979)
-   Julio M. Fuentes (1975):[135] First Hispanic American male appointed as a Judge of the United States Court of Appeals for the Third Circuit (2000)
-   Todd M. Hughes (1992):[136] First openly LGBT male appointed as a judge on a federal circuit court (2013)[137]

Customs court

-   Irvin Charles Mollison (1923):[138] First African American male to serve on the U.S. Customs Court (1945)

Tax court

-   Juan Vasquez:[139] First Latino American male appointed as a Judge of the U.S. Tax Court (1995)

Appellate court

-   Reynaldo Guerra Garza (1939):[140] First Mexican American male appointed to a U.S. Court of Appeals circuit (1979)
-   Wade H. McCree (1948):[141][142] First African American male appointed as a Judge of the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Sixth Circuit (1966)
-   A. Wallace Tashima (1961):[143] First Japanese American male appointed to a U.S. Court of Appeals (1995)
-   David S. Tatel (1966):[144] First blind male appointed as a federal appellate judge (1994)
-   Roger Gregory (1978):[145] First African American male appointed as a Judge of the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Fourth Circuit (2000)
-   Sri Srinivasan (1995):[146][147] First South Asian American male appointed as a federal appellate judge (2013)

Justices

-   Louis Brandeis (1878):[148] First Jewish male appointed as Justice of the U.S. Supreme Court (1916)
-   Thurgood Marshall (1933):[149] First African American male appointed a Justice of the U.S. Supreme Court (1967)
-   Antonin Scalia (1960):[150] First Italian American male appointed as Justice of the U.S. Supreme Court (1986)

Judge advocate general

-   John Fugh (1960):[151][152] First Chinese American to become The Judge Advocate General of the U.S. Army (1991-1993)

Attorneys General

Federal

-   Edward H. Levi (1938):[153] First Jewish American male to serve as the U.S. Attorney General (1975-1977)
-   Eric Holder (1976):[154] First African American male to be appointed as the U.S Attorney General (2009-2015)
-   Alberto Gonzales (1982): First Hispanic American male to be appointed as the U.S. Attorney General (2005-2007)

State

-   Edward Brooke (1948):[155] First African American male to serve as a state attorney general in the U.S. and Massachusetts (1962)
-   Larry Echo Hawk:[156][157] First Native American male to serve as a state attorney general in the U.S. and Idaho (1991)
-   Gurbir Grewal (1999):[158] First Sikh American male to serve as a state attorney general in the U.S. and New Jersey (2018)
-   William Tong:[159][160] First Asian American male elected as a state attorney general in the U.S. and Connecticut (2018). Tong is the second elected Asian-American attorney general in the nation's history, though, as female Senator Kamala Harris became the first in 2011.

Deputy Attorneys General

Federal

-   Eric Holder (1976):[161][162] First African American male to be appointed as the Deputy Attorney General of the United States (1997-2001)

State

-   Chuck Mau:[163][164] First Chinese American male to serve as a Deputy Attorney General in the U.S. (c. 1936)

Associate Attorney General

Federal

-   Wayne Budd:[165] First African American male to serve as the Associate Attorney General of the U.S. (1992-1993)

Deputy Associate Attorney General

Federal

-   Nelson Dong (1974):[166][167] First Chinese American male appointed as a Deputy Associate Attorney General of the United States (1979-1980)

Assistant Attorney General

Federal

-   William H. Lewis (c. 1890s):[168][169] First African American male appointed as the United States Assistant Attorney General (1910)

Civil Rights Division

-   Bill Lann Lee:[170] First Asian American male (Chinese American) to serve as the United States Assistant Attorney General for the Civil Rights in the U.S. Department of Justice (1997-2001)
-   R. Alexander Acosta:[171] First Latino American male to serve as the United States Assistant Attorney General for the Civil Rights in the U.S. Department of Justice (2003-2005)
-   Wan J. Kim:[172] First immigrant and Korean American male to serve as the United States Assistant Attorney General for the Civil Rights in the U.S. Department of Justice (2005-2007)

Special Assistant to the Attorney General

Federal

-   Nelson Dong (1974):[173][174] First Chinese American male appointed as a Special Assistant to the U.S. Attorney General (1978-1979)

Solicitor General

Federal

-   Thurgood Marshall (1933):[175] First African American male appointed as the Solicitor General of the United States (1965)
-   Noel Francisco (1996):[176] First Asian American (who is Filipino American) appointed as the Solicitor General of the United States (2017)

United States Attorney

-   Cecil F. Poole (1939):[177] First African American male to serve as a U.S. Attorney in the U.S. (1961)
-   Mickey Michaux (1964):[178] First African American male to serve as a U.S. Attorney in the South since Reconstruction (1977)
-   Terry L. Pechota (1972):[179][180][181][182] First Native American male (Sioux Indian) to serve as a U.S. Attorney in the U.S. (1979-1981)
-   Norman Bay (1986):[183] First Chinese American male to serve as a U.S. Attorney in the U.S. (2000-2001)

Assistant District Attorney

-   Leonard Staisey (1948):[184] First blind male lawyer (who later became a judge) to serve as an Assistant District Attorney (1950) in the U.S.

County Attorney

-   John C. Choi (1995):[185][186][187] First Korean American male appointed as a County Attorney in the U.S. (2011)

Political office

-   Octaviano Ambrosio Larrazolo (1888):[188] First Mexican American male (a lawyer) to serve as a U.S. Senator (1928)
-   Wing F. Ong (1943):[189] First Chinese American male lawyer to enter the House of Representatives and State Legislature in the U.S.
-   Leonard Staisey (1948):[190] First blind male lawyer (who later became a judge) to serve as a Senate member in the U.S. (1961)
-   James Abourezk (c. 1966):[191][192] First Lebanese / Arab American male (a lawyer) to become a member of Congress in the U.S. and South Dakota (1973)
-   John D. Waiheʻe III (1976):[193] First male American of Native Hawaiian descent (a lawyer) to elected as a state governor in the U.S. (1986-1994)
-   Ed Flanagan (1976):[194] First openly LGBT male (a lawyer) to serve as a state comptroller in the U.S. and Vermont (1993-2001)
-   David Cicilline:[195][196][197] First openly gay male (a lawyer) to become the mayor of a US state capital (2003-2011)
-   Keith Ellison (1990):[198][199] First Muslim American male lawyer (who is African American) elected to the U.S. Congress (Minnesota, 2006)
-   Cyrus Habib:[200] First Iranian American male (a lawyer who is also blind) elected to a statewide office in the U.S. (2012)

Presidential Candidates / President

-   Frank R. Beckwith:[201] First African American male (a lawyer) to run as a candidate for the President of the U.S. in a major-party primary (1960)
-   Hiram Fong:[202][203][204] First Asian American male (a lawyer) to run as a candidate for the President of the U.S. (1964)
-   Barack Obama (1991):[205] First African American male (a lawyer) to become President of the United States (2009-2017)

Bar Associations

Federal

-   Bernard G. Segal:[206] First Jewish American male to serve as the President of the American Bar Association (1969)
-   Dennis Archer:[207] First African American male to serve as the President of the American Bar Association (2003-2004)
-   Michael S. Greco:[208] First foreign-born male of Italian and Calabrian descent to serve as President of the American Bar Association (2006-2007)
-   Stephen Zack:[209][210] First Hispanic American male to serve as the President of the American Bar Association (2010-2012)
-   Lawrence R. Baca:[211] First Native American (Pawnee) male to serve as the President of the Federal Bar Association (2009). He was the first Native American to lead a national non-minority bar association.

State

-   Judge John A. Howard:[212] First African American elected president of a state bar association.(1981-1982)
-   Wayne Budd:[213] First African American male to serve as the president of a state bar association (1979-1980)
-   Mark Johnson:[214] First openly LGBT male to serve as the president of a state bar association (1998)
