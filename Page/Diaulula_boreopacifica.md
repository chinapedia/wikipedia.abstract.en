_DIAULULA BOREOPACIFICA_ is a species of sea slug, a dorid nudibranch, a shell-less marine gastropod mollusc in the family Discodorididae.[1]


Distribution

This dorid nudibranch occurs in the northern Pacific, from Northern California to Alaska, Japan and Russia. It has been confused with _Diaulula sandiegensis_ which has a more southerly range from California south to Baja California, Mexico.[2]


Description

This nudibranch has a background colour which can be white or any shade of yellow to a yellowish brown with characteristic markings consisting of many brown spots of varying sizes. These spots are surrounded by a paler ring and extend into the outer part of the mantle.[3][4] It grows to about 70 mm (3") in length.


Life habits

This species feeds on the sponge _Haliclona_ sp.. It is most common in the intertidal region.[5]
