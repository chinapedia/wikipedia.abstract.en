PASCAL MÉRIGEAU (30 January 1953, Périgné in Deux-Sèvres) is a French journalist and film critic.


Biography

After studying in Poitiers, he settled in Paris in 1976 and became a journalist. He worked for film magazines, then for _Les Nouvelles littéraires_, _Le Point_ and _Le Monde_, before collaborating to _Le Nouvel Observateur_ from September 1997.

He participated in the selection of films for the Cannes Film Festival, currently replaced by Eric Libiot.

A novelist, he also writes short stories, including _Quand Angèle fut seule_ written in 1983.[1]
