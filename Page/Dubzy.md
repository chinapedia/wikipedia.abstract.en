Dubzy_Snazz.jpg JESSE JAMES (born Jesse James on 1 February), better known by his stage name DUBZY, is an English grime MC, actor and entrepreneur. He is the co-founder of music collective and fashion label Snazz. He won Red Bull's 2016 Grime-a-Side championship with the Derby team[1] and finished second in _UK Grime_'s best MC's of 2016.[2] His "Snazz" headwear products, dubbed 'SnazzBacks',[3] were the first snapbacks to feature in GQ Magazine.[4]

In November 2016, Dubzy signed a publishing deal with Sony ATV and Reprezent for a single alongside fellow grime artists Kannan & Eyez entitled 'Highs & Lows'.[5]


Early life

Jesse James was born in Tottenham, North London, before moving to Derby where he grew up with his mother and brother. He attended Stroud Green Primary School in Finsbury Park and Redwood Primary School as a child. After being expelled from Derby College, he moved back to London and attended Harrow College before graduating with a degree in BBA Management at Lancaster University's Management School.


Career

Dubzy started out producing on PlayStation's Music 2000 music creation program at the age of 12 before moving on to music production on FL Studio as well as picking up rapping in his early teens. At 15, he released his first LP entitled 'On Music Daily: Vol. 1'. James released a series of mixtapes between 2012 and 2014 including Apples & Blackberrys[6] and Beat Is My Counsellor,[7] the latter of which spent three consecutive months in Audiomack's top 5 most downloaded mixtapes/albums in 2012.[8] In 2014, he released Gift of the Gab; the CD features collaborations with Bloodline recording artist Paper Pabs as well as tracks with Roll Deep MC Trim.[9]

In 2015, BBC Radio 1 DJ Charlie Sloth selected Dubzy as one of his three 'MC's to watch in 2016' and praised Snazz headwear in a Fire in the Booth workshop episode of his live prime-time radio show. An invitation from Tim Westwood to perform a Crib Session[10] on Capital Xtra alongside fellow Grime artist Eyez followed.[11] The two also performed at every leg of the MOBO Awards' Unsung tour and released a single _Let's Play a Game_[12] in the same period. Dubzy has featured on a number of singles by other artists including _Hear Me_[13] which debuted on Sian Anderson's BBC 1xtra show and features him alongside Roll Deep's Manga, Rukus, Kobi Onyame and Eyez.[14] Dubzy also collaborated with Z Dot, Mez, Snowy, Row-d & Eyez on 'Raise The Bar' single last year.[15][16]

On 1 January 2016, Dubzy released single "Hungry For Dis" alongside Eyez and Kannan.[17] He became the face of high street retailer Footasylum's[18] #WeAreFootasylum campaign.[19][20][21]

In October 2016, Dubzy signed a publishing deal with Sony ATV and Reprezent for a single entitled 'Highs and Lows'.[22] He won Red Bull's Grime-a-Side 2016[23] national championship for the Derby team, the smallest team/city in the competition.[24][25]

James' Snazz headwear range has been worn by figures in the entertainment industry such as David Haye, Ashley Young and Antony Costa.[26][27][28][29] The brand has a strong working relationship with partners Footasylum as well as partnership deals with AG Barr,[30] iLuvLive,[31] and Risky Roadz.[32] Dubzy has also partnered with annual fashion show and exhibition, Worn Out Festival in the past to promote the event and boost its sales.[33]
