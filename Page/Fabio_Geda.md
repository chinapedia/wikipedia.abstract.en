FABIO GEDA (born 1 March 1972) is an Italian novelist.

Born in Turin, Geda graduated in Communication Sciences with a thesis in marketing.[1] He worked as an educator, first as a voluntary, later into a cooperative.[2] His debut novel _Per il resto del viaggio ho sparato agli indiani_ has been selected for the Strega Prize.[3]

Geda had his breakout in 2010 with the novel _In the Sea There Are Crocodiles_ (); based on a true story of Enaiat Akbari, the novel became a bestseller, selling over 400,000 copies in Italy alone and being translated in over 30 languages.[4][5][6]
