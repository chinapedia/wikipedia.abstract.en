_HALONGELLA SCHLUMBERGERI_ is a species of air-breathing land snail, a terrestrial pulmonate gastropod mollusk in the family Plectopylidae.

_Halongella schlumbergeri_ the type species of the genus _Halongella_.[1]


Distribution

The distribution of _Halongella schlumbergeri_ includes Hạ Long Bay area in Haiphong municipality and Quảng Ninh Province, Vietnam.[2]

The type locality is "Baie d’Along et montagne de l’Éléphant".[3][4]


Description

The most important shell characters for identification of _Halongella schlumbergeri_ include: the shell is robust; callus and aperture shape (including the formation of the fold).[5]

The size of the shell is medium to very large.[6] The shell is thick, almost smooth or with very fine periostracal ribs.[7] The width of the shell is 16.1–28.1 mm.[8] The height of the shell is 7.0–13.1 mm.[9]

The apertural lip is well-developed.[10] The apertural fold is long, more or less equally long in its total length, connected to the callus.[11]

Parietal wall is with missing or short anterior lamella (always distant from the upper plica) and well-developed posterior lamella.[12] The species is very variable in terms of shell size and the formation of plicae and lamellae on the parietal wall.[13]

Palatal plicae are depressed and Z-shaped.[14]

The radula of _Halongella schlumbergeri_ has 10 lateral teeth and 14 marginal teeth.[15] The size of the central tooth is smaller than the ectocone of the first lateral.[16] The shape of the first lateral is oval.[17] Marginal teeth are bicuspid or tricuspid with blunt inner cusp and shallow incision between the inner two cusps.[18]

Halongella_schlumbergeri_reproductive_system.jpg The reproductive system was described by Páll-Gergely et al. in 2015.[19] Embryos were recorded to be present in the uterus.[20] There were recorded flat, thin, with no particular shape calcareous granules on the internal surface of penis.[21]

Similar species: _Gudeodiscus dautzenbergi_ and some populations of _Gudeodiscus villedaryi_ resemble _Halongella schlumbergeri_ in terms of general, but the inner lamellae are entirely different.[22] Most _Halongella schlumbergeri_ shells lack the anterior lamella.[23] _Gudeodiscus dautzenbergi_ and _Gudeodiscus villedaryi_ have strong, well-developed anterior lamella with an anteriorly elongated lower “leg”.[24] It is possible to distinguish _Halongella schlumbergeri_ from the other two species without breaking the shell, on the basis of the long apertural fold reaching the callus, which is short in _Gudeodiscus dautzenbergi_ and _Gudeodiscus villedaryi_, and has an elevated “knob” part in some distance from the callus.[25]


Ecology

It is a ground-dwelling species as all other plectopylid snails in Vietnam.[26]
