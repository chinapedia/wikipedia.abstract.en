JEAN BOUGHTON WISHART (8 October 1920 – 16 November 2016) was a New Zealand journalist who was the editor of the _New Zealand Woman's Weekly_ from 1952 until 1985.


Biography

Born in the Auckland suburb of Remuera on 8 October 1920, Wishart was the daughter of Florence Olivia Marianne Wishart (née Cadman) and Robert Wishart.[1][2] She joined the _New Zealand Woman's Weekly_ as an office girl when she left school. She eventually rose to become the magazine's editor in 1952. During her 33-year tenure as editor—the longest in the magazine's history—circulation increased from about 100,000 to a peak of 250,000 in the early 1980s.[3]

She served as a director of NZ News Ltd from 1975, and was a member of the council of the Auckland Chamber of Commerce.[4]

In the 1985 Queen's Birthday Honours, Wishart was appointed an Officer of the Order of the British Empire, for services to journalism.[5]

Wishart never married. She died in Auckland on 16 November 2016 at the age of 96.[6]
