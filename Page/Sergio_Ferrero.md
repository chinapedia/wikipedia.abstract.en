SERGIO FERRERO (21 December 1926 â€“ 12 August 2008) was an Italian novelist.

Born in Turin, Ferrero made his literary debut at 40 years old, with the novel _Gloria_. In 1971, he was finalist at the Strega Prize with the novel _Il giuoco sul ponte_. After a 16 years hiatus, he reprised his literary activities in 1987.[1]

In 1996, Ferrero won the Bagutta Prize for the novel _Gli occhi del padre_.[2][3][4]

During his life Ferrero collaborated as an editor with various newspapers and journals, and also worked as an antiquarian.[5] He died on August 12, 2008 in Lezzeno, where he was spending his holidays.[6]
