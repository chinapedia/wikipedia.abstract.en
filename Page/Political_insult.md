POLITICAL INSULT refers to a statement from a politician about another one which contains disdainful purpose or notorious offense. They are not defined in any political protocol.


Notable political insults

-   1922, Georges Clemenceau, French statesman on David Lloyd George, British politician: "_Oh, if I could piss the way he speaks!_"[1]
-   1999, Mustafa Tlass, Syrian minister of Defense called Yaser Arafat "son of 60,000 whores".[2]
-   2006, Hugo Chavez, president of Venezuela named George Bush (President of United States) "devil".[3] (See 2006 Chávez speech at the United Nations)
-   2010, Mohammad-Javad Larijani, Iranian Secretary of High Council for Human rights, called Barack Obama, President of the United States "_Kaka Siah_", a Persian term akin to "nigger."[4]
-   2013, John McCain, U.S. Senator, compared Mahmoud Ahmadinejad, President of Islamic republic of Iran with the monkeys by twitting:"_So Ahmadinejad wants to be first Iranian in space – wasn't he just there last week? "Iran launches monkey into space"_"[5][6]
-   2013, Benjamin Netanyahu, Prime Minister of Israel called Hasan Rouhani, President of Islamic republic of Iran, "a wolf in sheep's clothing".[7]
-   2014, Tony Abbott, prime minister of Australia offensed Vladimir Putin, president of Russia by saying "shirtfront".[8]
-   2016, Rodrigo Duterte, president of Philippine used the words "Son of a whore" and "Go to the hell" for Barack Obama, President of the United States.[9][10] Duterte has also said some offenses about European Union, United Nations, Pope and God in his official speeches.[11]
-   2016, Donald Trump and Hillary Clinton, United States presidential election of 2016 nominees have insulted each other in presidential debates.[12] Trump has a long history of using unflattering monikers and slurs against various political opponents.
