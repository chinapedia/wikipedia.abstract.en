Professor CHELVANAYAKAM KANAGANAYAKAM (; May 7, 1952 â€“ November 22, 2014) was a Tamil Canadian translator, author and academic.


Early life and family

Kanaganayakam was born on May 7, 1952 in Colombo, Ceylon.[1][2] He was the son of V. Chelvanayakam, head of the Department of Tamil at the University of Ceylon, Peradeniya, and Kamalambikai.[3][4][5][6] He was educated at Trinity College, Kandy.[7] After school Kanaganayakam joined the University of Sri Lanka Peradeniya campus but following Osmund Jayaratne's "re-organisation" of universities, he and other language and literature students were moved to the university's campus in Kelaniya.[8][9] He graduated in 1976 with a B.A. degree in English language and literature.[10][11][12][13]

Kanaganayakam was married to Thirumagal.[14][15][16] They had a daughter (Shankary) and a son (Jegan).[17][18][19]


Career

Kanaganayakam was a lecturer in the Department of English at the University of Jaffna before joining the University of British Columbia on a Commonwealth scholarship, receiving a Ph.D. degree in 1985 after producing a thesis, supervised by W. H. New, on the writings of Zulfikar Ghose.[20][21][22][23] Kanaganayakam joined the University of Toronto's Department of English in 1989 to research and teach Commonwealth literature.[24][25][26] Appointed a professor in 2002, he went on to become director of the Centre for South Asian Studies at the Munk School of Global Affairs and co-ordinator of the independent studies program at Trinity College, Toronto.[27][28][29][30]

Kanaganayakam was a founding member of the Tamil Literary Garden and the Toronto Tamil Studies Conference.[31][32][33] On the morning of November 22, 2014 Kanaganayakam was inducted to the Royal Society of Canada as a fellow in Quebec City.[34][35][36][37] That evening, as he went to a celebratory dinner in Montreal, he suffered a heart attack and passed away.[38][39][40][41]


Works

Kanaganayakam wrote, translated and edited several books:[42]

-   _Structures of Negation: The Writings of Zulfikar Ghose_ (1993, University of Toronto Press)[43][44]
-   _Configuartions of Exile: South Asian Writers and Their World_ (1995, TSAR Publications)[45][46]
-   _Dark Antonyms and Paradise: The Poetry of Rienzi Crusz_ (1997, TSAR Publications)[47][48]
-   _Lutesong and Lament: Tamil Writing from Sri Lanka_ (2001, TSAR Publications, editor)[49][50]
-   _Counterrealism and Indo-Anglian Fiction_ (2002, Wilfrid Laurier University Press)[51][52]
-   _Moveable Margins: The Shifting Spaces of Canadian Literature_ (2005, TSAR Publications, editor)
-   _History and Imagination: Tamil Culture in the Global Context_ (2007, co-editors R. Cheran & Darshan Ambalavanar)[53][54]
-   _New Demarcations: Essays in Tamil Studies_ (2009, Brown Bear Press, co-editors R. Cheran & Darshan Ambalavanar)[55]
-   _Wilting Laughter: Three Tamil Poets_ (2009, translator)[56][57]
-   _Nedunalvaadai_ (2010)
-   _You Cannot Turn Away_ (2010, translator)[58]
-   _Ritual_ (2011, translator)[59]
-   _World Without Walls: Being Human, Being Tamil_ (2011, co-editor)[60]
-   _In Our Translated World: Contemporary Global Tamil Poetry_ (2013, TSAR Publications, editor)[61][62]
-   _A History of South Asian Writing in English_ (2014, Cambridge University Press)[63]
-   _Uprooting the Pumpkin: Selections from Sri Lankan Tamil Literature, 1950-2012_ (2016, Oxford University Press, editor)[64]
