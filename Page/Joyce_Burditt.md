JOYCE BURDITT (also as JOYCE REBETA-BURDITT) is a writer and network executive known for creating the TV series _Diagnosis: Murder_,[1][2] which ran for almost 200 episodes and TV movies. She has also been a longtime writer and producer on such TV series as _Perry Mason_, _Matlock_, and the _Father Dowling Mysteries_.[3] She wrote a best selling novel, _The Cracker Factory_, in 1977, about an alcoholic housewife, which is partly drawn from her own experience in and out of institutions.[4][5] It was made into an American TV movie of the same name.[6] This was followed by the sequel, _The Cracker Factory 2: Welcome to Women's Group_, in 2010. She wrote the humorous novel _Triplets_, in 1982, and the mystery novel _Buck Naked_, about a Los Angeles detective heroine, in 1998.[7]


Personal life

She married the writer George Burditt in 1957. She lives in Southern California. She was born in 1938 to a middle class family. She moved to California in 1969 to start her career.[8]
