GUY CHARLES ELSMORE (born 1966) is a British Anglican priest. Since July 2016, he has served as the Archdeacon of Buckingham in the Diocese of Oxford.[1][2]


Early life and education

Elsmore was born in 1966 in Sutton Coldfield, Birmingham, England.[3][4] He studied physics at the University of Edinburgh,[5] graduating with a Bachelor of Science (BSc) degree in 1988.[6] Having completed his undergraduate degree, he moved to Liverpool where he worked with the homeless.[7] He then entered Ridley Hall, Cambridge, an Open Evangelical Anglican theological college, to train for ordained ministry.[8][9] His training was completed by 1993, and that year he left the college to be ordained in the Church of England.[10]


Ordained ministry

Elsmore was ordained in the Church of England as a deacon in 1993 and as a priest in 1994.[11] He then ministered in the Diocese of Liverpool until his appointment as archdeacon.[12] From 1993 to 1998, he served his curacy a St Michael's Church, Huyton.[13] He was then Vicar of the Parish of St Basil and All Saints, Hough Green, Widnes:[14] this is a joint Church of England/Roman Catholic parish.[15][16] He was also Area Dean of Widnes between 2003 and 2005.[17]

In 2005, Elsmore was made Priest-in-Charge of the St Luke in the City Team in Liverpool.[18] This benefice contains three parish churches; St Michael's Church, Upper Pitt Street, St Bride's Church, Percy Street, and the Church of St Dunstan, Edge Hill.[19] In 2010, he was made Team Rector of the benefice.[20] From 2014 to 2016, he also served as the Area Dean of Toxteth and Wavertree.[21][22] On 2 February 2014, he was made an honorary canon of Liverpool Cathedral.[23]

In May 2016, it was announced that Elsmore would be the next Archdeacon of Buckingham in the Diocese of Oxford.[24][25] On 18 July 2016, he was welcomed and collated as archdeacon during a service at Christ Church Cathedral, Oxford.[26]

Views

In July 2013, Elsmore was elected General Secretary of Modern Church, an organisation that promotes Liberal Christianity.[27] In October 2016, he resigned as General Secretary due to his appointment as Archdeacon of Buckingham.[28] As of 2016, he is a novice of the Third Order of the Society of St Francis (TSSF).[29]


Personal life

Elsmore is married to Petra.[30] She is an Anglican priest and chaplain.[31] Together, they have five children; their youngest son is severely autistic.[32]
