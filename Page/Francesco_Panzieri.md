FRANCESCO PANZIERI is an Italian visual effects artist. He is a member of the Visual Effects Society [1], the Academy of Television Arts & Sciences[2] and the Italian Visual Effects Association (_Associazione Effetti Visivi_).[3]


Education

In 2008, Panzieri earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Film Science from the International Academy of Image Arts (_Accademia Internazionale Per Le Arti & Le Scienze Dell'Immagine_) in L'Aquila, Italy, which was founded in 1992 by Academy Award recipient for Best Achievement in Cinematography Vittorio Storaro and Gabriele Lucci. Interested in deepening his VFX knowledge in the United States of America, he then relocated to New York City, NY and enrolled himself in the one-year 3D-animation & visual effects conservatory program at the New York Film Academy.[4]


Compositing Career

Flash Film Works

In September 2009, Panzieri was hired to work at the visual effects boutique Flash Film Works in Hollywood, California; under the guidance of the studio's owner, _William Mesa_,[5] Panzieri started his career with his first assignment on the live-action web series _The Blood Factory_, a gory episodic created by screenwriter John Albo and actor & director Danny DeVito.[6] His second assignment was on the 2010 feature film Clash of the Titans, a remake of the original 1981 version.[7]

Star Wars: The Force Awakens

In 2015, Panzieri was called to join the in-house visual effects team at J.J. Abrams' production company Bad Robot Productions, to work on the feature film Star Wars: The Force Awakens.[8][9] The film broke various box office records and became, unadjusted for inflation, the highest-grossing installment in the franchise, the highest-grossing film in North America, the highest-grossing film of 2015, and the third-highest-grossing film of all time, with a worldwide gross of over $2 billion and a net profit of over $780 million.[10][11] The film also received five Academy Award nominations at the 88th Academy Awards, including Best Visual Effects[12] and garnered four nominations at the 69th British Academy Film Awards including one win for Best Special Visual Effects.[13]

Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU)

In 2017, Panzieri was called to join the in-house visual effects team at Marvel Studios, to work on the feature film Spider-Man: Homecoming.[14][15][16] Afterwards, he joined the compositing department at Digital Domain to work on the feature film Thor: Ragnarok.

In 2018, Panzieri extended his work collaboration with Digital Domain on the feature film Avengers: Infinity War[17]. The film received praise for the emotional weight of the story, as well as the visual effects and action sequences. It was the fourth film and the first superhero film to gross over $2 billion worldwide, breaking numerous box office records and becoming the highest-grossing film of 2018, as well as the fourth -highest-grossing film of all time and in the United States and Canada. The film received Academy Award and BAFTA nominations for achievements in visual effects.

In 2019, Panzieri was hired at Luma Pictures to work on the feature film Spider-Man: Far From Home.
