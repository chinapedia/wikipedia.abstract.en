The HONG KONG FILM AWARD for Best New Director is an annual Hong Kong industry award presented to a director who is considered the best of the year.


History

In 2002 the new category of _Outstanding Young Director_ was created as a way to recognise up-and-coming directors, the first award was presented to Stephen Chow for his 2001 film _Shaolin Soccer_. Two years later at the 23rd Hong Kong Film Awards the award was renamed _Best New Director_ and continues to be presented.[1]
