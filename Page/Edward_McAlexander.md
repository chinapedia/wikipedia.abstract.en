Colonel EDWARD MCALEXANDER (1833-1870) was an American Confederate veteran, physician and politician.


Early life

Edward McAlexander was born on March 2, 1833 in Alabama.[1] He graduated from LaGrange College, now the University of North Alabama.[2]


Career

McAlexander was a physician.[3] During the American Civil War of 1861-1865, he joined the Confederate States Army and served in the 26th Regiment Alabama Infantry.[4] He was promoted to the rank of Major on January 30, 1862, and as Colonel on April 9, 1865.[5]

McAlexander served as a member of the Alabama House of Representatives from 1865 to 1867.[6]


Personal life and death

McAlexander was married twice: first to Elizabeth J. Koger, and secondly to Sarah A. Koger.[7] He died on October 13, 1870 in Lauderdale County, Alabama. He was buried at the Florence Cemetery in Florence, Alabama.[8]
