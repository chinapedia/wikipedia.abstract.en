_EUCALYPTUS LATISINENSIS_, commonly known as WHITE MAHOGANY, is a eucalypt that is native to Queensland.[1]


Description

The tree typically grows to a height of and has a lignotuber.The soft, rough and finely fibrous to stringy bark has a grey or grey-brown colour and persists to the small branches. The slightly glossy or dull, green and concolorous adult leaves are coarsely textured and arranged alternately. The leaf blade is a lanceolate to falcate shape with a length of and a width of with the base tapering to the petiole and a pointed apex. It blooms around August and produces axillary unbranched inflorescences with white flowers.[2]


Taxonomy

The species was first formally described by the botanist Kenneth Hill in 1999 in the work _A taxonomic revision of the White Mahogonies, Eucalyptus series Acmenoideae (Myrtaceae)_ as published in the journal _Telopea_.[3]

_E. latisinensis_ is part of the white mahogany group as recognised by Ken Hill, others in the group include; _Eucalyptus acmenoides_, _Eucalyptus mediocris_, _Eucalyptus carnea_, _Eucalyptus apothalassica_, _Eucalyptus helidonica_, _Eucalyptus psammitica_ and _Eucalyptus umbra_.[4]
