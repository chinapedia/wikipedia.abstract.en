DENBO is a populated place in the Borough of Centerville Borough, Washington County, Pennsylvania, United States.[1] It is a coal patch town, located near the former Vesta No. 6 Mine.[2]


History

Vesta No. 6 was owned and operated by Jones and Laughlin Steel Company. It was located in the Klondike Coalfield.[3] A patch town was built to house miners. The mine ran from about 1903 until 1947. The entryway was sealed and covered over.[4]
