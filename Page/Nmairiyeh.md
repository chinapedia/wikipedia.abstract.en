NMAIRIYEH () is a village in the Nabatieh District in southern Lebanon.


History

In the 1596 tax records, it was named as a village, _Numayriyya_, in the Ottoman _nahiya_ (subdistrict) of _Sagif_ under the _liwa'_ (district) of Safad, with a population of 23 households and 6 bachelors, all Muslim. The villagers paid a fixed tax-rate of 25 % on agricultural products, such as wheat, barley, olive trees, goats and beehives, in addition to "occasional revenues" and a press for olive oil or grape syrup; a total of 2,492 akçe.[1][2]

Modern era

On July 29 2006, during the 2006 Lebanon War, Israeli war-planes killed 6 civilians, aged 8 to 56 years old. There were no Hizbollah presence in the village at the time of the attack.[3]
