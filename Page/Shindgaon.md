SHINDGAON is a village in India. It is situated in the Mawal taluka of Pune district in the state of Maharashtra. It encompasses an area of .[1]


Administration

The village is administrated by a sarpanch, an elected representative who leads a gram panchayat. At the time of the 2011 Census of India, the gram panchayat governed five villages and was based at Ambegaon.[2]


Demography

At the 2011 census, the village comprised 35 households. The population of 160 was split between 82 males and 78 females.[3]
