SCHOOLCRAFT COUNTY AIRPORT, is a county-owned public-use airport located 3 miles (5 km) northeast of the central business district of Manistique, Michigan, a city in Schoolcraft County, Michigan, United States.[1] It is included in the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) National Plan of Integrated Airport Systems for 2017–2021, in which it is categorized as a basic general aviation facility.[2]


Facilities and aircraft

Schoolcraft County Airport covers an area of 335 acres (135 ha) at an elevation of 684 feet (208 m) above mean sea level. It has two runways: 10/28 is 5,001 by 100 feet (1,524 x 30 m) with an asphalt surface with approved GPS approaches; and 1/19 is 2,501 by 50 feet (762 x 15 m) with an asphalt surface.[3]

For the 12-month period ending December 31, 2015, the airport had no recorded data regarding aircraft operations. In March 2017, there were 4 aircraft based at this airport: all 4 single-engine.[4]
