BRIGITTE GROS (1925-1985) was a French journalist and politician. She served as the mayor of Meulan-en-Yvelines and as a member of the French Senate. She was the author of several books.


Early life

Brigitte Gros was born as Brigitte Servan-Schreiber on 12 June 1925 in Saint-Germain-en-Laye, France.[1][2][3] Her father, Émile Servan-Schreiber, was a Jewish author.[4] Her mother, Denise Brésard, was Roman Catholic.

During World War II, she joined the French Resistance and served in the maquis of Ain under Léo Hamon.[5] However, she was arrested and tortured by the Gestapo on 15 August 1944.[6] During the liberation of France, she served under General Jean de Lattre de Tassigny.[7] She was a recipient of the Croix de Guerre for her service.[8][9]


Career

Gros first worked as a journalist for _Les Échos_ and _Paris-Presse_.[10] She joined _L'Express_ in 1955.[11] She publisher her first novel in 1960.[12]

Gros was elected to the city council of Meulan in 1965.[13] She served as its mayor from 1966 to 1985.[14] During her tenure, she oversaw the construction of the Paradis neighbourhood and the Henri-IV Hospital in Meulan.[15]

Gros served as a member of the French Senate from 1977 to 1985.[16] During her tenure, she worked on policies to support low-income housing known as HLM.[17] Additionally, she argued that each French family should be able to own a house.[18] Meanwhile, she authored a report in favour of the establishment of the Solidarity tax on wealth in 1980 (implemented in 1981).[19] She also voted in favour of abortion and looser divorce regulations as well as the repeal of the death penalty.[20]


Personal life, death and legacy

She married Emeric Gros; they had four children, Olivier, France, François and Catherine.[21]

Gros died on 11 March 1985 in Meulan-en-Yvelines, France.[22][23] The _Place Brigitte-Gros_, a town square, and the _Centre Brigitte Gros_, a hospital, both of which are based in Meulan, are named in her memory. Meanwhile, a ceremony to commemorate the 30th anniversary of her death was held in Meulan on 13 February 2016.[24]
