BERNARD ATTALI (born 1943) is a French business executive, political advisor and one-time novelist. He served as the chief executive officer of Air France from 1988 to 1993. He is a senior advisor to TPG Capital and Bank of America Merrill Lynch.


Early life

Bernard Attali was born on November 1, 1943 in Algiers, French Algeria (now Algeria).[1][2] His twin brother, Jacques Attali, is an economist and political advisor.[3][4] He also has a sister, Fabienne.[5] Their parents were Jewish.[6] Their father, Simon Attali, was trained as a rabbi and made a fortune in the fragrance industry.[7] The family moved to mainland France, settling in the 16th arrondissement of Paris in 1956, two years into the Algerian War.[8]

Attali took piano lessons from the age of five.[9] He graduated from Sciences Po in 1966 and the École nationale d'administration (ENA) in 1968.[10][11]


Career

Attali became an advisor to the Court of Audit in 1974; he was promoted to the rank of master advisor in 1991.[12]

Attali served as the financial director of the Club Med from 1980 to 1981. He then served as the head of DATAR from 1981 to 1984.[13] He served as the president of the Groupe des Assurances Nationales (GAN) from 1984 to 1986.[14]

Attali served as the chief executive officer of Air France from 1988 to 1993.[15] Under his leadership, he oversaw the acquisition of Union de Transports Aériens in 1989 and Union de Transports Aériens in 1992.[16]

Attali subsequently worked for Arjil & Associés Banque, an investment bank,[17] from 1993 to 1996.[18] He served as the chief executive officer of Bankers Trust France, the French subsidiary of Bankers Trust, from 1996 to 1998.[19] He served as the Vice-Chairman of Deutsche Bank Europe Investment Banking from 1999 to 2000.[20] In 2007, Attali joined TPG Capital as a senior advisor.[21][22] He is also a senior advisor to Bank of America Merrill Lynch.[23] Additionally, he serves on the board of directors of SFR.[24]

Attali is the author of two non-fiction books and a novel, _La mise en examen_.[25] In 2015, he authored a report about the future of the École Polytechnique for the French government.[26]

Attali became an Officer of the Legion of Honour on July 14, 1998.[27]
