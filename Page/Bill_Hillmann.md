Bill_Hillmann_American_Author,_Storyteller,_Journalist_and_Bull_Runner._Photo_by_Jacob_Knabb.jpg BILL HILLMANN (born 1982) is an American author, storyteller, and journalist. He is a bull-runner and former boxer.


Early life and education

Hillmann was born in Chicago in 1982,[1] and grew up on the city's North Side. He attended St. Joseph High School.[2] He has a bachelor's degree from Elmhurst College and an MFA from Columbia College Chicago.[3]


Writing and storytelling

As an author, Hillmann is the author of the novel _The Old Neighborhood_ (2014),[4][5][6][7] and the nonfiction book _Mozos: A Decade Running with the Bulls_ (2015)[8][9] which was translated and published in Spain as _Corriendo con Hemingway_ (2016).[10][11]

As a journalist, Hillmann has written for NPR as well as for publications including the _Toronto Star_, _Playboy_, _Chicago Tribune_, _Salon_, _Daily Mail_, _Los Angeles Times_, _Stuff,_ and _Washington Post_.[12][13][14][15][16]

As a storyteller, Hillmann is the creator of the Windy City Story Slam.[17][18] He also created the National Story Slam, in which ten storytellers representing different storytelling series across the country performed at the main stage of the Chicago Tribune Printer's Row Lit Fest.[19][20][21] He has told stories at storytelling series in Philadelphia,[22] Portland,[23] San Francisco,[24] and Boston,[25] and won the Boulder Story Slam in Boulder, Colorado in 2014.[26] He has produced and performed in Story Slam events in London and at the Edinburgh Fringe Festival,[27][28] has participated in Book Slam, London's literary nightclub,[29] and has told stories on NPR's _The Story_ and _Snap Judgment_.[30][31]


Boxing

Hillmann is a 2002 Chicago Golden Gloves boxing champion representing the Windy City Gym and a two time selection for the Chicago Golden Gloves International Traveling Team.[32][33][34] As a boxing journalist, Hillmann was the Chicago correspondent for _Fightnews_, and is the special boxing contributor for the _Chicago Tribune RedEye_.[35][36]


Bull-running

Hillmann is an experienced participant in running of the bulls, and has been described as "the best young bull runner from the United States".[37] He has given expert commentary on the running of the bulls for CNN,[38] _The Today Show_,[39] _CBS This Morning_,[40] BBC World Service,[41] and Esquire Network.[42] His audio essay "Running with the Bulls in Pamplona" for WBEZ Chicago Public Radio won an Edward R. Murrow Award from the Radio Television Digital News Association in 2010.[43]

He was inspired to join the tradition by Ernest Hemingway's book _The Sun Also Rises_,[44][45] and has taken part in the bull runs in Pamplona since 2005.[46][47][48] In 2011 and 2012 he wrote how-to guides on the running of the bulls for _Outside_ magazine.[49][50] By 2013 he had acquired the nickname "Buffalo Bill Hillmann", and in 2014 he co-authored the book _Fiesta: How to Survive the Bulls of Pamplona_ with Alexander Fiske-Harrison and John Hemingway.[51][52]

On July 9, 2014, a bull gored Hillmann twice in the thigh at the festival of San Fermín in Pamplona,[53][54]

but he returned to bull-running in Pamplona in 2015,[55] and published his memoir, _Mozos: A Decade Running with the Bulls_.[56] In the summer of 2016 he ran with the bulls more than 200 times in towns throughout Spain.[57][58] He has stated that his favorite running of the bulls is the oldest, in Cuéllar, a small town about two hours north of Madrid, where hundreds of horsemen guide the bulls 3 miles (5 km) to the town and into the run.[59][60][61]

On July 8, 2017, he was gored by one of the bulls from José Escolar in Pamplona.[62]
