First_page_of_first_issue_of_The_Hebrew_Standard_of_Australasia,_1_November_1895.pdf _THE AUSTRALIAN JEWISH NEWS_ is a newspaper published in Darlinghurst, Sydney, New South Wales, Australia.[1]


History

_The Hebrew Standard of Australasia_ was first published on 1 November 1895 in Sydney by founding editor Alfred Harris.[2] In 1953 John Shaiak purchased the newspaper and changed its name to _The Australian Jewish Times (AJT)_.[3][4] In 1987, Richard Pratt bought the AJT and merged it with the Melbourne-based _Australian Jewish News_. From 1990, the newspaper has been published weekly nationally as _The Australian Jewish News_.[5]


Digitisation

_The Hebrew Standard of Australasia_ has been digitised from 1895 to 1953 and is available online through Trove via the National Library of Australia.[6]
