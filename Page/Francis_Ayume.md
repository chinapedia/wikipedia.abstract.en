FRANCIS JOASH AYUME (1940 to 2004), was a Ugandan politician and lawyer. At the time of his death he was the incumbent Attorney General of Uganda.[1]


Early life and education

He was born on 18 August 1940, in Koboko District to Misaeli Onale, an elementary school teacher. Ayume attended _Nyangilia Primary School_ in Koboko for his primary schooling. He attended Busoga College Mwiri for his O-Level and A-Level studies, graduating in 1964.[2]

He was admitted to the University of Dar es Salaam, where he studied law, graduating in 1967. Later he was awarded a Diploma in Legal Practice from the Law Development Centre in Kampala, Uganda's capital city.[3]


Career

During the 1990s, Ayume entered Ugandan elective politics and was elected to Uganda's parliament to represent Koboko District. "In 2001, he was appointed Attorney General and represented Uganda in the International Court of Justice in a case where Uganda was accused of invading DR Congo and allegedly plundering its natural resources".[4] On Sunday 16 May 2004, he was involved in a fatal automobile accident at Nakasongola on the Kampala-Gulu Highway.[5]


Works

Francis Ayume authored a book "Criminal Procedure And Law in Uganda".[6] The book is incorporated in the curriculum at Makerere University School of Law.[7]
