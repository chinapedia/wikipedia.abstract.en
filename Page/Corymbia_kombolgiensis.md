_CORYMBIA KOMBOLGIENSIS_, commonly known as the SCARP GUM[1] or the PAPER-FRUITED BLOODWOOD,[2] is a member of the Corymbia genus native to the Northern Territory[3]

The tree typically grows to a height of and forms a lignotuber. The bark is grey rough, flaky and tessellated on the trunk and smooth creamy white to orange-brown sometimes powdery elsewhere.[4]
