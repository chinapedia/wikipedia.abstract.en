AMBROGIO LANDUCCI, O.S.A. (1596 – 16 February 1669) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Titular Bishop of _Porphyreon_ (1655–1669).[1][2]


Biography

Ambrogio Landucci was born in Siena, Italy and ordained a priest in the Order of Saint Augustine.[3] On 30 August 1655, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Alexander VII as Titular Bishop of _Porphyreon_.[4][5] On 12 September 1655, he was consecrated bishop by Giovanni Battista Maria Pallotta, Cardinal-Priest of San Pietro in Vincoli, with Patrizio Donati, Bishop Emeritus of Minori, and Taddeo Altini, Bishop of Civita Castellana e Orte, serving as co-consecrators.[6] He served as Titular Bishop of _Porphyreon_ until his death on 16 February 1669.[7][8]


Episcopal succession

While bishop, he was the principal consecrator of:[9]

-   Zacharie de Metz, Titular Bishop of _Tralles in Asia_ and Coadjutor Apostolic Vicariate of Batavia (1656);
-   Giuseppe Maria Sebastiani, Titular Bishop of _Hierapolis in Isauria_ and Vicar Apostolic of Malabar (1659);

and the principal co-consecrator of:[10]

-   Henri Borghi, Bishop of Alife (1658).
