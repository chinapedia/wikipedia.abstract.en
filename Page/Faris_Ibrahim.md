FARIS IBRAHIM HASSOUNA EL-BAKH (born June 4, 1998) is a Qatari weightlifter and Olympian competing in the 94 kg category until 2018 and 96 kg starting in 2018 after the International Weightlifting Federation reorganized the categories.[1]

He is currently the junior world record holder in the clean & jerk and total in the 96 kg division.


Career

Olympics

He initially placed eighth at the men's 85 kg event at the 2016 Summer Olympics,[2] but was updated to seventh[3][4] after the original bronze medalist Gabriel Sîncrăian failed a drug test in October of that year.[5]

World Championships

In 2017 he moved up to the 94 kg category and competed at the 2017 World Weightlifting Championships, winning a silver medal in the clean & jerk and placing fourth overall.[6]

The next year he competed in the 2018 World Weightlifting Championships winning a bronze medal in the clean & jerk portion in the new 96 kg category.[7] During the competition he set the junior world record in the clean and jerk.[8]

Other Competitions

He competed at the 2016 Junior World Weightlifting Championships winning a bronze medal in the total in the clean & jerk and total in the 85 kg category. The following year he competed again at the Junior World Weightlifting Championships this time winning a gold medal in the 85 kg category.[9]

In 2018 he became Junior World Champion again, this time winning a gold medal at the Junior World Weightlifting Championships in the 94 kg category. He also won the Asian Junior Championships in the 94 kg division,[10] later in 2018 he competed at the 2018 Asian Games and won the silver medal in the 94 kg category.[11] At the 5th International Qatar Cup[12][13] he swept gold in the 96 kg class,[14] setting junior world records in the clean & jerk with 225 kg and total with 397 kg.[15][16]
