Vice-Admiral WILLIAM FORTESCUE SELLS CMG (19 January 1881 – 31 March 1966) was a Royal Navy officer who became Flag Officer, Dungeness Station.


Naval career

Sells joined to Royal Navy in the late 19th century and was promoted to lieutenant on 19 January 1901.[1] He served as naval attaché at the British Embassy in Athens during the First World War for which he was appointed a Companion of the Order of St Michael and St George.[2] Promoted to captain on 31 December 1918, he became commanding officer of the depot ship HMS _Sandhurst_ in March 1919, Captain of Devonport Gunnery School in May 1923 and Captain of the cruiser HMS _Cleopatra_ in November 1925.[3] He went on to be Captain of the battleship HMS _Emperor of India_ in January 1928 and Captain of the battleship HMS _Benbow_ in May 1929, before retiring in April 1930.[4] He was recalled and served as Royal Naval Officer, Carnarvon from November 1942 and as Flag Officer at Dungeness Station from June 1944 during the Second World War.[5]
