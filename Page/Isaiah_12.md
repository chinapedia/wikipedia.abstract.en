ISAIAH 12 is the twelfth chapter of the Book of Isaiah in the Hebrew Bible or the Old Testament of the Christian Bible. This book contains the prophecies attributed to the prophet Isaiah, and is a part of the Book of the Prophets.[1] The Cambridge Bible for Schools and Colleges describes this chapter as "the lyrical epilogue to the first great division of the book (chapters 1â€“12)".[2]


Text

The original text was written in Hebrew language. This chapter is divided into 6 verses and consists of two short hymns of praise.[3]

-   Protestant theologian Heinrich Ewald argued that these songs contain little of the distinctive language used elsewhere by Isaiah and was probably a later addition to the book.[4]
