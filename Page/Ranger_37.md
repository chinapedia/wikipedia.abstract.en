The RANGER 37, also called the RANGER 1 TON, is an American sailboat, designed by Gary Mull as an International Offshore Rule one-ton racer and first built in 1973. The design is out of production.[1][2][3]

The boat was built by Ranger Yachts in the United States and 100 examples were completed.[4]


Design

The Ranger 37 is a small recreational keelboat, built predominantly of fiberglass. It has a masthead sloop rig, an internally-mounted rudder mounted on a skeg and a fixed fin keel. It displaces and carries of ballast.[5][6]
