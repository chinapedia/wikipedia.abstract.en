EDWARD BOWER CARTY (24 April 1916 â€“ May 23, 2001) was a Canadian public servant who served as the Chairman of the World Scout Committee. In 1975, Carty was awarded the 95th _Bronze Wolf_, the only distinction of the World Organization of the Scout Movement, awarded by the World Scout Committee for exceptional services to world Scouting, at the 25th World Scout Conference.[1] He was also a recipient of the Silver World Award.[2]

He received his degree in communication from McGill University in 1939.[3] He was awarded an honorary doctorate by Carleton University in 1994.[4]
