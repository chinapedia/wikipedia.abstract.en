MARINE MIROUX (born April 14, 1977) is a French architect who lives and works in Berlin.[1][2]

She was born in Fontainebleau[3] and qualified as an at the École Nationale Supérieure d'Architecture de Paris-Belleville.[4] In 2008, she founded the collective Berlin Süd Architecture with Christoph Hager.[5]

In 2008, with Christoph Hager, she won the Europan 9 competition for their project "Over the Train".[6] In 2010, in partnership with Christoph Hager and Hüller Rudaz Architekten, she received first prize for their project "The Line" in the FLOW competition sponsored by Joël Claisse Architectures, the Urban Land Institute and the businesses of the Port of Brussels. In the same year, she received the awarded by the French Académie des Beaux-Arts for the project "Better, Cheaper Helping".[7]
