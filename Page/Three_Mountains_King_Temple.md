The THREE MOUNTAINS KING TEMPLE () is a temple in Jiuru Township, Pingtung County, Taiwan.


Name

The _Three Mountains_ part of the temple name refers to the three mountains in Mainland China, which are Mount Du, Mount Ming and Mount Jing.[1]


History

The temple was constructed in 1651.[2]


Architecture

The temple is a traditional Hakka-style building listed as the 3rd category of historical building by the government. The roof of the temple is a tail-shaped structure divided into three sections. The temple building is divided into three prayer rooms and two wings.[3]
