Biography

On 16 November 1620, Tommaso Ximenes was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul V as Bishop of Fiesole.[5][6] On 22 November 1620, he was consecrated bishop by Giovanni Garzia Mellini, Cardinal-Priest of Santi Quattro Coronati, with Attilio Amalteo, Titular Archbishop of _Athenae_, and Paolo De Curtis, Bishop Emeritus of Isernia, serving as co-consecrators.[7] He served as Bishop of Fiesole until his death on 3 November 1633.[8]
