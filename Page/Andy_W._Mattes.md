ANDY W. MATTES (born c. 1963) is a German businessman. He served as the president and chief executive of Diebold, an ATM and security company until December of 2017.


Early life

Andy W. Mattes was born as ANDREAS WALTER MATTES circa 1963 in Nuremberg, Germany.[1][2][3] He was an exchange student in the United States while he was in high school.[4] He graduated with an MBA from the Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich in 1985, where he was on a Studienstiftung scholarship.[5][6]


Career

Mattes worked his way up in managerial positions at Siemens from 1995 to 2005.[7][8] He worked in China and Brazil,[9] until he was promoted to Chief Executive Officer of Siemens Communications in Boca Raton, Florida.[10] According to _The Wall Street Journal_, the company "became embroiled in a massive bribery scandal during his tenure".[11]

Mattes served as the Senior Vice President and General Manager of Enterprise Services for the Americas at Hewlett-Packard.[12] He subsequently served as the Senior Vice President of Global Strategic Partnerships at Violin Memory.[13][14] Additionally, he served as the chairman of Mphasis.[15] He also served on the boards of directors of Radvision.[16]

Mattes has served as the president and chief executive of Diebold from 2013 until December 13th 2017.[17][18] He succeeded Thomas W. Swidarski.[19][20] His initial goal was to reduce costs.[21] In 2014, he oversaw the acquisition of Cryptera, a Danish PIN pad company.[22] A year later, in 2015, he oversaw the merger of Wincor Nixdorf, a German company, with Diebold.[23] He described the merger as " adding $2.5 billion in revenues, 9,000 employees" and expanding their marketshare in Europe.[24]


Personal life

Mattes has a wife, Andrea, and three children.[25] He resides in Atherton, California.[26]
