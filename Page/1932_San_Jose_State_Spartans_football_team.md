The 1932 SAN JOSE STATE SPARTANS FOOTBALL TEAM represented State Teachers College at San Jose[1] during the 1932 college football season.

San Jose State competed in the Far Western Conference (FWC).[2] The team was led by first-year head coach Dudley DeGroot, and they played home games at Spartan Field in San Jose, California. The team finished the season undefeated and as co-champions of the FWC with a record of seven wins, no losses and two ties (7–0–2, 3–0–2 FWC). The Spartans outscored their opponents 116–27 for the season, with no team scoring more than a touchdown against the Spartans.
