MCKIBBON HOUSE is a historic mansion in Montevallo, Alabama, U.S..


History

The house was built for Robert Fulton McKibbon in 1900,[1] and designed in the Queen Anne architectural style.[2] It served as a meeting place for the Montevallo Presbyterian Church, as Fulton was a staunch Presbyterian.[3] In 1944, it was purchased by J. W. D. Galloway, only to be purchased by Melvin T. Smitherman in 1952.[4] It was purchased by Leonard Lawley in 1968.[5]


Architectural significance

The house has been listed on the Alabama Register of Landmarks and Heritage since 1978,[6] and on the National Register of Historic Places since December 31, 2001.[7]
