_RHODIOLA RHODANTHA_, common name REDPOD STONECROP[1] or QUEEN'S CROWN, is a perennial flowering plant in the family Crassulaceae.[2][3]


Distribution

_Crassulaceae_-_Rhodiola_rhodantha-001.JPEG This species is present in the United States (Arizona, Colorado, Montana, New Mexico, Utah, and Wyoming).[4] It is native to the Rocky Mountains, at an elevation up to above sea level.


Description

_Rhodiola rhodantha_ can reach a height of about . These plants have small, lanceolate and succulent leaves without petiole. They are green at the bottom of the plant whereas at the top they are reddish. The flowers are hermaphrodite, may be rose or reddish and form an inflorescence. They bloom from July to August and the seeds ripen from August to September.[5][6]
