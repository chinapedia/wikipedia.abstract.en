ELI AZUR (Hebrew: אלי עזור) is an Israeli businessman and the head of Mirkaei Tikshoret, a Tel Aviv-based media company.[1]


Career

Azur started his career as a sportswriter for _Hadashot_.[2]

Azur is the owner of advertising rights for Israel Plus.[3] With Pini Zahavi, he also runs Charlton, a company which owns broadcasting rights for football matches in Israel.[4][5]

Through Mirkaei Tikshoret, he runs radio stations in Israel and _The Jerusalem Post_.[6] Canadian investor Leonard Asper sued for a controlling stake in the Post in 2005 after failed negotiations with Azur. The lawsuit was dismissed in 2006.[7][8][9] In 2012, he launched _Sof Hashavua_, a newspaper only published on Fridays.[10] In 2014, he acquired _Maariv_, another Israeli newspaper.[11]
