MARTIN NEYLAND (29 September 1877 – 1947), sometimes known as MARTIN NALAN, was an English professional football forward who played in the Football League for Bolton Wanderers.[1][2][3]


Personal life

Neyland served in the Territorial Force with the Lancashire Fusiliers prior to the First World War.[4] Shortly after the outbreak of the war in August 1914, he enlisted in the Loyal North Lancashire Regiment.[5] He transferred to the Royal Engineers in February 1917 and held the rank of pioneer.[6]
