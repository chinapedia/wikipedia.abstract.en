RAUL RADICE (19 March 1902 - 1988) was an Italian novelist and journalist.

Born in Milan, Radice was a theatrical and film critic for various publications, including _Il Giornale d'Italia_ and _Corriere della Sera_. Also a novelist, in 1934 he won the Bagutta Prize for his novel _Vita comica di Corinna_.[1][2]

After the death of founder Silvio D'Amico, Radice served as president of the Accademia d'Arte Drammatica in Rome.[3]
