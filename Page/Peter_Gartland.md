PETER GARTLAND (14 May 1893 – June 1973) was an English professional football left back who made one appearance in the Football League for Manchester City.[1]


Personal life

Gartland served as a gunner in the Royal Field Artillery during the First World War.[2] In September 1918 he received a small shrapnel wound in the leg from a gas shell, which necessitated the leg being amputated.[3]
