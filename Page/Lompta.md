LOMPTA is a village in the commune of Galim-Tignère in the Adamawa Region of Cameroon. It lies on the left bank of the Beli River.


Population

In 1971, Lompta contained 493 inhabitants, mainly Fula people.[1]

At the time of the 2005 census, there were 500 people in the village.[2]
