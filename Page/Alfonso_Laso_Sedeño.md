ALFONSO LASO SEDEÑO (died 1607) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Archbishop (Personal Title) of Mallorca (1604–1607),[1] Archbishop of Cagliari (1596–1604),[2][3] and Bishop of Gaeta (1587–1596).[4]


Biography

On 12 October 1587, Alfonso Laso Sedeño was appointed during the papacy of Pope Sixtus V as Bishop of Gaeta.[5][6][7] On 7 February 1588, he was consecrated bishop by Enrico Caetani, Cardinal-Priest of Santa Pudenziana, with José Esteve Juan, Bishop of Vieste, and Cristóbal Senmanat y Robuster, Bishop of Orihuela, serving as co-consecrators.[8][9] On 7 February 1596, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Clement VIII as Archbishop of Cagliari.[10][11][12][13] On 1 December 1604, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Clement VIII as Archbishop (Personal Title) of Mallorca.[14][15][16] He served as Archbishop of Mallorca until his death on 21 August 1607.[17][18][19]


Episcopal succession

While bishop, he was the principal consecrator of:[20]

-   Francisco Esquivel, Archbishop of Cagliari (1605);

and the principal co-consecrator of:[21]

-   Carlos Muñoz Serrano, Bishop of Barbastro (1596).
