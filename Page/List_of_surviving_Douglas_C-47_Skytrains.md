This is a list of surviving DOUGLAS C-47 SKYTRAIN and variant aircraft, including the C-53 SKYTROOPER, C-117 and R4D.

-   LV-BEH, C-47B-15-DK c/n 15349/26794 - ex-Argentine Air Force TC-35, ex-United States Army Air Force 43-49533.[1]

Australia

United Kingdom

-   44-76384/KN353 – C-47B/Dakota IV on display at the Yorkshire Air Museum in Elvington, York.[43][44]

-   unknown – on display as a gate guardian at RAF Brize Norton; painted as _FZ262_, a participant in Operation Market Garden in World War II.[47]
-   ZA947 – C-47A/Dakota 3 During the Second World War this aircraft served entirely in Canada. It is airworthy and forms part of the BBMF. It was originally given the serial number KG661 and served with the Royal Aircraft Establishment, but in the late 1970s it was realized that the serial number KG661 had, in fact, previously been allocated to another Dakota which had been destroyed in an accident. The error was reported and in July 1979 the Dakota was allocated the ‘modern’ serial ZA947, which explains why this serial does not match the age or era of the aircraft.[48]
