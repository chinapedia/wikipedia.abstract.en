BHASKARABHATLA RAVI KUMAR is an Indian lyricist predominately works in Telugu cinema.[1][2][3] He worked for more than 125 films and penned lyrics for more than 390 songs.[4] He extensively worked with director Puri Jagannadh and music director Chakri and S. S. Thaman. He is famous for songs appealing to the masses.[5] His primary genres are mass, romantic, and melodies. He won two Santosham Film Awards in 2006 and 2008 and SIIMA award in 2013.


Personal life

Ravi Kumar was born on 5 June 1974[6] in Srikakulam[7] and brought up in Rajahmundry among five siblings.[8] In his childhood he got interested in literature from his maternal grand father Aravelli Rajagopalacharya. While in Rajahmundry he used to frequent Gouthami Library and it influenced him to venture into poetry.[9] Since his childhood he used to get inspired from the film posters. He completed B.A in Telugu and worked as a film journalist in Eenadu, Hyderabad for 10 years.[10] Later he left his job to pursue a career in film industry.

He married Lalitha who is a B.Sc graduate from Warangal on 13 August 1998. The couple has two daughters Amanta, Samhita.


Career

He started his career as a lyricist in 2000 with the film Goppinti Alludu directed by E. V. V. Satyanarayana starring Nandamuri Balakrishna.[11] He was encouraged to be a lyricist by actor Tanikella Bharani and composer Chakri.[12] He got break with the 2001 film Itlu Sravani Subramanyam directed by Puri Jagannadh which was a musical hit. He worked with Puri Jagannadh for 15 films and worked with composer Chakri for 65 films.[13] Chakri and Ravi Kumar both started their careers together.[14] He also worked with popular directors like E. V. V. Satyanarayana, Trivikram Srinivas, Harish Shankar etc.

His popular songs include _Malli Kuyave Guvva_ from Itlu Sravani Subramanyam, _Ippatikinka Naa Vayasu_ from Pokiri, _Bommanu Geesthe_ from Bommarillu, _Gallo Thelinattunde_ from Jalsa, _Pellenduke Ravanamma_ from Bumper Offer.

Awards

In 2006 he won Santosham film awards for his song in Bommarillu.[15] In 2008 he won Santosham film award for the film Neninthe. In 2013 he won SIIMA award for the song _Sir Osthara_ in Businessman.[16][17]
