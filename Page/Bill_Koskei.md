'''William "Bill" Koskei ''' (born 28 December 1947) was a Kenyan athlete who specialised in 400 metre events.


Biography

Koskei trained in Uganda early in his career and won a silver medal for his adopted country in the 400 metres hurdles at the 1970 British Commonwealth Games.[1]

His appearance at the 1972 Summer Olympics came after he switched allegiances back to his native Kenya.[2] He competed in the 400 metres hurdles where he was eliminated following his first heat, which he finished in fourth position.[3]

He won further 400 metres hurdles medals over the next two years, a silver at the 1973 All-Africa Games and a bronze at the 1974 British Commonwealth Games in Christchurch. At the 1974 British Commonwealth Games he was also a member of Kenya's gold medal winning 4 x 400 metres relay team.

He won another gold medal with the 4 x 400 metres relay team at the 1978 Commonwealth Games.
