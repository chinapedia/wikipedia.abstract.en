The SANTANA 39 is an American sailboat, that was designed by Gary Mull and W. Shad Turner and first built in 1972.[1][2][3]


Production

The boat was built by W. D. Schock Corp in the United States between 1972 and 1979, with 20 examples completed. The design is out of production.[4]


Design

The Santana 39 is a small recreational keelboat, built predominantly of fiberglass. It has a masthead sloop rig, a skeg-mounted rudder and a fixed fin keel. It displaces and carries of lead ballast. The boat has a draft of with the standard keel.[5][6]
