BELLE SHENKMAN, (June 24, 1928 - March 11, 1995) was a Ukrainian-born, London-based Canadian arts patron and fundraiser.


Early life

Belle Shenkman was born as Bell Gubler was born on June 24, 1928 in Kiev, Ukraine.[1] She emigrated to Canada with her parents when she was three months old.[2]


Career

Shenkman organized the first European performance by the National Ballet of Canada, held at the London Coliseum in 1972.[3] She became a member of the Order of Canada in 1979.[4]

Shenkman raised Â£30,000 for the Royal Winnipeg Ballet in 1982.[5] As a result, it performed at Sadler's Wells in London.[6]


Personal life, death and legacy

Shenkman, then Miss Gubler, married Harold Shenkman in 1948.[7] They had a son, William, and a daughter, Dasha, and later divorced.[8] She married Desmond Smith in 1979; he died in 1991.[9]

Shenkman died on March 11, 1995 in London, U.K.[10] The Belle Shenkman Room at the Royal Academy of Arts is named in her memory.[11]
