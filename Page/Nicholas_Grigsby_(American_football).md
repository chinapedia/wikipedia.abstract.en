NICHOLAS MARIO GRIGSBY (born July 2, 1992) is an American football linebacker who is currently a free agent. He most recently played for the Detroit Lions of the National Football League (NFL). He played college football at Pittsburgh. Grigsby was signed by the Los Angeles Rams as an undrafted free agent in 2016. He has also played for the Baltimore Ravens and New England Patriots.


Professional career

Los Angeles Rams

After going undrafted in the 2016 NFL Draft, Grigsby signed with the Los Angeles Rams on May 4, 2016.[1] On September 9, 2016, he was waived.[2] Grigsby was signed to the Rams' practice squad on September 12, 2016. On November 22, 2016, he was promoted from the practice squad to the active roster.[3]

On September 3, 2017, Grigsby was waived by the Rams.[4]

Baltimore Ravens

On September 19, 2017, Grigsby was signed to the Baltimore Ravens' practice squad.[5]

New England Patriots

On November 28, 2017, Grigsby was signed by the New England Patriots off the Ravens' practice squad.[6] He appeared in 5 games and had 5 tackles. Grigsby and the Patriots made it to Super Bowl LII, but the Patriots lost 41-33 to the Philadelphia Eagles.

On November 13, 2018, the Patriots released Grigsby.[7]

Detroit Lions

On November 17, 2018, Grigsby was signed to the Detroit Lions practice squad.[8] He was promoted to the active roster on November 26, 2018.[9] On February 15, 2019, Grigsby was released by the Lions.[10]
