At age 19 Higman matriculated on 2 June 1812 at Trinity College, Cambridge, where he graduated B.A. (3rd Wrangler) in 1816 and M.A. in 1819. In he was a Fellow of 1818 of Trinity College and from 1822 to 1834 he was a tutor there.[2] The students he tutored included Augustus De Morgan[3] and John Grote.[4] On 23 May 1820 Higman was elected F.R.S. On 28 October 1821 he was ordained an Anglican priest (at Ely).[5] In 1823 he gave the Rede Lecture in Philosophy.[6] In From 1834 to 1855 he was Rector of the Anglican Parish of Fakenham, Norfolk.[7]

On 28 March 1837 he married Anastasia Read (born 1818).
