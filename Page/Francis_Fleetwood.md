FRANCIS FLEETWOOD (1946-2015) was an American architect. He designed over 200 mansions in The Hamptons, mainly in the shingle style.


Early life

Francis Fleetwood was born on June 17, 1946 in Santiago, Chile.[1][2] His father, Harvey Fleetwood, was a banker.[3] His mother, Maria Freile, was a psychoanalyst.[4] He had a brother, Blake, and two sisters, Carmen and Charlotte.[5] The family moved to the United States in 1948, settling in New York.[6]

Fleetwood was educated at the Dalton School, the Fessenden School and the Riverdale Country School.[7][8] He graduated from Bard College with a bachelor of arts degree in 1970, and earned a master's degree in architecture from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) in 1973.[9][10]


Career

Fleetwood started his career by working for Pielstick & Roselack, an architectural firm based in Aspen, Colorado.[11][12] From 1977 to 1979, he worked for Philip Johnson in New York.[13][14]

Fleetwood founded Francis Fleetwood and Associates, an architectural firm, in 1980.[15][16] He designed over 200 mansions in The Hamptons, mainly in the shingle style.[17][18][19] Notable customers included Nicole Miller, Neil Hirsch, Gerald Clarke, Alec Baldwin,[20] as well as Lauren Bacall, Calvin Klein, Paul McCartney,[21] and George Stephanopoulos.[22] By 2001, a mansion he designed for commodities trader David Campbell on Georgica Pond was listed as one of the most expensive properties in the United States, at $50 million;[23] it sold for $45 million in 2004.[24] While most of his work was in The Hamptons, he also designed properties in Florida and Connecticut.[25]

Fleetwood was a member of the American Institute of Architects (AIA).[26]


Personal life and death

Fleetwood married Stephanie Turner. He had a daughter, Catherine Newsome, and a stepson, Michael Orhan.[27] He resided in Amagansett, New York, where he was a member of the Devon Yacht Club,[28] and Wellington, Florida,[29] where he died on May 8, 2015.[30][31]
