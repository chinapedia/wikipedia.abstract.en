SAM THOMSON (born 23 January 1994) is a Scottish rugby union player. He currently plays in Australia for the Warringah Rats in the Shute Shield, and NSW Country Eagles in the NRC. His usual position is at lock.


Early life and career

Born in in Edinburgh, Scotland, Thomson was raised in Glasgow where he attended Kelvinside Academy. He represented Glasgow District at U15, U16 and U17 age grades.[1]

He moved south to England where he played with Lydney RFC and while attending the University of Worcester,[2] utilising the training link that the university had with Worcester Warriors. He played for the Scottish Exiles U18s while based in England.

He then moved to New Zealand for six months where he played for Southbridge Rugby Club, an amateur club in the Canterbury Rugby Football Union, famous for being Dan Carter's first club.[3]


Rugby career

France

In the 2015–16 season Thomson signed for Perpignan to play in their academy. He played in the Espoir competition for the French side but never played in the Pro D2.[4]

Scotland

Thomson enrolled in the BT Sport Scottish Rugby Academy in 2016–17 as a Stage 3 player.[5] Stage 3 players are assigned to a professional club and Thomson has been assigned to Glasgow Warriors for the 2016–17 season.[6] When not in Warriors duty, he played for Glasgow Hawks.[7]

Thomson made his debut for the Warriors in the pre-season match against Canada 'A' on the 30 August 2016.[8] He made his Pro12 debut for the Warriors against Ulster on the 23 September 2016 when he replaced Tjiuee Uanivi.[9] After sustaining a shoulder injury in late 2016 that ended his season, he moved to Australia.[10]

Australia

Joining the Warringah Rats on Sydney's Northern Beaches,[11] Thomson played in the club's Shute Shield premiership-winning team of 2017. He played in the National Rugby Championship for the Greater Sydney Rams in 2017[12] and the Sydney Rays in 2018.[13]


Life outside rugby

Thomson studied an outdoor leadership-based degree at Worcester University.[14] He is a qualified canoe/kayak instructor, has passed mountain leader training and is white water safety and rescue qualified.[15] After his rugby career finishes, Sam Thomson wants to join his father Bill Thomson—who played rugby for Scottish colleges[16]—into the events, hospitality and tourism industry.[17]
