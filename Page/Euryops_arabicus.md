_EURYOPS ARABICUS_ is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family that grows in the form of a bush. It is found on the Arabian Peninsula, Socotra, Somalia and Djibouti. Its natural habitat is subtropical or tropical dry shrubland.


Description

Euryops_arabicus_Djibouti.jpg _Euryops arabicus_ is a small shrub up to about tall. The narrow lanceolate leaves are leathery and are concentrated towards the tips of the branches.[1]


Distribution and habitat

_Euryops arabicus_ is found in the Arabian Peninsula where it grows in the cloud forest on north-facing slopes in montane woodland,[2] and it is also found on the higher parts of Socotra, in Somalia and Djibouti.[3]


Ecology

In Saudi Arabia it grows alongside _Juniperus procera_, draped with the lichen _Usnea articulata_.[4] It is unpalatable and may flourish in places where other shrubs are overgrazed.[5]
