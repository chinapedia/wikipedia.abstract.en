Simpson Desert}} __NOTOC__ SIMPSON DESERT is a locality in the Australian state of South Australia located about north of the capital city of Adelaide and which is located within the desert of the same name.[1]

The locality was established on 26 April 2013 in respect to “the long established local name” which is derived from the desert of the same name.[2][3]

The principal land use within the locality is conservation with the full extent of the locality being occupied by the following protected areas - the Munga-Thirri—Simpson Desert Conservation Park and the Munga-Thirri–Simpson Desert Regional Reserve.[4][5]

Simpson Desert is located within the federal division of Grey, the state electoral district of Stuart, the Pastoral Unincorporated Area of South Australia and the state’s Far North region.[6][7][8][9]
