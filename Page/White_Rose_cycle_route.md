The WHITE ROSE CYCLE ROUTE in Yorkshire, England, part of the National Cycle Network (NCN), was opened by Sustrans in 1998. It linked Middlesbrough with the City of Kingston upon Hull via the North York Moors, the Vale of York, the Yorkshire Wolds, a distance of [1][2] and in some descriptions continued to Hornsea on the coast ().[3][4] A map and guide for the route were published in 1999[5] and 2000.[6]

The route is no longer branded as the White Rose Route.
