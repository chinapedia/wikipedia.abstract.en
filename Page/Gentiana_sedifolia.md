_GENTIANA SEDIFOLIA_ is a species of plant in the family Gentianaceae. It can be found from Costa Rica to northern Chile.[1][2]


Description

Small herbs with narrow, opposite leaves; leaves up to 0.7 cm long.[3] Small funnel shaped flowers up to 1 cm in diameter, pale blue or violet, with a yellow, white or pale yellow center.[4] Flowers close at night or during cold and cloudy days.[5]


Distribution and habitat

Bogs at high elevation grasslands (puna and páramo),[6][7][8] from Costa Rica to northern Chile.[9][10] It is present at protected areas such as Cajas National Park,[11] Huascarán National Park[12] and Junín National Reserve.[13]


Vernacular names

Genciana (Colombia);[14] pinjachi (Bolivia).[15]
