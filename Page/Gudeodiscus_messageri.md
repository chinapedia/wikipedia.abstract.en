_GUDEODISCUS MESSAGERI_ is a species of air-breathing land snail, a terrestrial pulmonate gastropod mollusk in the family Plectopylidae.


Subspecies

_Gudeodiscus messageri_ include two subspecies:

-   _Gudeodiscus messageri messageri_ (Gude, 1909)
-   _Gudeodiscus messageri raheemi_ Páll-Gergely & Hunyadi, 2015[1]


Distribution

Gudeodiscus_map.jpg''. The small circles in Laos indicates the 2016 localities of _Gudeodiscus messageri raheemi_.]] The distribution of _Gudeodiscus messageri messageri_ is known from Vietnam along the Chinese border (Yunnan Province).[2]

The distribution of _Gudeodiscus messageri raheemi_ is known from Vietnam from Ninh Bình Province, Thanh Hóa Province, Sơn La Province, Hòa Bình Province and Nghệ An Province[3] and from Luang Prabang Province in north Laos and Vientiane Province in northwest Laos.[4] _Gudeodiscus messageri raheemi_ in Laos is the westernmost record of the genus _Gudeodiscus_.[5]


Description

The shell of _Gudeodiscus messageri_ is small to medium-sized, with slightly elevated spire.[6] The dorsal surface is somewhat domed.[7] The aperture is almost circular.[8] The apertural fold is missing.[9] Callus is rather blunt and only slightly curved.[10] Parietal wall has two lamellae (the anterior lamella may be dissolved into small denticles).[11] Lower parietal plica is free or connected to the anterior lamella.[12] Palatal plicae are oblique, or depressed Z-shaped, usually in contact with each other.[13]

Gudeodiscus messageri messageri

The most important shell characters for identification of _Gudeodiscus messageri messageri_ include: slightly elevated spire, callus is not angled in the middle, apertural fold is always missing.[14] Other characteristics include: anterior lamella is normal (it is not dissolved into small denticles).[15] Lower parietal plica does not extend beyond the anterior lamella in the anterior direction.[16] The width of the shell is 12.75–18.5 mm.[17]

Scale bar is 5 mm.]] The radula of _Gudeodiscus messageri raheemi_ has 8 lateral teeth and 16 marginal teeth.[19] The size of the central tooth is as large as or larger than the ectocone of the first lateral.[20] The shape of the first lateral is rhomboid and pointed.[21] Marginal teeth are tricuspid with rather sharp inner cusp and deep incision between the cusps.[22]

Embryos were recorded to be present in the uterus.[23] There were not recorded calcareous granules on the internal surface of penis.[24]


Ecology

Gudeodiscus_messageri_raheemi_habitat.jpg It is a ground-dwelling species as all other plectopylid snails in Vietnam.[25] It lives in primary forests and in old secondary broad-leaved forests.[26] It can be found in the humid microenvironments under leaves, logs, limestone rocks and in black soil accumulated inside limestone pockets.[27]

It co-occur with other plectopylids in Vietnam: with _Gudeodiscus phlyarius_ and with _Gudeodiscus hemmeni_.[28] It also co-occur with clausiliid _Garnieria mouhoti_, that is also associated with the moderate humidity of broad-leaved forests.[29]
