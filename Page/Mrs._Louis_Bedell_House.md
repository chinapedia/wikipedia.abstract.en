MRS. LOUIS BEDELL HOUSE, also known as WHITEHALL, is a historic home located at Grandin, Carter County, Missouri. It was built between 1888 and 1909 by the Missouri Lumber and Mining Company. It is a two-story, vernacular frame dwelling with a gable roof and weatherboard siding and one-story wing. The building contains 12 rooms. It features a wraparound porch with an enclosed railing. It housed a boarding house for women.[1]

It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1980.[2]
