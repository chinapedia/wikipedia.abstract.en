SHLOMO NIAZOV (שלמה ניאזוב; born May 12, 1962) is an Israeli former Olympic boxer.[1]

Niazov is Jewish, and was born in Tel Aviv, Israel.[2][3]


Boxing career

When Niazov competed in the Olympics, he was 5-7.5 (172 cm) tall, and weighed 132 lbs (60 kg).[4] He is left-handed.[5]

Niazov competed for Israel at the 1984 Summer Olympics in Los Angeles, at the age of 22, in Boxing--Men's Lightweight, and came in tied for 33rd.[6] Niazov is the first Israeli Olympic boxer.[7]

In 1985 and 1986, Niazov fought five professional matches as a super lightweight.[8][9] He won four of them, all by knockout.[10]


Coaching career

In 2000, he and Tal Niazov founded the Israel Professional Boxing Organization (IPBO) in Raanana, Israel.[11]

They operate Combined Martial Arts (CMA), which runs 12 gyms in Israel where some 500 people come to train in all kinds of martial arts and boxing.[12] Their best students as of 2007 were Elad Shmouel, Ran Nakash, and Hagar Finer.[13]
