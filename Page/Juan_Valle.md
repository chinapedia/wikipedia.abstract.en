JUAN VALLE or JUAN DEL VALLE (died 1563) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as the first Bishop of Popayán (1546–1563).[1]


Biography

Juan del Valle was born in Spain. On 27 Aug 1546, Juan Valle was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul III as Bishop of Popayán.[2][3] In 1547, he was consecrated bishop in Spain.[4] He served as Bishop of Popayán until his death in 1563 in France.[5][6]
