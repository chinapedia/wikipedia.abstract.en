ALBERT EDWARD CURLY DENYER (9 April 1893 – 1969) was an English professional football forward who made over 320 appearances in the Football League for Swindon Town.[1][2][3][4]


Personal life

Denyer's brother Frank and son Bertie were also footballers.[5][6] Denyer served as a sergeant in the Royal Fusiliers (City of London Regiment) during the First World War and was severely wounded, losing half of his intestines.[7][8] After his retirement from football he became landlord of the Running Horse pub in Swindon.[9]
