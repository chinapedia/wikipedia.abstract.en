MICHAEL "MITCH" DARLING (born 3 July 1988) is an Irish field hockey player who plays as a forward for Three Rock Rovers.

He played a total of 183 matches for the Irish national team and he scored 51 goals in those matches.[1] He made his debut in 2006 and announced his retirement from the national team in 2019.[2] He competed for the Ireland men's national field hockey team at the 2016 Summer Olympics.[3]


Club career

Mitch played in Ireland for Three Rock rovers until 2009, when he transferred to KHC Leuven in Belgium. He only played there for one season; in 2010 he moved to the Netherlands to play for HC Den Bosch.[4] After one year with Den Bosch, he went to another Dutch club, SCHC.[5] After his stint at SCHC, he returned to Ireland to play for Three Rock Rovers again. In 2015, he returned to the Netherlands to play for HC Rotterdam. After one year with Rotterdam, he returned to Three rock Rovers.[6]
