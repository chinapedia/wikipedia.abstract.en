_RHOPALOCARPUS UNDULATUS_ is a tree in the family Sphaerosepalaceae. It is endemic to Madagascar.


Distribution and habitat

_Rhopalocarpus undulatus_ is known only from eight locations in the northern regions of Diana and Sava. It is present and protected in the Ankarana Special Reserve.[1][2] Its habitat is dry deciduous forests from to altitude.[3]


Threats

_Rhopalocarpus undulatus_ is threatened by shifting patterns of agriculture. Because the species is used as timber, subsistence harvesting is also a threat. Wildfires also threaten the species.[4]
