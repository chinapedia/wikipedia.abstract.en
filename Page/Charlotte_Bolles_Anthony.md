CHARLOTTE BOLLES ANTHONY (August 18, 1841 â€“ July 8, 1877), also known as Lottie B. Anthony, was one of the 14 women arrested with Susan B. Anthony after they illegally voted in Rochester, N.Y. on November 5, 1872.[1][2]

"Lottie" as she was known, began teaching at age 14. She also performed in concerts for local charities. In 1866, she married Daniel B. Anthony, a third cousin of Susan B. Anthony. The couple had four children.[3] Southern University's College Administration Building was named Lottie Anthony Hall in her honor.[4]
