BARTOLOMEO VISCONTI or BARTOLOMEO AICARDI VISCONTI (died 1457) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Novara (1429â€“1457).[1][2]


Biography

Bartolomeo Visconti was born in 1402.[3][4] On 4 November 1429, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Martin V as Bishop of Novara.[5][6][7][8] He served as Bishop of Novara until his death on 28 April 1457.[9][10][11][12]

While bishop, he was the principal co-consecrator of Carlo Gabriele Sforza, Archbishop of Milan (1454).[13]
