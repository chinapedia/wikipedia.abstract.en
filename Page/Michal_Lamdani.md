MICHAL LAMDANI (; born December 5, 1944), also MICHAL LAMDANI-COHEN, is and Israeli former Olympic high jumper, eight-time Israeli champion, and former Maccabiah Games gold medal winner.[1]

She was born in Israel, and is Jewish.[2]


High jumping career

Lamdani was the Israeli Women's high jump champion in 1960-66, and in 1968 (as Michal Cohen), with a best jump of 3.65 metres.[3]

Lamdani competed for Israel at the 1964 Summer Olympics in Tokyo, Japan, at the age of 19.[4] In the Women's High Jump she tied for 19th out of 26 competitors, with a best height cleared of 1.65 metres.[5] When she competed in the Olympics, she was 5-7.5 (172 cm) tall and weighed 141 lbs (64 kg).[6]

She competed for Israel at the 1965 Maccabiah Games, winning a gold medal in the high jump.[7][8]
