MUHAJBIB, Mhaibib, () is a small village in the Marjeyoun District in southern Lebanon.


Name

E. H. Palmer wrote that the name _Neby Muheibîb_ meant "the prophet Muheibîb", "beloved".[1]


History

In 1881, the Palestine Exploration Fund's _Survey of Western Palestine_ (SWP) described _Neby Muheibib_ as: "a small village round the stone _Neby_, containing about seventy Moslems, situated on top of ridge, with olives and arable land; there are two cisterns in the village."[2]
