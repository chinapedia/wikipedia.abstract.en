FEDA CHURCH () is a parish church in Kvinesdal municipality in Vest-Agder county, Norway. It is located in the village of Feda, on the shore of the Fedafjorden. The church is part of the Feda parish in the Lister deanery in the Diocese of Agder og Telemark. There has been a church in Feda for centuries. The oldest known record of a church in Feda dates back to 1302, but the church may have existed prior to that time. The present, white, wooden church was built in 1802 and it seats about 300 people.[1][2][3]
