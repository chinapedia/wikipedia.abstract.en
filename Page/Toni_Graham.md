TONI GRAHAM (born 1945) is an American fiction writer. She is a Professor of English at Oklahoma State University; she also serves as Editor and Fiction Editor for _The Cimarron Review_.


Biography

Graham earned an MFA in fiction writing from San Francisco State University.[1] She has taught fiction writing at Oklahoma State University since 2000 and is Professor of English/Creative Writing.[2] She has served as Editor for the Cimarron Review since 2011 and as Fiction Editor from 2000 to the present.

Before being appointed to the faculty at Oklahoma State University, Graham taught creative writing at a number of California universities, including University of San Francisco; Santa Clara University; University of California, Santa Cruz; and Chico State.


Books

-   _The Suicide Club: Stories_, University of Georgia Press, (2015) [3]
-   _Waiting for Elvis_, Leapfrog Press, (2005) [4][5]
-   _The Daiquiri Girls_, University of Massachusetts Press, (1998) [6][7][8]


Awards

-   Regents Distinguished Teaching Award, 2016[9]
-   Flannery O'Connor Award for Short Fiction, 2014, for _The Suicide Club_.[10]
-   John Gardner Book Award, 2006, for _Waiting for Elvis_[11]
-   Grace Paley Award for Short Fiction, 1997, for _The Daiquiri Girls_[12]
