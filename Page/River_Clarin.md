The RIVER CLARIN ([1]) is a fast-flowing river in Ireland, flowing through southern County Galway.[2]


Course

Clarin_River_bursts_its_banks.jpg]] The River Clarin rises north of Attymon and snakes westwards, turning southwards through Athenry. Athenry Castle was built at a fording point on the river. It flows southwestwards and passes under the N18 at Clarinbridge and enters Dunbulcaun Bay.[3][4]


Wildlife

Fish species include trout, salmon, lamprey and eel.[5]
