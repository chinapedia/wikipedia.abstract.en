ALFONSO DAL POZZO FARNESE or THOMAS POZZI (1582 – 25 August, 1626) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Borgo San Donnino (1620–1626).[1]


Biography

Alfonso Pozzi was born in Piacenza, Italy in 1582.[2] On 30 March 1620, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul V as Bishop of Borgo San Donnino.[3][4] On 5 April 1620, he was consecrated bishop by Roberto Ubaldini, Bishop of Montepulciano, with Galeazzo Sanvitale, Archbishop Emeritus of Bari-Canosa, with Orazio Mattei, Bishop of Gerace, serving as co-consecrators.[5] He served as Borgo San Donnino until his death on 25 August 1626.[6][7]
