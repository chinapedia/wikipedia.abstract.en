PIERRE BÉNICHOU (born 1 March 1938) is a French journalist.


Early life

Pierre Bénichou was born on 1 March 1938 in Oran, French Algeria.[1] His father, André Bénichou, a philosophy professor who opened a private school after he was dismissed from his job for being Jewish in 1941, in the midst of World War II.[2] One of his father's pupils was author Albert Camus.[3] His paternal uncle, Paul Bénichou, was a historian.[4] His paternal grandparents were practising Jews.[5] He moved to Paris, France, in 1949.[6]


Career

Bénichou is a journalist.[7] He started his career as an intern for _France Soir_.[8] He joined _France Dimanche_ in 1956.[9] He became a reporter for _Jours de France_ in 1961.[10] He became the editor-in-chief of _Le Nouvel Observateur_, another magazine, in 1966.[11] He subsequently joined _Les Grosses Têtes_, a radio programme.[12] He was also a contributor to _Vivement Dimanche_, a television programme hosted by Michel Drucker.[13]

Bénichou became a Commander of the Legion of Honour on 25 March 2016.[14]


Personal life

Bénichou was married to Alix Dufaure; she died in 2012.[15] Actor Vincent Lindon is his stepson by his widow's first marriage.[16]
