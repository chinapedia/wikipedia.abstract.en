The BULLNOSE RAY (_Myliobatis freminvillii_) is an eagle ray, which is widely distributed in the western Atlantic. It is found at depth above in coastal waters from Cape Cod down to Argentina, but is absent from parts of the western central Atlantic. It reaches a maximum size of in disc width and gives birth to six young per litter. It is often confused with the southern eagle ray (_M. goodei_).[1]
