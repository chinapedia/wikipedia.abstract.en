AADCHIT, Atshis, () is a village in the Marjeyoun District in South Lebanon.


Name

According to E. H. Palmer, the name _Atshis_ comes from a personal name.[1]


History

In 1881, the Palestine Exploration Fund's _Survey of Western Palestine_ (SWP) described it: "a small village, built of mud and stone, containing about 100 Metawileh, situated on a low ridge surrounded by small gardens and olives. The water supply is from four rock-cut cisterns."[2]
