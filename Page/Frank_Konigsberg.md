Early life

Frank Konigsberg was born on March 10, 1933.[1][2] He graduated from Yale University and Yale Law School.[3][4]


Career

Konigsberg started his career as a lawyer for CBS.[5][6] He later worked for the International Famous Agency.[7][8] He was Bing Crosby's agent.[9] In 1975, he joined ICM Partners and became its second largest shareholder after Marvin Josephson.[10] Meanwhile, he also founded Konigsberg Co., merged it with Telepictures Productions in 1983 and subsequently served as the president of Telepictures until its merger with Lorimar in 1986.[11] With producer Larry Sanitsky, he co-founded Konigsberg Sanitsky, a television production company.[12]

Konisberg worked as a producer on _Breaking Away_, _Rituals_, _The Tommyknockers_, _Gene Kelly: An American in Pasadena_, _Bing Crosby: His Life and Legend_, _Charles and Diana: Unhappily Ever After_, _Jesus_, _William & Kate_, _Ellis Island_, _Ben Hur_ and _Titanic_.[13] He received nine Emmy Award nominations for his work on _The Guyana Tragedy_, _The Last Don_, _The Oldest Living Confederate Widow Tells All_ and _Children of the Dust_.[14][15]


Personal life and death

Konigsberg had a wife, Susanne.[16] He died on November 12, 2016.[17][18]
