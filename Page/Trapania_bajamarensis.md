_TRAPANIA BAJAMARENSIS_ is a species of sea slug, a dorid nudibranch, a marine gastropod mollusc in the family Goniodorididae''.[1]


Distribution

This species was described from the intertidal region at Bajamar, La Laguna Tenerife with additional specimens from Tenerife and Lanzarote.[2] It was previously reported as _Trapania luquei_, a species from the Cape Verde Islands.[3]


Description

This goniodorid nudibranch is translucent white with an irregular pattern of pale brown forming patches on the body. There are yellow spots on the head, the body, the tail and on the rhinophores and gills. There is white opaque pigment on the head and tail and on the appendages.[4]


Ecology

_Trapania bajamarensis_ probably feeds on Entoprocta, which often grow on sponges and other living substrata.
