QAAQAAIT AL JISR () is a village in the Nabatieh District in southern Lebanon.


History

In the 1596 tax records, it was named as a village, _Qa'qayit an-Nahr_, in the Ottoman _nahiya_ (subdistrict) of _Sagif_ under the _liwa'_ (district) of Safad, with a population of 40 households, all Muslim. The villagers paid a fixed tax-rate of 25 % on agricultural products, such as wheat, barley, fruit trees, goats and beehives, in addition to "occasional revenues" and a press for olive oil or grape syrup; a total of 3,695 akçe.[1][2]
