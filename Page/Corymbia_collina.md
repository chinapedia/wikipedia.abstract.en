_CORYMBIA COLLINA_, commonly known as the SILVER-LEAVED BLOODWOOD,[1] or the FROSTED BLOODWOOD,[2] is a tree member of the genus _Corymbia_ native to Western Australia.[3]

The tree typically grows to a height of . It has rough fibrous and flaky bark and produces cream-white flowers from April to June.
