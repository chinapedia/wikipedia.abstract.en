JOCELYN GOLDFEIN is an American technology executive and investor. She is the managing director and a general partner at venture capital firm Zetta Venture Partners. Previously she was a director of engineering at Facebook and vice president of engineering at VMware.[1]


Biography

Goldfein attended Stanford University and graduated with a bachelor's degree in computer science in 1997.[2] After college, Goldfein started her career at Trilogy.[3] Later, she spent seven years at VMware, eventually becoming vice president of engineering.[4] While at VMware, she worked on their core virtualization offering and began their desktop business.[5] She moved on to become director of engineering at Facebook where her teams worked on several features, including news feed and search.[6] She is the managing director and a general partner at venture capital firm Zetta Venture Partners, as well as a guest lecturer at Stanford University, angel investor, and advisor.[7][8] She is a member of the board of Harvey Mudd College.[9]

She spoke at the Grace Hopper Celebration of Women in Computing in 2012,[10] and delivered a keynote address at Women 2.0's HowTo conference in 2014.[11] In 2017, she was named to _Business Insider's_ list of "43 Most Powerful Female Engineers."[12]
