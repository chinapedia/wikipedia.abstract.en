PAOLO DE CURTIS, C.R. (died 1629) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Isernia (1600–1606) and Bishop of Ravello (1591–1600).[1]


Biography

Paolo De Curtis was ordained a priest in the Congregation of Clerics Regular of the Divine Providence.[2] On 26 April 1591, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Gregory XIV as Bishop of Ravello.[3][4] On 15 March 1600, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Clement VIII as Bishop of Isernia.[5][6] He served as Bishop of Isernia until his resignation in 1606.[7] He died in 1629 in Rome, Italy.[8]
