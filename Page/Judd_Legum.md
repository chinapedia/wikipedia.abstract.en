JUDD LEGUM is an American journalist and political staffer. He was the founder and editor-in-chief of _ThinkProgress_, a news site based at the Center for American Progress.


Early life

Legum graduated from Georgetown Law School in 2003.[1]


Career

Legum founded _ThinkProgress_ in 2005,[2] running it for two years before leaving in 2007 to join Hillary Clinton's presidential campaign as research director.[3] Following the 2008 campaign, he practiced law in Maryland before returning to _ThinkProgress_ in 2011, and became the site's editor-in-chief in May 2012.[4] Under his supervision, the site grew up to a 40-person newsroom that earned 10 million unique visitors a month.[5]

Legum has drawn notice for reporting and commentary on a range of political topics, including the 2016 presidential campaign,[6] campaign finance,[7] the legacy of Martin Luther King in contemporary politics,[8] and the media's role in politics.[9]

In 2018, Legum announced he was leaving _ThinkProgress_ to develop an independent newsletter, to be published through Substack.[10] Legum joins Matt Taibbi and Daniel Ortberg as early participants in the company's publishing model.[11] Legum's newsletter, called "Popular Information", is Substack's first politically-focused publication. It launched July 23, 2018.[12][13]
