MICHEL FIELD (born 1954) is a French journalist, television presenter, philosopher and novelist. He is the author of several novels. He serves as the political director of France Télévisions.


Early life

Michel Field was born as MICHEL FELDSCHUH on July 17, 1954 in Saint-Saturnin-lès-Apt, Vaucluse, France.[1][2][3] His father, Erwin Feldschuh, was an Austrian Jew who emigrated to France.[4]

Field was educated at the Lycée Claude Bernard, the Lycée Balzac, and the Lycée Condorcet.[5] He joined the Revolutionary Communist League at the age of 14, and he was expelled from his school because of his activism.[6] He graduated from Paris West University Nanterre La Défense.[7] He earned the CAPES and the agrégation in philosophy.[8]


Career

Field started his career as a philosophy teacher in Douai from 1979 to 1982, and in Versailles from 1982 to 1993.[9]

Field is a journalist and television presenter.[10] He became a co-presenter of _Panomara_, a radio programme on France Culture.[11] He was a contributor to _Les Nouvelles littéraires_ from 1984 to 1985.[12] In 1992, he became a co-presenter on _Ciel mon mardi!_.[13] He subsequently presented _Le Cercle de minuit_ and _Ça balance à Paris_,[14] followed by _Au Field de la nuit_ on TF1 and _Ring_ on LCI.[15] He has been a co-presenter of _Médiapolis_ alongside Olivier Duhamel on Europe 1 since 2007.[16] He later served as the head of France 5.[17] Since December 2015, he has served as the political director of France Télévisions.[18]

Field is the author of several novels and non-fiction books.
