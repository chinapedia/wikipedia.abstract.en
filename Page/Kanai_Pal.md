KANAI PAL was an Indian Trotskyist politician. A labour leader active in Santipur, West Bengal, he represented the area in the state legislature 1962–1969.


Independence struggle

As a politician, Pal was known to be self-sacrificing and sincere.[1] He was jailed a number of times.[2] Pal took part in the struggle for Indian independence and was convicted during the 1942 Quit India movement.[3] He played a leading role in sabotage action group in Nadia District.[4] In one confrontation during the August Revolution his spinal cord was badly injured.[5] He later lost a finger in police lathi charge in protest for right for refugees from the Partition of India.[6]


1952 elections and break with the RCPI

At the time of the 1951–1952 elections, Pal was a member of the Tagore faction of the Revolutionary Communist Party of India.[7] He donated all his personal belongings, estimated at 15,000 Indian rupees, to the party.[8] Pal contested the Santipur constituency in the 1952 West Bengal Legislative Assembly election on a RCPI ticket and finished in second place with 4,564 votes (21.43% of the votes in the constituency).[9] He finished in fourth place with 2,920 votes (6.9%).[10] However, Pal and his followers split away from the Tagore faction of RCPI in 1953.[11] Pal was disappointed with the lack of support from the party during his election campaign.[12] Pal's Santipur-based faction merged into the United Marxist League in 1954, which soon renamed itself as the Communist League.[13]

Pal again contested the Santipur seat in the 1957 West Bengal Legislative Assembly election, this time on an independent ticket.[14] He finished in second place with 13,470 votes (36.78%).[15] In 1958 the Communist League merged into the Revolutionary Workers Party.[16] The RWP merged into the Dasgupta-led RCPI in 1960.[17]


Legislator and prisoner

Pal won the Santipur seat in the West Bengal Legislative Assembly in the 1962 and 1967 elections.[18] In 1962 he ran as an independent.[19] With 26,553 votes (53.80%) he defeated the incumbent Indian National Congress legislator Haridas De.[20] Soon after being elected, on November 6, 1962 he was jailed under the West Bengal Security Act.[21][22] He was again arrested in 1965, under the Defense of India Rules.[23] In December 1965 the Fourth International Congress elected Pal, along with other jailed Trotskyist leaders as member of its 'honorary presidium'.[24]

In 1967 he contested on a Communist Party of India (Marxist) ticket.[25] He retained the seat, obtaining 20,695 votes (47.63%).[26]


Later years

In the 1969 West Bengal Legislative Assembly election Pal ran as an independent in Santipur, against the official RCPI candidate M. Mokshed Ali.[27] He finished in third place with 3289 votes (7.15%).[28]

In the 1971 West Bengal Legislative Assembly election Pal ran as an independent in Santipur, against the official RCPI candidate Bimalananda Mukherjee.[29] He finished in fourth place with 2,920 votes (6.9%).[30] In 1972 he played a role building the Association for Protection of Democratic Rights in Nadia District.[31][32]

Pal died, having suffered from kidney failure, anemia, high blood pressure and paralysis.[33]
