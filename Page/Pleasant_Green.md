PLEASANT GREEN, also known as the ANDREWS-CHESNUTT HOUSE and WINSTON WALKER HOUSE, is a historic home located near Pilot Grove, Cooper County, Missouri. It was built about 1825, and is a two-story, five bay, Classic Revival style brick dwelling with a two-story wood frame addition. It features a front portico supported by six columns. The house also has a 1 1/2-story brick section and one-story kitchen wing. Also on the property are the contributing smokehouse, a slave structure, and hexagonal wood-frame barn (c. 1900). It was the home of journalist Stanley Andrews (1894-1994).[1]

It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1977.[2]
