Early life

He was born in 1877 in Kensington, London,[1] the son of Sidney Rawlins Chesterton and Katherine Eleanor Chesterton.[2] His first cousin was the author G. K. Chesterton.[3]


Career

Chesterton designed the entire terrace of 12-54 Hornton Street, now Grade II listed, and built from 1903.[4]

Chesterton served in the First World War, as a second lieutenant with the Royal Field Artillery, and died on 11 November 1916, aged 39, in the Battle of the Somme. He is buried at Grove Town Cemetery, Meaulte.[5]


Personal life

Chesterton was married to Nora Chesterton, and they lived at 28 Warwick Gardens, Kensington, London,[6] and later at Scarsdale Villas, Kensington.[7]

He was the father of Sir Oliver Chesterton, fifth-generation head of the family's estate agency business, and chairman of the Woolwich Building Society.[8]
