YELL, also known as Yell UK, is the subsidiary in the United Kingdom of global media organization, hibu. Operating in the online marketing space, as of , Yell has created over 54,000 websites and managed 20,000 PPC campaigns for customers in the United Kingdom.[1][2] Yell has been publishing yellow pages since 1966, originally as a portion of General Post Office, and launched its Yell.com website in January 1996.


History

General Post Office first included Yellow Pages in its telephone directory for Brighton of 1966.[3] The General Post Office expanded its Yellow Pages throughout the United Kingdom in 1973.[4]

In January 1996, Yell.com was launched as a local search engine for businesses in the United Kingdom.[5][6] Yell announced a demerger from its parent company BT in January 2001.[7] In May 2001, Yell was officially sold to venture capitalists Apax Partners and Hicks, Muse, Tate & Furst for Â£2.1 billion.[8][9]

In April 2005, it was announced that Yell was partnering with Google to make local classified content available through Google's search engine.[10] Yell integrated Apptus technology into its search engine to broaden is search capacity and enhance site development in January 2008.[11] In May 2009, Yell expanded its offerings to include Google AdWords.[12]

Yell threatened Yellowikis with legal action in July 2006, claiming that consumers would confuse the two organisations.[13]

In October 2009, an app was launched for Yell.com on the iPhone app store.[14] Yell UK announced that it was changing its traditional Yellow Pages A4 format to a smaller size in February 2010 in order to improve usage of the directory. At that time, the directories contained an average of 54% recycled fiber content.[15]

In October 2010, Yell moved in their new headquarters at One Reading Central, Reading, Berkshire, United Kingdom.[16] Richard Hanscott was named CEO of Yell in June 2011.[17] In May 2012, Yell's parent company Yell Group announced that it was changing its name to _Hibu_.[18] In August 2014, Hibu UK changed its customer facing brand back to Yell.

They relaunched with updated websites, pay per click advertising and a new display advertising proposition.[19] In November 2016, Glassdoor named Richard Hanscott to its list of Highest Rated CEOs in the United Kingdom.[20] In September 2017, it announced that the Yellow Pages will be no longer published on paper from 2018 since it launched in 1966.[21] The final edition was published in January 2019.
