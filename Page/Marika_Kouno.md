is a Japanese voice actress and singer from Tokyo. She is affiliated with the agency Mausu Promotion. Debuting as a voice actress in 2013, she played her first main role as Rin Kohana in the 2015 anime television series _Seiyu's Life!_. She, together with the other main cast members of _Seiyu's Life_, are also members of the music group Earphones. She is known for her roles as Niwaka Denkigai in _Akiba's Trip: The Animation_, Yua Nakajima in _Hinako Note_, and Silence Suzuka in _Uma Musume Pretty Derby_.


Biography

Kouno was born in Tokyo on February 22, 1994.[1][2] She is the second of three sisters;[3] her older sister Rina is a dancer.[4][5] As a child already, she was fond of reading aloud during elementary school classes.[6] She first became interested in voice acting from watching anime series. After learning that the characters Pikachu from _Pokémon_ and Chopper from _One Piece_ were voiced by the same person, Ikue Ōtani, Kouno began to aspire pursuing a career in voice acting.[7]

Upon entering high school, Kouno joined her school's voice acting club, where she studied the performances of voice actors. She also participated in other activities such as playing basketball. After her graduation, she enrolled in a training school operated by the Yoyogi Animation Academy.[8][9] She completed her studies in 2014, and became affiliated with the Mausu Promotion agency the same year.[10][11][12]

Kouno began her career playing minor roles in anime series such as _Aikatsu!_, _Log Horizon_, and _Rail Wars!_.[13] She then played the roles of Utako Uta and Pakuko in _Chikasugi Idol Akae-chan_, which was also the first time she performed a song in an anime.[14] The following year, she was cast in her first main role as Rin Kohana in the anime series _Seiyu's Life!_.[15] She, together with her _Seiyu's Life_ co-stars Rie Takahashi and Yuki Nagaku, formed the music group Earphones.[16] She also became a member of the idol group Team Ohenro.[17]

In 2017, Kouno played the roles of Niwaka Denkigai in _Akiba's Trip: The Animation_, Yua Nakajima in _Hinako Note_, and Yumina Urnea Belfast in _In Another World With My Smartphone_.[18][19] She was also announced to be voicing the character Silence Suzuka in the multimedia franchise _Uma Musume Pretty Derby_.[20] In 2018, she played the role of Sat-chan in _Mitsuboshi Colors_,[21] and she reprised the role of Silence Suzuka for the anime series of _Uma Musume Pretty Derby_.[22]
