SANAN DEVAHASTIN NA AYUDHYA (also spelled THEPHASADIN among other variants; , , ; 1 January 1877 – 1 February 1943), better known by his noble title CHAOPHRAYA THAMMASAKMONTRI (, ), was an educator, writer and senior government officer of Siam/Thailand. He served as Minister of Public Instruction from 1915–1926, and was the first President of the National Assembly, serving during 28 June – 1 September 1932 and 15 December 1933 – 26 February 1934.[1][2]
