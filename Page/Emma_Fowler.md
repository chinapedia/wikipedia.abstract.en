Corporal EMMA FOWLER (born 5 June 1979) is a British biathlete who in 2006 became the first British female biathlete to compete at the Winter Olympics. She has been a member of the Royal Logistic Corps, and is from Taunton, Somerset.[1]


Career

Fowler began competing in biathlon in 1997, and made her international debut in 1998.[2] She qualified to compete in the 2006 Winter Olympics in Turin, becoming the first female British biathlete to do so.[3] She finished 78th in the 15km individual event, and 67th in the 7.5km sprint event.[4][5] She competed at the Biathlon World Championships in 2001, 2005, 2007, 2008, and 2009.[6] She failed to qualify for the 2010 Winter Olympics.[7] Fowler is coached by Walter Pichler.[8] Fowler was selected for the London 2012 torch relay in Taunton; she was handed the torch by will.i.am.[9]

Aside from her biathlon career, Fowler has served in the 1 Regiment RLC of the Royal Logistic Corps.[10]
