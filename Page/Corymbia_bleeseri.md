_CORYMBIA BLEESERI_, commonly known as the SMOOTH STEMMED BLOODWOOD[1] or the GLOSSY-LEAVED BLOODWOOD,[2] is a bloodwood native to Western Australia.[3]

Th tree typically grows to a height of . It blooms between May and July producing white-cream flowers.[4]

It is found in lateritic or sandstone areas in the Kimberley region of Western Australia where it grows in skeletal soils.[5]

The species was first formally described as _EUCALYPTUS BLEESERI_ by the botanist Blakely in 1927 in the _Journal and Proceedings of the Royal Society of New South Wales_. It was subsequently reclassified into the _Corymbia_ genera by Kenneth Hill and Lawrence Alexander Sidney Johnson in 1995 in the work _Systematic studies in the eucalypts. A revision of the bloodwoods, genus Corymbia (Myrtaceae)_ in the journal _Telopea_.[6]
