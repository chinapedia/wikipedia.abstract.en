ROCK CANDY FUNK PARTY is an American jazz-funk band from Los Angeles, California. It was formed in 2009 by drummer Tal Bergman and guitarist Ron DeJesus, who had previously released _Grooove, Vol. 1_ in 2007. After adding guitarist Joe Bonamassa, bassist Mike Merritt and keyboardist Renato Neto, the band released its debut album _We Want Groove_ in 2013, which reached number 5 on the US _Billboard_ Jazz Albums chart and number 6 on the UK Jazz & Blues Albums Chart. Percussionist Daniel Sadownick joined after the album's release, featuring on the band's first live album _Rock Candy Funk Party Takes New York: Live at The Iridium_ in 2014.

In 2015, Rock Candy Funk Party released its second studio album _Groove Is King_, which featured additional contributions from musicians including trumpeter Randy Brecker, saxophonist Ada Rovatti and ZZ Top frontman Billy Gibbons. The album reached number 5 on the _Billboard_ Jazz Albums chart and number 7 on the UK Jazz & Blues Albums Chart. The band is set to release its third album _The Groove Cubed_ in October 2017, which also features vocalists Ty Taylor and Mahalia Barnes.


History

Joe_Bonamassa_-_Radio_City_Music_Hall_Jan_2014.jpg joined Rock Candy Funk Party the following year.]] Drummer Tal Bergman and guitarist Ron DeJesus first collaborated on the jazz/funk album _Grooove, Vol. 1_ in 2007, before performing a number of jam-based shows at Hollywood jazz club The Baked Potato with various guests.[1] This led to the official formation of Rock Candy Funk Party "around 2009",[2] with bassist Mike Merritt and keyboardist Renato Neto finalising the group's initial lineup.[3] After Bergman joined the guitarist's band in 2010, Joe Bonamassa also joined Rock Candy Funk Party in 2011,[4] performing for the first time with the group in early 2012.[5] The band recorded its debut album _We Want Groove_ at Bergman's own studio in Los Angeles later in the year, which was released on January 29, 2013.[6] The album registered at number 5 on the US _Billboard_ Jazz Albums chart,[7] number 8 on the Heatseekers Albums chart,[8] number 37 on the Independent Albums chart,[9] number 6 on the UK Jazz & Blues Albums Chart,[10] and number 41 on the UK Independent Albums Chart.[11]

On February 25, 2014 the band released its first live album and video set _Rock Candy Funk Party Takes New York: Live at the Iridium_, which was recorded across three nights at the Iridium Jazz Club in New York City, New York in June 2013.[12] The album features percussionist Daniel Sadownick, who had been performing live with the group since earlier in the year.[13] _Live at the Iridium_ registered at number 29 on the _Billboard_ Jazz Albums chart and number 14 on the UK Jazz & Blues Albums Chart.[14][15] The group's second studio album _Groove Is King_ followed on July 31, 2015, featuring contributions from Sadownick, trumpeter Randy Brecker, saxophonists Ada Rovatti and James Campagnola, and keyboardist Fred Kron, as well as appearances from Billy Gibbons (under the name "Mr. Funkadamus") as the master of ceremonies.[16] The album reached number 5 on the _Billboard_ Jazz Albums chart,[17] number 22 on the Heatseekers Albums chart,[18] and number 7 on the UK Jazz & Blues Albums Chart.[19]

Rock Candy Funk Party released its third studio album _The Groove Cubed_ on October 20, 2017, which features vocalists Ty Taylor and Mahalia Barnes on a song each.[20] The Taylor-featured song, "Don't Even Try It", was released as the lead single from the album, with an accompanying music video, on September 29.[21] The album reached number 5 on the _Billboard_ and number 10 on the UK Jazz & Blues Albums Chart.[22][23]
