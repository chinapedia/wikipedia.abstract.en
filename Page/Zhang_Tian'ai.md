ZHANG TIAN'AI (; born 28 October 1988), also known by her English name CRYSTAL ZHANG, is a Chinese actress and model. She is best known for her breakout role in the 2015 hit web-drama _Go Princess Go_.


Early life and education

Zhang Tian'ai was born 28 October 1988 in Harbin, Heilongjiang, China. Zhang attended high school in Japan, majoring in costume design. She then enrolled in the Beijing Film Academy.[1]


Career

Beginnings

In 2009, Zhang was invited by director Woody Han to star in the Korean short film _Cherry Blossom_ alongside Korean actor Kim Soo-hyun.[2] After which, she continued to appear in minor supporting roles whilst working as an advertisement model.[3] Zhang made her official acting debut in the drama _The Second Life of My Husband_, which aired in 2013.

2014–2015: Rising popularity and breakout

Zhang first gained attention in 2014 when she acted alongside Sun Honglei in spy drama _The Legendary Sniper_.[4] She won the Best Supporting Actress award at the 2nd Hengdian Film and TV Festival of China for her performance.[5][6]

In 2015, Zhang starred in the historical romance web drama _Go Princess Go_. The low-budget series unexpectedly became a commercial hit, and shot Zhang to fame.[7] Following her fame, Zhang was suceedingly cast in bigger projects.

2016–present: Leading roles

In 2016, Zhang starred in the romance film _I Belonged to You_ alongside Deng Chao.[8] The film was a commercial success and earned more than 800 million yuan ($118 million) at the box office.[9]

In 2017, she starred in comedy film _Father and Son_ with Da Peng and Fan Wei, and in fantasy adventure film _Legend of the Naga Pearls_ with Darren Wang.[10][11] She also featured in the war film _The Founding of an Army_ as Soong Mei-ling,[12] as well as fantasy-mystery film _Legend of the Demon Cat_ directed by Chen Kaige.[13] Zhang was nominated at the Hundred Flowers Award for Best Supporting Actress with her performance as Soong.[14]

In 2018, Zhang starred in the romantic comedy drama _The Evolution Of Our Love_ alongside Zhang Ruoyun.[15] The same year, she starred as the female lead in fantasy-action drama _Martial Universe_ alongside Yang Yang.[16]

In 2019, Zhang starred in the aviation film _The Chinese Pilot_ as a flight attendant.[17][18]
