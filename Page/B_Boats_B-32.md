The B-32 is an American racing sailboat, that was designed by Lief Beiley and first built in 1995. The design is out of production.[1][2][3]

The boat was built by B Boats in the United States, who constructed just 14 examples before production ceased.[4]


Design

The B-32 is a small racing keelboat, built predominantly of fiberglass. It has a fractional sloop rig, an internally-mounted spade-type rudder and a fixed fin keel with a weight bulb. It displaces and carries of lead ballast.[5][6]
