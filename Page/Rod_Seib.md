RODNEY SEIB (born 1975) is an Australian professional rugby union coach[1] and a former player for Queensland Reds[2][3] and Glasgow Warriors.[4] He also played for Australia in rugby sevens.[5]


Family and early life

Rod Seib attended Brisbane State High School, graduating in 1992.[6] His father, Ian Seib, played first-class cricket for Queensland in the 1970s.[7]


Rugby career

Australian Sevens and Queensland

Seib joined the Sunnybank rugby club in Brisbane.[8] He played for the Queensland Under-21 team in 1995,[9] and was capped for the Australia 7s side,[10] touring in 1999 to Uruguay and Argentina.[11]

Later that year, after a standout season with Sunnybank for which he was awarded Queensland Rugby Union's XXXX Medal for player of the year (shared with Richard Graham of Easts),[12] Seib was selected to play for the Queensland Reds in the Ricoh National championship.[13] He was capped three times for Queensland,[14] including a match against on their 2000 tour.[15][16]

Aberdeen and Glasgow

Seib moved to Scotland the following year. Signing in 2001,[17] he played for Aberdeen GSFP.[18][19][20] Seib captained the Aberdeen side from 2002-06.[21][22] He was also included in Aberdeen's Sevens squad.[23]

In season 2002-03, Seib was called up to Glasgow Warriors back up squad.[24][25] Such was his form for club side Aberdeen,[26][27][28] there was much talk of Seib's move to the provincial Glasgow side being made permanent,[29] however, work commitments prevented him joining Glasgow full-time.[30] He played in the Warriors' match against Harlequins in 9 August 2002.[31]

Seib won the Player's player of the year for the BT Premiership in 2003-04.[32]


Coaching career

Seib was made Head Coach of Aberdeen in 2011.[33] From 2012 he coached Sunnybank Rugby.[34] He later became an Assistant Coach at Brisbane City,[35] with responsibility for the team's attack.[36] In 2016 he was promoted to Brisbane City's Head Coach.[37][38][39] Seib became head coach of in 2018.[40]


Outside rugby

Away from rugby, Seib was a physical education teacher. He started teaching at Saint Stephen's College, Upper Coomera. While in Aberdeen he taught at Bridge of Don Academy.[41]
