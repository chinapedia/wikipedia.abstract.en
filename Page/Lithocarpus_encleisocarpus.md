_LITHOCARPUS ENCLEISOCARPUS_ is a tree in the beech family Fagaceae. The specific epithet __ is from the Greek meaning "enclosed fruit", referring to the acorns and cupules.[1]


Description

_Lithocarpus encleisocarpus_ grows as a tree up to tall with a trunk diameter of up to . The greyish brown bark is smooth or scaly or lenticellate. The coriaceous leaves measure up to long. Its dark brown acorns are ovoid to roundish and measure up to across.[2]


Distribution and habitat

_Lithocarpus encleisocarpus_ grows naturally in Thailand, Peninsular Malaysia, Singapore, Sumatra and Borneo.[3][4] Its habitat is mixed dipterocarp to lower montane forests from to altitude.[5]


Uses

The timber is locally used as firewood and in construction. The bark's tannins are used in dyeing.[6]
