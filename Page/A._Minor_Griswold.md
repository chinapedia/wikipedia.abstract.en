ALPHONSO MINOR GRISWOLD (January 26, 1834 â€“ March 14, 1891) was an American humorist, journalist, and lecturer, known by his pen name THE FAT CONTRIBUTOR.[1] Born in Oneida County, New York, and educated at Hamilton College, he began his career in Buffalo, first working at a small paper called the _Times_ and then at the _Republic_, where he began penning humorous articles signed "The Fat Contributor". Griswold joined the Cleveland _Plain Dealer_ in 1860 as associate editor, and after nearly five years joined the Cincinnati _Evening Times._[2][3] He toured the country giving humorous lectures, and died of apoplexy in Sheboygan Falls, Wisconsin, while on tour at the age of 57.[4][5]
