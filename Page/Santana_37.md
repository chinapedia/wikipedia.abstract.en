The SANTANA 37 is an American sailboat, that was designed by Gary Mull and first built in 1969. The design is out of production.[1][2][3]

The boat was built by W. D. Schock Corporation in the United States, who completed 21 examples between 1969 and 1972.[4]


Design

The Santana 37 is a small recreational keelboat, built predominantly of fiberglass, with wood trim. It has a fractional sloop masthead sloop rig, an internally-mounted spade-type/transom-hung rudder and a fixed fin keel. It displaces and carries of ballast.
