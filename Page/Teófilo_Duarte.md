TEÓFILO DUARTE (6 October 1898 – 16 May 1958) was a Portuguese colonial administrator, a military officer and a politician. He was a supporter of the Sidonist movement and the _Estado Novo_.[1]

He was born on 6 October 1898 in Idanha-a-Nova, eastern Portugal. An army officer, he was governor of Cape Verde from 9 March 1918 to 1919.[2] Having participated in movements against the democratic governments, he was dismissed from the Portuguese Army in 1920, only to be reinstated after the 28 May 1926 coup d'état.[3] He was governor of Portuguese Timor from 30 September 1926 to 22 December 1928.[4] He encouraged Portuguese immigration to the colony, including political deportees.[5] Under his rule, forced labour took a rise in East Timor.[6]

He was Minister of Colonies from 4 February 1947 to 2 August 1950 under Prime Minister Salazar.[7] On 1 September 1950, he was awarded with the Grand Cross of the Military Order of Christ.[8]
