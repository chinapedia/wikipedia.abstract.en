SILVESTRO ANDREOZZI (1575–1648) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Penne e Atri (1621–1648).[1][2]


Biography

Silvestro Andreozzi was born in Lucca, Italy in 1575.[3] On 17 March 1621, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul V as Bishop of Penne e Atri.[4][5] On 18 April 1621, he was consecrated bishop by Giovanni Garzia Mellini, Cardinal-Priest of Santi Quattro Coronati with Attilio Amalteo, Titular Archbishop of _Athenae_, and Paolo De Curtis, Bishop Emeritus of Isernia, serving as co-consecrators.[6] He served as Bishop of Penne e Atri until his death in January 1648.[7] While bishop, he was the principal co-consecrator of Costantino de Rossi, Bishop of Cefalonia e Zante (1634).[8]
