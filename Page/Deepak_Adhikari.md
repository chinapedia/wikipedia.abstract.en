DEEPAK ADHIKARI is a Nepali freelance journalist based in Kathmandu. He has written for AFP,[1] Kantipur Daily and Nepal Magazine[2] and has written for TIME,[3] The Guardian,[4] The Caravan,[5] Pittsburgh Post-Gazette,[6] Anadolu Agency,[7] Al Jazeera English,[8] Outside Magazine,[9] The New York Times,[10] Himal Southasian,[11] among others.


Biography

In 2009, he wrote a piece for TIME about Somali refugees in Kathmandu.[12] In 2008, he spent six months in the United States as an Alfred Friendly Press Fellow.[13] He wrote a profile of Maoist chairman Prachanda which was published in February, 2013 in The Caravan.[14] The piece is among 12 profiles included in The Caravan Book of Profiles edited by Supriya Nair and published by Penguin Books.[15] In 2013, he won special recognition in the regional Indigenous Voices in Asia (IVA) award for his story on endangered language Kusunda on Human Right day.[16][17] He has also been awarded Access to Energy Journalism Fellowship.[18] He is one of 12 contributors on the book _Garrisoned Minds_.[19]
