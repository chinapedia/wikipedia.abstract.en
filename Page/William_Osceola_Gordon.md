Early life

William Osceola Gordon was born on October 23, 1837 in Maury County, Tennessee.[1] He was a direct descendant of Pocahontas.[2] His father, Powhatan Gordon, was a farmer and politician.[3] His middle name, 'Osceola', comes from Osceola, a Native American chief that his father fought against in the Second Seminole War.[4]

Gordon graduated from Jackson College.[5] He was admitted to the bar in 1885.[6]


Career

Gordon was a jurist.[7] He served on the county court from 1902 to 1904.[8]

Gordon was a member of the Democratic Party.[9] He campaigned for Thomas Clarke Rye to become Governor of Tennessee.[10]


Personal life and death

Gordon married Virginia C. Graham on May 3, 1859.[11] They had a son, Powhatan Graham Gordon, and a daughter, Louisa, who married Willis E. Jones.[12] He was a Freemason, and a member of Royal Arcanum.[13]

Gordon died on December 31, 1914 in Columbia, Tennessee.[14] His funeral was held at St. John's Episcopal Church.[15]
