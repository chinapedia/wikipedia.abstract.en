Baroness NOÉMIE DE ROTHSCHILD (1888-1968) was a French philanthropist and property developer.


Early life

Noémie de Rothschild was born as NOÉMIE HALPHEN on 29 June 1888 in Paris, France.[1] She was the granddaughter of financier Eugène Péreire of the Sephardic-Jewish Péreire family of Portugal who were banking rivals of the Rothschilds.


Career

Rothschild turned her hôtel particulier in Paris into a hospital during World War I.[2] In 1916, she decided to develop a ski resort in France to avoid having to holiday alongside the Germans in St. Moritz, Switzerland.[3][4] By 1919, she founded Société Française des Hôtels de Montagne.[5] Mont_d'Arbois_1027.JPG Rothschild developed the Domaine du Mont d'Arbois, a luxury hotel in Mont d'Arbois near Megève in Haute-Savoie.[6] It was completed in 1921.[7]


Personal life and death

She married Maurice de Rothschild, a banker and politician. They had a son, Edmond Adolphe de Rothschild. She died on 15 March 1968 in Paris.[8]
