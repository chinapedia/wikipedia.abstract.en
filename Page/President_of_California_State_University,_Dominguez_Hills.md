California State University, Dominguez Hills, also known as CSUDH, Dominguez Hills, or Cal State Dominguez Hills, is a public university located in the city of Carson, California in the South Bay region of Los Angeles County and was founded in 1960. The university is part of the 23-school California State University system. It offers 45[1] majors for a Bachelor's degrees, 22 different Master's degrees, and 17 types of teaching credentials.[2][3] The university does not confer doctoral degrees.

Since its establishment in 1960,[4] the university has had ten (10) presidents. By name and years of service, they are:

-   Leo F. Cain (1962-1976)
-   Donald R. Gerth (1976-1984)
-   Richard Butwell (1984-1987)
-   John A. Brownell [Interim] (1987-1989)
-   Robert C. Detweiler (1989-1998)
-   James E. Lyons, Sr. (1999-2007)
