GÍSLI RÚNAR JÓNSSON (born 20 March 1953) is an Icelandic comedian, actor, voice actor, talkshow host, screenwriter, producer and director.


Early years

Gísli was born in Reykjavík, Iceland in 1953 to parents Guðrún Valgerður Gísladóttir, a housewife and Jón Konráð Björnsson, a local business man.[1] Gísli has three siblings.[2]


Carrier

Gísli is mostly known for his involvement with Icelandic comedies and sitcoms in the 1980s. He wrote and directed the sitcoms _Heilsubælið í Gerfahverfi_ and _Fastir liðir eins og Venjulega_, which become hugely successful in Iceland and have been played on reruns every few years for the last 30 years.[3]

In 1996 Gísli hosted his own talk show called _Gott Kvöld með Gísla Rúnari_ (English: _Good Evening with Gísli Rúnar_) on Stöð 2.[4]

In addition to starring in numerous plays Gísli is also a vivid screenwriter and translator of foreign plays into Icelandic. Ta name a few of his translations are _Little Shop of Horrors_,[5] _Grease_ and _Mary Poppins_.[6]

In 2001 Gísli wrote the biography of singer Björgvin Halldórsson.[7] In 2011 he published the book _Ég drepst þar sem mér sýnist_ (English: I'll die wherever I feel like), a book filled with funny stories from his early days as a comedian.[8]


Personal life

Gísli was married to actress Edda Björgvinsdóttir. They met in Drama school in the 1970s and got divorced in 2000. They remain close friends.[9] They have two sons, who like their parents both took too acting, Björgvin Franz Gíslason and Róbert Ólíver Gíslason.[10] He has two other children from other relationships.[11]

His son Róbert was diagnosed with Tourette syndrom as a child. Both Gísli himself and his older son Björgvin are thought to have the disease all though never diagnosed by a professional.[12]

Gísli was an alcoholic and stopped drinking in 1987.[13]
