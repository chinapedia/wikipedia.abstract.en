The following is a timeline of the history of the city of Camagüey, Cuba.


Prior to 20th century

-   1528 - Santa María del Puerto Príncipe established by settlers relocating from Caonao, and previously from Punta del Guincho.[1]
-   1599 - Convento de San Francisco founded.[2]
-   1616 - Fire.
-   1617 - Cathedral built.
-   1668 - City raided by Welsh pirate Henry Morgan.
-   1720 - San Francisco de Paula monastery rebuilt.[3]
-   1723 - Santo Cristo del Buen Viaje church built.[4]
-   1728 - Hospital de Caridad de San Juan de Dios established.[5]
-   1730 - Hospital de Nuestra Senora del Carmen founded.
-   1733 - City Hall construction begins.
-   1737 - San Lázaro hospital built.[6]
-   1741 - Epidemic outbreak.
-   1779 - Iglesia de Nuestra Señora de la Soledad (church) built.[7]
-   1800 - Royal Audiencia of Santo Domingo (Spanish colonial supreme court) relocated to Puerto Principe from Santo Domingo.
-   1814 - Future poet Gertrudis Gómez de Avellaneda born in Puerto Principe.[8]
-   1817 - Town becomes a city.[9]
-   1842 - Filarmónica (music society) founded.
-   1850 - El Principal theatre opens.
-   1851 - Puerto Principe and Nuevitas Railroad begins operating.[10]
-   1864
    -   Benemérita Popular Santa Cecilia (music society) formed.
    -   Our Lady of Candelaria Cathedral rebuilt.
-   1872 - Casino Español (music society) formed.
-   1874 - March: fought; Cuban rebels win.
-   1886 - _El Arrebol_ newspaper begins publication.[11]
-   1898 - Pedro Mendoza Guerra becomes governor of province.[12]
-   1899
    -   _El Eco Mercantil_ newspaper begins publication.[13]
    -   Population: 25,102 city; 53,140 district; 88,234 province.[14]


20th century

1900s-1940s

-   1902 - _El Camagüeyano_ newspaper begins publication.
-   1903
    -   Porto Principe renamed "Camagüey."[15]
    -   City becomes seat of Camagüey Province.[16]
-   1907 - Population: 29,616 city; 66,460 municipality; 118,269 province.[17]
-   1912
    -   Roman Catholic diocese of Camagüey established.[18]
    -   Ignacio Agramonte monument erected in Agramonte Park.[19]
-   1913
    -   Teatro Avellaneda opens.[20]
    -   Camagüey Jazz Band formed.
-   1919
    -   Banda Municipal de Música formed.
    -   Population: 98,193.[21]
-   1932 - Hurricane.[22]
-   1935 - Hurricane.[23]
-   1938 - Biblioteca Municipal (library) established.[24]
-   1948 - Cine Casablanca opens.[25]

1950s-1990s

-   1956 - _El Cubano Libre_ student newspaper begins publication.
-   1959 - Huber Matos becomes governor of province.[26]
-   1961 - Coro de Camagüey (musical group) formed.
-   1963 - Biblioteca provincial de Camagüey Julio Antonio Mella (library) established.[27][28]
-   1964 - Population: 153,100.[29]
-   1965 - Estadio Cándido González (stadium) built.
-   1966 - Population: 171,000.[30]
-   1967
    -   established.[31]

    -   Ballet de Camagüey founded.[32][33]

-   1968 - Archivo Histórico provincial de Camagüey (archives) established.

-   1970 - Population: 197,720.[34]
-   1976
    -   Instituto Superior Pedagógico established.[35]
    -   Museo Estudiantil "Jesús Suárez Gayol" opens.[36]
-   1981 - Instituto Superior de Ciencias Médicas de Camagüey established.[37]
-   1983 - Festival de Teatro de Camagüey (theatre festival) begins.[38]
-   1988 - Sister city relationship established with Madison, Wisconsin, USA.
-   1994 - Creole Choir of Cuba established.
-   1998
    -   January: Catholic pope visits Camagüey.
    -   Office of City Historian established.
-   1999 - Population: 306,049 city; 785,800 province.[39]


21st century

-   2008
    -   September: Hurricane Ike occurs.[40]
    -   Old town designated an UNESCO World Heritage Site.
    -   Museo de San Juan de Dios (museum) opens.[41]
-   2014 - Population: 304,027.[42]
