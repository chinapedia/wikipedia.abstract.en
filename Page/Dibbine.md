DIBBINE () is a small village in the Marjeyoun District in southern Lebanon, located just north of Marjeyoun.


History

In 1596, it was named as a village, _Dibin_, in the Ottoman _nahiya_ (subdistrict) of Tibnin under the _liwa'_ (district) of Safad, with a population of 41 households and 6 bachelors, all Muslim. The villagers paid a fixed tax-rate of 25 % on agricultural products, such as wheat, barley, olive trees, "dulab harir", goats, beehives; in addition to occasional revenues, a press for olive oil or grape syrup; a total of 3,969 akçe.[1][2]
