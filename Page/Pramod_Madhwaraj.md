In June 2016 Madhwaraj was inducted into the Siddaramaiah led government of Karnataka as a cabinet minister with Independent Charge. Madhwaraj holds the Youth Services Department, Fisheries from Animal Husbandry and Fisheries Department portfolio in the Government of Karnataka.Madhwaraj is the 10th richest minister in India to be sworn into the Karnataka Cabinet.[1][2][3][4]


Political life

Madhwaraj is from the Indian National Congress, Madhwaraj had represented Udupi Assembly Constituency of Udupi district, Karnataka from 2013 to 2018. Mr. Pramod Madhwaraj is married to Mrs. Supriya Madhwaraj. They have a daughter named Prathyaksha. Pramod Madhwaraj unsuccessfully fought the 2008 Karnataka Legislative Assembly election on a Indian National Congress ticket losing to K. Raghupati Bhat of the Bharatiya Janata Party by about 2500 votes. However, in the following 2013 Karnataka Legislative Assembly election Madhwaraj won the seat defeating B. Sudhakar Shetty of the Bharatiya Janata Party by over 39000 votes.[5] In the 2018 Karnataka Legislative Assembly election, Madhwaraj lost to K. Raghupati Bhat of the Bharatiya Janata Party by about 2000 votes and a margin of 5%.[6] Madhwaraj was educated in Mahatma Gandhi Memorial College and National Institute of Technology, Karnataka. Pramod Madhwaraj is the managing Trustee of M Madhwaraj Charitable Trust which is named after his late father M. Madhwaraj. The trust is very popular among the poor and helps a lot of poor people to make their ends meet.[7].He joined the JD(S) ahead of the 2019 Lok Sabha Elections but still campaigns for Congress president Rahul Gandhi as his party is in alliance with the Congress . He contested unsuccessfully from the Udupi-Chikmangalur Lok Sabha Constituency against Shobha Karandlaje of the BJP and lost by a margin of 28% or over 350000 votes.


Ministry

Besides holding the Youth Services Department, Fisheries from Animal Husbandry and Fisheries Department portfolio Madhwaraj also Serves as the District In-Charge Minister for Udupi district.[8] As the Fisheries minister Madhwaraj has tried to regulate fishing in coastal areas due to the depletion of aquatic animals on the count of over fishing.[9]
