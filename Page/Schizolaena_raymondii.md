_SCHIZOLAENA RAYMONDII_ is a tree in the family Sarcolaenaceae. It is endemic to Madagascar. It is named for the botanist Raymond Rabevohitra.[1]


Description

_Schizolaena raymondii_ grows as a tree up to tall. Its subcoriaceous leaves are elliptic to obovate in shape and coloured greenish brown above and khaki green below. They measure up to long. The inflorescences are found near branch tips, each bearing up to 12 flowers. Each flower has three sepals and five petals. The roundish fruits measure up to in diameter.[2]


Distribution and habitat

_Schizolaena raymondii_ is known only from the northern region of Sava[3] where it is restricted to a single locality in the Ambondrobe forest.[4] Its habitat is humid forest from sea-level to altitude.[5]


Threats

Although abundant in its single location, the species is not in a protected area, leaving it threatened by human pressures. The preliminary status of the species is Critically Endangered.[6]
