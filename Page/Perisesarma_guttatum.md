The RED-CLAW MANGROVE CRAB[1] (_PERISESARMA GUTTATUM_) is a crab species in the genus _Perisesarma_. It is distributed in coastal brackish water habitats of the western Indian Ocean.[2][3]


Distribution

This is a mangrove inhabitant that mostly inhabit _Avicennia marina_.


Description

Claws are reddish in color with bright orange internal palms. Carapace is about 3Â cm in length.


Ecology

The species use burrows and natural crevices made by _Neosarmatium smithi_ to hide from predators and also for survival. It is an omnivore, feed on anything found on bottom of estuary and forest floor.[4]
