LUIGI DE AMATO (died 1530) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of San Marco (1515–1530), Bishop of Lipari (1506–1515), and Bishop of Rapolla (1497–1506).


Biography

On 12 September 1497, Luigi de Amato was appointed during the papacy of Pope Alexander VI as Bishop of Rapolla.[1][2] On 19 September 1506, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Julius II as Bishop of Lipari.[3] On 26 January 1515, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Leo X as Bishop of San Marco.[4] He served as Bishop of San Marco until his death in 1530.[5]
