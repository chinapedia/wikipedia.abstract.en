ELISA MÚJICA VELÁSQUEZ (21 January 1918 – 27 March 2003) was a Colombian writer.


Career

Beginning in the 1980s, Mújica was a member of the Academia Colombiana de la Lengua and Real Academia Española.[1]

She worked in newspapers and magazines, publishing commentaries and book reviews in _El Tiempo_ and _El Espectador_.

Her works are tied to Colombia, and tell stories from a social point of view.[2] She was also devoted to children's literature.

She lived in Spain in the 1950s and was an influence to her niece, the poet and journalist María Mercedes Carranza.[3]
