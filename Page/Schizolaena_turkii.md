_SCHIZOLAENA TURKII_ is a plant in the family Sarcolaenaceae. It is endemic to Madagascar. It is named for the botanist Daniel Turk.[1]


Description

_Schizolaena turkii_ grows as a shrub or tree up to tall with a trunk diameter of up to . Its papery leaves are elliptic to ovate in shape and measure up to long. The inflorescences are small and bear up to 15 flowers, each with three sepals and five pink petals. The roundish fruits are yellow and measure up to in diameter.[2]


Distribution and habitat

_Schizolaena turkii_ is known only from the east central region of Vatovavy-Fitovinany. Its habitat is humid forest from altitude. Some subpopulations are within Ranomafana National Park.[3]
