The VIOLET VINEGAR CRAB (_Episesarma versicolor_) is a swimming crab species in the genus _Episesarma_.[1] Distributed all over marine and brackish waters of Indo-West Pacific regions. It is harvested by many local fishermen for rich proteinaceous food.


Distribution

A mangrove inhabitant, it is found all over Southeast Asian countries such as Southern China, Hong Kong, Indonesia, Malaysia, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, northern Australia and South Asian countries like Bangladesh, India and Sri Lanka.[2]

Male grows up to maximum length of 5Â cm. Carapace square-shaped and relatively flat. Body color brown to brownish grey in carapace with violet colored outer surface of palm with proximal parts. Fingers are whitish.[3]


Ecology

The species is well distributed in mangrove regions, inhabits burrows at tree bases or sometimes within mounds created by _Thalassina_ lobsters. It is feed primarily on calyx and leaves of water plants, margroves and mangrove associates, usually at night. During high timed, they usually climb as high as 6m up trees.[4]
