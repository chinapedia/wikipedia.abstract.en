TIRSO MARTÍNEZ (c. 1760-1820s) was a Spanish jurist, who served as notary in the Court of Accounts of Buenos Aires, during the Viceroyalty of Rio de la Plata.[1]


Biography

Martínez was born in Astorga, Spain, son of Matheo Martínez and María Marcos, belonging to a distinguished family. He arrived at the port of Buenos Aires from Cádiz, circa 1790, commissioned by the Spanish authorities to perform duties at the Court of Auditors of Buenos Aires and also in Lima.[2]

Martínez take part in the defense and reconquest of Buenos Aires during the British invasions of the River Plate of 1806 and 1807.[3] He was notary of the court of auditors for many years, being seconded in office by the accounting officer Juan de Canaveris.[4]

Tirso Martínez was married in the Cathedral of Buenos Aires to Juana Fernánedez, daughter of Gil Fernández and Rosa Sánchez. His daughter Agustina Josefa Martínez Fernández was goddaughter of Pedro Andrés García,[5] a distinguished army officer and geographer, born in Caranceja (Cantabria).[6]
