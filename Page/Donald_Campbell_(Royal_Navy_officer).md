Naval career

Born the eldest son of Colin Campbell of Auchendoun, Argyll, Campbell joined the Royal Navy on 4 June 1791.[1] He became commander of the schooner HMS _Tobago_ in February 1805, the packet boat HMS _Lily_ in September 1805 and the sloop HMS _Pert_ in May 1807.[2] He went on to be command the sloop HMS Espiegle in September 1809, the sloop HMS _Port d'Espagne_ in April 1810 and the fifth-rate HMS _Rosamond_ in September 1810.[3] He became commander-in-chief of the Leeward Islands Station in 1818[4] and Inspecting-Commander in the Coast Guard in 1822 before retiring in 1832.[5]
