SABAA TAHIR is a Pakistani-American[1] young-adult fiction writer, who is best known for her young-adult series the "Ember Quartet," including 2015 _New York Times_ bestselling novel _An Ember in the Ashes_, the 2016 novel _A Torch Against the Night,_ and the 2018 novel _A Reaper at the Gates._ Her novels are published through Razorbill, an imprint of Penguin Random House.[2]


Biography

Tahir grew up in the Mojave Desert in Ridgecrest, California with her parents and two older brothers.[3] Her parents emigrated from Pakistan to the United Kingdom before moving their family to the United States.[4] Since she did not have cable, she grew up surrounding herself with books. In high school, she attended and graduated from Sherman E. Burroughs High School in Ridgecrest, California. She attended UCLA, during which time she interned at the Washington Post. After graduation, she took a job as a copy editor at _The Washington Post_.[5] She currently lives in the San Francisco Bay Area.[6]


Bibliography

_An Ember in the Ashes_

Tahir's first novel, An Ember in the Ashes was published on April 28, 2015.[7] The story takes place in an ancient fantasy world where a teenage girl is fighting to save her brother from prison, and a soldier battles to free himself from the regime. The novel instantly jumped to being a New York Times Bestseller where it held the number two spot.[8] Amazon ranked it the best book for May, the best young adult book for 2015, and the fourth best book overall. Tahir sold the film rights to Paramount Pictures before the book was released, and it is now slated to be produced by Mark Johnson (_The Chronicles of Narnia_, _The Notebook_, _Breaking Bad_) and Haroon Saleem.[9]

_A Torch Against the Night_

Tahir's second novel was published on August 30, 2016 and is a continuation on the story from her previous novel.[10] Penguin Random House describes this addition to the series as such: “Set in a rich, high-fantasy world inspired by ancient Rome, Sabaa Tahir’s _An Ember in the Ashes_ told the story of Laia, a slave fighting for her family, and Elias, a young soldier fighting for his freedom. Now, in _A Torch Against the Night_, Elias and Laia are running for their lives."[11]

_A Reaper at the Gates_

Sabaa Tahir's latest book, _A Reaper at The Gates_, was published on June 12, 2018.[12]


Personal Life

Tahir is married to Kashi Tahir and has two children.[13]
