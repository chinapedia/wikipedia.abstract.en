HAMPTON J. CHENEY (1836-1927) was an American Confederate veteran and politician. He served as a member of the Tennessee Senate.


Early life

Hampton J. Cheney was born on June 2, 1836 in Rapides Parish, Louisiana.[1] He graduated from the Western Military Institute in 1858.[2]


Career

Cheney became a farmer.[3] During the American Civil War of 1861-1865, he served as adjutant under General John C. Brown in the Confederate States Army.[4][5]

Cheney served as assistant postmaster of Nashville from 1888 to 1892.[6][7] He served as a member of the Tennessee Senate in 1892.[8] He served as the City Comptroller of Nashville from 1898 to 1906.[9][10]


Personal life and death

Cheney married Amanda E. Stratton, the daughter of Colonel Madison Stratton, in 1858.[11] They had a daughter, Leonora.[12] He was a member of the Improved Order of Red Men, the Fraternal Order of Eagles and the Knights of Pythias.[13] He was also a member of the United Confederate Veterans.[14]

Cheney died on September 12, 1927 in Nashville, Tennessee. His portrait is displayed at the Tennessee State Museum.[15]
