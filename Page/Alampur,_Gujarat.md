Alampur}} ALAMPUR is a town and former Rajput petty princely state on Saurashtra peninsula, in Gujarat, western India.


History

The princely state, in Gohelwar _prant_, was ruled by Gohel Rajput Chieftains. During the British Raj, it was in the charge of the colonial Eastern Kathiawar Agency.

It comprised only the single village, with a population of 497 in 1901, yielding 4,500 Rupees state revenue (1903-4, mostly from land), paying 1,397 Rupees tribute to the Gaekwar Baroda State and Junagadh State.
