thumb, 1973]] ANDRÉ LARONDE (19 June 1940, Grenoble – 1 February 2011, Paris, aged 70) was a French hellenist archaeologist.


Career

After he taught at the University of Grenoble, André Laronde was a professor at the Sorbonne[1] in which he presented in 1976 his thesis for the State doctorate « Recherche sur l'histoire de Cyrène », which studied the history of Cyrenaica under the domination of the Lagides.[2]

He assisted François Chamoux since the creation in 1976 of the French Archaeological Mission in Tripoli, then became director from 1981. He also conducted the excavations in Cyrene and Apollonia, Cyrenaica.[3]

He was elected a member of the Académie des Inscriptions et Belles-Lettres in 2002.

He died of a heart attack on 1 February 2011 at the age of 70.[4]
