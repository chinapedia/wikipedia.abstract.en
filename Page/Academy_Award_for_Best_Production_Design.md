The ACADEMY AWARD FOR BEST PRODUCTION DESIGN recognizes achievement for
art direction in film. The category's original name was BEST ART
DIRECTION, but was changed to its current name in 2012 for the 85th
Academy Awards.[1] This change resulted from the Art Director's branch
of the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences (AMPAS) being renamed
the Designer's branch. Since 1947, the award is shared with the set
decorator(s). It is awarded to the best interior design in a film.[2]

The films below are listed with their production year (for example, the
2000 Academy Award for Best Art Direction is given to a film from 1999).
In the lists below, the winner of the award for each year is shown
first, followed by the other nominees in alphabetical order.
