MARC-OLIVIER FOGIEL (born July 5, 1969) is a French television and radio presenter and producer.


Early life

Marc-Olivier Fogiel was born on July 5, 1969 in Neuilly-sur-Seine, France.[1] His father is a dentist; his mother was an estate agent who retired when she had children.[2] His paternal grandfather was a Holocaust survivor who lost many members of his family because they were Jewish.[3] He has a brother and a sister.[4]

Fogiel graduated from Panthéon-Assas University, where he earned an associate degree in economics in 1988.[5][6]


Career

Fogiel started working for RTL at the age of 16.[7] He subsequently worked as an assistant producer to Patrick Sabatier for _Avis de recherche_ and _Tous à la une_, two television programs on TF1.[8] He became an accredited journalist in 1993.[9]

Fogiel was a presenter on _Télés Dimanche_, a television program on Canal+ hosted by Michel Denisot.[10] He was the host of _TV+_ from 1996 to 1998, and _Un an de +_ from 1998 to 2000, both of which were on Canal+.[11][12] From 2000 to 2006, he presented _On ne peut pas plaire à tout le monde_, a talk show on France 3.[13] He then presented _T'empêches tout le monde de dormir_, another talk show on M6, from 2006 to 2008.[14]

Fogiel was the host of _Europe 1 Matin_, a radio morning program on Europe 1 from 2008 to 2012.[15][16] He returned to RTL in September 2012, where he presents _RTL soir_ and _On refait le monde_.[17] Since 2015, he is also the host of _Le Divan_ on France 3.[18]


Personal life

Fogiel married François Roelants in 2013.[19] They have two daughters.[20]
