"LOVIN' THINGS" is a 1968 song recorded by Scottish group The Marmalade, later covered by The Grass Roots. The song was written by Artie Schroeck and Jet Loring. It was the band's first successful single release, reaching number six on the UK singles chart.[1] Their version was not, however, released in North America.


Grass Roots cover

"Lovin' Things" was released in 1968 by Bobby Rydell then covered the following year by The Grass Roots. It was the lead single from their album of the same name. The song became a modest hit in both Canada and the United States.

Other versions

-   -   "Lovin' Things" was first released by the writer, Artie Schroeck on Columbia Records in early 1967.
    -   Bobby Rydell did a version of the song in 1967. It was released as the B-side of "That's What I Call Livin'," a non-charting single. He performed "Lovin' Things" on the Dick Clark ABC-TV Saturday-afternoon program, _American Bandstand_ on February 28, 1970.
