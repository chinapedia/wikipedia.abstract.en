ANDREAS ALBERS (born 23 March 1990) is a Danish professional footballer who plays for SSV Jahn Regensburg in the 2. Bundesliga.


Club career

Skive IK

Albers was promoted to the first team squad in the summer 2009.[1] He played eight league games in his first season, scoring one goal.

A transfer to Vejle Boldklub failed in December 2011.

Vejle Boldklub

On 7 January 2011, it was confirmed that Albers had signed a two-and-a-half-year contract with Vejle Boldklub.[2] Albers revealed that this transfer maybe was a to big step for him, and he started out playing some games for the reserve team. He played for the reserve team, Vejle-Kolding, from 2011 to 2013 and established himself as a starter in Vejle Boldklub in the 2012â€“13 season. Albers played 30 league games, scoring 7 goals.

After a good last season with 11 goals in 31 games, he left the club.

Silkeborg IF

Silkeborg IF confirmed on 22 May 2015 that they had signed Albers on a two-year contract.[3] After his transfer, Albers stated that he had rejected offers from many Danish Superliga clubs, in favor of joining Silkeborg.

Albers revealed in December 2016 that he and the club had some difficulties about extending his contract. So the plan was to sell him in the January window, but however, he stayed in the club.[4] Albers was not happy with his stay at Silkeborg because he sat to many minutes on the bench. He went out to medias in May 2017 and said, that it was his last season in Silkeborg and he would leave the club in the summer window.[5]

Viborg FF

On 21 June 2017, Albers signed with Danish 1st Division club Viborg FF.[6]

SSV Jahn Regensburg

On 1 July 2019, Albers joined SSV Jahn Regensburg on a free transfer.[7]
