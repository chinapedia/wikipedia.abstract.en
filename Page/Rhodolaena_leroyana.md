_RHODOLAENA LEROYANA_ is a plant in the family Sarcolaenaceae. It is endemic to Madagascar. The specific epithet is for the botanist Jean-Fran√ßois Leroy.[1]


Description

_Rhodolaena leroyana_ grows as a shrub or small tree up to tall.[2] The twigs are hairless.[3] Its subcoriaceous leaves are elliptic to obovate in shape and measure up to long. The solitary inflorescences have one or two flowers on a peduncle measuring up to long.[4] Individual flowers are large with five sepals and five purple-pink petals, measuring up to long. The fruits are large and woody, with a fleshy involucre.[5]


Distribution and habitat

_Rhodolaena leroyana_ is known only from the region of Atsinanana where it is confined to Betampona Reserve.[6] Its habitat is humid evergreen forest from to altitude. The preliminary status of the species is Vulnerable.[7]
