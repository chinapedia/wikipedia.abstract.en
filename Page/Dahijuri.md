DAHIJURI is a village in Binpur I CD Block in Jhargram subdivision of Jhargram district in the state of West Bengal, India.


Socio-political turmoil

Dahijuri was a centre of violent Maoist activity during the Lalgarh movement days around 2009-2011.[1][2][3] Now Dahijuri has become stable and free from violent and Maoist activity. Total 10 Gram Panchayat[4] is under Dahijuri Gram Panchayat.[5].Current(2018-2023) Gram Panchayet Pradhan is Ms. Falguni Dey.


Demographics

As per 2011 Census of India Dahijuri had a total population of 3,237 of which 1,597 (49%) were males and 1,640 (51%) were females. Population below 6 years was 364. The total number of literates in Dahijuri was 2,066 (63.82% of the population over 6 years).[6]


Economy

The State Bank of India has a branch at Dahijuri.[7] Economy mainly depends on the cultivation here. Now S.B.I[8] came with their first A.T.M to give a new economic push.


Transport

SH 5 running from Rupnarayanpur (in Bardhaman district) to Junput (in Purba Medinipur district) passes through Dahijuri.[9]Dahijuri-Kanajpal-Lalgarh, Dahijuri-Dherua, Dahijuri-Parihati Roads converge on this place.[10]


Education

Dahijuri Mahatma Vidyapith was established in 1948. It is a co-educational Bengali-medium higher secondary school.[11]
