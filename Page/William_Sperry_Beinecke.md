Career and philanthropy

Beinecke studied at Westminster School[2][3][4], Yale University and Columbia University and served in the U.S. Navy during the Second World War, retiring with the rank of Lieutenant Commander.[5]

In 1952 he joined Sperry & Hutchinson, the company founded by his great-uncle Thomas Sperry, which was best known for its S&H Green Stamps, and served as chairman and CEO before his retirement in 1980.

He was the principal benefactor of the Yale Golf Course at Yale University and the William Miller Sperry Observatory at Union County College in Cranford, New Jersey.[6] He was a donor to Yale University's Beinecke Rare Book & Manuscript Library and also founded the Central Park Conservancy. [7]

He published his memoirs in 2000.[8]


Family

Bill Beinecke[9] was the son of Frederick W. Beinecke and Carrie Regina Sperry Beinecke (the daughter of William Miller Sperry) and lived in Cranford, New Jersey, until the age of 11.[10]

Beinecke had four children, including the environmentalist Frances Beinecke. He turned 100 in May 2014.[11]


Death

William Beinecke died on April 8, 2018 at the age of 103.[12]
