CRISTÓBAL GUZMÁN SANTOYO (1 November 1578 – 17 November 1656) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Palencia (1633–1656).[1]


Biography

Cristóbal Guzmán Santoyo was born in Guzmán, Spain on 1 November 1578.[2] On 6 June 1633, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Urban VIII as Bishop of Palencia.[3] On 29 January 1634, he was consecrated bishop by Martín Carrillo Alderete, Bishop of Oviedo.[4] He served as Bishop of Palencia until his death on 17 November 1656.[5]


Episcopal succession

While bishop, he was the principal consecrator of:[6]

-   Bartolomé Santos de Risoba, Bishop of Almería (1634);

and the principal co-consecrator of:[7]

-   Gonzalo Sánchez de Somoza Quiroga, Bishop of Mondoñedo (1639); and
-   Juan Queipo de Llano Flores, Bishop of Pamplona (1639).
