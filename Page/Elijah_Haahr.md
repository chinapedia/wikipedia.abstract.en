ELIJAH J. L. HAAHR (born May 28, 1982) is an American politician. He is a member of the Missouri House of Representatives, having served since 2013. He is a member of the Republican Party.[1]


Early life, education, and career

Haahr grew up in southwest Missouri. He attended Ozarks Technical Community College and was a member of the National Dean's List and Phi Theta Kappa. After graduating from OTC with honors in 2002, Haahr received an academic scholarship to attend Missouri Western State University. He graduated _cum laude_ from MWSU in 2005. He then attended the University of Missouri School of Law on academic scholarship and graduated with honors in 2008. At MU, he served as editor in chief of the _Environmental Law Review_ and represented MU at the regional level in mock trial and moot court competitions.

Haahr is an attorney with Lathrop Gage and focuses on product liability and personal injury. He is licensed to practice in Missouri and Oklahoma.[2]

During his time in the Missouri House of Representatives, Haahr chaired the Emerging Issues Committee and is currently the Speaker Pro Tem. In September 2017, he was elected to be the next Speaker of House starting in January 2019. He will be the first Speaker in Missouri history from Springfield.

In 2019, Haahr spoke in favor of a criminal justice reform measure to undo mandatory minimum sentencing laws for nonviolent offenders and give judges the discretion to determine sentencing on the basis of the offender's character, rehabilitation prospects and threat to society.[3]

Personal life

Elijah lives in Springfield with his wife, Amanda, and four children. He is a member of the National Rifle Association.[4]
