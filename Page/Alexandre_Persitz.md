ALEXANDRE PERSITZ (4 June 1910 – 15 July 1975) was a Russian-born French Modernist architect.


Early life

Alexandre Persitz was born in 1910 in Moscow, Russia.[1] He emigrated to France as a child.[2] During World War II, he was sent to concentration camps by the Nazi invaders.[3] He survived The Holocaust.[4]


Career

Paris_Synagogue_Roquette_75.JPG Persitz began his career as an architect by redesigning the port of Le Havre with Auguste Perret shortly after World War II.[5] In 1953-1956, he designed the Memorial to the Unknown Jewish Martyr on Rue Geoffroy-l'Asnier in the 4th arrondissement of Paris with Georges Goldberg.[6]

With Arthur-Georges Héaume, Persitz designed the Synagogue Don Isaac Abravanel, also known as the Synagogue de la Roquette, in the 11th arrondissement of Paris in 1962.[7] In 1964, they designed a skyscraper at 38-40 Rue des Épinettes in the 17th arrondissement of Paris.[8] In 1968, they designed two skyscrapers in the 14th arrondissement of Paris: the Le Méridien de Paris at 24-34 Rue Dareau and another building at 29 Rue de la Tombe Issoire.[9][10] In 1973, he designed Tour Nova, a skyscraper in La Garenne-Colombes.[11]

Persitz designed several skyscrapers in Puteaux with Héaume, Bernard Zehrfuss, and J. Merski: the Tour Diamant,[12] Tour Emeraude,[13] and the Résidences Bellerive.[14][15][16]

Persitz was the editor-in-chief of _L'Architecture d'aujourd'hui_, a French magazine about architecture, from 1949 to 1965.[17]


Death

Persitz died in 1975.[18]
