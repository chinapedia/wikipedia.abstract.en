LAURENCE HENRY MAGUIRE (born 8 February 1997) is an English professional footballer who plays as a defender for Chesterfield.


Club career

Maguire started his career in the academy at Chesterfield, where he was the captain before being handed his first professional contract by the Spireites on 1 June 2015. He made his professional debut in the club's 2–1 EFL Trophy victory against Wolverhampton Wanderers U23 on 30 August 2016.[1] He then made his League One debut in the 3–3 home draw with Gillingham on 27 September.[2] He scored his first senior goal on 6 December in a 2–0 EFL Trophy win at Rochdale.[3]

On 17 February 2017, Maguire was sent out on loan to A.F.C. Fylde of National League North until 21 March.[4] The next day, he made his debut in a 3–1 win over Brackley Town.[5][6]


International career

In October 2018 he was called-up to the England C squad.[7]


Personal life

Maguire's older brothers, Joe and Harry, are also footballers. Joe plays for Gainsborough Trinity[8] and Harry plays for Leicester City and the England national team.[9]
