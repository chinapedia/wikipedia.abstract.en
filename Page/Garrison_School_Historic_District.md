GARRISON SCHOOL HISTORIC DISTRICT is a national historic district located at Liberty, Clay County, Missouri. It encompasses six contributing buildings associated with the African-American community of Liberty. The district developed between about 1900 and 1942, and contains a variety of National Folk housing types, as well as the brick Garrison School and the limestone African Methodist Church.[1]

It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2001.[2]
