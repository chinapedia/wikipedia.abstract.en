THOMAS MASCHMEYER is an Australian chemist and a Professor of Chemistry at the University of Sydney[1]. He is the Founding Director of the Australian Institute for Nanoscale Science and Technology,[2] Laboratory of Advanced Catalysis for Sustainability, and University of Sydney Energy Storage Research Network. He has published several highly cited articles and books.[3]

Aside from being an Elected Fellow of the Royal Society of New South Wales,[4] he also holds the Honorary Distinguished Professorship at University of Cardiff and was also previously an Australian Bicentennial Fellow at the Royal Institution in 1994, working with John Meurig Thomas; and was also previously Vice-Chairman of the Delft Institute of Chemical Technology at Delft University of Technology.[5]

In 2011 he was elected a Fellow of the Australian Academy of Science.[6]
