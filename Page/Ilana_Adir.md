ILANA ADIR (-Kamarshik; אילנה אדיר-קמרשיק; born June 28, 1941) is an Israeli former Olympic sprinter, and former Israeli Women's Champion in the 100 metre sprint.[1]

She was born in Tel Aviv, Israel, and is Jewish.[2][3]


Sprinting career

Her personal best in the 100 metres was 12.3 in 1960.[4] That year she was Israeli Women's Champion in the 100 metres, with a time of 12.4.[5]

She competed for Israel at the 1960 Summer Olympics in Rome, Italy, at the age of 19.[6] In the Women's 100 metres she came in 6th in Heat 5, with a time of 12.9.[7] When she competed in the Olympics, she was 5-4.5 (165 cm) and weighed 128 lbs (58 kg).[8]
