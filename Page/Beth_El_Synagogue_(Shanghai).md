Beth-El}} BETH EL SYNAGOGUE was a synagogue located in Shanghai, China.

Jews began to settle in Shanghai in 1848.[1] Most were Sephardic Jews from Baghdad and Bombay.[2] During the 1870s, the Baghdadi Jewish community used rented space for religious worship.[3] Beth El Synagogue was established in 1887.[4] It was located on Peking Road, a major thoroughfares in the English settlement.[5]

Jacob Elias and Edward Elias Sassoon built the Ohel Rachel Synagogue to replace Beth El Synagogue.[6] Ohel Rachel Synagogue opened in March 1920.[7]
