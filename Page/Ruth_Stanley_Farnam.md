Family

Ruth Stanley Farnam was born at Patchogue, New York, the daughter of William Henry Stanley and Ida Jay Overton Stanley. She married and later, Baron . She went by the name of Baroness de Luze until her death in 1956.[2]


War work

She originally served as a volunteer nurse in a medical unit attached to the Serbian army. She was present during the Battle of Brod on 11 October 1916[3] and, when a soldier asked if she was afraid, answered: "Do you think I am scared? I have never lived before".[4] After this, she was allowed to enlist in the Serbian army as a volunteer soldier. She was decorated three times by the King of Serbia.[5]

In 1918, she published her autobiography, _A Nation at Bay: What an American Woman Saw and Did in Suffering Serbia_.[6] She died in 1956, aged 83 years.
