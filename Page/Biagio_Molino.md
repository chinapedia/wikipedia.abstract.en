BIAGIO MOLINO or BIAGGIO MOLINA or BIAGEO DE MOLINA (1380–1447) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Titular Patriarch of Jerusalem (1434–1447),[1] Patriarch of Grado (1427–1434),[2] Archbishop of Zadar (1420–1427),[3] and Bishop of Pula (1410–1420).[4]


Biography

Biagio Molino was born in Venice, Italy in 1380.[5][6] On 19 February 1410, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Gregory XII as Bishop of Pula.[7][8][9] On 4 March 1420, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Martin V as Archbishop of Zadar.[10][11][12] On 17 October 1427, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Martin V as Patriarch of Grado.[13][14][15] On 20 October 1434, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Eugene IV as Titular Patriarch of Jerusalem.[16][17][18] He served as Titular Patriarch of Jerusalem until his death in 1447.[19][20][21] While bishop, he was the principal consecrator of Giacomo Veneri de Racaneto, Archbishop of Dubrovnik (1440).[22]
