EDUARD PAPIROV (אדי פפירוב; born March 3, 1964) is an Israeli former Olympic sport shooter.[1][2][3]

He was born in Israel, and is Jewish.[4] When he competed in the Olympics he was 5-7.5 (172 cm) tall, and weighed 161 lbs (73 kg).[5]


Shooting career

He competed for Israel at the 1988 Summer Olympics in Seoul at the age of 24.[6] He competed in Shooting--Men's Small-Bore Rifle, Three Positions, 50 metres, and came in tied for 21st.[7] He competed as well in Shooting--Men's Small-Bore Rifle, Prone, 50 metres, and came in tied for 24th.[8] He competed also in Shooting--Men's Air Rifle, 10 metres, and came in tied for 34th.[9]

At the European Shooting Championships, he finished 38th in the 50-meter free rifle event, with 590 points.[10]
