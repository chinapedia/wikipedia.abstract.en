PIER ANGELO SOLDINI (25 May 1910 â€“ 12 July 1974) was an Italian novelist, essayist and journalist.

Born in Castelnuovo Scrivia, in 1935 Soldini won a special Viareggio Prize for best first work thanks to his debut novel _Alghe e meduse_. In 1937, he won the Bagutta Prize for the book _Sole e bandiere_.[1]

Soldini collaborated as a journalist with various publications, and was editorial director of the publishing house Palazzi.[2] He died on July 12, 1974 in Volpedo, where he was spending his holidays.[3]
