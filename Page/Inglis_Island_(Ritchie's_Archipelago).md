INGLIS ISLAND is an island of the Andaman Islands. It belongs to the South Andaman administrative district, part of the Indian union territory of Andaman and Nicobar Islands.[1] the island is lying northeast from Port Blair.


Etymology

Inglis island is named after Major-general Sir John Eardley Wilmot Inglis.


Geography

The island belongs to the Ritchie's Archipelago and is located west of John Lawrence Island.[2] Chain Nalah point is a popular tourist beach located on the island's west coast. it has some picnic tables on it.


Administration

Politically, Inglis Island is part of Port Blair Taluk.[3]


Demographics

The island is uninhabited.
