JOSEPH BEVAN ROBERTSON WHITNEY (born London, England; 1928 – 2016) was a political and environmental geographer.[1][2] He was a professor of geography at the University of Toronto,[3] and studied Chinese culture and politics.[4][5]


Early life and education

Whitney graduated from the University of Cambridge with a degree in geography in 1950.[6] He then taught geography at St. Mark's School in Hong Kong for twelve years.[7] He subsequently studied and conducted research at the University of Chicago,[8] earning a Ph.D. in 1969. Whitney then joined the geography department at the University of Toronto.[9][10]

Whitney served as department head from 1988 to 1993, after which he retired and became a Professor Emeritus.[11] He later wrote about the political, economic, and environmental geography of China.[12][13][14][15]

Personal

Whitney was married to Diana Baxter.[16]
