AMAR EZZAHI (January 1, 1941 – November 30, 2016) was an Algerian singer and MANDOLE player. He was the figurehead of Chaabi, the traditional music of Algiers.


Early life

Amar Ezzahi was born as AMAR AIT-ZAÏ in Ain El Hammam, a village of Kabylie, Algeria, on January 1, 1941.[1][2][3] He grew up in the Casbah of Algiers.[4][5] He was orphaned as a child.[6]


Career

Ezzahi was a singer and mandole player.[7] He started recording songs in 1963.[8][9] In 1976, he recorded two albums.[10] He only gave one concert on February 10, 1987 in Algiers.[11] Instead, he performed in open spaces like cafes and terraces, mostly during family gatherings.[12] Moreover, he shunned the media and turned down copyright checks.[13] He was the figurehead of Chaabi, the traditional music of Algiers.[14]


Personal life and death

Ezzahi lived an ascetic life: he was not married, and had no children.[15][16]

Ezzahi died on November 30, 2016.[17][18] Upon his death, Azzedine Mihoubi, the Algerian Minister of Culture, visited his house to pay homage to him.[19][20] His funeral was held in a mosque the following day, on December 1, and he was buried in the El Kettar Cemetery.[21][22]

A celebration in honour of Ezzahi, with performances by Abdelkader Chaou and Kamel Aziz, was held at the Arab World Institute on December 3, 2016 in Paris, France.[23]
