JEAN-MICHEL DAMIAN (8 September 1947 – 1 November 2016) was a French radio and television music journalist. He hosted and produced radio programmes on France Musique from 1980 to 2009. He was the author of two books.


Early life

Jean-Michel Damian was born on 8 September 1947, in Chambéry, Savoie, France.[1][2][3]

He learned to play the piano, the harpsichord and the pipe organ as a child.[4][5] He graduated from Sciences Po.[6][7][8]


Career

Damian was a radio journalist.[9] He began his career as a music journalist on France Culture in the early 1970s.[10][11] He was also a book reviewer _Politique Hebdo_, a radio programme on France Culture.[12] He joined France Inter in 1975, where he was a music reviewer on _Temps de vivre_, hosted by Jacques Pradel, until 1977. He was the host of _Espace musical_, a television programme on France 3, in 1978.[13][14]

Damian was a music journalist on France Musique, a French radio station, from 1980 to 2009.[15] He became the host of _L’Air du temps de la musique_ in 1980.[16] He was subsequently the host and producer of _Désaccord parfait_, _Les Imaginaires_, _Cordes sensibles_, and _Ce soir on dîne_.[17][18][19]

Damian was the author of two books, including a novel, _Scène dans le bleu_.[20][21]


Death

Damian died during the course of an operation in a Paris hospital on 1 November 2016, aged 69.[22][23]
