BATTISTA DE VENTURA (died 1492) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Avellino e Frigento (1465–1492)[1] and Bishop of Frigento (1455–1465).[2]


Biography

On 12 September 1455, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul II as Bishop of Frigento.[3][4][5] On 20 May 1465, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul II as Bishop of Avellino e Frigento.[6][7] He served as Bishop of Avellino e Frigento until his death in 1492.[8][9] While bishop, he was the principal co-consecrator of Pietro Guglielmo de Rocha, Archbishop of Salerno (1471).[10]
