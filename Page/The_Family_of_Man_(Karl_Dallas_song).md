THE FAMILY OF MAN is a song written in 1955 by Karl Dallas, under the name Fred Dallas. It was inspired by Dallas' visit to a touring photography exhibition, _The Family of Man_, when it visited the Royal Festival Hall in London.[1][2]

It was recorded by The Spinners,[3] with the _Oxford Dictionary of National Biography 2005-2008_ entry for the band's Cliff Hall saying:[4]

It was also recorded by the London Youth Choir for their "Songs from Aldermaston" EP;[5] by Bitter Withy,[6] and by Colin Wilkie & Shirley Hart. The song has also been included in school song books and hymn books.[7] A choral arrangement was made by the composer Alan Bush.[8]
