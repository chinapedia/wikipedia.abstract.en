Biography

On 27 June 1611, Rodolfo Paleotti was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul V as Bishop of Imola.[2][3] On 2 July 1611, he was consecrated bishop by Giovanni Garzia Mellini, Cardinal-Priest of Santi Quattro Coronati, with Marco Antonio Salomone, Bishop Emeritus of Sora, and Alessandro Borghi (bishop), Bishop Emeritus of Sansepolcro, serving as co-consecrators.[4] He was installed on 15 October 1611.[5] He served as Bishop of Imola until his death on 24 May 1619.[6][7]

While bishop, he was the principal co-consecrator of Gilles de Souvre, Bishop of Comminges (1617).[8]
