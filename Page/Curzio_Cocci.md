Biography

On 3 March 1614, Curzio Cocci was appointed during the papacy of Pope Paul V as Archbishop of Conza.[4] On 9 March 1614, he was consecrated bishop by Giovanni Garzia Mellini, Cardinal-Priest of Santi Quattro Coronati with Ulpiano Volpi, Archbishop of Chieti, and Tommaso Confetti, Bishop of Muro Lucano, serving as co-consecrators.[5] He served as Archbishop of Conza until his death in November 1621.[6]
