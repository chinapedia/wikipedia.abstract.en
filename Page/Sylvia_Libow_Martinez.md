SYLVIA LIBOW MARTINEZ an American engineer whose book _Invent To Learn: Making, Tinkering, and Engineering in the Classroom_[1] has been acknowledged as the "bible" of the school Maker Movement. She, with this one book, is largely collected by libraries worldwide.[2]

Sylvia Martinez received her bachelor's degree in electrical engineering from UCLA, and a master's degree in educational technology from Pepperdine. She began her career as an engineer for Magnavox Research Labs focusing on the development of high frequency receiver systems and navigation software for GPS satellites. After six years, she transitioned to educational software development where she presided over the design and development at several software publishers (Davidson & Associates, Knowledge Adventure, and Vivendi Universal) for over a decade. She then shift to the non-profit world, where held the position of President at YES. She sought to champion young people in the use of modern technology to advance education and community. This cumulative work provide the groundwork for her co-authorship of the book Invent to _Learn: Making, Tinkering, and Engineering in the Classroom_. Her time in now spent advancing the Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math (STEM) and Maker Movement initiatives through speaking engagements and workshops.
