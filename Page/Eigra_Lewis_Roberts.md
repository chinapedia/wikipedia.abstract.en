EIGRA LEWIS ROBERTS (born 7 August 1939) is a Welsh-language author who has written about 30 plays, short stories, children's books and novels.[1] She has won numerous awards at the National Eisteddfod of Wales.[2]


Personal life

Roberts was born in . She attended Ffestiniog County School, at the same time as fellow author John Rowlands and poet Gwyn Thomas,[3][4] and later graduated from University College of North Wales in Bangor.[5] She then taught in Holyhead and , and now lives in .[6][7] Roberts has an honorary MA from the University of Wales.[8][9]


Career

Aged 20, Roberts won the open novel prize at the 1959 National Eisteddfod of Wales.[10][11] During the 1960s and 1970s, she was known for writing about contemporary life and the dissatisfaction of Welsh women in Post-war Britain, a topic not covered much by Welsh authors at the time.[12][13][14] In the 1980s, she screenwrote and adaptation of her novel __ to create the Welsh television programme _Minafon_.[15][16]

In 2006, Roberts wrote her first English-language novel, _Return Ticket_. The novel was semi-autobiographical.[17] In the same year, she won the Crown in the Swansea National Eisteddfod for a collection of poems about Sylvia Plath.[18][19][20] In 2013, her book __ was shortlisted for the Wales Book of the Year award.[21]
