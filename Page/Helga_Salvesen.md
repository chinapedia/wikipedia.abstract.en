HELGA BIRGITTE SALVESEN (23 December 1963[1] – 20 January 2016) was a Norwegian physician and professor of medicine at the University of Bergen.

Salvesen was born in Asker, but moved to Bergen at the age of four.[2] She took the cand.med. degree in 1991 and the dr.med. degree in 2000, with the doctoral thesis _Tumor Biomarkers and Prognostic Factors in Endometrial Carcinoma_. She became chief physician at Haukeland University Hospital, and in 2003 also professor of gynecology and obstetrics at the University of Bergen. Here, she became co-director of the _excellent research_ centre Centre for Cancer Biomarkers. In 2015 she was elected as a fellow of the Norwegian Academy of Science and Letters.[3]

She was married to fellow physician Pål Rasmus Njølstad since 1986, and had four children, all boys.[4] She died suddenly in January 2016.[5]
