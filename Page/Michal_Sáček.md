MICHAL SÁČEK (born 19 September 1996) is a professional Czech football midfielder currently playing for Sparta Prague in the Czech First League.

Club career

He made his career league debut for Sparta Prague on 14 May 2016 in a Czech First League 5–0 home win against Jihlava. While at Sparta Prague, he won the Revelation of the Year award for the 2016–17 Czech First League season.[1]

International career

He was called up to the Czech national team on 2 June 2017 to face Belgium and Norway as a replacement for the injured Antonín Barák, but failed to make an appearance.[2] He represented his country on the 2017 UEFA European Under-21 Championship. In the first match, a 0–2 loss to Germany, his defensive error led to a goal by Serge Gnabry.[3]
