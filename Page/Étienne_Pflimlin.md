ÉTIENNE PFLIMLIN (born 16 October 1941) is a French high-ranking civil servant and banker. He served as the CEO of the Crédit Mutuel from 1987 to 2010.


Early life

Étienne Pflimlin was born on 16 October 1941 in Thonon-les-Bains, Haute-Savoie, France.[1] His father, Pierre Pflimlin, was a politician.[2]

Pflimlin graduated from the École Polytechnique, Sciences Po and the École nationale d'administration (ENA).[3]


Career

Pflimlin started his career as a high-ranking civil servant.[4] He worked for Prime Minister Pierre Messmer in 1973 and Culture Minister Michel d'Ornano in 1977.[5] He subsequently worked for the Court of Audit.[6]

Pflimlin joined the Crédit Mutuel, a French mutual bank, in 1984.[7] He succeeded Théo Braun as its CEO in 1987, retiring in 2010.[8][9][10] He argued that shareholders were a critical part of mutual banking, and that the industry would be over if shareholders stopped being engaged.[11] He earned 630,141 Euros in 2010, 600,000 of which was his net salary.[12] He was succeeded by Michel Lucas.[13]

Pflimlin was elected to the board of the International Co-operative Alliance in 1994.[14] He has served on the board of directors of FIMALAC, a credit rating and risk assessment company, since 2006.[15] Pflimlin first met Ladreit as a student at the ENA.[16]

Pflimlin became a Commander of the Legion of Honour in 2012.[17]


Personal life

Pflimlin is married to Sophie, an editor, and he has two children, Edouard and Thomas.[18][19] He resides in Strasbourg with his family.[20]
