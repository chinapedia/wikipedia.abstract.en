COTTONWOOD BRANCH or COTTONWOOD CREEK is a stream in Henry County in the U.S. state of Missouri.[1] It is a tributary of Honey Creek.

The stream headwaters arise at just west of Missouri Route 13 north of Quarles and it flows west-southwest crossing under Missouri Route N north of Huntingdale to its confluence with Honey Creek in an old mined area at at an elevation of .[2][3]

Cottonwood Branch was so named due to the presence of cottonwood timber along its banks.[4]
