Naval career

Wells joined the Royal Navy as a cadet on 15 September 1899.[1] Promoted to captain on 31 December 1924, he was given command of the cruiser HMS _Diomede_ in 1929.[2] He joined the staff at the Royal Naval War College in 1931 and became captain of the aircraft carrier HMS _Eagle_ in 1933 before going on to be Director of the Tactical School in 1935 and Rear-Admiral 3rd Carrier Squadron in 1937.[3] He served in the Second World War as Vice-Admiral, Aircraft carriers from 1939 and as Admiral Commanding, Orkneys and Shetlands from January 1943 before retiring in 1944.[4]
