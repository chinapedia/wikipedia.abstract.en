Captain HENRY MITCHELL JUNIOR, known as HARRY MITCHELL (1862-1894), was managing director of a brewery company co-founded by his father Henry Mitchell.[1][2] He was also superintendent of the fire brigade at the company's Cape Hill brewery,[3][4] and a Captain in the Smethwick Rifle Volunteers.[5]

He founded a brewery football team, Mitchells St George's, later Birmingham St George's F.C..[6]

Harry was born on 11 September 1862, in Smethwick (then part of Staffordshire), and died from typhoid at the age of just 32.[7]
