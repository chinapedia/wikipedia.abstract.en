PORNPETCH WICHITCHOLCHAI (, ; born 1 August 1948 in Bangkok) is a Thai lawyer. He has been serving as the President of the Senate of Thailand.


Education

Pornpetch graduate high school from Vajiravudh College, studied a bachelor's degree from Faculty of Law Chulalongkorn University, and a master's degree from the Harvard Law School, Harvard University.[1]


Career

He began work in government service for the first time in the legal position for Council of State. Previously served as a Supreme Court judge, chairman of the Court of Appeal Region 4, Chief Justice of the Supreme Court. He used to be a professor of law in the Faculty of Law of many universities. He also plays an important role in foreign countries as the Executive Director of the ASEAN Law Association since 1987, having been the Chairman of the National Law Committee of the ASEAN Law Association. While serving as President of the National Legislative Assembly, he was invited by the Inter-Parliamentary Union (IPU) to attend the World Conference of Speakers of Parliaments and gave a speech at the United Nations Conference Room, New York City.[2]

On 1 December 2016, Pornpetch as the President of the National Legislative Assembly invited Crown Prince Vajiralongkorn ascend to the throne as Thai King after the death of King Bhumibol.[3][4][5]

On 24 May 2019, The junta-appointed Senate convened for its first session, in which Pornpetch was elected Senate speaker without opposition.[6]
