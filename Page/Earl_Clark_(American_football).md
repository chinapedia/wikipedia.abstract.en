EARL F. "CLICK" CLARK (1891 â€“ April 10, 1959) was an American football player and coach.


College

Clark lettered at the University of Washington in 1912.[1] He then lettered at the University of Montana from 1914 to 1916 as a 152-pound end.[2][3] At Montana, he was a captain of the football team and an "all-star northwest" end for three seasons, after which he enlisted in the United States Navy in 1917.[4]


Coaching

Clark served as the head coach at the University of Montana from 1924 to 1925, the second alum to do so.[5] He then became a trainer at the University of Washington in 1926 until his retirement.[6]
