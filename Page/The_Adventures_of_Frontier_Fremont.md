_THE ADVENTURES OF FRONTIER FREMONT_ (known in the U.K. as _SPIRIT OF THE WILD_) is a 1976 film starring Dan Haggerty. It was one of the better Sunn Classic Pictures. Fremont (Dan Haggerty), of 19th-century St. Louis, gives up his job as a tinsmith in favor of a life in the mountains. Here he becomes friends with the animals and with other like-minded frontiersmen. Denver Pyle, a veteran Sunn Classics performer, co-stars as "the old mountaineer." _The Adventures of Frontier Fremont_ stars, Haggerty, Pyle and Don Shanks; all three appeared In the TV series _The Life and Times of Grizzly Adams_ and Fremont, just as Adams in the TV series, protects his family, the animals, from those that wish to harm them.


Production

Parts of the film were shot in Park City, Kamas, and Uinta National Forest in Utah.[1]
