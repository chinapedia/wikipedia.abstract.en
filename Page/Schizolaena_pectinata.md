_SCHIZOLAENA PECTINATA_ is a tree in the family Sarcolaenaceae. It is endemic to Madagascar.


Description

_Schizolaena pectinata_ grows as a tree up to tall. Its elliptic to ovate leaves measure up to long.[1] The small flowers are white or pink.[2] The involucre of the flowers is fleshy and laciniate.[3] It is thought to attract lemurs, bats and birds who in turn disperse the tree's seeds.[4]


Distribution and habitat

_Schizolaena pectinata_ is known only from the eastern regions of Vatovavy-Fitovinany, Alaotra-Mangoro and Atsinanana. Its habitat is humid and subhumid forests from sea-level to altitude.[5]


Threats

Two subpopulations of the species are in the protected areas of Betampona Integral Natural Reserve and Perinet-Analamazaotra Special Reserve. Outside of these areas, the species is threatened by shifting patterns of agriculture.[6]
