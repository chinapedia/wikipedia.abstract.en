Early life

Tayssir Akla was born in Syria circa 1939.[1][2] He emigrated to Algeria in 1962.[3][4]


Career

Akla conducted the orchestra of the Algerian Radio in 1970.[5][6] He composed songs about the Algerian War, including _Ana el Djazair_ and _Thawrat al ahrar_, performed by Saliha Essaghira.[7][8]


Personal life and death

Akla married Nadia Kerbache, an Algerian singer of Syrian descent.[9][10] He became a widow when she died in 2010.[11][12] Akla died six years later, on November 22, 2016, in Brussels, Belgium.[13][14] Upon his death, Azzedine Mihoubi, the Algerian Minister of Culture, called him a "big composer adopted by Algeria".[15][16]
