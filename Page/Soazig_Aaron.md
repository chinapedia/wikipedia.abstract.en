SOAZIG AARON (born 1949, Rennes) is a French woman of letters.[1]


Biography

After studying history, Soazig Aaron worked for a few years in a bookshop in Paris. Today, she lives in Rennes in Brittany.[2]

Her first novel, _Le Non de Klara_, appeared in 2002. The author, who is not Jewish, recounts in a fictitious diary the fate of Klara, a survivor of Auschwitz who returned in 1945 to Paris, trying to resume a normal life.[3]

For this work, Soazig Aaron received the prix Goncourt du premier roman and the prix Emmanuel Roblès of the city of Blois in 2002.[4][5] These two prizes reward a first novel. In 2002, it also obtained the Prix du Roman of the city of Carhaix. In 2004, _Le Non de Klara_ was crowned with the Grand Prix des Libraires. In Germany, the novel was awarded the Geschwister-Scholl-Preis.[6]

Her second novel, _La Sentinelle tranquille sous la lune_, was published by éditions Gallimard in 2010.
