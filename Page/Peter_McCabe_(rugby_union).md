PETER MCCABE (born 5 March 1992) is an Irish rugby union player. He plays as a prop. McCabe most recently played professionally for Irish provincial side Connacht in the Pro14. He previously played for his native province of Munster. In addition to playing at professional level, McCabe has represented Cork city club Dolphin in club rugby.[1]


Munster

On 4 November 2016, McCabe made his competitive debut for Munster when he came on as a substitute against Ospreys in a 2016–17 Pro12 fixture.[2] On 3 February 2017, McCabe made his first start for Munster in the Pro12 fixture against Edinburgh.[3] On 19 May 2017, it was announced that McCabe would leave Munster at the end of the 2016–17 season.[4]


Connacht

On 10 January 2017, it was announced that McCabe had joined Munster's provincial rivals Connacht on loan.[5] By the end of January 2017, he had returned to training with Munster following the completion of his loan.[6] Following his release by Munster, McCabe returned to Connacht permanently on a one-year contract, a move that was announced in June 2017.[7] His contract was extended in March 2018 for a further season.[8] In April 2019, it was announced that McCabe would leave Connacht at the end of the 2018–19 season.[9] He made a total of 37 appearances in his time with the team.[10][11]
