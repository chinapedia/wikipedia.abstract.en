_CORRIERE DELLE DAME_ was a weekly Italian-language fashion magazine published in Milan, Italy, between 1804 and July 1875. The magazine is one of the pioneers in women's emancipation in Italy.[1]


History and profile

_Corriere delle dame_ was established in 1804.[2][3] The founder was Carolina Arienti (also known as Carolina Lattanzi),[4] who was married to Giuseppe Lattanzi.[5] She edited the magazine,[6][7] which included articles about literature and theatre of France and Italy.[8] It targeted men, women and children.[9] However, it was mostly read by women from higher social class.[10] The magazine is the origin of the Lombard costume.[11]

The headquarters of _Corriere delle dame_, published on a weekly basis,[12] was in Milan.[13] In 1811 the number of subscribers was 700.[14] The magazine existed until July 1875.[15]
