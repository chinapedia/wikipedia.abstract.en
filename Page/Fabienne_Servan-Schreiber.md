FABIENNE SERVAN-SCHREIBER (born 23 March 1950) is an award-winning French film and television producer. She is the founder and president of Cinétévé, a production company.


Early life

Fabienne Servan-Schreiber is the daughter of Jean-Claude Servan-Schreiber, a politician, and Christiane Laroche.[1] Her paternal uncles were Jean-Jacques Servan-Schreiber and Jean-Louis Servan-Schreiber, while her aunts are Brigitte Gros and Christiane Collange.[2] She is of Jewish-Prussian descent on her paternal side.[3] Her parents divorced when she was three years old, and her mother later remarried into the Stern family.[4] She grew up in Paris.[5]

Servan-Schreiber was educated at the Lycée Victor Duruy.[6] She graduated from the University of Paris, where she earned a bachelor's degree in history.[7]


Career

Servan-Schreiber started her career as an assistant to director Henri de Turenne on _C’était hier_.[8] She then served as an assistant and later a director of several documentaries, and she worked for directors Frédéric Rossif, Vincent Malle and Claude Berri.[9][10][11] She subsequently produced _Les Murs de Santiago_, directed by Carmen Castillo.[12][13]

Servan-Schreiber started her own production company, Cinétévé, in 1982.[14] She serves as its president.[15] She has produced films, documentaries, and television series like _Witnesses_.[16][17] Some of the films she produced are _Lumière et compagnie_ _La Fille de Keltoum_, _Calle 54_, _Jean de La Fontaine_, _Le défi_, and _Les Ponts de Sarajevo_.[18] She directed a deradicalisation campaign for the French Ministry of the Interior in partnership with the Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe (OSCE) in 2016.[19]

Servan-Schreiber won a 7 d'Or for _Jalna_ in 1994, a Fipa d’Or and another 7 d'or for _Fatou, la Malienne_ in 2001, and a Fipa d’Argent for _Mais qui a tué Maggie_ in 2009.[20] She won the Best Fiction Producer of the Year Award from Procirep in 2016.[21]

Servan-Schreiber serves as the vice president of the Union Syndicale de la Production Audiovisuelle.[22] She is a Knight of the Ordre des Arts et des Lettres, the Legion of Honour and the National Order of Merit.[23]


Political activism

In May 2012, Servan-Schreiber co-authored a petition alongside Jean-Pierre Mignard and Bertrand Monthubert expressing their concern about the rise of the far right in France.[24] By April 2012, she co-authored an op ed encouraging French people to vote for Francois Hollande as President.[25]


Personal life

Servan-Schreiber married Henri Weber, a Socialist politician, in 2007.[26]
