Alexandra_Flood.jpg ALEXANDRA FLOOD (born 26 February 1990) is an Australian operatic soprano. __TOC__


Career

Born in Cowes, Victoria, Flood attended the Methodist Ladies' College, Melbourne[1] and studied from 2007 until 2010 at the University of Melbourne (B. A., Diploma in Music). In 2011, she gained a Graduate Diploma of Journalism at the Royal Melbourne Institute of Technology. She then continued from 2014 to 2016 at the Hochschule für Musik und Theater München (Master's degree in Music Theatre).

In 2014 she was awarded a Mike Walsh Fellowship.

Flood was part of the 2014 Salzburg Festival's and made her European stage debut there as Blonde in a production for children of Mozart's _Die Entführung aus dem Serail_ and as Modistin in Richard Strauss's _Der Rosenkavalier_.[2] Her first leading role was Marguerite in May 2015 in _Le petit Faust_ by Hervé at the Staatstheater am Gärtnerplatz, Munich. This was followed in October by the title role in Janáček's _The Cunning Little Vixen_ for Pacific Opera, Sydney.[3]

In 2016, Flood became a Studio Artist at the Wolf Trap Opera Festival.[4] She sang Violetta in _Traviata Remixed_ at the Grachtenfestival in Amsterdam.[5] She sang Serpetta in Mozart's _La finta giardiniera_ in September at the in Hanover, Germany.[6] Her first appearance in Poland was as Norina opposite Mariusz Kwiecień in Donizetti's _Don Pasquale_ at Opera Krakowska in 2017,[7] a role she reprised in 2018 at the Vorarlberger Landestheater, Austria.[8] 2018 saw her Australian stage debut as Tell's son, Jemmy, in Rossini's _William Tell_ for Victorian Opera, Melbourne.[9]

On the concert stage, Flood sang in Carl Orff's _Carmina Burana_, Vivaldi's "Gloria", Saint-Saëns' _Oratorio de Noël_, Handel's _Messiah_, Haydn's _The Seasons_, and Vaughan Williams' _A Pastoral Symphony_. She sang the Edna in Jonathan Dove's church opera _Tobias and the Angel_ with the Munich Radio Orchestra under the baton of Ulf Schirmer.

Flood has won grants and awards in Australia, Austria, and Germany. The actress Georgia Flood is her sister, the author Morris West her grand-uncle.[10]
