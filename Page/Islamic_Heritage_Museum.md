The ISLAMIC HERITAGE MUSEUM () is a museum in Kuching, Sarawak, Malaysia.[1] It is part of the Kuching Heritage Trail.[2]


History

The museum was originally constructed as the JAMES BROOKE MALAY COLLEGE school building. It was then later changed to the SARAWAK MALAY MADRASA in 1930.[3][4] In 1992, the building was converted into the ISLAMIC HERITAGE MUSEUM on 22 May 1992.[5]


Architecture

The museum building uses raised floor and it has two inner courtyards. The material used for the building construction are concrete, timber and bricks.[6] It consists of seven galleries, which are:

-   History of Islam in Sarawak
-   Islamic Architecture
-   Islamic Science, Technology, Economy, Education and Literature
-   Islamic Costume, Music and Personal Collections
-   Islamic Weaponry
-   Islamic Decoration Art and Domestic Utensil
-   Quran Collections[7][8]


Exhibitions

The museum exhibits the history and culture of the Muslim community in Sarawak and Malay archipelago, such as the development of Islam.[9]


Opening time

The museum opens everyday from 9:00 a.m. to 4:45 p.m. on weekdays and from 10:00 a.m. to 4:00 p.m. on weekends free of charge.[10]
