_GIRLBOSS_ is an American comedy web television series created by Kay Cannon.[1] The series was released on Netflix on April 21, 2017.[2][3] The show was cancelled after one season.[4]


Premise

The show is based on Sophia Amoruso's autobiography _#Girlboss_,[5] which tells the story of how Amoruso started the company Nasty Gal while working as a campus safety host for San Francisco's Academy of Art University.

Production

In February 2016, it was announced that Netflix had ordered a series based on Amoruso's autobiography _#Girlboss_.[9] It was announced that the first season had gotten an order of 13 episodes.[10] In June 2016, Britt Robertson joined the cast of the series.[11] That same month, Johnny Simmons, Alphonso McAuley and Ellie Reed joined the cast.[12][13][14] In July 2016, Dean Norris joined the cast in a recurring role.[15]

Principal photography took place in San Francisco and Los Angeles.[16]


Reception

Review aggregator Rotten Tomatoes gives the first season a 32 percent rating based on 25 reviews and an average rating of 5.94/10.[17] Metacritic gives the series a score of 53 out of 100, based on 13 critics, indicating "mixed or average reviews."[18]

Some critics criticized the main character's unlikeability, but praised Robertson's portrayal.[19][20][21]

Critics were divided as to whether the show presented a feminist message. Jennifer Wright from _New York Post_ called the show a "feminist fraud" because of Sophia's selfishness.[22] However, Amy Otto from _The Federalist_ praised it as a story of empowerment in the way Sophia creates her own brand based on her vision.[23] On 18 April 2018, Zimbio criticized the show saying it was "like the weird forgotten cousin of Degrassi".[24]
