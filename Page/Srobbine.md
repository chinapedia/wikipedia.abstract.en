SROBBINE, Surubbin, () is a village in the Bint Jbeil District in southern Lebanon.


Name

According to E. H. Palmer, Surubbin comes from a personal name.[1]


History

In 1852, Edward Robinson noted the village (_Seribbin_) on his travels in the region.[2]

In 1881, the Palestine Exploration Fund's _Survey of Western Palestine_ (SWP) described it as: "a small stone village, containing about eighty Metawileh, situated on hill-top, with olives and arable cultivation ; springs near, and cisterns and birket."[3]
