ERIC ARTHUR TOMLINSON (8 January 1931 in Preston, Lancashire â€“ 24 November 2015 in Exeter, Devon) was a British recording engineer, who recorded and mixed the scores to more than 120 movies.[1]

He had an aeronautical engineering apprenticeshipship at Fairey Aviation, and served in the Royal Air Force.[2] He then began doing small jobs for Radio Luxemburg, and later joined the International Broadcasting Company, where he recorded John Dankworth, Cleo Laine and Ted Heath among others.[3]

At the end of the 1950s, he moved to cinematic recordings, first with Muir Mathieson.[4] He joined Cine-Tele Sound (CTS) Studios in Bayswater in 1959, where some of his first projects were early Bond movies.[5]
