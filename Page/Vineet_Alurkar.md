VINEET ALURKAR is an Indian musician and singer-songwriter from Pune, India.[1] He is most noteworthy as the frontman of bands The Black Birds and Yoga Logik.[2]


Biography

Alurkar was born to music entrepreneur Suresh Alurkar and wife Anuradha Alurkar.[3] Alurkar's father was known in music circles across India for his vast collection of rare classics and was the owner of Alurkar Music House.[4] Alurkar's father was found murdered in his Pune home in 2008.[5] Alurkar has one brother[6] and is married as of 2016.

Alurkar conceived of the Austrian-Indian folk rock 6 member band,[7] Yoga Logik in 2005 when he jammed with Austrian guitarist Wolfgang Sambs during Sambs' visit to India.[8] The band's music is inspired by lyrical poetry. Critics have appreciated its blend of contemporary jazz and funk clubbed with Indian classical styles of music.[9] The band have extensively toured Europe and India from 2012â€“16.[10][11] The band released an album titled _Live at Vidya Valley_ in 2014.[12][13]

Alurkar was also one of the founding members of the band The Black Birds in 2011.[14] The band began as a school reunion between Alurkar and fellow musician Dhruv Bhate.[15] The band released an EP that same year.[16]

As of 2016, Alurkar has been performing over 13 years in India and around the globe.[17] Alurkar plays the guitar as his favored instrument[18] and runs his music teaching practice from Aundh in Pune.[19] He is a permanent musical faculty with the Pune based Vidya Valley School[20] and is visiting faculty in Rungsted Gymnasium, a high school in Copenhagen, Denmark.[21]
