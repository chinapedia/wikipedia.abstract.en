CHARLES DYMOKE GREEN SENIOR (England 1873-1954) was an early leader of the Scout Association, District Commissioner at Saint Albans from at least 1912 to 1948, and a personal friend of Robert Baden-Powell.

His son Charles Dymoke Green Junior, Chairman of the World Scout Committee, was awarded the 63rd _Bronze Wolf_, the only distinction of the World Organization of the Scout Movement, awarded by the World Scout Committee for exceptional services to world Scouting, in 1971.[1]
