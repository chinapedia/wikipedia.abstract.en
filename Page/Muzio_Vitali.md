MUZIO VITALI (died 1615) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Vieste (1613â€“1615).[1]

On 13 November 1613, Muzio Vitali was appointed during the papacy of Paul V as Bishop of Vieste.[2] On 17 November 1613, he was consecrated bishop by Giovanni Garzia Mellini, Cardinal-Priest of Santi Quattro Coronati with Giovanni Battista del Tufo, Bishop Emeritus of Acerra, and Antonio Seneca, Bishop of Anagni, serving as co-consecrators.[3] He served as Bishop of Vieste until his death in 1615.[4]
