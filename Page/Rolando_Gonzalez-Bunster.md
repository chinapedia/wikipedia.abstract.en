ROLANDO GONZALEZ-BUNSTER (born c. 1947) is a US-based Argentine businessman. He is the founder, chairman and chief executive officer of InterEnergy Holdings, a Cayman Islands-based energy company active in the Dominican Republic, Panama, Chile and Jamaica. A college friend of former President Bill Clinton, he is a director of the Clinton Foundation and a major donor to Hillary Clinton's 2016 presidential campaign.


Early life

Gonzalez-Bunster was born circa 1947 in Argentina.[1][2] He graduated from Georgetown University in Washington, D.C. in 1968.[3][4] While he was in college, he was friends with Bill Clinton.[5]


Career

Gonzalez-Bunster built a barge-mounted power plant with the Seaboard Corporation in the 1980s.[6]

Gonzalez-Bunster served as the vice president of Gulf and Western Industries.[7] He founded Basic Energy Ltd. (Bahamas), an operator and distributor of energy in the Dominican Republic, Panama and Jamaica.[8] He also served as the president of the Board of Directors of Empresa Generadora de Electricidad Haina,[9] a coal, oil and wind energy company active in the Dominican Republic.[10] Additionally, he was a partner in Remington Realty, a Texas-based real estate company, and an investor in AquaCube, a Scottish water purification company.[11]

Gonzalez-Bunster founded InterEnergy Holdings, a Cayman Islands-based energy company active in the Dominican Republic, Panama, Chile and Jamaica,[12] in 2011.[13] He serves as its chairman and CEO.[14][15] Gonzalez-Bunster also serves as the president and director of Consorcio Energetico Punta Cana Macao (CEPM),[16] a wind and solar energy producer which distributes electricity in the Dominican towns of Punta Cana, BÃ¡varo and Bayahibe.[17]


Philanthropy and political contributions

Gonzalez-Bunster is a member of the Institute of the Americas.[18] He serves on the board of directors of the Latin American Board of his alma mater, Georgetown University.[19] In 2013, he organized the launch of the Georgetown Alumni Club in the Dominican Republic.[20]

Gonzalez-Bunster visited Haiti alongside his daughter, Dr Paul Farmer and Bill Clinton shortly after the 2010 earthquake.[21][22] has served on the board of directors of the Clinton Foundation since 2013.[23]

Gonzalez-Bunster donated $30,000 to Virginia Governor Terry McAuliffe in 2009 and 2013.[24] He donated US$100,000 to the Hillary Victory Fund, a SuperPAC which supports Hillary Clinton's 2016 presidential campaign.[25]


Personal life

Gonzalez-Bunster resides in Greenwich, Connecticut,[26] with another property, Casa Pacifica, in Casa de Campo, La Romana, the Dominican Republic.[27] He has five children,[28] including a son, Luis, who is paraplegic;[29][30] and two daughters, Adriana and Carolina.[31] His daughter Carolina Gonzalez-Bunster, a former Goldman Sachs banker and the founder of the Walkabout Foundation,[32] is married to Stefano Bonfiglio, the co-founder of private equity firm Stirling Square Capital Partners, and they live in Knightsbridge, London.[33][34] Her wedding was attended by Hillary and Bill Clinton.[35]
