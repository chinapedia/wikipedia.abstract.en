_PSEUDOCOREMIA LUTEA_ is a species of moth in the Geometridae family. It is endemic to New Zealand. It is classified as "At Risk, Naturally Uncommon" by the Department of Conservation.


Taxonomy

This species was first described by Alfred Philpott in 1914 using specimens collected at Bold Peak, Humboldt Range by C. Fenwick and George Howes in December and January.[1] Philpott named the species _Selidosema lutea._ George Hudson, using the same name, described and illustrated this species in his 1928 book _The Butterflies and Moths of New Zealand_.[2] In 1988 John S. Dugdale assigned the species to the genus _Pseudocoremia_.[3] The holotype specimen is held at the Museum of New Zealand Te Papa Tongarewa.[4]

The biology and host species of this moth is unknown.[8]


Conservation status

This species has been classified under the New Zealand Threat Classification system as being "At Risk, Naturally Uncommon".[9]
