The following is a timeline of the history of the city of Cádiz, Spain.


Prior to 20th century

_See also: Cádiz_

-   1104 BCE - Gadir founded by Phoenicians.[1]
-   530 BCE - Gades occupied by Carthaginians.
-   711 CE - Moors in power; city called "Jezirat-Kadis."
-   1241 - Roman Catholic Diocese of Cádiz established.[2]
-   1262 - Cadiz taken by Alfonso X of Castile.
-   1587 - Spanish fleet attacked and Cadiz raided by Drake of England.
-   1596 - Capture of Cádiz by English and Dutch forces; city sacked.[3]
-   1602 - rebuilt.[4]
-   1625 - November: Attempted English and Dutch Cádiz Expedition.
-   1656 - 9 September: Battle of Cádiz; English win.
-   1702 - Battle of Cádiz.
-   1717 - Casa de Contratación (House of Trade) relocated to Cadiz from Seville.[5]
-   1722 - Cádiz Cathedral construction begins.
-   1748 - Real Colegio de Cirugía de la Armada established.^((es))
-   1749 - Jardín Botánico (garden) founded (approximate date).
-   1778 - "Colonial monopoly of the with the American colonies is abolished."[6]
-   1797 - June: British Assault on Cádiz; Spaniards win.
-   1810
    -   February: French Siege of Cádiz begins.
    -   24 September: Cádiz Cortes (national assembly) convenes in Cadiz.
-   1812
    -   19 March: Spanish Constitution of 1812 adopted after deliberations in Cadiz.
    -   August: Siege of Cádiz ends.
-   1823
    -   May: King of Spain Ferdinand VII imprisoned at Cadiz.
    -   31 August: Battle of Trocadero.
-   1829 - "Cadiz declared a free port."
-   1838 - Cádiz Cathedral construction completed.
-   1842 - Population: 53,922.[7]
-   1867 - _Diario de Cádiz_ newspaper begins publication.[8]
-   1873 - Cantonalist proclaimed.
-   1900 - Population: 69,382.[9]


20th century

-   1905 - Gran Teatro Falla (theatre) built.
-   1910 - Cádiz Club de Fútbol formed.
-   1932 - Cine Gades (cinema) active.[10]
-   1949 - (theatre) opens.
-   1950 - Population: 100,249.[11]
-   1955 - Estadio Ramón de Carranza (stadium) opens.
-   1969 - (film festival) begins.[12]
-   1970 - Museum of Cádiz established.
-   1979 - University of Cádiz established.
-   1986 - begins.[13]
-   1991 - Population: 157,355.[14]
-   1995 - Teófila Martínez becomes mayor.


21st century

-   2011 - Population: 124,014.[15]
-   2015 - becomes mayor.
