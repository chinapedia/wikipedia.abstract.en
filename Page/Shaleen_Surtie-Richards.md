SHALEEN SURTIE-RICHARDS (born 7 May 1955, Upington, Cape Province [now Northern Cape Province], South Africa)[1] is a South African television, stage, and film actress. She is perhaps best known for her starring roles in the 1988 film _Fiela se Kind_ and the long-running series _Egoli: Place of Gold._ She has performed in both Afrikaans and English.


Early life and career

Surtie-Richards was born in Upington, Cape Province, South Africa, on 7 May 1955 and schooled both there and in Cape Town. Her father was a school principal, and her mother was a teacher.[2] Despite growing up during the Apartheid era, she has stated that her background was not a disadvantaged one.[3] As a child, when a local music school would not admit her because of her colour, her father bought her a piano and hired a teacher.[4] She also took ballet and tennis classes.[5]

Qualifying as a kindergarten teacher, Surtie-Richards taught in Upington and Cape Town between 1974 and 1984.[6] She took many roles in several amateur stage productions between 1974 and 1981 and was also active in productions of the South African Department of Educationâ€™s Theatre-in-Education from 1982 to 1984.[7] She launched her professional acting career in 1984.[8]


Television career

Surtie-Richards had a starring role in _Egoli: Place of Gold,_ South Africa's first television soap opera.[9] She appeared in most of the show's 18-year run as Ester (Nenna) Willemse.[10]

She has also appeared in a number other South African television programmes, including the soap opera's _7de Laan,_ _Villa Rosa,_ and _Generations._[11] In 2000, she hosted her own talk show, _Shaleen,_ on South Africa's M-Net channel.[12]

Surtie-Richards was also a judge on the reality competition television series _Supersterre_ from 2006-2010.[13]

In 2013, she appeared as one of the "roasters" in _The Comedy Central Roast of Steve Hofmeyr,_ the inaugural roast conducted by the Comedy Central South Africa channel, which was hosted by Trevor Noah.[14]


Film career

Surtie-Richards has played starring or supporting roles in the following films:[15]

-   _Fiela se Kind_ (1988)
-   _Mama Jack_ (2005)
-   _Egoli: Afrikaners Is Plesierig_ (2010)
-   _Knysna_ (2014)
-   _Treurgrond_ (2015)
-   _Twee Grade van Moord_ (2016)
-   _Vaselinetjie_ (2017)


Theatre career

Surtie-Richards has appeared on stage throughout South Africa and in London.[16][17] She has also appeared at major theatre and arts festivals in South Africa and abroad, including the Aardklop Festival,[18] the Klein Karoo Festival,[19] the Grahamstown Festival,[20] Suidoosterfees,[21] and the Edinburgh Fringe Festival.[22]

She has appeared on stage in works by Shakespeare,[23] Willy Russell,[24] Athol Fugard,[25] and Pieter-Dirk Uys.[26]


Awards

During her career, Surtie-Richards has won more than 40 awards.[27]

Surtie-Richards won the Best Actress Award at the 1985 Fleur du Cap Theatre Awards for her role as Hester in Athol Fugard's _Hallo en Koebaai (Hello and Goodbye)._[28] In 2009, she won another Fleur du Cap Award for her 2008 performance in _Shirley Valentine._[29] In 2008, for her performance in _Shirley Valentine,_ she also won the Herrie Prize for most popular production at the Klein Karoo Arts Festival.[30]

At the 2014 Royalty Soapie Awards, Surtie-Richards received the Lifetime Achievement Award for her 30-year contribution to television and her roles on series such as _Egoli: Place of Gold, Generations,_ and _7de Laan._[31] Surtie-Richards was also nominated for Outstanding Lead Actress for her role in _Villa Rosa_ at the 2014 awards.[32]

Surtie-Richards was honoured with the Lifetime Achievement Award at the 2015 Naledi Theatre Awards.[33]
