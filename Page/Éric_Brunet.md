ÉRIC BRUNET (born 22 July 1964) is a French author, political commentator and radio host best known for his support of right-wing policies. He has often supported the political agenda of President Nicolas Sarkozy and later criticised the views of François Hollande.

Since 2010, he has hosted _Radio Brunet_ (known as _Carrément Brunet_ until 2016) on RMC with 700,000 daily listeners as of 2018. Since 2012, he regularly appears on BFM TV, most notably in shows hosted by Apolline de Malherbe; starting 2018 he hosts _Brunet Vendredi 19h_ on Fridays at 7 p.m. He has also regularly appeared as a guest on Jean-Jacques Bourdin's daily radio show, _Bourdin Direct_, since 2014. Brunet published several books and opinion articles, in various magazines including _L'Express_ and _Valeurs Actuelles_;[1] he is known abroad for calling France "irreformable".

Brunet grew up in Nantes and studied at the French Press Institute and Panthéon-Assas University. Following a proposition of Édouard Courtial, a member of the National Assembly for Oise, he was made a Knight of the Legion of Honour in 2012 by the Ministry of Culture, as part of its annual promotion.[2]
