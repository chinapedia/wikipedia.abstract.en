MARY VINGOE is a Canadian playwright and theatre director.[1] Her play _Refuge_ was a shortlisted nominee for the Governor General's Award for English-language drama at the 2016 Governor General's Awards.[2]

Originally from Dartmouth, Nova Scotia,[3] Vingoe studied theatre at Dalhousie University and the University of Toronto's Graduate Centre for Study of Drama.[4] She was a founder of Toronto's Nightwood Theatre in 1979,[5] and later co-founded Ship's Company Theatre in Parrsboro and Eastern Front Theatre in Halifax.[6] Plays she has directed or produced include Wendy Lill's _The Glace Bay Miner's Museum_, George Boyd's _Consecrated Ground_, Catherine Banks's _It Is Solved By Walking_ and George Elliott Clarke's _Whylah Falls_ and _Beatrice Chancy_.[7]

From 2002 to 2007, she was artistic director of the Magnetic North Theatre Festival.[8]

Her plays have included _The Company Store_, _Herring Gull's Egg_, _Holy Ghosters_, _Hooligans_ and _Living Curiosities: A Story of Anna Swan_.[9]

She was named an Officer of the Order of Canada in 2010.[10]
