SIGISMONDO ISEI (1 May, 1620 – September 1670) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Comacchio (1655–1670).[1]


Biography

Sigismondo Isei was born in Cesene, Italy on 1 May 1620 and ordained a priest on 16 September 1645.[2] On 30 August 1655, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Alexander VII as Bishop of Comacchio.[3][4] On 12 September 1655, he was consecrated bishop by Giovanni Battista Maria Pallotta, Cardinal-Priest of San Pietro in Vincoli, Patrizio Donati, Bishop Emeritus of Minori, Taddeo Altini, Bishop of Civita Castellana e Orte, serving as co-consecrators.[5] He served as Bishop of Comacchio until his death in September 1670.[6][7]
