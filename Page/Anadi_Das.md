ANADI CHARAN DAS was an Indian politician, belonging to the Revolutionary Communist Party of India.[1]

Das was a Central Committee member of RCPI.[2] Das contested the Howrah West constituency seat of the West Bengal Legislative Assembly in the 1957 election.[3] Das finished in third place with 5,378 votes (20.04%).[4]

Das won the Howrah West constituency seat in the West Bengal Legislative Assembly in the 1962 election.[5][6] He defeated the incumbent assembly speaker Bankim Chandra Kar.[7][8][9] Das obtained 19,770 votes (44.59%).[10] He contested the Howrah Central constituency seat in the 1967 election.[11] Das finished in second place with 15,663 votes (32.48%).[12] As of 1967 he served as president of the National Screw & Wire Products Workmen's Union as well as the Asia Electric Workers' Union, both being affiliated with the All India Trade Union Congress.[13]

He won the Howrah Central seat in the 1969 election.[14] Das obtained 28,522 votes (58.85%).[15]

Das and the other RCPI legislator M. Mokshed Ali were expelled from RCPI by party general secretary Sudhindranath Kumar in July 1969 for "anti-Party and anti-UF activities".[16][17][18] The expulsion provoked a split in RCPI, with Anadi Das leading his own RCPI faction.[19] Ahead of the 1971 West Bengal Legislative Assembly election the RCPI (Anadi Das group) joined the Communist Party of India-led Eight Party Coalition.[20] Das ran on an independent ticket in Howrah Central, against Kumar.[21] Das finished in fourth place with 2,711 votes (8.55%).[22]

Das' faction later joined the S.N. Tagore-led RCPI faction.[23] After the death of Tagore, RCPI (S.N. Tagore group) was split with Das leading one of the factions.[24]
