EDSON SECCO (December 26, 1976, São Paulo, Brazil) is a Brazilian artist, composer, musician and sound designer.[1]


Biography

He began his professional career in the late 90s as music producer and sound engineer. In the early 2000s he began producing soundtrack for plays, as well as performing the songs live. In 2006 he started his film career with the documentary _Days in Sintra_[2] as composer, sound editor and sound re-recording mixer.

In 2005 he joined the _Dry Opera Company_, directed by Gerald Thomas, being responsible for the composition, sound design and execution of several theatrical soundtracks.

His work on the film industry earned him two nominations for The 3rd Platino Award For Iberoamerican Cinema (2016),[3] and the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences of Argentina (2015),[4] respectively, as well as three prizes of Best Sound Design at the Brazilia Film Festival (2010[5] and 2013[6]) and Gramado Festival (2013[7]).

He is best known for his work with directors Eryk Rocha, Petra Costa, Walter Salles and Daniela Thomas.

As a musician he is part of the Brazilian electronic duo NU[8] (abbreviation for Naked Universe), alongside the singer and songwriter Ligiana Costa.[9]
