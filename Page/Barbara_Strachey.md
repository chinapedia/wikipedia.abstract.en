BARBARA HALPERN STRACHEY (17 July 1912 – 15 October 1999 in Oxford, England) was a British author.[1]

Barbara Strachey was born to parents Oliver Strachey, a cryptographer in World War I and World War II, and the feminist politician, writer, and amateur painter Ray Strachey. She attended schools in Switzerland, Vienna, and Oxford High School.[2] She studied history at Lady Margaret Hall, Oxford University.[3] Later she worked for the BBC for a period, and was involved in the transformation of the General Overseas Service into the World Service in 1965.[4]

In 1934, Barbara Strachey married Olaf Hultin, the son of Professor Arvid Hultin of Helsingfors, but they divorced in 1937.[5] They had one son, Roger. She married for a second time in 1937, to Wolf Halpern, the son of Dr George Halpern of Jerusalem, who was killed in WWII. After the death of her brother, the computer scientist Christopher Strachey, she moved to a small house in Jericho, Oxford in 1975. She took up writing and was interested in the works of J. R. R. Tolkien.


Books

Strachey published a number of books:[6]

-   _Remarkable Relations_ (1980)
-   _Journeys of Frodo_ (1981)
-   _Mary Berenson – A Self-Portrait from Her Diaries and Letters_ (1984, with Jayne Samuels)
-   _The Strachey Line_ (1985)
-   _L'atlas du Seigneur des Anneaux_ (with Jérôme Lereculey)
-   _The Cause: A Short History of the Women's Movement in Great Britain_ (with Ray Strachey)
