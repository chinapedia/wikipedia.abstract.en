BERNARD LAMARCHE-VADEL (16 July 1949, Avallon.[1] – 2 May 2000, La Croixille in Mayenne) was a French writer, poet, art critic and collector.


Life

The son of a veterinarian, self-taught, his tastes for art and literature earned him a paternal anathema. At 19 years he met whose name he joined to his. A graduate from the École pratique des hautes études in art sociology (1970), he subsequently taught at the Paris-I Panthéon-Sorbonne University and then at ICART in 1979. A poet and short stories writer, Bernard Lamarche-Vadel composed a work of art critic in the 1970s and founded the magazine _Artistes_.[2] He also organized numerous exhibitions ("Jean Degottex. Rétrospective", 1978, musée d'Art moderne (ARC), Paris / « Finir en beauté », juin 1981[3])

He committed suicide in 2000 aged 50, in his castle of La Rongère.

His photographic collection was stored at in Chalon-sur-Saône.

An exhibition devoted to his work of art critic was presented in 2009 by the Musée d'Art Moderne de la Ville de Paris.
