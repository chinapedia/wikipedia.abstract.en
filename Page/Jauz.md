SAM VOGEL, better known by his stage name JAUZ ( ),[1] is an American DJ and electronic dance music producer based in San Francisco, United States.[2] He is also the founder and CEO of "Bite This"


Early life

Vogel grew up in Northern California.[3] He began playing guitar and producing electronic music on his computer at the age of 15.[4][5][6] He went to Icon Collective music production school.[7][8][9] He previously went to a film school in Loyola Marymount University before deciding to pursue a musical career, prompting him to enroll in Icon Collective.[10] He began releasing future house tracks before adopting the stage name Jauz.[11] His career kickstarted when fellow musician Kennedy Jones had played one of his remixes.[12] Jones also had introduced him to his manager, Moe Shalizi which helped Vogel to release music on Borgore's record label, Buygore.[13]


Musical style

Vogel produces chill trap, bass house and dubstep.[14] He also incorporates heavy bass sounds similar to the styles in the 80s and 90s.[15] Vogel uses Ableton as his digital audio workstation.[16]


Career

2013

Before starting his professional career, he used "Escape Dubstep" as a stage name.[17] He later used 'Jauz' because he thought it was short, simple and catchy despite hating the word.[18] The name was also described by Vogel as a slang word for 'bullshit' and a liar.[19] He started his career by uploading remixes.[20] His songs caught the attention of well-known DJs such as Diplo, Skrillex and Borgore.[21]

2014

He released the single "Feel the Volume" via Diplo's record label, Mad Decent.[22]

2015
