GANDHOL is a village and former non-salute only koli samaj princely state on Saurashtra peninsula in Gujarat, western India.


History

The petty princely state, _prant_, was ruled by Chieftains. In 1901 it comprised a single village, with a population of 137, yielding 2,000 Rupees state revenue (1903-4, mostly from land), paying 111 Rupees tribute, to the Bhavnagar[1]
