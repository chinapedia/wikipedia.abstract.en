ELIAS UDDIN MOLLAH (born 2 March 1971) is a Bangladesh Awami League politician and a member of National Parliament of Bangladesh.[1] [2]He was elected from Dhaka-16 constituency.[3]


Career

Elias was born to Harun al-Rashid Mollah, who was also an MP.[4] He has not studied beyond intermediate level.[5]


Controversies

2014 Kalshi clashes

In 2014 he was accused of being involved in an arson attack on a refugee camp of Stranded Pakistanis in Mirpur, Dhaka in which 10 people died.[6][7] Mollah denied involvement and blamed a "vested conspiracy" against him.[8]

Racist comments

In 2015 he was made racists comments about black people.[9] After a recent trip to the Congo, he said to journalists:[10]

  Our army has gone there (Africa) to civilise those black people. I am sure they will accomplish the task.

He constantly referred to the Congolese as "uncivilized black people" and added "People there are yet to become civilised. They take bath every 15 days. After applying soaps before bath, they do not even use water in a bid to retain the aroma."[11] When pointed out that he was being racist and whether the UN had entrusted the Bangladeshi peacekeepers with the responsibility to make Africans "civilised", Mollah said, "No... we are only assisting them to get civilised."[12]

Corruption allegations

In 2016 he accused of trying to illegally occupy private homes.[13]
