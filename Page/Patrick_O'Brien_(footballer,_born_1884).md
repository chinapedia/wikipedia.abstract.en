PATRICK ALOYSIUS O’BRIEN was a Scottish amateur football centre forward who appeared in the Scottish League for Queen's Park.[1]


Personal life

O'Brien had a wife, four sons and two daughters.[2] He served as a captain in the Royal Army Medical Corps during the First World War and later became a casualty surgeon for the Glasgow Police.[3] In May 1941, during the Second World War, O'Brien was called to examine Nazi Deputy Führer Rudolf Hess, who had landed near Eaglesham on an attempted peace mission.[4]
