The WINNEBAGO RIVER BRIDGE is a historic structure located north of Mason City, Iowa, United States. The span carries U.S. Route 65 over the Winnebago River for .[1] This is the second span at this location. The stone abutments from the previous bridge were utilized in this one. They were sheathed in concrete by the Concrete Engineering Company, and William Henkel of Mason City constructed the bridge. It is composed of three concrete spans with a center span cantilevered from shorter anchor spans.[2] It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1998.[3] A second span has subsequently been built to the west.
