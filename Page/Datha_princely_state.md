DATHA is a town and former non-salute Rajput princely state on Saurashtra peninsula in Gujarat, western India.

The village lies on the Bagad river, in Kathiawar.


History

The princely state, in Gohelwar _prant_, was ruled by Sarvaiya Rajput Chieftains.

In 1901 it comprised 24 villages, covering 69 square km, with a population of 9,452, yielding 31,339 Rupees state revenue (1903-4, mostly from land), and paid 5,398 Rupees tribute, to the Gaikwar Baroda State and Junagadh State.
