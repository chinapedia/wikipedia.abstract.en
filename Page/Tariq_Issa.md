TARIQ AHMED ISSA (born 2 September 1997) is a footballer who plays as a forward for club Colchester United.

Issa joined Colchester United's Academy at the age of 12 from Trinity Football Club based in Southend-on-Sea. He made his professional debut for the club in 2016. In August 2018, he joined Needham Market on loan.


Career

Forward Issa joined the Colchester United Academy at the age of 12 from Trinity Football Club in Southend-on-Sea. He broke into Colchester's under-18 side while still an under-16 player,[1] and was the first Colchester Academy player to come through the educational system associated with Thurstable School in Tiptree.[2] He and teammate Cameron James signed four-year contracts with the club in July 2015,[3] before agreeing a new four-year deal in July 2016.[4]

Issa made his first-team debut in the EFL Trophy on 4 October 2016, replacing Dion Sembie-Ferris at half-time in Colchester's 2–1 defeat to Southampton U23.[5] He made his English Football League debut on 19 August 2017 as a substitute for Drey Wright in Colchester's 3–0 defeat against Luton Town.[6]

On 30 August 2018, Issa signed for Southern League Premier Division Central side Needham Market on loan until January 2019.[7][8] He made his debut on 1 September as a half-time substitute in Needham's 3–0 defeat at Alvechurch.[9]
