SCOTT SAVITT is a former foreign correspondent for The Los Angeles Times, although according to Los Angeles Times records he has never held a staff position there, and United Press International in Beijing. His articles have been published in The Los Angeles Times,[1][2] Washington Post,[3] Wall Street Journal,[4] New York Times,[5] and many other publications.[6][7][8]

He has been interviewed on NPR, BBC, ABC’s Nightline and the CBS News. He is the in-house Chinese-English translator for numerous human rights organizations. In 1994, he founded Beijing Scene, China’s first independent weekly newspaper.[9] In 2003 he published China Now magazine. http://www.chinanowmag.com

He’s the founding editor of the award-winning Contexts magazine http://www.contexts.org. He was a visiting scholar at Duke University until his recent relocation to Ann Arbor, Michigan.[10][11][12]
