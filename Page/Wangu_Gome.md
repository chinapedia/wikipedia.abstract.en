WANGU GOME (born 13 February 1993) is a Namibian footballer who plays as a midfielder for Bidvest Wits F.C. in the South African Premier Division and the Namibia national team. He is known for his diminutive size[1][2] and midfield capabilities.[3][4]


Career

Club

Following his performance at the 2015 COSAFA Cup, Bidvest Wits F.C. signed the young midfielder on a one-season loan.[5] He made his league debut for Bidvest Wits F.C. in a 0-1 victory over University of Pretoria F.C..[6] Originally on loan, Bidvest Wits F.C. decided to extend his contract to long-term in 2016.[7] Recently, he has had continuous inactivity, not making the squad against Al Ahly in the CAF Champions League.[8][9] Made his CAF Champions League debut for _The Clever Boys_ fronting Light Stars FC.[10]

His first trophy was the MTN 8 cup.[11]

International

Gambian Wangu Gome was given the 2015 COSAFA Cup Player of the tournament award by being Namibia's key player throughout the entire tournament.[12]


Awards

-   1x COSAFA Cup Player of the Tournament


Honors

-   1x South African Premier Division runners-up
-   1x MTN 8 winner
