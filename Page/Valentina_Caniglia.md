VALENTINA CANIGLIA is an Italian-American cinematographer and director, who has received awards for her work. She is a member of the Italian Society of Cinematographers (AIC) and European federation of Cinematographers (IMAGO).[1]


Early life and education

Caniglia is from Naples, Italy.[2] When she was seven years old, her father bought her a Kodak film camera. She became interested in cinematography at the age of eight when her father showed her two films, _The Conformist_ (il Conformista) directed by Bernardo Bertolucci and _The Godfather_ directed by Francis Ford Coppola.[3]

Caniglia graduated from Istituto Tecnico 'Padre Pio'.[4] She moved to London to begin a career as a cinematographer. Then, she moved to New York City on a fellowship. She graduated from New York University with a Bachelor of Arts in Film Production[5] and began work in feature films, commercials, music videos and documentaries.[6]


Career

Caniglia is known for her lighting and camera work on the film _The Stand_.[7] For her work on the period film _Madeline's Oil_, she won Best Cinematography award at the Louisiana International film festival in 2015.[8][9] She worked on the film _Pomegranates and Myrrh_ (2008),[10][11] winner of the Golden Dagger at the Muscat Film Festival for Best Cinematography in 2009.[12] The film premiered at the Sundance Film Festival,[13] and received the Best Arab Film Award at Doha Tribeca Film Festival.[14] Among other films she worked on _Are You For Great Sex?_, winner of best cinematography award at the Hoboken International Film Festival;[15] _Fred Won't Move Out_ starring Elliott Gould;[16] and _Kelly & Cal_ (2014) starring Juliette Lewis.[17]

Caniglia has worked as a cinematographer for HBO, Voyage TV and Showtime.[18] She has lensed numerous nationally aired commercials for British Airways, Ford Motor Company, Nike, GBX Shoes, which received the Telly Award.[19] Caniglia has worked on music videos for Aesop Rock, which was number one on MTV's top ten chart in 2005; Enzo Gragnaniello; The Roots; Articolo 31, and The Stein.[20]

She has been on the faculty of the School of Visual Arts in New York City.[21]
