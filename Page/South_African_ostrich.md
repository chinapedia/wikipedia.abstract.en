The SOUTH AFRICAN OSTRICH (_Struthio camelus australis_), also known as the BLACK-NECKED OSTRICH, CAPE OSTRICH or SOUTHERN OSTRICH is a subspecies of the common ostrich endemic to Southern Africa. It is widely farmed for its meat, eggs and feathers.


Habitat and distribution

Ostriches_cape_point_cropped.jpg The South African ostrich is found in South Africa, Namibia, Zambia, Zimbabwe, Angola and Botswana.[1] It lives in south of the rivers Zambezi and Cunene.


Threats

It is farmed for its eggs, meat, leather and feathers in the Little Karoo area of Cape Province.
