SÉBASTIEN BOTTIN (1764-1853) was a French statistician and politician.


Early life

Sébastien Bottin was born on December 17, 1764 in Grimonviller, Meurthe, France.[1][2][3]


Career

Bottin served as a member of the Chamber of Representatives during the Hundred Days from May 11, 1815 to July 13, 1815, representing Nord.[4]

Bottin was the editor of the _Almanach du commerce de Paris_, founded by Jean de La Tynna.[5]

Bottin was also the co-founder of the Société libre des Sciences et Arts de Strasbourg in 1799.[6] He became a Knight of the Legion of Honour on July 30, 1814.[7] He became a member of the Société des Antiquaires de France on February 28, 1818.[8] He was awarded and honored by a prize in statistics from the Académie des Sciences in 1824, and received the honorary medal from the Société française de statistique universelle in 1833.[9]


Death and legacy

Rue_Sébastien-Bottin_-_nouvelle_plaque.jpg Bottin died on March 28, 1853 in Paris, France.[10][11] He was buried at the Père Lachaise Cemetery.[12] The _Rue Sébastien Bottin_ in the 7th arrondissement of Paris was named in his honor in 1929.[13]
