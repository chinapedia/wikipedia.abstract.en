_IDIOGLOSSA MIRACULOSA_ is a moth of the family Batrachedridae. It was described by Frey and Boll in 1878. It is found in North America, where it has been recorded from Florida, Illinois, North Carolina, Ohio, Tennessee and Texas.[1][2]

The wingspan is about 10Â mm.[3] Adults are yellow with metallic fasciae on the hindwings only. There are two or three generations per year.

The larvae feed on _Dichanthelium clandestinum_. They skeletonize the leaves of their host plant.[4]
