BANGLADESH FILM CENSOR BOARD is a regulatory agency that is responsible for the censorship of movies and is located in Dhaka, Bangladesh.[1]


History

The censor board was set up in 1977 following the framing of Bangladesh Censorship of Film Rules.[2] The agency is responsible for the censorship of locally produced movies, foreign imports and acts as the registration of film clubs in Bangladesh.[3] Martuza Ahmed the secretary of Ministry of Information is the ex-officio Chairman of the Board.[4] The board in 2015 blocked My Bicycle, the first chakma language (a minority language) in Bangladesh.[5] The Censor also blocked a movie about the Rana Plaza building collapse in which more than a thousand garment workers died.[6][7] A movie was banned where the villain wore a Mujib coat, a style associated with the founding president of Bangladesh, Sheikh Mujib.[8]
