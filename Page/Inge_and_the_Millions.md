_INGE AND THE MILLIONS_ (German: _INGE UND DIE MILLIONEN_) is a 1933 German comedy film directed by Erich Engel and starring Brigitte Helm, Carl Esmond and Paul Wegener.[1] The film's sets were designed by Otto Erdmann and Hans Sohnle. Location filming took place in Berlin and around Lake Constance.

In line with the policies of the new Nazi government, the film was fiercely anti-capitalist and attacked in particular Jewish financial speculators.[2]
