_THE WEEK_ was a seminal literary magazine in Canada published between 1883 and 1896.[1][2] It was subtitled as _Canadian Journal of Politics, Society and Literature_.[3] It was "Canada's leading political and literary periodical".[4] The magazine was headquartered in Toronto.[5][6] Prominent contributors included poet Charles G. D. Roberts; journalist and novelist Sara Jeannette Duncan; and political critic and intellectual Goldwin Smith.[7][8] Smith also edited the magazine.[9]
