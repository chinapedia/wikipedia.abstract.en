INTRAUTERINE DEVICE (IUD) WITH COPPER also known as INTRAUTERINE COIL, is a type of intrauterine device which contains copper.[1] It is used for birth control and emergency contraception within five days of unprotected sex.[2] It is one of the most effective forms of birth control with a one-year failure rate around 0.7%.[3] The device is placed in the uterus and lasts up to twelve years.[4][5][6] It may be used by women of all ages regardless of whether or not they have had children.[7] Following removal, fertility quickly returns.[8]
