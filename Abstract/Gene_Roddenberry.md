EUGENE WESLEY RODDENBERRY (August 19, 1921 – October 24, 1991) was an American television screenwriter, producer and creator of the original _Star Trek_ television series, and its first spin-off _The Next Generation_. Born in El Paso, Texas, Roddenberry grew up in Los Angeles, where his father was a police officer. Roddenberry flew 89 combat missions in the Army Air Forces during World War II, and worked as a commercial pilot after the war. Later, he followed in his father's footsteps and joined the Los Angeles Police Department, where he also began to write scripts for television.
