MARGO GRANT WALSH is a 1987 _Interior Design Magazine_ Hall of Fame Inductee[1] and connoisseur of Silver serving pieces, mostly English and American. She graduated from the University of Oregon Summa Cum Laude honors with a Bachelor of Interior architecture in 1960.[2] She followed her education with 13 years at design firm Skidmore, Owings & Merrill in San Francisco. At the time the world's largest architecture firm, Grant Walsh quickly rose through the ranks at SOM, and became the highest-ranking executive in the firm's burgeoning interiors business. She later took a position with Gensler and Associates in 1973, eventually becoming one of its vice presidents. Since retirement in 2004, Walsh has spent her time curating her vast silver collection.
