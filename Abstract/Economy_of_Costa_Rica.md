The ECONOMY OF COSTA RICA has been very stable for some years now, with continuing growth in the GDP (Gross Domestic Product) and moderate inflation, though with a high unemployment rate: 8.2% in 2016.[1] The economy emerged from recession in 1997 and has shown strong aggregate growth since then.[2][3] The estimated GDP for 2017 is US$61.5 billion, up significantly from the US$52.6 billion in 2015[4] while the estimated 2017 per capita (purchasing power parity) is US$12,382.[5]
