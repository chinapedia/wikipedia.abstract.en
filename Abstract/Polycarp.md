POLYCARP (; , _Pol√Ωkarpos_; ; AD 69 155) was a 2nd-century Christian bishop of Smyrna.[1] According to the _Martyrdom of Polycarp_ he died a martyr, bound and burned at the stake, then stabbed when the fire failed to touch him.[2] Polycarp is regarded as a saint and Church Father in the Eastern Orthodox, Oriental Orthodox, Catholic, Anglican, and Lutheran churches. His name 'Polycarp' means 'much fruit' in Greek.
