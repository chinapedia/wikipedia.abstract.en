The PALEOZOIC (or PALAEOZOIC) ERA (; from the Greek _palaios_ (παλαιός), "old" and _zoe_ (ζωή), "life", meaning "ancient life"[1][2]) is the earliest of three geologic eras of the Phanerozoic Eon. It is the longest of the Phanerozoic eras, lasting from , and is subdivided into six geologic periods (from oldest to youngest): the Cambrian, Ordovician, Silurian, Devonian, Carboniferous, and Permian. The Paleozoic comes after the Neoproterozoic Era of the Proterozoic Eon and is followed by the Mesozoic Era.
