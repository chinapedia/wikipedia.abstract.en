RIO DE JANEIRO (; ;[1] _River of January_), or simply RIO,[2] is anchor to the Rio de Janeiro metropolitan area and the second-most populous municipality in Brazil and the sixth-most populous in the Americas. Rio de Janeiro is the capital of the state of Rio de Janeiro, Brazil's third-most populous state. Part of the city has been designated as a World Heritage Site, named "_Rio de Janeiro: Carioca Landscapes between the Mountain and the Sea_", by UNESCO on 1 July 2012 as a Cultural Landscape.[3]
