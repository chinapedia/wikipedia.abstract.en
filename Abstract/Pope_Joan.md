Bibliothèque nationale de France, c. 1560.]] Joannes_septimus.jpg's religious Nuremberg Chronicle, published in 1493]] POPE JOAN (_Ioannes Anglicus_; 855–857) was, according to legend, a woman who reigned as pope for a few years during the Middle Ages. Her story first appeared in chronicles in the 13th century and subsequently spread throughout Europe. The story was widely believed for centuries, but most modern scholars regard it as fictional.[1][2][3]
