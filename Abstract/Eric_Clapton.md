ERIC PATRICK CLAPTON, (born 1945) is an English rock and blues guitarist, singer, and songwriter. He is the only three-time inductee to the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame: once as a solo artist and separately as a member of the Yardbirds and of Cream. Clapton has been referred to as one of the most important and influential guitarists of all time.[1] Clapton ranked second in _Rolling Stone_ magazine's list of the "100 Greatest Guitarists of All Time"[2] and fourth in Gibson{{'}}s "Top 50 Guitarists of All Time".[3] He was also named number five in _Time_ magazine's list of "The 10 Best Electric Guitar Players" in 2009.[4]
