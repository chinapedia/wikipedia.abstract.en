MARIAN STOLTZ-LOIKE is an American professor of psychology and human resources development who currently serves as the dean Lander College for Women and as Vice President of Touro College in charge of online education. She has served several roles as a leader in the American Orthodox Jewish community, and is an associate vice president of the Orthodox Union, one of the first women to hold this role.[1] She is a contributor for U.S. News & World Report.[2]
