Emanuel_Goldenweiser.jpg EMANUEL ALEXSNDROVICH GOLDENWEISER was born in Kiev, Russia, on July 31, 1883. His father was a prominent member of the Kiev bar. The family was modestly wealthy and cosmopolitan. Upon graduation from the First Kiev Gymnasium in 1902, Emanuel followed his older brother, Alexander (Shura), to the United States. He received a BA from Columbia University in 1903, and MA from Cornell in 1905, and a Phd from Cornell in 1907. His PhD thesis subject was “Russian Immigration to the U.S.” (Roughly two million Jews emigrated from Russia between 1880 and 1920; many came to the U.S.) He became a U.S. citizen in the same year.
