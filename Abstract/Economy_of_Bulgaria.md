The ECONOMY OF BULGARIA functions on the principles of the free market, having a large private sector and a smaller public one. Bulgaria is an industrialised upper-middle-income country according to the World Bank,[1] and is a member of the European Union (EU), World Trade Organization (WTO), Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe (OSCE) and Organization of the Black Sea Economic Cooperation (BSEC). The Bulgarian economy has experienced rapid growth reaching estimated gross domestic product (GDP) of $162.68 billion (PPP, 2018 est.), GDP per capita of $23,207 (PPP, 2018 est.)[2], and average monthly gross salary of 1205 leva (616 euro) (December, 2018).[3] The national currency is the lev (plural leva), pegged to the euro at a rate of 1.95583 leva for 1 euro.[4] The lev is the strongest and most stable currency in Eastern Europe.[5][6]
