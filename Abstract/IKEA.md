IKEA (, ) is a Swedish-founded multinational group that designs and sells , kitchen appliances and home products, among other useful goods and occasionally home services. It has been the world's largest furniture retailer since at least 2008.[1][2][3][4][5] It was founded in Sweden in 1943 by 17-year-old carpenter Ingvar Kamprad, who was listed by _Forbes_ in 2015 as one of the ten richest people in the world, worth more than $40 billion.[6][7][8] The company's name is an acronym that consists of Kamprad's initials with those of Elmtaryd (the farm where he grew up), and Agunnaryd (his hometown in Sm√•land, southern Sweden).[9][10]
