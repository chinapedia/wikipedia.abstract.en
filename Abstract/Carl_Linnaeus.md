CARL LINNAEUS (;[1][2] 23 May[3] 1707 – 10 January 1778), also known after his ennoblement as CARL VON LINNÉ[4] (), was a Swedish botanist, physician, and zoologist who formalised binomial nomenclature, the modern system of naming organisms. He is known as the "father of modern taxonomy".[5] Many of his writings were in Latin, and his name is rendered in Latin as (after 1761 CAROLUS A LINNÉ).
