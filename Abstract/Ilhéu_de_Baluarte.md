ILHÉU DE BALUARTE is a small uninhabited islet of the Cape Verde archipelago located 2 km off the northeastern coast of the island of Boa Vista. It is the easternmost land mass of Cape Verde. Its distance to the African mainland is about 560 km. The islet and its surrounding marine area forms the protected area _Reserva Natural Integral Ilhéu de Baluarte_.[1] Its area is and the protected marine area around it is 87 hectares.[2][3] Its highest point is less than five meters; it is a flat and rocky isle made up of basaltic rocks.[4] It became an integral protected natural area in 1990, visits can only be done by the permission of local authorities.[5]
