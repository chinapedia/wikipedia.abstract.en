SABRINA SAQEB is an Afghan politician who served as a member of parliament from 2005 to 2010.[1][2][3][4] She received media attention in 2009 when she participated in and helped organize protests against a law passed by Afghan president Hamid Karzai pertaining to Shiite personal law.[5][6] Among other things, the law required women to have sex with their husbands at a certain frequency, and required women to get permission before leaving their house.[7] After completing her tenure in parliament, Saqeb co-founded an organization called the "Research Institute for Women, Peace and Security," and became an advocate for Women's rights.[8]
