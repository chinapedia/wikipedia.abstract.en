He was also a charter member of the Fellowship of Southern Writers. He founded the literary journal _The Southern Review_ with Cleanth Brooks in 1935. He received the 1947 Pulitzer Prize for the Novel for his novel _All the King's Men_ (1946) and the Pulitzer Prize for Poetry in 1958 and 1979. He is the only person to have won Pulitzer Prizes for both fiction and poetry.[1]
