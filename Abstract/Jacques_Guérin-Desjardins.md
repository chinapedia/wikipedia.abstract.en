JACQUES GUÉRIN-DESJARDINS (; 1894 in November – 1982) was the National Commissioner of Eclaireurs Unionistes de France from 1923 to 1936.[1] He had been a Boy Scout in Britain where he was educated, attended the Birmingham Scout Rally in 1913, and served as the interpreter of Lord Baden-Powell[2][3] at International Conferences and World Jamborees. He was a recipient of the Silver Wolf Award, the highest award made by The Scout Association "for services of the most exceptional character.".
