Blessed COLOMBA MATYLDA GABRIEL (3 May 1858 - 24 September 1926) - in religious JANINA - was a Ukrainian Roman Catholic professed religious and the founder of the Benedictine Sisters of Charity.[1] Gabriel studied under the Order of Saint Benedict in Lviv and later became a Benedictine herself while dedicating herself to teaching at her old school before she was forced to relocate to Rome in 1900 where she founded her order and joined a Benedictine branch there.[2]
