The MERCEDES-BENZ OM 138 is a diesel engine manufactured by Daimler-Benz. In total, 5,719 units were produced between 1935 and 1940.[1][2][3] It was the first diesel engine especially developed and made for a passenger car. The first vehicle powered by the OM 138 was the Mercedes-Benz W 138.[4] The light Mercedes-Benz trucks L 1100 and L 1500 as well as the bus O 1500[5] were also offered with the OM 138 as an alternative to the standard Otto engine.[6]
