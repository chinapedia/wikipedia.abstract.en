JOHN THE BAPTIST ( _Yokhanan HaMatbil_, , _Iōánnēs ho baptistḗs_ or , _Iōánnēs ho baptízōn_,[1][2][3][4][5] ,[6] [7][8][9]; Late 1st century BC – 28–36 AD) was a Jewish itinerant preacher[10] in the early first century AD. Other titles for John include JOHN THE FORERUNNER in Eastern Christianity and "the prophet John (_Yaḥyā_)" in Islam. To clarify the meaning of "Baptist", he is sometimes alternatively called JOHN THE BAPTIZER.[11][12][13]
