Hydroponic_onions_nasa.jpg researcher checking hydroponic onions (center), Bibb lettuces (left), and radishes (right)|alt=|border]] HYDROPONICS is a subset of hydroculture, which is a method of growing plants without soil by instead using mineral nutrient solutions in a water solvent.[1] Terrestrial plants may be grown with only their roots exposed to the nutritious liquid, or the roots may be physically supported by an inert medium such as perlite or gravel.
