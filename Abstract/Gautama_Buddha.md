GAUTAMA BUDDHA (c. 563/480 – c. 483/400 BCE), also known as SIDDHĀRTHA GAUTAMA (सिद्धार्थ गौतम) in Sanskrit or SIDDHATTHA GOTAMA (𑀲𑀺𑀥𑀣 𑀕𑁄𑀢𑀫) in Pali , SHAKYAMUNI (𑀲𑀓𑁆𑀬𑀫𑀼𑀦𑀻, i.e. "Sage of the Shakyas") BUDDHA, or simply the BUDDHA (𑀩𑀼𑀥), after the title of _Buddha_, was a monk (śramaṇa),[1][2] mendicant, sage, philosopher, teacher and religious leader on whose teachings Buddhism was founded.[3] He is believed to have lived and taught mostly in the northeastern part of ancient India sometime between the 6th and 4th centuries BCE.
