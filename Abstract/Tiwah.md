COLLECTIE_TROPENMUSEUM_Model_van_een_zielenboot_TMnr_4946-1.jpg TIWAH is the Festival of the Dead of the Ngaju people of Central Kalimantan, Indonesia. It is basically a secondary mortuary rituals, where the bones of the deceased are taken from the cemeteries, purified and finally placed in an ossuary. The feast celebrates the final entry of the deceased into paradise where they would meet the ancestors.
