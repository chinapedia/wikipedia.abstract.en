The INTERNATIONAL MARITIME ORGANIZATION (IMO) (French: _Organisation Maritime Internationale_ (OMI)), known as the INTER-GOVERNMENTAL MARITIME CONSULTATIVE ORGANIZATION (IMCO) until 1982,[1] is a specialised agency of the United Nations responsible for regulating shipping. The IMO was established following agreement at a UN conference held in Geneva in 1948[2] and the IMO came into existence ten years later, meeting for the first time in 1959.[3] Headquartered in London, United Kingdom, the IMO currently has 174 member states and three associate members.[4]
