JAMES MAURY HENSON (September 24, 1936 – May 16, 1990) was an American puppeteer, animator,[1] cartoonist, voice actor, inventor, filmmaker, and screenwriter who achieved worldwide notice as the creator of The Muppets (1955–) and _Fraggle Rock_ (1983–1987); and as the director of _The Dark Crystal_ (1982) and _Labyrinth_ (1986). He was born in Greenville, Mississippi and raised in Leland, Mississippi and University Park, Maryland.[2]
