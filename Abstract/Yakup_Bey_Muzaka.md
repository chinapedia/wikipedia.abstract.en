YAKUP BEY (; 1437–d. September 1442) was the sanjak-bey of Albania in 1437 (during the revolt staged by his father, Teodor III Muzaka),[1] replaced by Hadım Şehabeddin (sanjak-bey until 1439),[2] then served again in 1441–42,[3] until September 1442[4] when he was killed in action along with several other sanjak-beys under the command of Hadım Şehabeddin (since 1439 the beylerbey of the Rumelia Eyalet[5]) at the battle near the Ialomița River against Christian forces under Janos Hunyadi.[6]
