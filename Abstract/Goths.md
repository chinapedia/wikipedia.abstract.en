GothicSoldiersMissoriumOfTheodosius.jpg, made in 388 AD]] Tomb_of_Theodoric_the_Great_Ravenna_(cropped).jpg, a Gothic monarch, in Ravenna, Italy]] The GOTHS (; ) were an East Germanic people, two of whose branches, the Visigoths and the Ostrogoths, played an important role in the fall of the Western Roman Empire through the long series of Gothic Wars and in the emergence of Medieval Europe. The Goths dominated a vast area,[1] which at its peak under the Germanic king Ermanaric and his sub-king Athanaric possibly extended all the way from the Danube to the Don, and from the Baltic Sea to the Black Sea.[2]
