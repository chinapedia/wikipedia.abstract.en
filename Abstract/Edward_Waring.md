EDWARD WARING (15 August 1798) was a British mathematician. He entered Magdalene College, Cambridge as a sizar and became Senior wrangler in 1757. He was elected a Fellow of Magdalene and in 1760 Lucasian Professor of Mathematics, holding the chair until his death. He made the assertion known as Waring's problem without proof in his writings _Meditationes Algebraicae_. Waring was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society in 1763 and awarded the Copley Medal in 1784.
