_EUCALYPTUS CELASTROIDES_, commonly known by the Noongar name of MIRRET,[1] is a species of eucalypt that is endemic to the south-west of Western Australia. It is a mallee or sometimes a tree and has rough bark on about half of the lower half of its tunk, smooth above, narrow lance-shaped adult leaves, flower buds in groups of seven or nine, white flowers and urn-shaped fruit.Eucalyptus_celastroides_-_Flickr_-_Kevin_Thiele_(1).jpgEucalyptus_celastroides_flowers.jpgEucalyptus_celastroides_subsp._celastroides_fruit.jpg
