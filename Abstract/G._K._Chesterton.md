GILBERT KEITH CHESTERTON, KC*SG (29 May 1874 – 14 June 1936), was an English writer,[1] poet, philosopher, dramatist, journalist, orator, lay theologian, biographer, and literary and art critic. Chesterton is often referred to as the "prince of paradox".[2] _Time_ magazine has observed of his writing style: "Whenever possible Chesterton made his points with popular sayings, proverbs, allegories—first carefully turning them inside out."[3]
