The JAPANESE PINE SAWYER (_MONOCHAMUS ALTERNATUS_) is a species of beetle in the family Cerambycidae. It was described by Frederick William Hope in 1842. It is known from Hong Kong, Vietnam, Laos, North Korea, South Korea, Japan, China, and Taiwan.[1] It feeds on _Pinus banksiana_, _Abies firma_, _Pinus armandii_, _Pinus massoniana_, and _Pinus densiflora_. It serves as a vector for the nematode _Bursaphelenchus xylophilus_.[2]
