Thomas_Martin_Lowry.jpg THOMAS MARTIN LOWRY CBE FRS[1] (; 26 October 1874 – 2 November 1936) was an English physical chemist who developed the Brønsted–Lowry acid–base theory simultaneously with and independently of Johannes Nicolaus Brønsted and was a founder-member and president (1928–1930) of the Faraday Society.[2]
