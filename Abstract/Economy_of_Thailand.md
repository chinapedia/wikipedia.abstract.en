Thailand is a newly industrialized country. Its economy is heavily export-dependent, with exports accounting for more than two-thirds of its gross domestic product (GDP). In 2018, according to the World Bank, Thailand had a GDP of 16.316 trillion baht (US$504.9 billion), the 8th largest economy of Asia.[1] As of 2018, Thailand has an average inflation of 1.06% percent[2] and an account surplus of 7.5 percent of the country's GDP.[3] The Thai economy is expected to post 3.8% growth in 2019.[4] Its currency, the Thai Baht, also ranked as the tenth most frequently used world payment currency in 2017.[5]
