HALL OF WATERS, also known as SILOAM PARK AND SPRINGS, is a historic building located at Excelsior Springs, Clay County, Missouri. It was designed by the architectural firm Keene & Simpson and built in 1934 as Public Works Administration Project #5252. It is a five level, reinforced concrete "T"-shaped building with strong Art Deco and Depression Modern features. It features a decorative boiler stack tower with cast stone and an aluminum cap 30 feet high. It is the site of the first spring of many discovered in Excelsior Springs in the 1880s and 1890s.[1]
