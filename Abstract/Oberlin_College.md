OBERLIN COLLEGE is a private liberal arts college and conservatory of music in Oberlin, Ohio. It is the oldest coeducational liberal arts college in the United States and the second oldest continuously operating coeducational institute of higher learning in the world. The Oberlin Conservatory of Music is the oldest continuously operating conservatory in the United States.[1] In 1835 Oberlin became one of the first colleges in the United States to admit African Americans, and in 1837 the first to admit women[2] (other than Franklin College's brief experiment in the 1780s[3]). Today, it its known for its progressive student activism.[4]
