VEENA POOVU (English: Fallen Flower) is a 1907 Malayalam poem written by Kumaran Asan. The poem is considered the beginning of a new era in Malayalam literature, and is one of Asan's most significant works.[1] Various translations and other artistic representations of the poem have been produced, and its centennial was celebrated in Kerala.[2] Composed of forty-one stanzas, the poem is a philosophical treatment of death by looking at the life cycle of a flower.[3]
