ISAIAH 3 is the third chapter of the Book of Isaiah in the Hebrew Bible or the Old Testament of the Christian Bible. This book contains the prophecies attributed to the prophet Isaiah, and is a part of the Book of the Prophets. This chapter describes how the corrupt leadership brought about the collapse of the social condition of Jerusalem and contains Isaiah's prophecies that "For the sin of the people, God will take away the wise men, and give them foolish princes".[1]
