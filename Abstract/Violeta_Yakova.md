VIOLETA JAKOVA or VIOLETA YAKOVA (Bulgarian: ВИОЛЕТА ЯКОВА; June 2, 1923 in Dupnitsa – June 18, 1944 in Radomir) was a Jewish partisan, member of the Bulgarian Communist Party. Jakova, whose _nom de guerre_ was "Ivanka",[1] participated in the assassination of several Nazi and pro-German Bulgarian military officers operational, including the assassination of the chief of the Bulgarian Police, Atanas Pantev and Lieutenant general Hristo Lukov.[2] She was persecuted by the Bulgarian security forces, and in June 1944, injured during clashes with police in the city of Radomir, was captured and tortured to death.
