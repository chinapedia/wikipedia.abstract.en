The CAPHIRIDZANGE EXPLOSION occurred on 17 November 2016.[1][2] 80 people were killed and more than a hundred people injured in a fuel tanker explosion in the town of Caphiridzange in Mozambique's Tete Province.[3][4][5][6][7][8][9] The fuel tanker was en route to Malawi at the time and carried 30,000 liters of gasoline.[10] The government subsequently declared three days of national mourning on 19 November to pay tribute to the victims.[11]
