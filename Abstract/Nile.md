The NILE (, written as al-Nīl; pronounced as an-Nīl) is a major north-flowing river in northeastern Africa, and is the longest river in Africa and the disputed longest river in the world[1][2] (Brazilian government claims that the Amazon River is longer than the Nile).[3][4] The Nile, which is about long, is an "international" river as its drainage basin covers eleven countries, namely, Tanzania, Uganda, Rwanda, Burundi, the Democratic Republic of the Congo, Kenya, Ethiopia, Eritrea, South Sudan, Republic of the Sudan and Egypt.[5] In particular, the Nile is the primary water source of Egypt and Sudan.[6]
