PETE KOSTELNICK (born September 12, 1987) is an American Ultramarathon runner most well known for his world record for fastest coast-to-coast crossing of the United States by foot, in 42 days, six hours and 30 minutes.[1][2][3] In addition he is a two time Badwater Ultramarathon champion,[4] current Badwater course record holder,[5] and the 5th fastest North American, all-time, at the 24-hour run, covering 163.5 miles (an average pace of 8:48 per mile).[6]
