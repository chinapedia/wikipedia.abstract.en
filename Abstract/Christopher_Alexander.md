CHRISTOPHER WOLFGANG ALEXANDER (born 4 October 1936 in Vienna, Austria)[1][2] is a widely influential British-American architect and design theorist, and currently emeritus professor at the University of California, Berkeley. His theories about the nature of human-centered design have affected fields beyond architecture, including urban design, software, sociology and others.[3] Alexander has designed and personally built over 100 buildings, both as an architect and a general contractor.[4][5]
