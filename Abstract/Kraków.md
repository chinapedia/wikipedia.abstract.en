KRAKÓW (, also , ,[1][2] ), is the second largest and one of the oldest cities in Poland. Situated on the Vistula River in the Lesser Poland region, the city dates back to the 7th century.[3] Kraków was the official capital of Poland until 1596[4] and has traditionally been one of the leading centres of Polish academic, economic, cultural and artistic life. Cited as one of Europe's most beautiful cities,[5] its Old Town was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
