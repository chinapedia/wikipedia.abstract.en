Kaiser_Heinrich_V._besucht_seinen_Vater_im_Gefängnis.jpg entered into an agreement with Pope Callixtus II known as the Concordat of Worms.]] The CONCORDAT OF WORMS (),[1] sometimes called the PACTUM CALLIXTINUM by papal historians, was an agreement between Pope Callixtus II and Henry V, Holy Roman Emperor on September 23, 1122, near the city of Worms. It brought to an end the first phase of the power struggle between the Papacy and the Holy Roman Emperors and has been interpreted as containing within itself the germ of nation-based sovereignty that would one day be confirmed in the Peace of Westphalia (1648).
