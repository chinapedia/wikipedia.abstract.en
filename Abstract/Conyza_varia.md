_CONYZA VARIA_ is a species of flowering plants of the family Asteraceae. The species is endemic to Cape Verde.[1] It is listed as an endangered plant by the IUCN.[2] First described as _Erigeron varius_ by Philip Barker Webb, it was placed in the genus _Conyza_ by Hiram Wild in 1969. Its local name is _marcelinha_[3] or _tabua_, _tabuinha_. The plant plays a role in traditional medicine: crushed and heated leaves are used to treat external inflammations.[4]
