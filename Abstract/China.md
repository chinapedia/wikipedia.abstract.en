CHINA (; lit. "Middle Kingdom") , officially the PEOPLE'S REPUBLIC OF CHINA (PRC), is a country in East Asia and the world's most populous country, with a population of around [[Demographics_of_China|{{#expr: According to official data, China's GDP in 2018 was 90 trillion Yuan ($13.28 trillion).[1] Since 2010, China has been the world's second-largest economy by nominal GDP[2] and since 2014, the largest economy in the world by purchasing power parity (PPP).[3] China is also the world's largest exporter and second-largest importer of goods.[4] China is a recognized nuclear weapons state and has the world's largest standing army and second-largest defense budget.[5][6] The PRC is a permanent member of the United Nations Security Council as it replaced the ROC in 1971, as well as an active global partner of ASEAN Plus mechanism. China is also a leading member of numerous formal and informal multilateral organizations, including the Shanghai Cooperation Organization (SCO), WTO, APEC, BRICS, the BCIM, and the G20. China has been characterized as a potential superpower, mainly because of its massive population, economy, and military.[7][8][9]
