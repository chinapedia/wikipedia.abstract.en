ḤASAN IBN AL-HAYTHAM (Latinized as ALHAZEN[1] ;[2] full name ; ) was an Arab[3][4][5][6][7] mathematician, astronomer, and physicist of the Islamic Golden Age.[8] Sometimes called "the father of modern optics",[9][10] he made significant contributions to the principles of optics and visual perception in particular, his most influential work being his _Kitāb al-Manāẓir_ (, "Book of Optics"), written during 1011–1021, which survived in the Latin edition.[11] A polymath, he also wrote on philosophy, theology and medicine.[12]
