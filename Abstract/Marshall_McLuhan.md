HERBERT MARSHALL MCLUHAN (; July 21, 1911December 31, 1980) was a Canadian philosopher. His work is one of the cornerstones of the study of media theory.[1][2] Born in Edmonton, Alberta, McLuhan studied at the University of Manitoba and the University of Cambridge. He began his teaching career as a professor of English at several universities in the U.S. and Canada before moving to the University of Toronto in 1946, where he remained for the rest of his life.
