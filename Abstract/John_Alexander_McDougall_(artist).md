McDougall was good friends with painters George Inness and Asher B. Durand, as well as writers Washington Irving and Edgar Allan Poe, whom he painted.[1][2] His miniatures, some of which were unusually small, are found in the permanent collections of the Metropolitan Museum of Art[3] and the Smithsonian American Art Museum.[4] He died in Newark on July 29, 1894.[5] He had a daughter and five sons, including the cartoonist Walt McDougall, the artist John A. McDougall Jr. (also a miniaturist),<ref name='"Who Was Who 1999"/> and Harry C. McDougall, proprietor of the Newark _Sunday Call._[6]
