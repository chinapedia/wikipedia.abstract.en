Prof._Johann_Heinrich_Alsted_Kupferstich_1610_(DSFSi04).jpg JOHANN HEINRICH ALSTED (March 1588 – November 9, 1638), "the true parent of all the Encyclopædias",[1]s:Budget of Paradoxes/O. was a German-born Transylvanian Saxon Calvinist minister and academic, known for his varied interests: in Ramism and Lullism, pedagogy and encyclopedias, theology and millenarianism. His contemporaries noted that an anagram of Alstedius was _sedulitas_, meaning "hard work" in Latin.[2]
