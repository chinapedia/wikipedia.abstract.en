NEIL ELLWOOD PEART, (; born on September 12, 1952), is a Canadian author and retired musician, best known as the drummer and primary lyricist for the rock band Rush. Peart has received numerous awards for his musical performances, including an induction into the _Modern Drummer_ Hall of Fame in 1983, making him the youngest person ever so honoured.[1] His drumming has been known for its technical proficiency, and his live performances for their exacting nature and stamina.
