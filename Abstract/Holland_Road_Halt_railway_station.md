HOLLAND ROAD HALT was a railway station in Hove, East Sussex, which opened in 1905 and closed in 1956.[1][2] It lay to the west of the original Hove station (1840–1880) and to the east of the current station of that name (1865–present) as well as the Cliftonville Curve.[3] It was mainly used during rush hours by stopping trains to Worthing.[4] Holland_Road_Railway_Bridge_-_geograph.org.uk_-_109997.jpg
