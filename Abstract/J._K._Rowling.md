JOANNE ROWLING ( "rolling";[1] born 31 July 1965), better known by her pen names J. K. ROWLING and ROBERT GALBRAITH, is a British novelist, screenwriter, producer, and philanthropist. She is best known for writing the _Harry Potter_ fantasy series, which has won multiple awards and sold more than 500 million copies,[2][3] becoming the best-selling book series in history.[4] The _Harry Potter_ books have also been the basis for the popular film series of the same name, over which Rowling had overall approval on the scripts[5] and was a producer on the final films.[6]
