QUEVA GRIFFIN (born 10 August 1983[1] - May 2003[2]) was an Irish poet.[3][4][5] A sufferer of Cystic fibrosis, Griffin was a double organ recipient, the first person under-25 to survive a heart-lung double transplant.[6] She published a book of poetry as a fundraiser for the costs associated with her illness.[7] She was named _Young Person of the Year_ in 1998 for her courage during the transplant process.[8][9] She died in May 2003, after picking up an infection.[10]
