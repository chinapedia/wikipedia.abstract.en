He was born in Liverpool and died in Homburg. He appeared in two first-class matches.[2] Bland entered Eton College in 1818, and joined Christ Church college at Oxford in 1823, where he studied Persian language. He graduated with his BA in 1825.[3] He was also a bibliophile and much of the oriental manuscripts at the John Rylands Library can be traced back to Bland.[4] Bland was a member of the Royal Asiatic Society of Great Britain and Ireland.
