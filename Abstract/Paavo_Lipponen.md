PAAVO TAPIO LIPPONEN () (born 23 April 1941) is a Finnish politician and former reporter. He was Prime Minister of Finland from 1995 to 2003,[1] and Chairman of the Social Democratic Party of Finland from 1993 to 2005. He also served as Speaker of the Parliament of Finland from 2003 to 2007[2] and was his party's nominee in the 2012 Finnish presidential election but received only 6.7% of the votes,[3] making it the biggest defeat the Social Democratic Party has ever had in Finnish Presidential elections.[4]
