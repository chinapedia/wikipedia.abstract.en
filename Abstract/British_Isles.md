UTC |timezone1_DST = Western European Summer Time |utc_offset1_DST = +1 |additional_info = |footnotes = }} The BRITISH ISLES are a group of islands in the North Atlantic off the north-western coast of continental Europe that consist of the islands of Great Britain, Ireland, the Isle of Man, the Hebrides and over six thousand smaller isles.[1] They have a total area of about 315,159 km²[2] and a combined population of almost 72 million, and include two sovereign states, the Republic of Ireland (which covers roughly five-sixths of Ireland)[3], and the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland. The islands of Alderney, Jersey, Guernsey, and Sark, and their neighbouring smaller islands, are sometimes also taken to be part of the British Isles,[4] even though, as islands off the coast of France, they do not form part of the archipelago.[5]
