ELIZABETH JEAN "BETSY" REITZ is a zooarchaeologist and professor in the Department of Anthropology at the University of Georgia.[1] In 2012, she was elected a Fellow of the American Association for the Advancement of Science,[2] and in 2014, she was named to the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.[3][4] She was the "recipient of the 2016 Fryxell Award for Interdisciplinary Research in Archaeology... The 2016 Fryxell Award specifically recognizes a scholar who has made significant contributions in the application of the zoological sciences in archaeology."[5] She is a member of the Committee of Honor of the International Council for Zooarchaeology (ICAZ).[6]
