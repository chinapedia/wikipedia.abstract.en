ROBERT I (11 July 1274 – 7 June 1329), popularly known as ROBERT THE BRUCE (Medieval Gaelic: __; modern Scottish Gaelic: __; Norman French: __ or __; Early Scots: _Robert Brus_; ), was King of Scotland from 1306 until his death in 1329. Robert was one of the most famous warriors of his generation, and eventually led Scotland during the First War of Scottish Independence against England. He fought successfully during his reign to regain Scotland's place as an independent country and is today revered in Scotland as a national hero.
