המילה_עברית_בכתב_ובכתב_העברי_הקדום.jpg (bottom)]] HEBREW (; or ) is a Northwest Semitic language native to Israel; the modern version of which is spoken by over 9 million people worldwide.[1] Historically, it is regarded as the language of the Israelites and their ancestors, although the language was not referred to by the name Hebrew in the Tanakh. The earliest examples of written Paleo-Hebrew date from the 10th century BCE.[2] Hebrew belongs to the West Semitic branch of the Afroasiatic language family. Hebrew is the only living Canaanite language left, and the only truly successful example of a revived dead language.[3][4]
