The COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION, referred to in the treaties and other official documents simply as THE COUNCIL, is the third of the seven Institutions of the European Union (EU) as listed in the Treaty on European Union.[1] It is one of three legislative bodies and together with the European Parliament serves to amend and approve the proposals of the European Commission.[2][3][4] The Council represents the executive governments of the EU's member states[5][6] and is based in the Europa building in Brussels.[7]
