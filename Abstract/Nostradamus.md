MICHEL DE NOSTREDAME (depending on the source, 14 or 21 December 1503 – 1 or 2 July 1566), usually Latinised as NOSTRADAMUS, was a French astrologer, physician and reputed seer, who is best known for his book _Les Prophéties_, a collection of 942 poetic quatrains allegedly predicting future events. The book was first published in 1555 and has rarely been out of print since his death.
